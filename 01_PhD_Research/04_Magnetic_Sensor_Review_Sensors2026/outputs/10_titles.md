# Stage 10 — Title candidates for the magnetic-field-sensors review

Target: MDPI *Sensors* (review article). Scope anchors: sensor families
(commercial + pioneering) → applications in energy, transportation,
industrial & manufacturing, biomedicine → future directions (data-driven
modeling, ML control, multi-modality / digital twins) → standards and
commercial potential for a business/investor audience.

Fifteen candidates, deliberately spanning six framing strategies:
(a) broad survey, (b) applications-forward, (c) AI/digital-twin-forward,
(d) devices-to-deployment/standards, (e) crisp/punchy, (f) Special-Issue-ready.

---

1. **Magnetic-Field Sensors: A Review of Device Technologies, Applications, and Emerging Directions**
   *Broad-survey framing (a); the safest, most discoverable classic review title. Appeals primarily to technical readers; keyword-rich for search.*

2. **A Review of Magnetic-Field Sensing Technologies and Their Applications in Energy, Transportation, Industry, and Biomedicine**
   *Applications-forward (b), naming all four domains explicitly. Strong discoverability across each domain's literature; technical audience first, but domain names also pull applied/industry readers.*

3. **Magnetic Sensors from Hall Effect to Quantum Magnetometry: Technologies, Applications, and Outlook**
   *Broad survey (a) with a technology-span hook (established → pioneering). Signals depth of the taxonomy; appeals to technical readers who want the full device landscape.*

4. **Magnetic-Field Sensors for Energy, Transportation, Industrial, and Biomedical Systems: Principles, Performance, and Future Trends**
   *Applications-forward (b) with a principles/performance promise matching the comparison-table core of Section 2. Technical audience; good keyword coverage of both devices and domains.*

5. **Data-Driven and AI-Enabled Magnetic Sensing: A Review of Sensor Technologies, Machine-Learning Methods, and Digital-Twin Integration**
   *Future-forward (c); leads with the Section 3 material (learned calibration, ML readout, twins). Appeals to the ML-instrumentation crossover crowd; risks under-selling the applications survey.*

6. **Magnetic-Field Sensors in the Age of Machine Learning and Digital Twins: A Review of Devices, Applications, and Emerging Methods**
   *Future-forward (c) but keeps devices and applications visible, so it does not misrepresent scope. Both audiences; "age of" phrasing is slightly editorial but readable.*

7. **From Sensing Elements to Deployed Systems: Magnetic-Field Sensor Technologies, Industry Standards, and Commercial Readiness**
   *Devices-to-deployment framing (d); the standards/TRL/qualification angle is in the title itself. Strongest pull for the business/investor audience while staying technically credible.*

8. **Magnetic Sensors from Laboratory to Market: A Review of Technologies, Applications, Standards, and Technology Readiness**
   *Deployment/standards framing (d) with explicit TRL language. Dual-audience by design; useful if the editor should see the business section as a differentiator, not an appendix.*

9. **Measuring Magnetic Fields: Sensor Technologies and Where They Are Used**
   *Crisp/punchy (e). Very readable, low keyword density — memorable but weaker for database discovery; better as a talk title than a journal title unless paired with a subtitle.*

10. **The Magnetic Sensor Landscape: Devices, Markets, and the Road Ahead**
    *Crisp/punchy (e) with a business inflection ("markets"). Appeals to investors and newcomers scanning titles; light on searchable technical nouns, so higher risk for discovery.*

11. **Advances in Magnetic-Field Sensing: Sensor Families, Cross-Domain Applications, and Pathways to Intelligent, Multi-Modal Systems**
    *Special-Issue-ready (f); "Advances in ..." matches recurring MDPI Special-Issue naming, and "intelligent, multi-modal" nods to Section 3. Technical audience; fits a "Sensors in 2026"-type issue.*

12. **Magnetic Sensing Technologies for Emerging Applications: A Review of Devices, Machine-Learning Integration, and Industry Standards**
    *Special-Issue-ready (f); balances all three middle thirds in one line. Dual audience; generic enough to fit several open Special Issues without misstating scope.*

13. **Commercial and Pioneering Magnetic-Field Sensors: A Comparative Review of Performance, Applications, and Maturity**
    *Broad survey (a) with the commercial-vs-pioneering axis — the paper's actual organizing contrast — in the title. Technical readers first; "maturity" quietly flags the TRL/business content.*

14. **Magnetic-Field Sensors Across the Technology Readiness Ladder: Device Physics, Four Application Domains, and Investment Signals**
    *Deployment/business framing (d), the most explicitly investor-facing option. Differentiates strongly from existing sensor reviews; "investment signals" may read as unusual for Sensors, which is both its risk and its point.*

15. **Hall, Magnetoresistive, Fluxgate, and Quantum Magnetometers: A Cross-Domain Review of Applications and Future Intelligent Sensing**
    *Applications+taxonomy hybrid (b); front-loads the family names people actually search for (Hall, TMR-adjacent "magnetoresistive", fluxgate, quantum). Technical audience; maximally search-friendly on device keywords.*

---

## Top 3 picks

1. **#2 — "A Review of Magnetic-Field Sensing Technologies and Their Applications in Energy, Transportation, Industry, and Biomedicine"** — It states exactly what the paper is, names all four domains for discovery, matches MDPI's descriptive-title norm, and cannot be accused of over- or under-claiming. *Subtitle variant:* append "…: From Established Devices to Machine Learning and Digital Twins" if the future-methods third deserves title billing.

2. **#7 — "From Sensing Elements to Deployed Systems: Magnetic-Field Sensor Technologies, Industry Standards, and Commercial Readiness"** — The only shortlist option that makes the paper's genuine differentiator (standards + TRL + investor framing) visible from the title, which helps it stand apart from the many existing magnetic-sensor reviews. *Subtitle variant:* swap the tail to "…Technologies, Applications, and Commercial Readiness" if the four-domain survey needs explicit representation.

3. **#6 — "Magnetic-Field Sensors in the Age of Machine Learning and Digital Twins: A Review of Devices, Applications, and Emerging Methods"** — Best forward-looking hook while the subtitle keeps the full scope honest; likely the strongest click-through among the three and well suited to an AI-in-sensing Special Issue. *Subtitle variant:* "…: Devices, Cross-Domain Applications, and Emerging Methods" to echo the four-domain breadth.

**Recommendation note (unattended decision):** if a single default must be carried into Stage 20, use **#2** — it is the lowest-risk fit for *Sensors*' descriptive-title convention and constrains none of the outline choices.
