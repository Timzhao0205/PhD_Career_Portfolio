# Stage 10 - Fifteen title options

think hard

Read `docs/PROJECT_BRIEF.md` and, if present, `outputs/00_target_journal_brief.md`.

Produce **15** title candidates for this review and write them to
`outputs/10_titles.md`. Requirements:
- All must fit the actual scope: magnetic-field sensor *types* (commercial +
  pioneering), their applications across **energy, transportation, industrial &
  manufacturing, biomedicine**, the future directions (**data-driven modeling,
  ML control, multi-modality / digital twin**), and the standards/potential angle
  for a business+investor audience. A title need not name all of these, but must
  not misrepresent scope.
- Give real variety in strategy, not 15 rewordings of one phrase. Deliberately
  span: (a) broad-survey framing; (b) applications-forward framing naming the
  four domains; (c) future/《AI-and-digital-twin》forward framing; (d) a
  "from devices to deployment/《standards》" framing that signals the business
  angle; (e) a couple of crisp, punchy options; (f) one or two that read well as
  a *Sensors* Special-Issue review.
- MDPI *Sensors* review titles are typically descriptive and keyword-rich (good
  for discovery) rather than clever-but-vague. Favor searchable nouns.
- Avoid over-claiming ("comprehensive", "exhaustive") unless the outline truly
  supports it; avoid buzzword salad.

Format `outputs/10_titles.md` as:
- A numbered list of the 15 titles.
- After each, one short italic line: the angle it takes and who it appeals to
  (technical vs business vs both).
- A closing "Top 3 picks" block with one sentence each on why, and any subtitle
  variants worth considering.

No new literature is required for this stage, so a refs log is optional. Finish
by writing the file.
