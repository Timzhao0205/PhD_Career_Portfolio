## 1. Landscape read

**Assignee x filing-year picture — four populations, no layer overlap:**

- **LEM (CH), densest assignee (5/22 records), component layer.** Transferred-in Danfysik zero-flux detector (2005 priority, displays expired); 2010 dual-mode wide-range closed-loop fluxgate (WO2010131187A1); 2014 packaging claims (US9841441B2, displays expired-fee-related); then a **2021-priority refresh granted twice in 2025**: analog-measurement-channel fluxgate with ADC-ratiometricity reference compensation (US12203964B2) and FPGA 1-bit sigma-delta electronics replacing DSP (US12467957B2). LEM is re-fencing transducer *electronics* through ~2041 — but never claims the converter chain around the component.
- **CN institutes, 2021-2022 wave, system/controller layer.** IHEP/CSNS bidirectional field-to-current (I/B) conversion family (WO2023273577A1 displays Ceased; five CN grants 2023-2026 live to ~2041); IMP standardized ARM+FPGA/EPICS supply controller with runtime-reconfigurable regulator modules (CN115529711B, active to 2042) plus multi-module commissioning/monitoring (CN115356654A, pending). All CN-domestic; no US/EP members observed.
- **CN merchants and newcomers.** Actionpower: 2014 seven-level low-ripple topology (CN104539160B, active to 2034, assigned out to Beijing Lanjun 2020) and 2024 limit-cycle-suppression application (CN119448728A, pending). New this pass: **ShanghaiTech + United Imaging Healthcare co-assigned digital second-harmonic fluxgate** (CN110988430A, granted) — CN entering the component-electronics layer, with medical-magnet pull.
- **Western majors file off-lane.** Philips: expired 0.1-ppm dual-impedance MRI supply (US5570022A) and gradient digital state-space loop claiming mA *dynamic* accuracy, not ppm DC (US20130285664A1). Siemens: service rundown supply (US8699199). FSU/NHMFL: NI-HTS charging feedback (US11551840B2, ~2036). Schneider: dual-core zero-flux component (US20110156697A1, displays active to 2031). Non-filers confirmed both passes: CERN, Danisense, CAEN ELS, IECO, Heinzinger, OCEM, iTest, Bergoz, Kepco, Kikusui — nothing on-point surfaced; Bruker's trim-winding DCCT practice appears in the Muon g-2 TDR, no patent family surfaced.

**Dense vs empty.** Dense: transducer-component electronics (LEM 2021-2025; ShanghaiTech/United Imaging 2019) and CN facility controller/software (2021-2022). Empty on the evidence of both passes: converter-level ppm-stability claims in US/EP by anyone; the integrated converter + DCCT-class feedback + in-situ calibration chain; fleet calibration methods; any ppm+bandwidth+power combination claim.

**CN vs US center of gravity.** Regulation, controller and facility-software claims: CN, domestic-only (consistent with CS-G's no-export-ambition read; U-035 arbitrage stays frozen). Component measurement claims: Western (CH/US with EP/WO/JP families), with CN now probing that layer. Existing US filings are magnet-adjacent (MRI gradient, NI-HTS charging, service tooling) — the US/EU merchant converter-integration lane is un-patrolled on current evidence.

**Expiry horizon (indicative; est. 20y from priority, 10y CN UM).** Unser/CERN zero-flux principle art (1960s-70s) long gone; Danfysik detector chain and Philips ppm loop display expired; State Grid's DCCT test-rig UM expired 2024. Oldest still-live blocking-relevant art: Schneider's 2009-priority dual-core transducer, displayed to ~2031 — lapsing on its face within ~2 years of a 2029/2030 launch. Live perimeter is 2019-2024 priorities: United Imaging fluxgate ~2039, LEM electronics ~2041, IHEP family ~2041, IMP controller ~2042, Actionpower pending ~2044 if granted — each either component-level or CN-domestic.
