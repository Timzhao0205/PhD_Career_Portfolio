## Landscape read

**Assignee × filing-year picture.** Three waves. (1) 1986-88: General Dynamics files the original fusion-magnet metallic-insulation concept, including oxide-windowed shunt paths [US4760365A] — displays expired. (2) 2011-16 academic NI wave: RIST/POSCO's NI race-track with metal surface layers (priority 2011-07-19, the earliest engineered-metal-interface NI found) [KR20130010785A]; MIT's NI multi-width DP stack (2012) [US9117578B2] and partial-insulation hybrid (2013) [US10804018B2]; Northeastern University's CN claim on a specified turn-to-turn resistance band 0.1 Ω-100 kΩ (2012, lapsed 2021) [CN103035354A]; KERI's metal-insulator-transition "smart insulation" (2015) [WO2017039299A1]; FSU's field-feedback charging-delay control (2016) [US20190088391A1]. (3) 2018-2021 fusion-industrial wave, dominated by Tokamak Energy: base PI with windowed conductive interlayer (2018-02) [WO2019150123A1], reverse-current rapid dump (2018-11) [WO2020104807A1], HTS-bridge linked PI (2019-12) [WO2021122522A1], buffer-layer-insulation via edge material removal (2020-05) [CN115485868A], I/Ic-controlled ramping against screening currents (2021-11) [WO2023094611A1]; parallel FSU Rc-coating grant (2018) [US11282624B2] and MIT/CFS solder-filled cable-in-groove (2020) [WO2022019989A2]. A fourth, system-level wave is Chinese: HFIPS files insulated-CICC fusion magnet systems 2023 and — notably — takes them outbound to US/JP grants by 2026 [CN116072372A; US12555710B2], plus 2025 CN utility-model activity at cable level [CN223284772U] and HUST stress-modeling art [WO2024174338A1].

**Dense vs empty.** Dense: coil ARCHITECTURE (what sits between turns — NI, MI, PI, windowed strips, HTS bridges, MIT-material layers, buffer-layer insulation) and OPERATION (charging feedback, reverse-current dump, ramp control). Tokamak Energy is the densest assignee — five fetched families, each granted in multiple jurisdictions — consistent with its CS-H claim of 200+ patents including quench management. Empty in every pass run: process-side claims — nothing found claiming how a winding line achieves or verifies a target Rc (measurement during winding, tension→Rc control, dry-winding process parameters, spatially graded Rc, Rc acceptance protocols). The architecture layer is fenced; the manufacturing/QC layer around it is not (details and bounds below).

**CN vs US center of gravity.** Construction/method claims center on US/UK/KR assignees. In China the striking fact is that the PI/NI operational space is fenced largely by a UK company: TE holds granted CN family members for base PI [CN112041947B, family of WO2019150123A1], linked PI [CN114846566B], rapid dump [CN113330525B], and BLI [CN115485868B]. Domestic CN filings found are system-level (HFIPS CICC route, explicitly not NI [CN116072372A]) or early university art that lapsed [CN103035354A]; no NI/Rc-engineering grant surfaced from 能量奇点, 星环聚能, or 健信超导 in these passes — while HFIPS now enforces system-level claims in the US [US12555710B2]. For a US founder, the CN threat in this cluster is currently inbound-system-level, not Rc-engineering.

**Expiry horizon (indicative only).** The oldest blocking-relevant art displays as already expired [US4760365A — "Expired - Fee Related"; verify before reliance]. The MIT NI/PI core displays expirations ~2033-07/2033-09 [US9117578B2; US10804018B2] — inside a 2029/2030 launch's growth window; the KR 2011 race-track displays 2031. The operative fences (FSU 2038-2039, TE ~2038-2041, HFIPS 2044) run well past launch.
