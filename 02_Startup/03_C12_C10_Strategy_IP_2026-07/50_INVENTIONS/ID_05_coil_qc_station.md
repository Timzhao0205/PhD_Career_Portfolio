# ID_05 — Coil-level post-wind QC station: transient-signature grading of a single wound HTS/NI coil ("TapeStar for coils")

Slot W-06 (20/25, INVENT) · 2026-07-04 · U-008, U-027, U-011 · PL_P04 / DD_C33 · Rev 2 per IPRT_05 · Drafting aid, not claims.

## 1. Title & field

Standalone cryogenic acceptance instrument grading one wound HTS/NI coil via terminal transient excitation and multi-point external field response. Field: SC-coil manufacturing metrology (CPC G01R33/1238, G01R33/1246; H01F41/048 adjacency). Factory acceptance only — never in-service monitoring of energized magnets.

## 2. Problem & demand evidence

Winding and splice defects surface only at cryogenic current, and NI coils hide them from voltage taps (radial bypass) — the information lives in the external field. No coil-level QC station is sold (U-008): Fermilab self-built its Mu2e stack; Cryogenic Ltd tests only its own magnets; everyone else dunk-tests for a single Ic/n pair. Stakes: BNL rejects double-pancakes only after broad 77 K voltage onsets; NHMFL Rc spans 2.22/1.73/1.42 μΩ at <1/3/5 kgf tension; a scrapped fusion TF coil is $0.5-3M (DD_C33 §1). Buyers must trust seller reports (U-027); >20 T test access is scarce (U-011) — 77 K screening is the only scalable gate. Pool: 26-42 orgs, 8-15 realistic buyers, ASP $150-300K (DD_C33 §5) — the C12 QC module and certificate source.

## 3. Inventive concept (what is new)

A station receives a single wound coil in an LN2 fixture and executes at least two distinct stored transient-excitation protocols through its terminals — e.g., constant-rate ramp-hold-decay AND crowbar sudden discharge — recording the external magnetic response at plural locations for each protocol: either a fixed multiplexed array capturing all locations concurrently, or one transient-synchronized scanned sensor (protocol re-executed at each position, records time-registered to a common excitation trigger, reproducibility checked against a stationary reference channel). A grading engine jointly fits ONE few-parameter circuit model of that specific coil (zoned turn-to-turn Rc distribution, joint-resistance inventory, local Ic-depression terms, seeded from its build record) to both responses: the protocols excite distinct relaxation modes — azimuthal Rc anomalies shift local time constants, joints add discrete modes, Ic depressions distort the near-saturation pattern — so the joint fit is well-posed where any single protocol leaves Rc and joint parameters confounded. An identifiability check (stored bound on fitted-parameter uncertainty) gates output: only on pass does the station issue a machine-readable accept/reject certificate carrying extracted manufacturing quantities (zoned Rc, joint nΩ) and predicted charging time constant, referenced against an acceptance record stored before the coil arrived. This two-protocol joint fit with identifiability-gated certification is the residue over single-protocol parametric monitoring and stored-master graders (§4).

**Independent — apparatus.** Both live threats (TE US/CN) are methods on operating magnets — a stored-reference instrument omits their elements structurally; infringement attaches to the vendor at sale/import (Lake Shore-class fast-follower, DD_C33 §4.2); every feature shows on one purchased unit (§9); CN utility-model dual-filing needs apparatus. 1. A test station for grading a wound superconducting coil, comprising: (a) a fixture receiving a single wound coil in a cooling environment maintaining it superconducting; (b) a programmable terminal source executing at least two distinct stored transient-excitation protocols of different excitation classes — for example a ramp-hold-decay and a sudden discharge; (c) acquisition circuitry recording, for each protocol, the coil's external magnetic response at plural locations, either via plural fixed magnetic-field sensing elements recording concurrently, or via at least one sensing element repositioned among the locations with each protocol re-executed at each position and all records time-registered to a common excitation reference; (d) a grading engine jointly fitting a single stored parametric electrical model of the received coil — parameterized at least by turn-to-turn contact-resistance distribution and joint resistance, optionally local critical-current depression — to the recorded responses of the at least two protocols together, extracting parameter values; (e) an identifiability checker computing an uncertainty measure of the extracted values and blocking certification unless the measure satisfies a stored bound; (f) a comparator referencing extracted values against an acceptance reference stored before the coil is received; (g) an output issuing, only on identifiability pass, a machine-readable accept/reject certificate including extracted values and a predicted charging time constant.

Dependents (each from claim 1 only; each survives removal of any other):
2. Fixed species: sensing elements are multiplexed Hall devices on a support conforming to a coil face, with stored per-element cryogenic calibration applied.
3. Scanned species: a motion stage repositions the at least one sensing element; a stationary reference sensor verifies protocol-to-protocol reproducibility before records are composited.
4. The protocols comprise a constant-rate ramp with hold and decay, and a crowbar-initiated sudden discharge; the joint fit separates contact-resistance relaxation modes from joint-resistance modes by their distinct time constants.
5. On identifiability failure the station automatically appends a further protocol (altered ramp rate or discharge level) and refits before reporting indeterminate.
6. The model is partitioned into angular/radial zones; the certificate reports zones exceeding a stored deviation bound (defect localization).
7. The acceptance reference is a statistical envelope from stored records of previously graded coils of the same design; the certificate binds coil serial, joint inventory, Rc statistics, and predicted charging constant at a stated operating point (W-05/W-19 seam); the coil's deviation vector is appended to the store.
