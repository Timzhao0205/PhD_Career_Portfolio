# IPRT_09 — Adversarial review of ID_09 (multi-modal QD instrument)

Red-team run 2026-07-04. Everything cited fetched this mission; fragments/NPL at bottom. KSTAR FED/IEEE texts were paywalled (403/402) — not cited, noted as a counsel search item.

## 1. Closer prior art the disclosure missed

- **[NPL-09-1/-2] ITER magnet-protection engineering pages (iter.org, both fetched).** Deployed QD is already heterogeneous-latency defense-in-depth: primary voltage at 1 kHz that "must request discharge in less than 1.5 seconds"; secondary thermo-hydraulic ("abnormal increase in temperature, low helium flow or reverse helium flow"); tertiary differential-pressure switches; FPGA controllers in redundant pairs computing independently; protection against "faults in the protection system itself." Claim 1 minus self-measured latency is described system practice — PL_P09 Q1's bounded absence held because it searched only patents.
- **[NPL-09-3] LBNL diagnostics page (Marchevsky).** "Cryogenic linear Hall arrays" for "real-time measurements of quench propagation and detection of current sharing irregularities"; acoustic as "an additional level of magnet-protection redundancy." With Teyber 2022 (fetched, IPRT_05 NPL-1), dependent 2's sensing physics is published LBNL practice. Review [NPL-09-6] fetched at abstract depth (MDPI 403).
- **[NPL-09-4] SULTAN HTS conductors, SuST 2023:** taps + CERNOX + co-wound SQD wires + fiber interferometer recorded simultaneously on one HTS fusion conductor.
- **[NPL-09-5] IntelliMIK, Nucl. Fusion 2025:** EAST's deployed CWW primary + model-based secondary compensation (now CNN-LSTM fusing 28 signals), trip at "500 mV for a duration of 1 s" — hold-time confirmation and taps+model QD are published, in-field.
- **US7760102B2 (Siemens, priority 2005-06-10, displays Active to 2027-02-28):** granted multi-modality safety-detector claim — optical + temperature + humidity criteria fusion "to distinguish one or more deceptive phenomena from a hazardous condition," confirmation logic with configurable delays, adaptive thresholds, in the rated-response EN 54 field. The arm/confirm false-trip architecture exists as an instrument claim.
- **US7593205B2 (Pilz, displays Expired-Fee):** safety switching apparatus superimposing "an analog test signal on the analog input signal," digitized, evaluated, driving a failsafe output — element (d) stimulus-injection self-test is IEC 61508-class relay stock (CN sibling CN100570518C).
- **Startorus retry (assigned):** GP assignee query + two site-scoped passes 2026-07-04 — nothing indexed under 星环聚能. Bounded absence stands; 18-month lag caveat; keep the watch.

## 2. Obviousness attack

Strongest 103: ITER/EAST deployed QD [NPL-09-1/-5] gives fast electrical channel + slower confirming channels of other physics + redundancy + budget; US7760102B2 gives multi-modality confirmation-window fusion as an instrument; US7593205B2 gives per-channel test injection; LBNL/Teyber give the Hall array. Every claim-1 element performs its known function; motivation is the field's own tap-blindness literature. §3's bounding line ("automotive sensor fusion never meets the intersection") looked at the wrong neighbor fields — fire/gas instruments and fusion-lab QD are dense. Residue: (i) per-channel latency self-measurement gating a rated-latency status and reconfiguring fusion — in no fetched art, and it is structurally in claim 1(d)/(e); (ii) confirm window bound to NI current-redistribution physics — dependent 2 only. **Internal defect:** claim 1 arms only on a first-channel (tap) threshold, while §2 says NI bypass suppresses tap voltage and dependent 2 says the Hall signature appears "before a resistive tap threshold." In the tap-silent NI event the claimed instrument never arms — the flagship failure mode escapes the claim, and the §7 gate (Hall onset 3× earlier than bridge trip) demonstrates an ordering the claimed logic cannot act on.

## 3. Enablement / budget

$18K taps+Hall is credible; the fiber line is not — a used 4-ch kHz FBG interrogator at $5.5K is optimistic, and no claim requires fiber: cut it from RTP. Weakest BOM assumption: $1,400 of packaged GaAs dies + mux/AFE resolving the small external azimuthal-asymmetry signal of redistribution onset on ~60 m, 77 K pancakes through die-to-die calibration scatter, well enough to beat a tuned bridge 3×. 77 K self-field results may not transfer to 4-20 K MJ-class; pre-register the 3× metric.

## 4. Contamination probe

The "generic EE skill" line holds for the fusion/self-test shell, not the residue: an azimuthally multiplexed cryogenic Hall array with per-die 77 K calibration is the founder's funded magnetometry craft (IPRT_05 flagged DD_C33's "literal PhD craft" phrasing). Calibration-method provenance is again unaddressed (prong 3); a SOW reading onto magnetic diagnostics of SC systems flips prong 1, leaving only the obvious shell. SOW/OTL reading must gate drafting spend. UIUC v0 and Magnefy: clean.

## 5. Design-around

Cheapest: taps+model rack (IntelliMIK-style digital confirmation) — no second sensing element, escapes (b), published EAST practice, and exactly what a Cryomagnetics-class bundler would ship. The §11b hedge independent ("at least one input channel") is not credibly allowable over IntelliMIK + [P-185] persistence + [P-191] hold-period sampling. Second: reorder arming (Hall arms, taps corroborate) escapes the recited order — and is the better NI instrument.

## 6. Strategic value

As sketched, claim 1 blocks none of the Phase-1 incumbent responses: TE's in-magnet methods (outside), Cryomagnetics' free single-modality bundle (outside), taps+model racks (outside). It fences only clones of the founder's exact box — near-vanity — unless the NI-asymmetry confirmation with any-order arming is elevated; that element is simultaneously the 2029 NI/PI protection-attach wedge and the contamination-exposed part. Latency self-measurement + rated status + declared degraded mode is the defensible narrow core.

(5) Drop the FBG line from RTP; fund a Hall calibration rig. (6) Prong-1 SOW/OTL gate before drafting spend + provenance statement for the Hall calibration method (public TapeStar/LBNL practice).
