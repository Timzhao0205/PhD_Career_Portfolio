# ID_12 — Converter-embedded self-calibrating ppm chain: L-aware bounded-deviation calibration states + dissimilar-sensor divergence watchdog

Slot W-17 (20/25, INVENT) · rev 2 (rework per IPRT_12) · 2026-07-04 · U-033, U-018, U-032, U-015 · PL_P06 WS-P06-1/-3 · Drafting aid, not claims.

## 1. Title & field

Magnet power converter that calibrates its own current-feedback chain (transducer + burden + ADC) in situ and in operation, transfer-standard-free. Field: magnet power conversion and measurement calibration (CPC G01R35/00, G01R15/185, G01R19/00, H02M1/0009).

## 2. Problem & demand evidence

ppm without a DCCT stops at ±15 A (iTest ceiling); above that, ppm is a metrology-priced DCCT option (Magna-Power <50 ppm), fleet-hostile at the ±140 A/coil array archetype (U-033). The chain then drifts: labs run transfer-standard recal campaigns (CERN circulates calibration hardware, PL_P06 WS-P06-5; US labs nurse 1970s Transrex fleets, U-018); CERN's licensable ppm folders found one licensee ever, partly because CERN metrology does not travel (U-032); ppm into inductive loads still forces self-builds (ANL 2025, U-015). 100 channels on annual portable-standard recal = 100 metrology events/year plus downtime. The whitespace is bounded, not absolute (IPRT_12): adjacent fields are crowded — instrument ACAL (US4200933A), EV-drive dwell recal (CN101507096B), supply telemetry self-cal (US10666279B1), grid CT winding cal (CN113555201A, surfaced by the first ZH pass on 校准绕组) — and only the magnet-converter binding under continuous-operation constraints is unclaimed in fetched art (WS-P06-1/-3, restated).

## 3. Inventive concept (what is new)

All fetched self-calibration art needs the load quiet, halted, disconnected, or replaced by a calibrated substitute (§4). A superconducting magnet offers no such windows — current must hold its trajectory for hours — but supplies what none of that art uses: a large, well-characterized inductance that integrates volt-seconds into microscopic current deviation. **Element 1 — L-aware bounded-deviation calibration-state synthesis:** the controller, storing or identifying load L, computes and commands calibration states — volt-second-budgeted excitations (balanced pairs, micro-steps, slope segments) sized from L — so chain offset/gain observables are excited while magnet-current deviation stays inside a stored bound: the magnet itself is the integrator that makes in-operation calibration invisible. **Element 2 — dissimilar-sensor divergence watchdog:** because a self-correcting chain can self-corrupt, two output-current sensors of different physics (zero-flux fluxgate + temperature-compensated shunt) are trended continuously; the divergence trend gates every correction's validity and drives a chain-health output. Both elements are conjunctive in claim 1. The winding/reference anchor and dwell/reversal scheduling — each anticipated standalone — remain as fallback dependents. LEM/Schneider fences honored structurally: head bought in, nothing added in-package.

## 5. Claim sketch (plain language; drafting aid, not claims)

**Independent: apparatus** — both elements are structural, visible in one purchased unit (teardown: second sensor; telemetry: health output; terminals: state-synthesis signatures); infringement attaches at vendor sale; CN utility-model dual-filing is apparatus-only. Method mirror = counsel Q1.

1. A power converter for energizing a superconducting or electromagnet load, comprising, in combination: (a) a power stage; (b) a first current sensor (zero-flux/fluxgate class) providing regulation feedback; (c) a second current sensor of a different physical transduction principle (resistive-shunt class) sensing the same output current; (d) a controller that stores or identifies the load inductance and synthesizes calibration states — power-stage excitations whose volt-second content is computed from that inductance — such that offset and gain observables of the feedback chain are excited while load-current deviation from its commanded trajectory remains within a stored bound; (e) a correction engine updating feedback-chain corrections from those states; and (f) a divergence watchdog continuously trending the difference between the two sensors, the trend gating the validity of each correction update and driving a chain-health output.

Dependents (each from claim 1 only; each survives removal of any other):
2. Multi-turn calibration winding through the first sensor's aperture driven by a converter-internal chopper-stabilized reference: absolute ampere-turn gain anchor; watchdog gates anchor acceptance. (Standalone-anticipated: CN113555201A, Danisense NPL; fallback depth.)
3. Scheduler placing zero-current dwells and polarity reversals in load-profile gaps for absolute offset; bounded-deviation states cover the intervals between. (Standalone-anticipated: CN101507096B, US4200933A; fallback depth.)
4. Controller identifies L online from commanded volt-seconds vs observed current slope, rescaling states as L changes; slope disagreement between sensors feeds the watchdog.
5. Stored deviation bound derived from magnet ratings: a configured fraction of quench/trip margin or regulation-tolerance band.
6. Temperature sensors on head electronics, burden, shunt, reference, ADC; corrections and watchdog thresholds temperature-indexed, recursively updated.
7. Per-channel calibration record — events, drift and divergence trajectories, uncertainty bound — emitted over telemetry.
