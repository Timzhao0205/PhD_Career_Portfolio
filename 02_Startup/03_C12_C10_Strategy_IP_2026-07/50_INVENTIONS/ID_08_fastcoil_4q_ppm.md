# ID_08 — FastCoil core: interleaved two-level SiC 4Q converter co-claimed with a PWM-locked, ripple-null-sampled zero-flux regulation chain

## 1. Title & field

Four-quadrant switch-mode converter for superconducting/electromagnet loads: interleaved two-level SiC phases, in-loop zero-flux (DCCT-class) feedback, measurement-chain timing slaved to the switching ensemble. Field: magnet power conversion and precision feedback (CPC H02M3/1584, H02M3/1586, H02M7/797, G01R15/185).

## 3. Inventive concept (what is new)

JINST 2022 + PSI 2010 jointly teach most of pass-1's "binding" story (§4). Untaught anywhere: why the naive assembly fails at SiC edge rates — 10-50 V/ns edges push common-mode current and stray field into the fluxgate's second-harmonic detection band, where unsynchronized residue aliases to a drifting false DC the loop servos onto the magnet. The invention is the timing architecture that removes the alias by construction: fluxgate excitation/demodulation and feedback-ADC sampling form one clock domain phase-locked to the interleaved PWM ensemble, with sampling apertures at the ensemble's computed ripple-null phases where per-phase fundamentals cancel. Locked residue is deterministic — a fixed, auto-zero-removable offset, not drift. Locked-vs-free comparative data carries non-obviousness; the ≥1 kHz limitation excludes line-commutated art (§11).

## 5. Claim sketch (plain language; drafting aid, not claims)

**Independent: apparatus** — observable in one purchased unit (§9); CN utility-model dual-filing is apparatus-only; method claims face detection friction. (f) is PSI-taught, kept only to bind the chain; novelty rides on (g)+(h).

1. A power converter for a superconducting or electromagnet load, comprising: (a) a DC link having two voltage levels; (b) two or more four-quadrant switch-mode phases of wide-bandgap switches paralleled between link and common output, switched with interleaved timing; (c) an output magnetic network combining the phases at a ripple-reduced node; (d) a zero-flux current transducer sensing output current as the loop feedback element; (e) a regulator closing current control on (d) at ≥1 kHz into an inductive load; (f) a stability arrangement holding the chain at ppm-class bounds over ≥8 h: thermally regulated transducer electronics, burden, reference, ADC, plus scheduled re-zeroing; (g) a measurement-chain clock generator phase-locking both the transducer's excitation/demodulation clock and the feedback ADC's sampling clock to the interleaved PWM ensemble; and (h) sampling apertures placed at computed ripple-null phases of the interleave ensemble.

Dependents (each from claim 1 only; each survives removal of any other):
2. The (g) lock at a rational frequency ratio with per-operating-point programmed phase offset; samples blanked around residual switch edges.
3. Intercell transformer, N phases at 360°/N; (h) null phases computed from N and modulation index; recomputed on phase-shedding or module loss.
4. Measurement island: transducer electronics, burden, reference, ADC at ±0.1 K, galvanically separated, CM-choked supplies; the (g) clock crosses the barrier as a fiber link — timing crosses, ground does not.
5. Scheduled zero-current dwells (bipolar zero crossings, or a bypass maintaining load current); offset measured, stored, corrected.
6. Fast shunt/per-phase path frequency-crossed with the transducer path, monotonic handover, bandwidth beyond the transducer's filtered band (W-18 folded in).
