# ID_11 — Terminal-only retrofit protection for third-party NI/PI coils: self-acquired τ-baseline tracking + counter-pulse shaped from that baseline

Slot W-14 (20/25, INVENT) · Rev 2 per IPRT_11, 2026-07-04 · U-025 · PL_P09 WS-P09-3 · PL_P02 WS-P02-3 · Drafting aid, not claims.

## 1. Title & field

Protection apparatus for superconducting coils with turn-to-turn current sharing (NI/MI/PI), operating through ONLY the coil's two end terminals: the unit self-acquires the coil's terminal time-constant/dispersion baseline at commissioning, trips on shifts against it, and answers with a trajectory computed from that same baseline. CPC H02H7/001, H01F6/02, G01R27/02. Magnefy scope limit, explicit: cryogenic superconducting windings only — no transformer-condition-monitoring embodiment, example, or claim language anywhere.

## 2. Problem & demand evidence

"Self-protecting NI" holds at kJ scale only (Thea Canis 4.4 kJ/coil); multi-MJ NI/PI systems have no purchasable active-protection path — proven methods sit captive in magnet sales (U-025, [CS-H-19][CS-H-06][CS-H-07]). Fetched art either needs in-build access (heaters, subsections, fiber, field sensors) or, where terminal-connectable (CLIQ [P-183]), presumes design-time magnet knowledge: waveform fixed by capacitor selection for a known inductance. Owners of existing coils — bought, inherited, self-wound — have two terminals and no datasheet (U-022, U-024). Retrofit onto an uncharacterized coil is the wedge incumbents ignore.

## 3. Inventive concept (what is new)

From its terminals an NI/PI coil is a dispersive network: spiral inductance shunted everywhere by turn-to-turn resistance, knee at f_c = 1/2πτ, τ = L/Rc. The measurand is published (Wang 2013: τ/Rc from terminal transients; Zhang 2017: swept-AC frequency-dependent characteristic resistance), so detection alone cannot carry novelty. The unclaimed residue (IPRT_11): ONE apparatus-self-acquired baseline drives BOTH halves of protection. At commissioning the unit measures the unknown coil through its two terminals — probe spectra plus ramp steps at plural current setpoints — and stores a τ-spectrum/dispersion model. In service, synchronous demodulation of a ≤0.1%-of-transport multi-tone probe is tracked against that baseline (terminal-electrical only; no field sensor; zero construction knowledge). On anomaly, the response stage computes FROM the stored model the amplitude, duration, and spectral/ramp content of a bounded terminal trajectory maximizing dI/dt-driven transfer into the radial path — redistributing current and spreading dissipation volumetrically before breaker+resistor dump. CLIQ injects a predetermined oscillation to heat; this unit shapes a per-coil trajectory from a baseline it measured itself, to redistribute/suppress first, heat as consequence.

## 5. Claim sketch (plain language; drafting aid, not claims)

**Independent: apparatus** — the commercial gap is a shippable box (U-025); method claims would read on coil owners; apparatus supports CN utility-model dual-filing; FSU/TE/CERN art is method-heavy, adding distance.

1. A protection apparatus for a superconducting coil having turn-to-turn current sharing, connectable to the coil solely through its two end terminals, comprising: (a) a perturbation source superimposing a probe excitation on the transport current; (b) a measurement circuit recovering the terminal response; (c) a self-characterization controller that, at commissioning, acquires through the same two terminals a baseline model of the coil's terminal response — a time-constant-spectrum (τ) / dispersion parameterization derived from probe spectra and ramp-step responses at plural transport-current setpoints — and stores it; (d) a detector declaring an anomaly from shift of the measured response versus the stored baseline, using only electrical quantities measured at the two terminals; (e) a response stage applying, on anomaly, a shaped terminal current/voltage trajectory whose waveform parameters (amplitude, duration, spectral or ramp-rate content) are computed from the stored baseline model, driving transport current into the turn-to-turn path to distribute dissipation within the winding under a terminal-voltage bound; (f) a dump interface transferring remaining stored energy to an external absorber. *Old dependents 4-5 merged in per IPRT_11: the self-acquired baseline and the baseline-computed response are now structural limbs.*

Dependents (each from claim 1 only; each survives removal of any other):
2. Series-through two-port between existing supply and coil at full transport current; probe injected/sensed via galvanically isolated coupling; measurement plane defined at the unit's coil-side port.
3. Multi-tone probe ≤0.1% of transport current, tones placed above the baseline knee 1/2πτ where terminal impedance is dominated by the turn-to-turn network; synchronous demodulation.
4. Rate-discriminated tripping: drift slower than a bound adapts the stored baseline; faster shift trips.
5. Passive supplement: between probe intervals the detector also evaluates the coil's response to the supply's own ramp transients against the same stored baseline. *Choice stated: the former passive-ramp-tracking second independent is DEMOTED to this dependent — standalone passive tracking is anticipated by expired US6549017B2 plus published NI charging responses.*
6. Baseline-validity supervision: on supply or lead replacement, source-impedance change, or recalibration event, the apparatus invalidates the stored baseline and repeats the (c) sequence.
7. Dump handoff sequenced from the stored τ: trajectory precedes and overlaps breaker opening into an external resistor; freewheel continuity maintained.
