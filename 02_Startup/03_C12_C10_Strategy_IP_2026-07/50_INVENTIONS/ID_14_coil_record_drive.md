# ID_14 — Coil-record-driven converter/protection parameterization: drive controller computing and enforcing charge/trip envelopes from a per-coil manufacturing record

Slot W-19 (21/25, INVENT; mandated C12×C10 seam; software/data slot #2) · pass 2, 2026-07-04 (IPRT_14 applied) · U-001, U-009 × U-023, U-031, U-025 · PL_P10 WH-P10-1 · PL_P02 WS-P02-3 · Drafting aid, not claims.

## 1. Title & field

Powering/protection system for superconducting coils whose charging trajectory and trip thresholds are computed before first energization from spatially resolved manufacturing measurements — the turn-to-turn contact-resistance (Rc) distribution plus a per-joint resistance inventory (e.g., an ID_07-class record) — and enforced: no verifiable record, no full envelope. Field: SC-magnet supplies and protection (CPC G01R33/3815, H01F6/02, H02H7/001). Magnefy scope limit, explicit: cryogenic superconducting windings only — no transformer-condition-monitoring embodiment, example, or claim language anywhere.

## 2. Problem & demand evidence

Winding data dies before the drive: producer maps stop at the spool (U-009); no commercial line captures per-coil electrical provenance (U-001, severity 5). Drives run blind — datasheet ramps, coil-independent trip floors, or cold identification campaigns; no vendor owns detection-to-dump (U-023: ITER QD = 3,000 sensors, 20+ tenders); catalog supplies offer no coil-aware behavior (U-031); MJ-class NI/PI owners have nothing to buy (U-025). The link is quantitative: τ≈L/Rc and joint dissipation depend on exactly the fields the ID_01-ID_07 cell measures. PL_P10 WH-P10-1 / PL_P02 WS-P02-3: no US/CN/EP/WO family claims a record-consuming drive/protection controller. IPRT_14 sharpened the read: the configuration ACT is 1997 servo art — the whitespace is the computation, not the configuration.

## 3. Inventive concept (what is new)

Not "record configures drive" — US6211639B1 did that in 1997. New is what the record must contain and what the controller must compute from it. The payload is raw, spatially resolved manufacturing measurement — Rc values indexed over the winding and individually measured joint resistances — not stored limit values. A derivation engine computes that coil's envelope through two recited NI-specific relations: (i) the radial-bypass time response from the Rc distribution plus inductance (NI charging-delay relation), bounding dI/dt along the whole trajectory so computed bypass current and bypass heating stay inside a stored limit; (ii) the joint-voltage budget versus current from the inventory, setting per-coil deviation trip thresholds that resolve joints no two-terminal measurement can separate. Both are enforced: absent a verifiable record carrying both fields, the drive refuses or derates — Bosch's enforcement pattern bound to SC-physics computations Bosch cannot reach. The data class is the fence: per-joint and coil-coordinate values are invisible in any terminal aggregate and exist before first cooldown — beyond FSU field-feedback identification [P-028] and terminal-only identification (sibling ID_11's lane). Boundaries, stated in both specs: ID_07's hosts claim record generation; ID_14 claims consumption/derivation only; ID_11 claims probe/response apparatus — deps 5-6 here compare supply-visible responses and claim no probe hardware.

## 5. Claim sketch (plain language; drafting aid, not claims)

**Independent — system (apparatus class), the stronger form:** the enforceable locus is the converter vendor (method claims read on operating labs); power stage + protection unit + recited computation structure anchor US §101; CN outcomes favor hardware-anchored systems (PL_P10 §4: Injet method-only rejected, IHEP system framing granted). Companions, not sketched: E2 gateway device claim (CN utility-model-compatible); method claim third.

1. A powering system for a superconducting coil having turn-to-turn current sharing, comprising: (a) a converter power stage connectable to the coil terminals; (b) a record interface receiving — by any medium: machine-to-machine transfer, data carrier, tag-referenced download, or operator entry of the fields — a manufacturing record associated with the specific coil and comprising both (i) turn-to-turn contact-resistance data spatially resolved over the winding, values indexed by turn and/or angular position, measured or predicted at manufacture — a distribution, not a scalar — and (ii) a joint-resistance inventory listing each joint of the coil with its individually measured resistance; (c) a derivation engine computing, before energization and without in-situ identification or magnetic-field measurement: (c1) from the resolved contact-resistance data and the coil inductance, the coil's radial-bypass time response under the NI charging-delay relation — time constant and transient bypass-current share of the turn-to-turn network — and therefrom a charging trajectory whose ramp rate is bounded throughout such that the computed bypass current and bypass heating remain within a stored limit; and (c2) from the joint-resistance inventory, an expected joint-voltage budget as a function of operating current — summed inventoried resistances times current — and per-coil trip thresholds set as stored deviations from that budget; (d) a protection unit enforcing both computations: ramps executed only within the bounded trajectory, trips declared at the computed thresholds; (e) an interlock whereby, absent a received record containing both (b)(i) and (b)(ii) in verifiable form, the system refuses energization or restricts itself to a derated safe-default envelope.

Dependents (each from claim 1 only; each survives removal of any other):
2. Record further comprising a coil-coordinate defect map; the engine computing a current ceiling and/or position-weighted local ramp derate from mapped entries.
3. The engine partitioning dischargeable stored energy between turn-to-turn internal absorption (computed from the resolved Rc data) and an external absorber; discharge handoff sequenced from the computed radial-bypass response.
4. Provenance: fields (b)(i)-(ii) derived from measurements made during winding and joining, before the coil's first cooldown, the inventory resolving individual joints not separable in any aggregate two-terminal measurement.
5. Cross-check: the system acquiring a terminal-electrical commissioning response (low-current pre-ramp or step; no field quantity) and comparing the identified lumped response against the record-computed bypass response; disparity beyond a stored bound raising a nonconformity flag and safe-default restriction.
6. Drift: in-service charge/discharge responses compared in the controller against record-computed predictions; divergence raising an aging alarm and a derated envelope; alarm events and record identifiers exportable for fleet analysis.
7. Authentication: cryptographic signature verification binding the record to a coil identifier read from the coil; failed verification treated as absence under interlock (e).
