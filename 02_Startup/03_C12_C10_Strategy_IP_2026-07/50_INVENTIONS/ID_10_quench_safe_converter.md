# ID_10 — Quench-safe magnet converter: pre-ramp capacity-verification interlock, self-measured trip-latency contract, modular kA-class shared absorption

Slot W-12 (20/25, INVENT) · Rev 2, 2026-07-04 · U-016, U-024, U-025 · PL_P09 WS-P09-2, PL_P07 WS-P07-3 · IPRT_10 · Drafting aid, not claims.

## 1. Title & field

Magnet power converter system, 0.5-20 kA product class, built from paralleled converter+absorber modules: a hardware verification circuit gates every ramp on self-measured load inductance and measured absorber capacity; the quench-trip interface carries a self-measured, machine-published latency contract; dump duty is shared across modules with terminal continuity through any single-module fault. Field: magnet power conversion and quench protection (CPC H02M7/797, H02H7/001, H01F6/02). Superconducting/electromagnet loads only.

## 2. Problem & demand evidence

Catalog magnet supplies bundle protection only to ±120 A / lab-kJ scale; above that, protection is bespoke system engineering — DTT FDUs: EUR 8M, 1-year design phase; CAENels-class 4Q needs an external crate for stored energy (U-016). ASIPP tendered 2025-09 for a 定型产品 (productized) 100 kA QPS main switch because no off-shelf unit exists at any kA class (U-024) — direct proof buyers want protection as product, and that the 0.5-20 kA band is unserved. MJ-class NI/PI owners have no purchasable active-protection path; TE's methods are captive inside magnet sales (U-025). No vendor owns detection-to-dump latency (U-023). Target: 0.5-20 kA / ≤50 V test-stand and fusion-lab converters, 10 kJ-10 MJ magnets (BASELINE §B, C11 MPMU absorbed).

## 3. Inventive concept (what is new)

Conceded up front (IPRT_10): 4Q magnet supply + QD trip input + fail-safe crowbar/absorber in one cabinet is 20-year LTS catalog practice (Lake Shore 625; AMI 430/601; Cryomagnetics 4G [CS-H-16]) — §4 anchors it. The invention is the residue those boxes verifiably lack, recited structurally and bound to the absorption path. **(a) Pre-ramp stored-energy capacity verification as a hardware interlock:** before each ramp the unit measures the load inductance itself at the magnet terminals, reads absorber thermal state, compares the commanded profile's ½LI² against remaining absorption capacity, and asserts a ramp-permit signal that hardware-gates the stage's ramp command path — a claimed verification circuit and sequence, not firmware policy. **(b) A numeric latency contract at the trip interface:** a deterministic hardware trip-to-clamp path independent of the digital regulator, whose latency the unit periodically self-measures by masked test-trip injection and publishes as a machine-readable rated-latency status; stale or over-budget measurement revokes the permit. This mates with ID_09 across a stated boundary: ID_09 claims the detection instrument (any-modality arm/confirm, per-channel injection self-test, published detection latency); ID_10 claims the actuation side in the same status vocabulary, so a chain budget is the sum of the two published figures — neither claim set recites the other's elements. **(c) Modular kA-class scaling:** paralleled converter+absorber modules share dump duty under coordinated shares with cross-coupled trips; terminal continuity and aggregate absorption survive fault of any single module — the 0.5-20 kA class the ±60 A/±5 V boxes never reach and U-024 proves unserved. Boundary vs ID_08 unchanged: ID_08 claims the precision chain; ID_10 recites a generic 4Q stage and claims protection integration.

## 5. Claim sketch (plain language; drafting aid, not claims)

**Independent: apparatus** — every element is observable on one purchased system (§9); infringement attaches at vendor sale/import (a method would read on operators); CN utility-model dual-filing needs apparatus; against product prior art, distance must be structural.

1. A power converter system for a superconducting-magnet load, comprising: (a) a plurality of paralleled modules, each comprising a four-quadrant switch-mode stage section and an energy-absorption section (dump network and/or routing path to DC link or storage), the modules together rated for the magnet current and for a specified stored energy per a rating protocol defined in the specification; (b) a verification circuit executing before each commanded ramp a sequence that (i) measures load inductance via a test excitation at the magnet terminals, (ii) reads thermal-state sensors of each absorption section, (iii) compares the commanded profile's stored energy, computed from the measured inductance, with remaining absorption capacity, and (iv) asserts a ramp-permit signal only on a passing comparison, the permit hardware-gating the stages' ramp command path, else inhibiting or derating; (c) a trip input coupled to each module's clamp through a deterministic hardware path independent of the digital regulator; (d) a latency self-measurement arrangement periodically injecting a masked test trip through said path, measuring per-module trip-to-clamp latency, and publishing the measured figures as a machine-readable rated-latency status, the ramp permit further conditioned on that measurement being fresh and within a stored budget; (e) the trip inputs cross-coupled so a trip at any module commutates magnet current to the absorption sections of all modules under coordinated dump shares, each module comprising a continuity element whereby magnet-terminal current continuity and aggregate absorption duty are maintained through fault or loss of any single module.

Dependents (each from claim 1 only; each survives removal of any other):
2. The test excitation is a bounded small-signal or ramp-step sequence; measured inductance is also compared against a coil-record value, mismatch beyond tolerance blocking the permit (wrong-magnet / changed-load lockout).
3. Rating protocol = commissioning temperature-rise test of each dump section; thermal sensors plus a stored thermal-mass map yield remaining joules; derate-to-lower-setpoint is the non-inhibit outcome.
4. The masked test trip conducts sub-trip pulses through the clamp, verifying continuity while measuring latency; per-event timestamped records (trip receipt, clamp conduction, stage blocking); the rated-latency status is consumable alongside an upstream detection instrument's published detection latency for a summed chain budget (ID_09 seam).
5. A normally-armed trigger (stored-charge or normally-closed device) engages each module's absorption on auxiliary-power loss, watchdog expiry, or bridge fault, with make-before-break commutation [conceded lab-scale catalog behavior — claimed here per-module].
6. Active routing: the stages invert magnet energy into the DC link and a brake chopper absorbs into bus resistor/storage, with automatic commutation to the passive dump on headroom exhaustion or bridge fault, the handoff gated by the verification circuit's capacity accounting.
7. On a module fault, per-module dump shares are recomputed, the capacity verification re-executed against remaining modules, and the permit re-asserted or revoked; per-module absorbed energy logged per event.
