# ID_04 — REBCO joint fixture with cryogenic nano-ohm verification and two-station carrier-bound joint records

Slot W-04 (21/25, INVENT) · U-010, U-001, U-012 · lane C12 · 2026-07-04 · rev 2 (IPRT_04 rework).

## 1. Title & field

Production equipment that verifies each REBCO tape joint superconducting — cryogen-immersed, contacts engaged from joining through cooldown — gates accept/reject, and binds verdicts to serialized per-joint records; an integrated station (I1) and a two-station carrier-bound system (I2). Field: superconducting-magnet production equipment; nanovolt production metrology.

## 2. Problem & demand evidence

An NI/HTS magnet is a chain of joints: a 32-pancake stack carries ~31 inter-pancake splices plus terminations (能量奇点 archetype [U-001]). A good 4 mm lap (40–80 mm overlap, 30–100 nΩ·cm²) is ~15–60 nΩ at 77 K; a badly wetted one (>µΩ) surfaces at first cold test (days lost). Best published practice is batch QC apart from joining: NHMFL LN2-tested 211 production splices for its 32 T magnet [NPL-1] — demount-then-dunk, no trace binding, no gating. Nothing **sold** verifies at the joining station [U-001]; parameter→R know-how is lab-siloed [U-012]; merchant 1 nΩ weld testers (HIOKI RM3545A class; IPRT_04 NPL-2) are non-cryogenic. Buyers: insert teams, NI coil fabs, fusion coil shops [U-013]. P03: zero families claim in-fixture/production superconducting joint verification (PL_P03 §5).

## 3. Inventive concept

The joining tool owns the joint until proven: (a) heated platen + calibrated clamp form the lap; (b) fixture-positioned current contacts and voltage taps stay engaged from joining through cooldown and measurement, taps standardized outside the current-transfer zone; (c) the engaged joint is immersed and measured superconducting — polarity-reversed sub-Ic current with synchronous extraction cancels µV-class drifting thermal EMFs from the just-heated fixture (50 A × 5 nΩ = 250 nV vs <10 nV floor); (d) accept/reject gated at ≤5 nΩ; (e) serialized per-joint record. At scale, joining and verification split into two stations bound by a serialized carrier and its traveling record — the binding demount-then-dunk lacks; verified traceable joints become an equipment property, not lab discipline.

## 5. Claim sketch (plain-language drafting aid, not claims)

Hardware for both (GROUND RULES §5): visible in one purchased unit or plant tour, enforced against the vendor, no divided infringement; bare methods read on unclaimed four-probe craft and [NPL-1]. Co-equal independents for the provisional (IPRT_04 items 1–2):

- **I1 (apparatus, integrated station).** A joint fabrication-and-verification apparatus for superconducting tape comprising: a joining stage applying controlled temperature and clamping force to overlapped tape ends; fixture-positioned current contacts and voltage taps that remain engaged from joining through cooldown and measurement; a cryogen-immersion stage immersing the engaged joint until the tapes superconduct; a measurement circuit driving polarity-reversing or alternating test current below tape critical current and synchronously extracting the resistive voltage so thermoelectric voltages substantially cancel, resolving 5 nΩ or better; and a controller gating accept/reject while the joint stays engaged.
- **I2 (system, two stations).** A joint production system comprising: a joining station forming a lap joint between superconducting tape ends held on a serialized carrier; a verification station receiving the carrier, immersing the carried joint in cryogen, and measuring resistance in the superconducting state with sub-critical-current reversing current at a 5 nΩ-or-better floor; and a data system keeping a per-joint record keyed to the carrier serial — process trace plus measured resistance and verdict — gating release to coil assembly on the verdict. Co-equal: closes the published demount-then-dunk design-around [NPL-1]; covers split-station lines keeping per-joint traceability, the buyable feature.
