# ID_06 — Self-orienting tape-laying end-effector and strain-model trajectory×tension co-control for non-planar HTS winding

Slot W-07 (20/25, INVENT; urgency #1) · Pass 2, 2026-07-04 · U-003, U-005, U-006 · PL_P05 WH-P05-1/-3 · Drafting aid, not claims.

## 1. Title & field

Tape-laying end-effector for winding REBCO tape onto non-planar formers — head-local twist/camber sensing (non-optical primary, optical alternative), powered compliant correction, lay-point tension trim — plus co-control in which one live edge-strain model is enforced jointly by the head loop and the relative-motion drive. Field: SC-coil manufacturing machinery and robot tooling (CPC H01F41/06, /048, /082, /086, /094; B25J9/16, B25J15/00; adjacent AFP/ATL art at B29C70/38). The H01F41/08x base is OCCUPIED by expired US5547532A; the live gap is the in-process strain loop, not the head as such.

## 2. Problem & demand evidence

REBCO is an anisotropic brittle strip: ~0.4-0.45% irreversible tensile strain; easy-way bend fine to R≈11-25 mm; hard-way bend puts w/2R on the edge (0.1% at R=2 m for 4 mm tape); twist pitch under a few hundred mm costs Ic; tension (5-40 N ≈ 0.01-0.05%) biases the budget. Non-planar formers demand all four at once and the SUM degrades Ic. Today the workpiece or open-loop craft absorbs it: KIT published its dual-robot cell recipe open-access (NPL-1) — buildable by anyone, no in-process strain loop; Proxima fences machined plate-channels [P-082]; Type One forms cable [P-081]; USTC's CN 3D winder is unavailable to non-affiliates (U-003 severity 5; U-005 Ridgway captive into 2029; U-006). Nobody sells a strain-closed-loop tool.

## 3. Inventive concept (what is new)

Direct-wind SC heads laying arbitrary 3D patterns are 1994 art (US5547532A: guide tube, pinch-roller feed, rate-matched supply, apparatus claims 4/13); KIT adds the robotic non-planar HTS cell with closed-loop SUPPLY tension and OFFLINE strain-limited tool orientation (NPL-1); AFP heads sense laid-tape position but correct offline between test layups (US9694546B2 [ID06-B]). Nobody closes an in-process loop on the tape's strain STATE. The invention: a <4 kg flange-mount head whose two spaced sensing stations — gimbaled contact-roller encoder pairs plus capacitive edge electrodes (primary), line-laser edge-profile stations (alternative) — measure twist, camber, and in-plane curvature during laying; an estimator superposes tension + easy-way + hard-way + twist strain terms against a stored tape-lot envelope; the controller spends margin three ways at once: powered series-elastic pose micro-correction (roll ±10°, lateral ±5 mm, yaw ±15°) and voice-coil tension trim (±2 N about the 5-40 N supply setpoint) in a 20-50 Hz loop a decade above the trajectory band, while streaming residual margin to the relative-motion drive — a 6-axis planner, or a former rotary axis whose speed it modulates — for re-planning. The frequency split makes a used COTS arm, or one rotary axis, adequate; the cell winds bare formers, not machined plates.

**Independent — apparatus (end-effector + configured controller).** The method layer is crowded (US5547532A claim 1 is the method base; NPL-1 bars the cell recipe; NPL-2 bars strain-constrained planning as such); apparatus attaches infringement at head sale and every element is visible on one unit (§9). 1. A tape-laying end-effector for winding superconducting tape onto a non-planar former, comprising: (a) a mounting interface to a relative-motion drive — a multi-axis manipulator, a driven former axis, or both; (b) a tape guide path ending in a lay-on element; (c) head-local sensors measuring, during laying, at least twist about the tape's long axis and lateral/in-plane deviation, upstream of the lay-on element; (d) at least one powered compliant correction stage adjusting the tape path in head-local degrees of freedom; (e) a tension trim actuator varying lay-point tension relative to upstream supply tension; and (f) a controller configured to (i) compute a live edge-strain state by superposing at least tension, easy-way-bend, hard-way-bend, and twist strain terms and compare it against a stored tape-lot allowable envelope, (ii) close a correction loop through (d) and (e) at a bandwidth exceeding the trajectory bandwidth of the relative-motion drive, and (iii) stream the remaining strain margin to the relative-motion drive, which modulates speed and/or pose so the superposed state stays inside the envelope.

Dependents (each from claim 1 alone; survives removal of any sibling):
2. Optical sensing: two spaced edge-profile stations; twist from differential edge height, camber/in-plane curvature from edge-centroid trajectory.
3. Non-optical sensing: two gimbaled contact idler rollers whose encoder-read alignment angles give twist; capacitive edge electrodes give lateral deviation.
4. Series-elastic stages whose measured deflection feeds the estimator as a lay-force/disturbance estimate.
5. Retrofit: ISO 9409-1-pattern flange plus a calibration routine identifying lay-on TCP and compliance parameters to a general-purpose motion controller.
6. Two-actuator cell: the relative-motion drive is a former rotation axis whose speed the streamed margin modulates.
7. Recorder: per-position orientation/tension/strain-margin logged against former coordinates into a coil manufacturing record.
