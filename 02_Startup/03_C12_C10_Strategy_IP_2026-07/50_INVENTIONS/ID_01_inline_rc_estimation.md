# ID_01 — Inline turn-to-turn contact-resistance (Rc) estimation and gating during NI/MI REBCO coil winding

Slot W-01 (24/25, INVENT) · pass 2, 2026-07-04 (IPRT_01 applied) · U-001, U-007, U-012 · PL_P01/P02/P04 · Drafting aid, not claims.

## 1. Title & field

During-winding estimation of turn-to-turn contact resistance in NI/MI superconducting coils, with per-turn logging and accept/flag gating, embodied in the winding apparatus. Field: SC-coil manufacturing equipment and in-process electrical metrology (CPC H01F41/048, H01F41/06; G01R33/1238 adjacency).

## 2. Problem & demand evidence

In NI/MI coils turn-to-turn Rc sets charging delay, ramp loss, and quench behavior — the tension recipe IS the electromagnetic design (MIT canonical ~71 μΩ·cm²) — yet no commercial line measures anything electrical between spool and first cold test. U-001 (severity 5): HFIPS's 44.86 T team self-built only a laser-following winding monitor; ASIPP 26.8 T and 能量奇点 32-pancake stacks wound blind. U-007: marketed tension control is a 5-40 N handling band with no electrical output. U-012: tension→Rc knowledge is re-derived by every entrant. A blind pancake commits $10-30K of tape (300-600 m at $20-50/m) before the first Rc number exists; the C12 output spec (predicted Rc ±20%) requires this instrument.

## 3. Inventive concept (what is new)

The cell's existing tape-path metal becomes a four-terminal measurement port across the growing coil: a conductive tension/guide roller — or any coupling upstream of the lay-on point, down to the payoff-spool spindle — contacts the incoming tape (electrode A); the coil inner terminal/mandrel, or a trailing wheel on the last wound turn, is electrode B. A time-varying test current (0.1-1 A, DC-reversal up to ~30 kHz, synchronously detected) driven A-to-B crosses the newest turn-to-turn interface; Kelvin sense contacts null electrode resistance. Interfaces (~5.6 μΩ: 70 μΩ·cm² over 4 mm × π·100 mm) sit far below the ~33 mΩ/turn RT tape resistance, so the terminal response is essentially the radial interface ladder; each laid turn adds one unknown, and a recursive growing-ladder fit (spool-derived tape resistance plus prior estimates) converts the turn-over-turn change into the newest interface's Rc — 3-6 μV at 0.5 A. Two disciplines make that delta resolvable and are core to claim 1: measurement epochs synchronized to former azimuth, so the spiral-arc series resistance — which otherwise swings ~33 mΩ per revolution, four orders above signal — repeats epoch-to-epoch and cancels in the difference; and tape thermometry feeding a tempco correction (Cu stabilizer drifts ~130 μΩ/K/turn). Frequency dependence conditions the fit and tracks re-compaction of earlier interfaces as stack pressure grows. Per-turn estimates are logged and gated against a target band while rework costs one turn, not one coil. Chosen over per-turn taps and optical/pressure/eddy proxies: no new contact class, a true resistance output, well-posed by construction, no cryogenics in line; RT-to-cold prediction is a per-lot calibration transform.

## 5. Claim sketch (plain language; drafting aid, not claims)

**Independent — apparatus.** Still the stronger form: infringement attaches to the competing machine vendor at sale/import, not its factory customers; the feature set is visible on one purchased unit (§9); CN utility-model dual-filing is apparatus-only; CN method claims face subject-matter/detection friction. Per IPRT_01, the pass-1 broad version is obvious over US2703207A/US4416057A-class test-and-gate winding art plus SJTU's estimator — so the ladder recursion and azimuth-synchronized epochs move INTO claim 1.

1. A coil-winding apparatus comprising: (a) a drive rotating a former to wind superconducting tape so successive turns make surface electrical contact, directly or through a co-wound conductive interleave; (b) a first electrical coupling to the tape at any point upstream of the lay-on point, including through the payoff spool or its spindle; (c) a second electrical coupling to at least one previously wound turn or coil terminal; (d) a source driving a time-varying test current between the couplings, crossing at least the newest turn-to-turn interface; (e) a controller acquiring the response during measurement epochs synchronized to the former's angular position, each epoch at a repeatable azimuth of the rotating former; (f) a recursive estimator holding a ladder-network model of the partly wound coil, the model gaining one interface unknown for each newly laid turn or contiguous group of turns, the estimator producing, before winding completes, an updated estimate of turn-to-turn contact resistance for at least the newest interface; (g) a recorder logging each estimate against turn index.

Dependents (each from claim 1 only, so each survives removal of any other):
2. Four-terminal arrangement: separate current and voltage couplings; at least one is a rotating roller or wheel in the tape path or riding a wound turn.
3. Multi-frequency or step-transient test current with synchronous (lock-in) detection; the estimator uses frequency- or time-dependence plus a temperature input to separate interface resistance from along-tape resistance and thermal drift.
4. At least one epoch and exactly one new interface unknown per laid turn, an estimate logged for every turn, and re-estimation of at least one earlier interface to track compaction under growing stack pressure.
5. Comparator gating each estimate against a stored per-turn target band; out-of-band triggers alarm, pause, record marking, or a splice/rework prompt before the next turn covers the interface.
6. Stored per-lot calibration converting room-temperature estimates to predicted operating-temperature Rc, output for the finished coil.
7. At least one of the couplings is contactless: an inductive element magnetically coupled to the conductive loop formed by the tape path and wound turns, injecting or sensing the test signal without galvanic contact.
