(CERN+NTNU), "High precision scalable power converter for accelerator magnets," JINST 17 C03021 (2022) / arXiv:2201.10824 (full text fetched).** Five identical two-level 100 V/100 A SiC-MOSFET bricks paralleled and interleaved at 360°/N, 60 kHz+, explicit 4Q requirement, redundancy via modularity, 0.9 ppm target into a 0.05 H load "without active filters," with the ripple ∝ 1/(N·fsw) law. - **Emmenegger et al. (PSI), "A New Generation of Digital Power Supply Controllers," IPAC'10 WEPD071 (fetched).** Fig. 2: PWM converter "1Q up to 4Q" closing current control through a "Zeroflux Current Transformer (DCCT)" — (d)+(e) at 2.5-10 kHz on corrector supplies. ## 2. Obviousness attack

JINST (interleaved two-level SiC 4Q bricks, ppm target) + PSI DPC (in-loop DCCT, thermal shelf, switch-on auto-cal, isolation, kHz loop) + a Danisense head = claim 1, with explicit motivation (same field, same ppm problem; PSI's controller exists to drive such converters). **(g) is drafted "at least one of" three alternatives and PSI discloses the third (CM isolation)** — the examiner takes the cheapest branch and claim 1 falls without reaching the PWM-locked sensor clock. Locked-vs-free data rescues 2-3, not claim 1.

## 3. Enablement / budget realism

Weakest BOM assumption: the two used-DCCT lines ($3K loop + $4K reference). (i) Sealed catalog heads expose no excitation-clock pin (DS200ID pinout: outputs, status, supplies only) — **the headline PWM-locked-sensor-clock demo cannot run on the purchased transducer**; an accessible-excitation fluxgate head (build/OEM) is required and absent. (ii) Two uncalibrated same-principle fluxgates agreeing at ≤20 ppm proves correlation, not accuracy (16 ppm total, 15 ppm offset, <10 ppm magnetization shifts per the datasheet's ASPC note); shared CM artifacts can flatter the >20 dB claim. ≤10 ppm/8 h honestly lives in the $39.5K STRETCH. 255 evening-hours through 8-h campaigns is thin.

## 5. Design-around

## 6. Strategic value
