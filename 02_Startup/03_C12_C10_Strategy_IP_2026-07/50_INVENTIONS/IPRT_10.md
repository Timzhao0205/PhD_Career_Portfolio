# IPRT_10 — adversarial review of ID_10 (quench-safe 4Q magnet converter)

Red-team run 2026-07-04. New patents fetched this run at Google Patents; NPL fetched at listed URLs. Verdict: **REWORK**.

## 1. Closer prior art the disclosure missed

§4 charted fusion switchgear (Scibreak/NJIT/Siemens) — the wrong neighborhood. The killing art is catalog lab-magnet supplies and drive brake choppers:

- **Lake Shore Model 625** (NPL; catalog since ~2004, manual Rev 1.6, 2015-11-09): true 4Q ±60 A/±5 V superconducting-magnet supply with internal quench detection (dI/dt vs step limit; staff-confirmed 2018), "a remote inhibit line that can be connected to an external quench detection circuit" — a hardware QD trip input — and an output crowbar SCR "thrown" on hardware error/power loss, discharging the magnet with terminal continuity. Claim-1 elements (a), (c)-lite, (d) in one shipped cabinet.
- **AMI Model 601 Energy Absorber** (manual Rev 5, **July 2003**) + **Model 430** (Rev 9, 2017): absorber "designed to operate safely in the event of loss of facility AC power by drawing the necessary power for internal cooling **from the superconducting magnet**," ramping the system to zero at ~5 VDC; sold inside one-part-number integrated systems (05100PS-430-601; 4Q06125PS-430 class) whose 430 programmer offers internal/external quench detection with rampdown. Elements (b)+(d) bundled with a QD trip, 23 years old. Cryomagnetics 4G (corpus CS-H-16: QD built in, auto ramp-down on power loss) was in the mission's own Phase-1 sources and never charted.
- **US7141947B2** (Vacon/Danfoss, priority 2004-12-21, displays Expired): brake chopper = an inverter unit + dump resistors on the DC rails, with startup test pulses verifying resistor connection — dependent-5's continuity self-test, in universal drive art.
- **US7898778B2** (Toshiba, priority 2005-04-19, displays Active to 2027): SMES quench detection whose protective circuit (thyristor/diode short + current-source disconnect + protective resistor) coordinates detection, dump, and converter inside one power-storage unit.
- **CN103560483A** (Jastec, priority 2009-11-04, displays expired-fee): excitation PSU whose protection relay short-circuits its own output and re-matches PSU current to coil current before opening — PSU-integrated, continuity-preserving protection, CN-published; the P09 zh pass ("励磁电源 失超保护") missed it.

## 2. Obviousness attack

625 (4Q magnet PSU + QD trip + fail-safe crowbar) + AMI 601/430 (rated fail-safe absorber in an integrated magnet-supply system) + US7141947B2 (chopper + internal dump + startup continuity pulses) + textbook ½LI² dump sizing = claim 1 and dependents 2/3/5. Residue: pre-ramp absorption-capacity verification (e) and the numeric latency contract (claim 4) — both functional/firmware, attackable as an obvious bookkeeping step over drive brake-power-monitoring practice; "specified maximum latency" is a datasheet number, not structure. The invention that survives is the supervisor + evidence chain, not the converter.

## 3. Enablement / budget realism

$12K at 100-300 A/kJ is close to honest. Weakest BOM line: the **20 mH / 300 A gapped choke at $700** — 0.9 kJ of magnetic storage is tens of kg of iron+copper; surplus at that L·I rating is luck, new-build quotes run $2-5K, founder-winding is uncosted hours. Second: ≥1,000 trips through an SCR crowbar require reset-to-zero every shot — 45 h is ~2x optimistic. The kJ demo cannot exercise claim 7 (paralleled modules, cross-coupled trips); the spec must paper-enable MJ dump sizing and kA crowbar sharing, and 2029 LOIs will rest on unbuilt E2 hardware.

## 4. Contamination probe

Prong 1 "yes-arguable" holds — protection power electronics sits outside a WBG-device + magnetic-instrumentation dissertation, and detection is fenced out of the claims. Soft spots: (i) the bench is deliberately shared with ID_08; if ID_08's lab-instrument tripwire ever fires, this RTP inherits the taint — keep receipts and instruments severable. (ii) The "QD-instrument emulation" comparator board edges toward the founder's diagnostics lane; keep it a dumb harness, interface-level only, out of provisional figures. NI pancake hand-winding plausibly avoids v0, but the unread UIUC/Hinetics agreements must be a pre-drafting gate, not a §10 footnote.

## 5. Design-around ease

Cheapest around: the **smart external crate** (CAENels model) — dump crate with trip input, published latency, fail-safe engage, capacity readout, sold beside any converter; buyer wires them (divided infringement, no vendor target). §11(a)'s "functional integration, not one enclosure" is a squeeze, not a shield: construed to reach converter+crate systems, claim 1 reads onto AMI's 2003 integrated systems (invalid); construed to require one cabinet, the crate escapes. As sketched, the claim does not protect the 2029 test-stand wedge against the incumbents' natural response.

## 6. Strategic value

Of the Phase-1 incumbent responses: CAENels-style crates — around it; project houses' bespoke FDU NRE — untouched; CN institute switch estates and TE captive methods — different layers. A granted claim 1 blocks only literal single-cabinet copies. Real value sits in dependents 4-6 (timestamped trip-to-clamp evidence, pre-ramp ½LI² gating, sub-trip continuity pulses). Not vanity — mis-aimed.

## Verdict: REWORK

Required: (1) rebuild §4 around the bench-supply/drive art above. (2) Claim surgery: promote pre-ramp capacity verification + latency evidence chain + scheduled continuity test pulses into the independent claim, bound to the absorption path; define the joule-rating measurement protocol structurally. (3) Add a system claim on converter+absorber sold/configured as one part number WITH the supervisor gate — distinguishes AMI bundles (no pre-ramp accounting, no latency contract) and catches the smart crate; counsel weighs divided infringement. (4) RTP: reprice or redesign the 20 mH choke; rebudget the trip campaign. (5) §11(b): stop calling fail-safe-on-power-loss the moat — the 625 and the 601 have shipped it for two decades.
