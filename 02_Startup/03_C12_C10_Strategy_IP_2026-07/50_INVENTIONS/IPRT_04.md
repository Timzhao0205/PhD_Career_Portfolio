# IPRT_04 — Red-team review of ID_04 (in-fixture joint verification)

Adversarial posture; ID_04 + W-04 + U-010/U-001/U-012 + PL_P03 read, all new references below fetched 2026-07-04.

## 1. Closer prior art the designer missed (fetched this run; fragments at bottom)

P03's "zero families" holds only *inside* superconductivity. The claimed architecture is old outside it:

- **CN104457841B** (Jilin Univ., priority 2014-11-04, displayed active to 2034): online resistance-spot-weld QC — electrode voltage + weld current → dynamic resistance → cluster analysis → **"qualification probability" prediction**. Welding electrodes = current contacts + voltage sensing engaged through joining; the probability output anticipates D2's concept. Closest single patent.
- **US10942207B2** (GM, priority 2016-04-04, displayed active to 2037): production battery-connection QC — drive current through the weld, measure resistance, compare to a min/max nugget-resistance range, output "acceptable weld"/"failed weld". I1's controller limb, verbatim, in manufacturing.
- **US6064029A** (Inst. for Advanced Engineering, priority 1997-06-04, displayed expired-fee): RSW apparatus deriving a dynamic-resistance curve and gating weld current — resistance-driven closed-loop process control (D5's function) is 1990s welding art.
- **CN103376278A** (GCL, priority 2012-04-27, displayed granted): Li-ion **tab-weld** firmness judged by mΩ-resistance measurement + statistical accept/reject — the zh battery-tab line.
- **NPL-1 Marshall et al., IEEE TAS 32(6) 2022, DOI 10.1109/TASC.2022.3156958** (NHMFL): **211 REBCO lap joints** made under a standard soldering process and resistance-screened for the 32 T magnet (LN2 testing per indexed abstract). §2's "nothing verifies joints during production" overstates — nothing is *sold*, but the practice is published, superconductor-specific, and documents the demount-then-dunk workflow (§5).
- **NPL-2 HIOKI RM3545A/RM3546 product literature**: 4-terminal production weld-resistance testers, "1 nanoohm maximum resolution", EXT I/O / PLC line integration, "weld quality testing is carried out in every process that involves welding" — merchant nΩ-class inline joint verification is a catalog product.
- **NPL-3 Tektronix/Keithley low-resistance technote**: current reversal cancels constant thermoelectric EMFs; 3-point delta cancels drifting ones. I1 element (c) is textbook practice — embodied in the BOM's own 2182A.

## 2. Obviousness attack

Primary: KR101374212B1 [P-050] or CN1293986C [P-057] — REBCO joining fixture, heated platen, calibrated clamp. Secondary: CN104457841B / US10942207B2 — measure joint resistance at the joining station and gate accept/reject. Motivation is supplied by the field itself: NHMFL already resistance-screens every production splice (NPL-1); HIOKI sells the inline tester (NPL-2). Every element performs its known function with a predictable result — a clean KSR combination. The asserted nonobvious residue (nV floor on a hot fixture, EMF cancellation, RT→77 K transfer) sits in **dependents and spec, not I1**: reversal cancellation is NPL-3, the nV floor is a purchased 2182A, and I1's only superconductor hooks are an intended-use preamble plus functional "substantially cancel" language met by any delta-mode ohmmeter bolted to a welder. D1 (cryo-immersion with contacts engaged) is the survivable core; I1 as sketched likely dies.

## 3. Enablement / budget realism

Weakest BOM line: **$800 for "150 A used supply + founder-built regulator, H-bridge reverser, 0.1% shunt."** A 50–100 A reversing source that settles inside the <10 nV window without inductive kick — on a fixture loop with boiling-LN2 microphonics — is the actual hard instrument; used 150 A supplies are mV-noise switchers and a 100 A 0.1% shunt self-heats 10–50 W. Realistic ≥$2–3K (total stays <$25K). Second: 30 m tape cannot yield "~30 joints" plus a "100-joint classifier dataset" (≥33–39 m before scrap). Third, science: the RT screen must resolve ~µΩ anomalies under a 6–12 mΩ copper-dominated span with %-level lot variation, while the 77 K-dominant REBCO–Ag interface term has **no published RT correlate** — D2 is asserted, not evidenced. Boil-transient EMFs are not quasi-static; minutes-scale settle after dunk is the demo risk.

## 5. Design-around

Cheapest: solder in a dumb fixture, demount, batch-test in a separate LN2 dunk station with pass/fail — the exact published NHMFL workflow; cost = one carrier transfer and minutes. Reads on nothing in I1 ("remaining engaged from joining through measurement"). ID_04 §11(a) concedes this yet **defers the two-station system claim to the non-provisional** — so the provisional as sketched does not hold the wedge, and the standalone dunk station (WS-P03-4) is left unclaimed for anyone.

## 6. Strategic value

Against in-house fabs an apparatus claim is near-undetectable (nothing purchasable to inspect). Its real function is fencing merchant equipment rivals (Ridgway/TE-captive, 曦合/原力辰-class) out of adding verification to solder fixtures — which works only if the adjacent-station variant is fenced too. As drafted: vanity-filing risk. With the system claim in the priority text plus D1 folded into the independent body: a genuine 2029-wedge asset that also feeds the W-05/ID_07 birth-certificate layer.

## Verdict: REWORK

1. Promote the joining-station + verification-station **system claim** (shared serialized carrier, per-joint record, closed-loop trim) into the provisional as a co-equal independent — the demount workaround is published practice (NPL-1).
2. Move superconductor-specific limitations into I1's **body**: cryogenic immersion with contacts engaged, test current below tape Ic, ≤5 nΩ resolution — the preamble alone won't survive CN104457841B + [P-050] + NPL-2.
3. Data-back or demote **D2**: produce the single-lot RT→77 K worked example before 2027-05 or keep the classifier trade-secret only.
4. Re-price the reversing source ($2–3K), add ~15 m tape margin, and distinguish CN104457841B / US10942207B2 / US6064029A in the spec background.
