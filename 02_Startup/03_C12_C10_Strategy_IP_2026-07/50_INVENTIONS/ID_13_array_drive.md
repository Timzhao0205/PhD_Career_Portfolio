# ID_13 — Multi-channel per-coil 4Q array drive with per-channel protection and coordinated fault sequencing

Slot W-15 (19/25, INVENT) · 2026-07-04 Rev 2 post-IPRT_13 · U-017, U-025, U-031 · PL_P08 WS-1/WS-5 · Drafting aid, not claims.

## 1. Title & field

N-channel true-four-quadrant converter apparatus for arrays of independently regulated superconducting coils: per-coil 4Q channels on a shared DC bus and thermal spine, superconducting-load-specific per-channel protection, array-level fault choreography in the drive electronics. Field: magnet power conversion and protection (CPC H02J1/102, H01F7/064, H02M7/797 — neighborhood verified in PL_P08 §1).

## 2. Problem & demand evidence

Coil arrays multiply precision bipolar channels — ±140 A × 9 coils at Thea's Canis, hundreds at Eos scale — with no catalog multi-channel bipolar drive: Canis ran unipolar supplies plus mechanical relay H-bridges, itself a documented fault hazard (U-017). Multi-MJ NI/PI systems lack purchasable active protection (U-025); no >1 kW catalog family offers synchronized array control or telemetry streaming (U-031). RMP/trim retrofits, WHAM-class mirrors, and university arrays inherit the gap.

## 3. Inventive concept

Claim the structural triad that Canis-class practice and multi-axis servo art lack. (i) N ≥ 4 integrated true-4Q channels — no electromechanical polarity device in any load path — on one shared DC bus with inter-channel energy recycling: a ramping-down coil's energy routes through the bus into a concurrently ramping-up coil under bus-voltage envelope control; independent per-coil supplies structurally cannot exchange coil energy. (ii) Superconducting-load-rated freewheel continuity, dwell-rated per a stated protocol and held through single-channel fault and hot-swap by a make-before-break slot bypass — the opposite of relay isolation (opens the loop) and servo safe-cutoff (kills drive). (iii) A hardware quench-trip crosslink plus per-channel stored-response mechanism: trips propagate independent of any supervisory processor; survivors execute stored re-shape/derate patterns while the faulted channel executes its dump — a claimed signal path and mechanism, not supervision software in general.

## 5. Claim sketch (plain language; drafting aid, not claims)

**Independent: apparatus** — the triad is visible in one purchased cabinet (§9), infringed at vendor sale/import; a method reads on customers (divided infringement, CN friction) and forfeits CN utility-model dual-filing. N ≥ 4 keeps scope on arrays.

1. A power apparatus for an array of superconducting coils, comprising: (a) a shared DC bus feeding at least four integrated four-quadrant switch-mode channels, each bidirectional in current into its coil with no electromechanical polarity-reversal device in any load path; (b) a recycling regulator routing energy recovered from any ramping-down coil through the bus into any concurrently ramping-up coil while holding bus voltage inside a stored envelope; (c) per channel, a freewheel path rated per the dwell protocol below, and a make-before-break slot bypass maintaining freewheel continuity through single-channel fault and through channel extraction/replacement during array operation; (d) a hardware quench-trip crosslink conveying each channel's quench/fault signal to all other channels independently of any supervisory processor; (e) per channel, a stored-response mechanism that, on a crosslink trip, executes a stored current re-shape or derate pattern while the tripped channel executes its discharge path.

*Dwell protocol (definiteness anchor):* the path conducts the coil's rated current decaying over ≥3×L/R with junction temperature below rated maximum, verified by continuous conduction at rated current for equivalent seconds.

Dependents (each on claim 1 only; each survives removal of any other):
2. Backplane subassembly: bus, bypasses, and crosslink form a backplane preserving continuity and trip propagation with any channel module removed (catches de-integrated bricks).
3. Choreography modes: ≥2 stored patterns per channel (topology-preserving re-shape; whole-array derate/ramp-down), selected by fault class and remaining energy budget.
4. Budgeted discharge: each discharge element governed by a stored ½LI² budget selecting dump versus freewheel-only response.
5. Measurement option: zero-flux (DCCT-class) socket interchangeable with a shunt-grade sensor, protection unchanged.
