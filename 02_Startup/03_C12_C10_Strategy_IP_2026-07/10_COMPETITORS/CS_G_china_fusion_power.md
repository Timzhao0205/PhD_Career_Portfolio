## Segment structure read
Three tiers, three axes. (1) GW/pulsed EPC axis: 荣信汇科, 许继, 通达, 新风光 fight for ¥50-150M magnet-power and storage lots; winning bids land at 44-95% of budget (¥5.24M/¥12M; ¥76.91M/¥108M; ¥79.8M/¥81M) — a lowest-technically-acceptable price war that listed players' margins already show [CS-G-02][CS-G-03][CS-G-05]. (2) Precision fast/aux axis: effectively 爱科赛博 alone among listed firms (fast-control/correction/RMP), with 华耀 (PSM/RF) and 英杰 (heating/institute supplies) at the edges — yet even the leader loses money and sells fusion as customization, not catalog. (3) Institutional axis: ASIPP/SWIP capture design IP and prime international contracts (MCTB, ELM-PS); companies execute. Demand is a tender fire-hose (Dec-1-12-2025 awards alone ¥2.40B; Nov-2025 tenders ~¥3.9B [CS-G-05]) but it is domestic-champion-only and episodic. The axis NOBODY serves: productized, exportable, fast-delivery precision converters (catalog family, published specs/prices, weeks-class lead time) and storage-integrated mid-scale (0.1-10 MW) ramp supplies — IMP's 4.5-yr self-build and the institute-prime pattern are the demand-side proof [CS-G-06][CS-G-10]. Nobody here can or will sell into US/EU fusion (英杰 explicitly: no international plans [CS-G-11]) — the founder's exploitable asymmetry is reading this market's prices (≈$0.7M for a BEST fast-control package) while selling Western lead-time relief.
