# CS-E — West Precision Catalog Power Supplies (magnet/lab class, incl. bipolar niche)

Round 3 Phase 1 Wave 2. All specs/prices fetched 2026-07-04 from vendor/distributor pages; refs = CS_E_sources.json. Charter mandates: (1) verify reported Kepco BOP wind-down + who captured the bipolar-lab niche; (2) map exactly where the catalog envelope (power x precision x dynamics) stops.

**Mandate-1 verdict (Kepco).** No public wind-down/closure announcement is findable on the open web (searched en, multiple phrasings; Kepco's own news archive stops 2015). What IS verifiable: family-held Kepco Inc. (Flushing NY, f.1946) was acquired by Process Technology (Willoughby OH) on 2025-03-06 with "business as usual" continuity language [CS-E-01]; brand now "KEPCO Dynatronix" [CS-E-26]. The BOP 4-quadrant line survives but is frozen at its legacy linear envelope (±100 V, ≤20 A, ≤1 kW) [CS-E-02][CS-E-03]; first post-acquisition product energy went to the industrial HSP line (2025-09) [CS-E-04]. The bipolar-lab niche was NOT captured by one player — it fragmented: Kikusui PBZ BP took the high-current 4Q bench (±200 A/4 kW), IECO+GMW hold 6 kW 4Q with DCCT, AE Techron holds the amplifier/EMC end, Matsusada the fast low-power end. Spectrum Instruments = false lead (GPS timing firm, no Kepco tie). EA and TDK trails end in EV-test/1Q product lines, not bipolar capture.

## Segment structure — where the catalog world stops

**Axes and owners.** Precision axis: Heinzinger PCU (5 ppm) and Danfysik 9700 (10 ppm) win — but both are unipolar-DC-slow, bandwidth unpublished [CS-E-09][CS-E-11]. Dynamics axis: CAENels wins (2-5 kHz loops, SFP setpoint streaming, EPICS) — capped at 1.5 kW [CS-E-06][CS-E-07]. Power axis: Delta SM15K and TDK GENESYS+ win (15 kW/unit, kA by paralleling to 60-900 kW) — at 50-100 ppm, 1-2Q, ms programming [CS-E-13][CS-E-16]. Bipolar 4Q axis: bench-capped — Kikusui 4 kW [CS-E-17], IECO BPS 6 kW [CS-E-21][CS-E-22], Matsusada 2 kW [CS-E-18], Kepco 1 kW linear [CS-E-02]. Orthogonal: iseg (Radeberg DE) owns HV-precision (kV/mA, ripple 5E-7); explicitly no magnet-current products [CS-E-24]. Volume capital went elsewhere: Fortive paid $1.45 B for EA (2024-01-03) for EV/hydrogen 2Q regenerative test power under Tektronix [CS-E-20]; CN entrants (ITECH 2Q regenerative, 3ctest EMC 4Q bench [CS-E-25]) push price down from below, none ppm/scientific.

**The empty cell:** true 4Q x >=10 kW/unit x <=10 ppm (DCCT chain) x >=2 kHz closed loop x fleet/EPICS telemetry x weeks-not-quarters delivery. Nobody in this segment sells it; combining any three of those properties today forces either CAENels (power-capped), a paralleled 2Q workaround (precision-capped), or the CS-F custom-quote world (OCEM/Danfysik-custom/JEMA, 12-18 mo class — prior corpus DD_C10 §4). Customer suffering is documented, not speculative: ANL's 2025 nuCARIBU upgrade bought catalog SMPS, lost current-setting resolution, and had to self-build a sub-mA parallel current-sink trimmer [CS-E-23]. **Kepco gap net:** the wind-down report is unconfirmed as an event, but the strategic vacancy is real — the BOP line persists under an industrial-rectifier owner with legacy specs and no precision roadmap; its >20 A/fast-4Q successor demand is being split among Kikusui/IECO/AE Techron with no ppm-class winner. That vacancy is exactly the C10 FastCoil wedge.
