Written 2026-07-04. Sources: `CS_C_sources.json` ([CS-C-##]; load-bearing = fetched). Question: who sells coils/magnets instead of machines, to whom, at what signal prices — and what that removes from the buyable winding-machine market (C12) and implies for coil-array drive (C10).

## Card 1 — Commonwealth Fusion Systems (CFS), Devens MA

- **Identity/funding:** best-capitalized private fusion firm; magnet factory = "seven years and hundreds of millions of dollars" [CS-C-01].
- **Product line:** HTS magnets as product. Delivered WHAM mirror magnets (17 T record, first plasma 2024) [CS-C-02]. SPARC 70% complete; factory now runs SPARC + external orders simultaneously [CS-C-01].
- **Price signals:** Realta partnership (2026-04-02) "potential to reach a multi-billion dollar value"; magnets for demos AND commercial plants, plus talent-sharing [CS-C-02]. "Largest deal of this kind to date" — CCO Needham [CS-C-01].
- **2024-2026 moves:** WHAM (2024) → Type One VIPER-cable license (2025-02-11), **exclusive for stellarators** — CFS agreed not to supply its cable tech to other stellarator firms [CS-C-03] → Realta (2026-04).
- **Channel:** direct strategic partnerships + technology licensing; no catalog, no machines.
- **Patenting posture (Phase-2 assignees, do not fetch now):** "Commonwealth Fusion Systems LLC"; early VIPER/TF lineage under "Massachusetts Institute of Technology"; zh media name 联邦聚变系统 (check CN filings under both).
- **Weaknesses/gaps:** sells black-box coils, zero process/recipe transparency; Type One got a license, not magnets — manufacturing "could lead to that one day" [CS-C-01]; stellarator coil supply self-foreclosed by exclusivity [CS-C-03]; external buyers share capacity with SPARC ramp [CS-C-01]; prices opaque.

## Segment structure — what coils-as-product does to machine demand

Coils are now purchasable at three tiers: reactor-scale strategic deals (CFS-Realta, multi-billion potential [CS-C-02]), state/defense programs (TE: STEP £70M, DARPA PUMP [CS-C-04][CS-C-05]), and boutique custom (HTS-110, Canyon [CS-C-18][CS-C-19]). **Removed from the C12 machine market:** the mirror archetype and CFS-adjacent labs (buy coils); UK national program (TE); defense MHD (TE via GA); Chinese startups (self-build — ES, Startorus two winder generations [CS-C-17]); European leaders (Proxima self-equips; Thea redesigned the problem into planar arrays it mass-produces itself). That is the top ~8-10 budgets, confirming the Round-2 count.

**Where neutral-vendor demand survives — the axes incumbents structurally cannot serve:** (1) **Neutrality/IP:** the only Western merchant machine line is competitor-owned and does not disclose it [CS-C-07]; second-wave startups buying there leak process intent to a rival. (2) **Stellarator/non-planar:** CFS's cable tech is contractually exclusive to Type One among stellarators [CS-C-03]; Proxima's self-equipping proves no merchant alternative [CS-C-09]. (3) **Recipe/QC transparency:** every integrator sells (or keeps) coils as black boxes; nobody sells the tension→Rc process layer or per-coil QC data — the C33 attach space. (4) **>20 T merchant test access:** ES is productizing it for the Chinese market [CS-C-13]; the West has no equivalent batch service. (5) **Coil-array drive (C10):** Thea-class architectures multiply precision bipolar channels (±140 A/coil) with no catalog multi-channel vendor [CS-C-10]. The unserved axis is a **neutral coil foundry / winding cell with published QC + delivery SLA** — exactly the wedge between "buy black-box coils from a competitor" and "build a magnet team from scratch."
