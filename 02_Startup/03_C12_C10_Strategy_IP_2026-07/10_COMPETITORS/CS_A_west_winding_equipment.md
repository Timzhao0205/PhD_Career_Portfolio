# CS-A — West Winding & Coil-Production Equipment (verified 2026-07-04)

Charter question: after Tokamak Energy–Ridgway (2025-09), does any vendor-neutral REBCO/NI-native winding-equipment seller exist in the West? **Evidenced answer: no productized one.** Neutral supply = two micro-bespoke shops (Winding Services Ltd, Supertek) selling made-to-order mechanicals with no NI/Rc process layer; Broomfield markets fusion but specs only LTS wire. UKAEA/STEP itself commissioned a bespoke small REBCO winder (~£270K) [CS-A-11][CS-A-12]; KIT built its own robot cell [CS-A-13]; China already has a merchant vendor that delivered a 3D non-planar HTS winder to USTC [CS-A-15].

## Card 1 — TE Magnetics / Ridgway Machines (Leicester, UK) — captive incumbent
- **Identity:** Ridgway Machines Ltd, founded 1920; acquired by Tokamak Energy 2025-09-22, brand/site/workforce retained; new MD Mike Allen 2026-04 (ex-Rolls-Royce) [CS-A-01][CS-A-02].
- **Product/specs:** taping & insulation lines (Kapton/glass, multi-head); 2D and multi-axis 3D coil winders (fixtures to 400 mm dia); HTS cable wrap lines; pay-offs to 48 spools with load-cell tension feedback; cassette rewinder 4–12 mm tape, 5–40 N, 10 m/min. Customers: ITER (CS/PF/TF wrap), CERN, PSI, General Atomics. No REBCO/NI or during-winding QC claims on the product page [CS-A-04].
- **Price signals:** "multimillion-pound" taping-machinery contracts (2020, US customer) [CS-A-05]; no list prices.
- **2024–26 moves:** acquisition to scale TE Magnetics to production [CS-A-01]; TE named STEP Magnet Systems Partner, £70M to 2029-03, Ridgway the manufacturing accelerant — "the leading provider of bespoke equipment for superconducting magnets and cable production" [CS-A-03]; "strong start to 2026," expansion planned [CS-A-02].
- **Patent posture (Phase-2 variants):** Tokamak Energy Ltd/Limited; Ridgway Machines Ltd/Limited; zh 托卡马克能源. TE = active filer; check machine-side claims in P01/P05.
- **Channel/lead time:** direct bespoke projects; capacity shared with TE's own STEP load [CS-A-03].
- **Weaknesses (evidence):** neutrality gone — owned by a fusion competitor; Proxima ordered its own in-house cable line instead (2025-09) [CS-A-14]; product page still insulation-era, no NI/Rc layer [CS-A-04]; internal £70M program competes for Leicester capacity [CS-A-03].

## Segment structure — who wins on which axis; the empty axis

**Axis 1, bespoke mechanical NRE — Ridgway wins, now captive.** The only ITER/CERN/GA-referenced SC winding+taping machinery org sits inside Tokamak Energy, committed to £70M of STEP work to 2029 [CS-A-03][CS-A-04]. Rival fusion buyers inherit a conflict; Proxima's in-house line is the rational response [CS-A-14].

**Axis 2, catalog precision winders — Broomfield wins, LTS/copper only.** Owns the "buy from a catalog" motion; publishes zero REBCO capability [CS-A-06][CS-A-07]. Its new stellarator page says it smells fusion money — most likely fast-follower if a category forms.

**Axis 3, neutral HTS-capable bespoke — Supertek and Winding Services Ltd, micro-scale.** Quote-only machine shops, no NI/Rc/QC/software claims [CS-A-08][CS-A-10]. STEP's ~£270K award to a family firm is at once price anchor, demand proof, and proof no productized alternative existed [CS-A-11][CS-A-12].

**Axis 4, process/QC layer — nobody in the West.** No fetched vendor page claims during-winding Rc estimation, defect-map-aware feed, joint verification, or recipe software; the process knowledge lives in buyer labs (KIT cell; UKAEA "develop and establish" spec) [CS-A-13][CS-A-11]. 原力辰 productized non-planar mechanicals but publishes no QC layer either [CS-A-15].

**Empty axis = C12 thesis:** vendor-neutral NI/REBCO-native winding cell at a transparent £250K–1M point with inline QC + recipe/traceability software and quoted lead times. Buyers (STEP, KIT, USTC) are already spending to fill it themselves. Window cap: Broomfield's marketing drift, Aumann/Marsilli patent estates, and CN clone speed [CS-A-15] — roughly 2–3 years after the first public product.
