## 2. Field of the Invention

The invention relates to no-insulation (NI) and metal-co-wound high-temperature
superconductor (HTS) coils that are **conduction-cooled by a cryocooler cold head with a
finite rated cooling capacity — with no liquid-cryogen bath (no LN₂, no LHe bath)**. More
specifically, it relates to the design of the turn-to-turn interface layer that
simultaneously governs (i) the inter-turn contact resistance R_c (the radial electrical
bypass that sets charging/ramp behavior, field quality, and self-protection), and (ii) the
radial thermal drain by which locally generated heat reaches the cold head.

---

## 3. Problem / Background

### 3.1 The cryogen-free thermal buffer is gone
In a bath-cooled magnet, a surrounding liquid cryogen supplies a large distributed thermal
buffer: local disturbances are absorbed by boil-off and the coil is quasi-isothermal. In a
**conduction-cooled** coil there is no bath. Heat generated anywhere in the winding must
travel by **solid conduction** through the winding to the cold head, and the cryocooler can
remove only its rated lift (its cold-head power budget) at a given temperature. Thermal
margin is therefore low and strongly **location-dependent**, and hot spots are dangerous.
This is the regime of the anchor exemplar class: conduction-cooled NI racetrack field coils
for rotating machines (Hinetics CRUISE-class), and more broadly conduction-cooled HTS
windings for inserts, cables, and accelerator/confinement coils.

### 3.2 The single-layer, two-job conflict (the core insight)
In an NI (or metal-co-wound) coil the **turn-to-turn interface is a single physical design
layer that is forced to do two conflicting jobs at once**:

- **Electrical job.** The interface sets the inter-turn contact resistivity ρ_ct (hence R_c).
  R_c governs the radial bypass current — it sets the charging/ramp time constant, field
  homogeneity, and passive self-protection. A **higher** R_c (e.g. stainless
  metal-insulation co-wind) **shortens** the charging delay of the coil (this is precisely
  why metal-insulation windings were introduced over bare-contact NI). [SIMULATED-supported;
  see §9 and the G-PHYS record — direction independently verified.]

- **Thermal job.** The **same** interface is the radial heat path to the cold head. A
  high-R_c interface material (stainless, k ≈ 3 W/m·K) is a far poorer thermal conductor
  than a low-R_c copper-class co-wind (k ≈ 400 W/m·K) — an order-100 swing in thermal
  conductance from the **same single material choice**.

Because ρ_ct and thermal conductivity k are **coupled responses of one material/pressure
choice**, a designer choosing a uniform interface cannot independently satisfy the
electrical target and the thermal target. Choosing for fast charging (high R_c) starves the
thermal drain and raises hot-spot ΔT under the fixed cooling budget; choosing for a strong
thermal drain (copper) lengthens the charging delay. **This R_c-versus-thermal conflict is
the problem.**

### 3.3 Why prior interface engineering does not resolve it for this regime
Known interface engineering either (a) tunes the interface **uniformly** (uniform conductive
impregnation; uniform thermally conductive resin), which cannot escape the single-point
trade-off; or (b) makes electrical and thermal properties variable **independently** by
mechanically decoupling them — perforated/windowed partially-insulating layers and discrete
conductive HTS **bridges** (Tokamak Energy family) — a *different mechanism* aimed at
different goals (homogenizing per-turn resistance; circumferential/hot-spot-triggered
variation), and not keyed to a cryocooler cold-head power budget. See §10 IDS_pool.

## 4. Summary

The invention resolves the R_c-versus-thermal conflict **spatially**, within the constraint
that R_c and k are coupled properties of a single interlayer material. In a
conduction-cooled NI HTS coil, the turn-to-turn interface is divided into **at least two
co-wound metallic interlayer zones arranged radially**: a higher-contact-resistivity zone
over the inner turns (which shortens charging delay and shapes the electrical bypass where
it matters), and a **continuous high-thermal-conductance drain zone** that extends unbroken
from the protected region radially outward to the cold-head interface (which carries local
disturbance heat to the cold head within the cryocooler's rated capacity). Each zone's
contact resistivity and thermal conductivity are **joint properties of a single continuous
metallic interlayer material** — there is **no apertured/perforated insulating layer and no
discrete conductive bridge**. The radial zone boundary is selected so that the charging time
constant and the worst-case hot-spot temperature rise jointly meet targets within the
cryocooler cold-head power budget.

This mechanism — **coupled-property radial material zoning with a continuous
high-conductance drain to the cold head, free of bridges/apertures** — is the differentiator
that distinguishes the invention from the mechanism of the blocking Tokamak Energy art
(independent electrical/thermal decoupling via windows and bridges) and from uniform-interface
and self-protection-only graded-resistance prior art. It also preserves the commercially
operative embodiment: a coil wound exactly as a Hinetics-class conduction-cooled coil would
be wound, by switching the co-wind interlayer material at a predetermined turn.

**Claim 1.** A conduction-cooled superconducting coil apparatus, comprising:

**(a)** a no-insulation high-temperature-superconductor (HTS) winding wound in a plurality
of radial turns from an inner region to an outer region, the winding omitting turn-to-turn
electrical insulation;

**(b)** a cryocooler having a cold head of finite rated cooling capacity, thermally coupled
to the winding at a cold-head interface and configured to remove heat from the winding by
solid conduction with no liquid-cryogen bath in contact with the winding;

**(c)** a turn-to-turn interface between adjacent turns of the winding, the interface
comprising a co-wound metallic interlayer, wherein at each turn the interface's inter-turn
contact resistivity and its radial thermal conductivity are **coupled properties of a single
continuous metallic interlayer material at that turn**;

**(d)** the interface being arranged in **at least two radial zones**, comprising a first
zone of higher contact resistivity over inner turns of the winding, and a second, drain zone
of higher radial thermal conductivity and lower contact resistivity relative to the first
zone;

**(e)** the drain zone forming a **continuous, unbroken radial high-thermal-conductance
series path** from a protected region of the winding to the cold-head interface, such that
no zone of lower thermal conductivity is interposed in series along said path between the
protected region and the cold head;

**(f)** the interface being **free of apertured or perforated insulating layers and free of
discrete conductive bridges** interconnecting turns, such that the differing contact
resistivity and thermal conductivity of the first zone and the drain zone arise from the
respective single continuous interlayer materials of those zones rather than from openings,
windows, or bridge structures; and

**(g)** a radial boundary between the first zone and the drain zone positioned such that a
charging time constant of the winding and a worst-case hot-spot temperature rise of the
winding jointly satisfy respective target values while a steady-state heat load delivered to
the cold head remains within the finite rated cooling capacity of the cryocooler.

## 7. Embodiments

**Embodiment A — Zone-switched co-wound pancake (primary / commercially operative).** A single
NI REBCO pancake is wound on a bobbin. For the inner N−m turns the tape is co-wound with a
stainless (or comparable high-ρ_ct) metal interlayer (first zone). At a predetermined turn the
co-wind material is switched to a copper-class strip for the outer m turns (drain zone), which
abut the outer-diameter turn and, through it, the cold-head plate. No insulating tape, no
windows, no bridges are introduced; the two properties (R_c and k) change together at the
switch because they are properties of the one interlayer material. This is precisely how a
Hinetics-class conduction-cooled coil would be wound, so the invention adds no exotic process.

**Embodiment B — Axially graded pancake stack.** A stack of pancakes shares a common cold-head
plate at one axial end. Pancakes nearer the cold head use a smaller drain zone (their heat path
is short); pancakes farther from the cold head use a larger drain zone (longer series path to
the cold head), so the radial boundary migrates with axial position. Continuity of the drain
path to the cold head is preserved in every pancake (Claim 3/4/6).

**Embodiment C — Protected-region-centered drain.** Where a specific turn band is known to be
the most disturbance-prone or thermally weakest, the drain zone is centered so that a continuous
high-conductance series path runs from that protected band outward to the cold head, with the
higher-R_c first zone confined to turns where charging-delay control dominates and thermal risk
is low. (Note: the choice of protected band must be validated for worst-case disturbance
location — see R3, §11.)

## 8. Explicit Exclusions

The following are expressly **carved out** of the independent claim, because they are covered
by, or indistinguishable from, the blocking art and are surrendered per the G-CLAIM adjustment:

1. **Apertured / perforated / windowed partially-insulating turn-to-turn layers**, and
   **discrete conductive bridges** interconnecting turns through such a layer, as a means of
   varying interface properties (mechanism owned by the Tokamak Energy family —
   EP4078630A1, US11101060B2). The present interface is a **single continuous metallic
   interlayer per zone**, free of apertures and bridges.

2. **Independent / decoupled tuning of electrical versus thermal interface properties.** The
   present invention deliberately exploits that R_c and k are **coupled** and resolves the
   conflict spatially; it does not claim decoupling them.

3. **Circumferential-only or hot-spot-triggered (l/lc) variation maps**, and maps whose purpose
   is to **homogenize / hold per-turn resistance constant** (Tokamak Energy). The present zoning
   is **radial** and deliberately non-uniform in R_c by turn.

4. **Uniform interface solutions** — uniform conductive impregnation (KR101665038B1) and uniform
   thermally conductive ceramic-filled resin (US20060071747A1).

5. **Bath-cooled / liquid-cryogen applications.** The claim is limited to conduction-cooled,
   cryogen-free operation against a finite cold-head budget (element (b)).

6. **Graded-resistance maps whose sole objective is quench/hot-spot self-protection or field
   preservation** with no continuous thermal drain to a cold head and no cryocooler power-budget
   constraint (Wang/Hahn NPL). The present invention adds the cold-head-drain objective and the
   drain-continuity structure.

**MANDATORY CAVEATS attached to Examples 1–2 (from the G-PHYS record):**
- **R1 (Pareto rounding artifact).** On full-precision values, `uniform:ss` is **not
  actually dominated** by the spatial maps; the JSON's exclusion of it from the Pareto front
  is a 4-decimal rounding artifact. The defensible statement is that spatial maps strictly

  intermediate trade points **no uniform interface reaches**, with uniform stainless surviving
  only as the extreme minimum-τ corner. Do **not** state that spatial maps dominate all uniform
  interfaces.
- **R2 (cooling budget non-binding / units conflated).** The simulation uses a lift budget
  expressed in **W** directly as a cold-head conductance G_ch in **W/K** (a units conflation),

  trend-level only until the budget is enforced as a dimensionally correct binding constraint.
- **R3 (disturbance-location dependence).** The spatial map's advantage is **disturbance-location
