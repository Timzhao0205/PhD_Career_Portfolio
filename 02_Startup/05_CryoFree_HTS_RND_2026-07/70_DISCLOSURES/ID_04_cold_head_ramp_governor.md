## 1. Title

Ramp governor for a conduction-cooled superconducting winding that sets ramp rate by
inverting a stored ramp-loss model against a live power-unit thermal-headroom estimate.

---

## 2. Field

Control of the energization (charging / de-energization) rate of a conduction-cooled,
cryogen-free high-temperature superconducting (HTS) winding cooled by a cryocooler of
limited cooling power — no liquid-cryogen bath. Specifically, a real-time controller that
bounds the ramp-induced AC + eddy-current dissipation to the instantaneously available
cold-head cooling headroom, expressed in units of power (watts).

---

## 3. Problem / Background

> **Research aid — not legal advice.**

A conduction-cooled HTS winding lacks the thermal buffer of a liquid-cryogen bath. Heat
generated during ramping (AC/hysteresis + coupling + eddy losses, scaling steeply with
dI/dt) must reach the cold head through solid conduction, and the cold head has a finite,
**time-varying** lift capability that depends on its present temperature and on any prior
thermal transient it is still recovering from. The transient cooling power of a real
pulse-tube or GM cryocooler is not a single fixed number; it is a measurable, time-varying
quantity (T3-02, ScienceDirect; T3-03, arXiv:2111.06459).

Conventional practice bounds the ramp rate to a **static worst-case** limit — a single rate
that is safe even at the worst instant of the cold head's duty cycle. This is always safe
but needlessly slow whenever the cold head has more headroom than its worst instant, which
is most of the time. Fixed-schedule ramp-down controllers (e.g. 24–48 h ramp-down, T4-04
US8027139B2) and temperature-tolerant dual-cooling designs that simply absorb a ~10 K rise
(T2-01 US9305691B2) illustrate the conventional worst-case / heat-absorbing philosophy.

Prior control art already ties energization to the refrigerator's capacity or temperature:
US6094333A (Sumitomo, expired) bounds generated heat below an "effective cooling curve"
derived from the refrigerator's **rated** capacity plus measured thermal resistance, with a
threshold-trip real-time variant; JP2000012326A (Toshiba, expired) modulates excitation and
compressor inverter frequency from a **temperature-sensor** signal to keep magnetization
speed within a non-quench range. These teach the genus "do not ramp faster than the fridge
can absorb" (~25+ years old, both jurisdictions) but do so via a **precomputed static curve
plus threshold trip** (US'333) or **temperature-domain** feedback (JP'326).

What is not taught is a controller that continuously **estimates the instantaneous
available headroom in power units** — live lift capability inferred from measured cold-head
state against a characterized load line, minus the present measured load — and, each control
cycle, **inverts a stored ramp-loss model** q(dI/dt) to solve for the maximum ramp rate
whose *predicted* dissipation tracks a bounded fraction of that live watt budget. This is
the white space CF-4 occupies.

## 4. Summary

> **Research aid — not legal advice.**

A ramp governor for a cryocooler-conduction-cooled superconducting winding computes, each
control cycle, a **live thermal-headroom estimate in units of power** from measured
cryocooler state (cold-head temperature and/or its trajectory evaluated against a stored,
characterized cold-head load line) minus the present measured load, and sets the winding
ramp rate by **inverting a stored ramp-loss model** q(dI/dt) so that the predicted AC +
eddy dissipation continuously tracks a bounded fraction α (α < 1) of that live headroom.

The governor recovers most of the ramp time that a static worst-case limit surrenders,
because the achievable rate scales with √(headroom) and — by Jensen's inequality for the
concave √ — the time-average of √(headroom(t)) strictly exceeds √(min headroom) whenever the
headroom varies. The advantage therefore follows from the mathematics of any time-varying
headroom curve, not from a tuned shape (see G-PHYS, `60_PRIOR_ART/CF-4/gates.md`).

The controller senses **temperature, power, and compressor/cryocooler state only**. Magnetic-
field / Hall sensing is intentionally kept OUT of the independent claim — see §11 (this is a
deliberate funded-lane-adjacency avoidance per CLAUDE.md hard rule #1, and it also aids FTO
differentiation from the field-tracking FSU/NHMFL family; see §10).

## 5. Independent Claim (system)

> **Research aid — not legal advice.** Claim language is a drafting aid for counsel, not a
> filing-ready claim.

**Claim 1.** A ramp-control system for a superconducting winding conduction-cooled by a
cryocooler of finite cooling power without a liquid-cryogen bath, the system comprising a
controller configured to, in each of a succession of control cycles:

- **(i)** compute a live thermal-headroom estimate expressed in units of power, the estimate
  being derived from (a) a measured state of the cryocooler cold head — comprising at least
  a measured cold-head temperature or a temperature trajectory thereof — evaluated against
  (b) a stored load-line characterization relating cold-head cooling lift to cold-head state,
  minus (c) a present measured thermal load, such that the estimate is a continuously updated
  available-lift-minus-present-load watt budget;

- **(ii)** hold a stored ramp-loss model relating winding ramp-induced dissipation, comprising
  at least AC and eddy-current loss, to winding ramp rate; and

- **(iii)** each said control cycle, invert the stored ramp-loss model against the live
  headroom estimate to solve for a maximum winding ramp rate whose model-predicted
  dissipation is less than or equal to α times the live headroom estimate, where **α is a
  positive fraction strictly less than one (α < 1)**, and command the winding energization at
  a rate not exceeding said maximum ramp rate;

wherein the maximum ramp rate is determined from the live power-unit headroom estimate and
the model inversion, and is not a precomputed rated-capacity curve and not a fixed
predetermined ramp-rate setpoint.

> **Positive-limitation note for counsel:** elements (i) the power-unit headroom *estimator*
> from a stored load-line characterization, (iii) the *per-cycle inversion* of the stored
> loss model, and the **α < 1 margin** are recited as affirmative structural/functional
> limitations — not merely "in real time." Per G-NOVEL these three together are the surviving
> distinction over US6094333A (static curve + threshold trip) and JP2000012326A (temperature-
> domain feedback). An examiner may still assert an obviousness combination of US'333 +
> JP'326 + T3-02; the estimator and the inversion carry the non-obviousness argument and must
> stay in the independent claim.

## 7. Embodiments

> **Research aid — not legal advice.**

1. **Sensing suite (core).** Cold-head temperature sensor(s); optional second-stage / heat-
   station temperatures; cryocooler compressor state (e.g. inverter frequency, power draw);
   winding current and ramp-rate command. The headroom estimator consumes these plus the
   stored load-line and loss-model to produce, each cycle, a watt-budget number. **No magnetic-
   field / Hall sensor is used in the core estimator** (§11).

2. **Load-line characterization.** A stored relation lift = load-line(T_coldhead[, compressor
   state]) obtained by prior characterization of the specific cryocooler; used to convert a
   measured cold-head state into an available-lift number, from which the present measured load
   is subtracted to yield headroom.

3. **Ramp-loss model & inversion.** A stored q(dI/dt) relation (e.g. a quadratic proxy
   q ≈ k·(dI/dt)² as a first-order form, or a richer characterized model) is inverted each cycle
   to yield rate_max = f⁻¹(α·headroom). For q = k·rate², rate_max = √(α·headroom / k).

4. **α-margin.** A positive reserve fraction α < 1 (i.e. govern to α·headroom, never to 100%
   of headroom) so that sensor noise, estimator latency, and load-coupling do not push
   instantaneous dissipation past the true lift. See the tautology caveat in §9 and §12.

5. **NI winding embodiment.** Loss model augmented with radial bypass and charging-delay terms;
   governor deliberately avoids a field-tracking inner loop (Claim 3; FTO §10).

6. **Rotating-frame embodiment.** Applied to a spinning conduction-cooled field winding
   (e.g. Hinetics CRUISE-class relevance); headroom estimated for the rotating-frame extraction
   path (Claim 4). "Stellarator" and any prohibited application word are intentionally absent;
   framing throughout is conduction-cooled / cryogen-free HTS windings.

7. **Composition.** Composes with a converter/power-supply stage and, architecturally, with the
   CF-1 dual-function turn-to-turn interface work and CF-3/C10 current-steering; those
   compositions are noted, not claimed, here.
