## 2. Field

Current leads and terminations that carry transport current from a room-temperature
supply into the cold mass of a **cryogen-free, conduction-cooled** high-temperature
superconducting (HTS) magnet or device — i.e., a system cooled solely by a cryocooler
of **limited lift budget**, with **no LN₂/no cryogen bath** thermal buffer. More
specifically: the manufacturing acceptance qualification of such leads/terminations,
the verification hardware built into the shipped assembly, and the operational use of
each unit's certified thermal and electrical performance data by the host system's
cryogenic controller.

---

## 3. Problem / Background

In a bath-cooled magnet, a liquid-cryogen reservoir absorbs transient and steady
heat, so imperfect current-lead heat leak and joint heating are buffered. In a
**cryogen-free** system there is no such buffer: every watt entering the cold mass
through a current lead competes directly for a small, fixed cryocooler lift budget.
Two per-lead quantities dominate that budget and are physically coupled:

1. **Conductive heat leak** down the lead into the cold stage (a standard,
   Wiedemann–Franz-limited quantity; established benchmark figures exist in the
   literature — see IDS_pool T3-4/T3-5/T3-7), and
2. **Joint / contact resistance** at the superconducting-to-normal and demountable
   interfaces, whose Joule dissipation I²·Rⱼ under transport current **also lands in
   the same cold stage** as the conductive leak.

Because both loads share one lift budget, the true per-lead thermal burden of a
cryogen-free system is only known when **both** quantities are measured **on the same
physical unit**. Today, thermal qualification and electrical (joint-resistance)
qualification are institutionalized as *separate* disciplines (see IDS_pool: heat-leak-
specified lead design vs. dedicated joint-QC facilities), and where combined test
campaigns exist they were run on **research prototypes / mock-ups to release a
series-production process** (the ASIPP 13 kA and 68 kA ITER HTS-lead campaigns), with
results going into an **engineering report**, not shipped with each unit and not
consumed by an operating controller.

Two practical failure modes follow in cryogen-free service:

- A lead may meet a heat-leak spec on paper yet, once its as-built joint resistance is
  included, exceed the cold-stage budget under load — a coupling invisible to either
  test alone.
- A factory heat-leak certificate is **silently voided** by the field-made thermal
  joint when a lead is installed or a demountable cartridge is exchanged, because the
  as-installed interface conductance differs from the as-certified one (cf. interface-
  conductance sensitivity, IDS_pool S2-3 / US4,876,413).

The problem addressed here is: how to make a conduction-cooled lead/termination ship
**"born-qualified"** — carrying a per-unit, matched thermal+electrical acceptance
record whose values remain valid in the field and are **operationally load-bearing**
(consumed by the cryogenic controller to allocate lift budget and set operating
margin), rather than a paper certificate with no functional relationship to the
running machine.

## 4. Summary

Disclosed is a conduction-cooled HTS current-lead / termination assembly for a
cryogen-free (cryocooler-only, limited-lift) system, together with the system that
consumes its qualification data. The assembly integrates, into the shipped article
itself, **verification structures** — permanent joint-spanning voltage taps and
calibrated thermal-anchor reference stations — that make economical per-unit
co-measurement of (a) conductive heat leak and (b) joint/contact resistance possible
as a 100% factory-acceptance test rather than a one-off instrumented rig. Each unit
carries a **unit-specific, machine-readable acceptance record** co-verifying both
quantities under defined measurement states, and that record's certified values are
**consumed by the cryogenic system controller** to allocate the cryocooler's limited
lift budget and set operating margin (e.g., composing with a cold-head-budget ramp
governor, CF-4).

A co-equal alternative independent direction is a **calibrated demountable lead
interface** whose registration-controlled thermal joint preserves the validity of the
factory-certified heat-leak value through field installation/removal within a stated
tolerance — attacking the otherwise-unaddressed voiding of a factory certificate by a
field-made joint.

### 5.1 Option A — Integrated verification structures + controller-consumed record
*(G-CLAIM ladder rung 3 apparatus core + rung 1 system/combination)*

**A1 (apparatus).** A current-lead/termination assembly for a cryogen-free,
conduction-cooled superconducting device cooled by a cryocooler of limited lift
budget, the assembly comprising:

- (a) a current-carrying lead body having a warm-end terminal and a cold-end
  termination thermally anchored to a cryocooler-cooled cold stage, configured to
  conduct transport current with no cryogen-bath cooling;
- (b) one or more **superconducting/normal or demountable joints** in the current path;
- (c) **permanent joint-spanning voltage taps** integrated into the assembly and
  positioned to bound each said joint, enabling four-wire measurement of that joint's
  resistance on the finished unit under transport current;
- (d) **calibrated thermal-reference stations** integrated at the thermal anchor(s)
  along the lead, enabling per-unit determination of conductive heat leak into the cold
  stage by temperature-gradient inference and/or calorimetry at the anchor;
- (e) a **unit-specific, machine-readable acceptance record** affixed to or associated
  with the assembly and encoding co-verified values of (i) conductive heat leak and
  (ii) joint/contact resistance for that individual unit, said record defining the
  **measurement states** under which each value was obtained — at minimum a
  zero-current conductive-heat-leak state and a rated-current joint-resistance state,
  each with units and traceability (per G-PHYS condition C2);

wherein the integrated structures (c)/(d) render 100% per-unit co-measurement of the
coupled thermal and electrical quantities economical on the shipped article.

**A2 (system / combination — the strongest rung).** A cryogen-free superconducting
system comprising the assembly of A1 and a **cryogenic system controller** configured
to **read the machine-readable acceptance record of (e)** and to **allocate the
cryocooler's lift budget and/or set an operating margin, ramp rate, or current limit**
using the record's certified per-unit heat-leak and joint-resistance values against a
measured or modeled cryocooler headroom.

*Distinguishing note (from G-NOVEL/G-CLAIM):* the controller consumption in A2 creates
a **functional relationship** between the record and the operating article, defeating
the printed-matter / non-technical-feature discount that kills a bare "ships with a
record" framing; and it distinguishes the ASIPP research campaigns, whose data went
into an engineering report, not a control loop. Composes naturally with CF-4
(cold-head-budget ramp governor).

### 5.2 Option B — Calibrated demountable interface preserving the factory certificate
*(G-CLAIM ladder rung 4; flagged in the gate record as possibly the strongest actual
invention in CF-7)*

**B1 (apparatus).** A demountable current-lead assembly for a cryogen-free,
conduction-cooled superconducting device, comprising:

- (a) a lead cartridge with a cold-end **demountable thermal interface** to a
  cryocooler-cooled cold station;
- (b) a **registration / alignment feature** that mechanically constrains the mating
  geometry, contact pressure, and/or interstitial thermal-interface condition of said
  demountable interface to a reproducible, calibrated state on each installation;
- (c) a **factory-issued heat-leak certificate** for the cartridge whose certified
  conductive-heat-leak value is defined **as-installed at the calibrated interface
  state of (b)**, such that the certified value remains valid after field
  installation/removal within a stated tolerance;

wherein the registration-controlled interface preserves the validity of the
factory-certified heat leak across field demount/remount cycles that would otherwise
void it.

## 7. Embodiments (qualitative / structural only — NO invented numbers)

*Per G-PHYS condition C1, all embodiments below are described structurally and
qualitatively. No heat-leak wattages, resistance values, tolerances, or temperatures
are asserted as prophetic results, because no minimal lead model yet exists to derive
them (see §11). Where a quantity is named, it is named as a **variable to be
determined**, not a value.*

**E1 — Born-qualified conduction-cooled magnet lead (Option A core).** A conduction-
cooled HTS magnet lead body runs from a warm terminal through one or more thermal-anchor
stages to a cold-end termination bonded to the cryocooler cold stage. Permanent voltage
taps bracket each superconducting-to-normal and demountable joint; calibrated
thermometry sits at each anchor stage. At factory acceptance, the unit is measured in
the defined states (zero-current conductive leak; rated-current joint resistance; and
optionally total-under-load with the closure check). The co-verified pair of values,
with units and traceability, is written to a unit-affixed machine-readable record. In
service, the host controller reads the record and treats each lead's certified
heat-leak and joint values as inputs to its cold-stage budget allocation.

**E2 — Controller-consumed record in a lift-budget-limited system (Option A / A2).** The
cryogenic controller maintains a running estimate of cryocooler headroom (from cold-head
state and the conduction model of the host system) and subtracts each installed lead's
**certified** contribution rather than a nominal catalog figure — so a unit that
qualified at the favorable end of its distribution earns more usable ramp/margin, and a
unit at the unfavorable end is automatically de-rated. This is the functional
relationship that distinguishes a report-only research record.

**E3 — Single-fixture co-measurement station (method, dependent claim 3 / rung 2).** A
factory station applies rated transport current while calorimetrically monitoring the
anchor; the zero-current baseline gives conductive leak, the Joule rise gives joint
resistance, and the closure criterion (total ≈ leak + I²·Rⱼ) is the pass/fail gate. The
integrated taps/thermometry of E1 make this a fast per-unit test rather than a
bespoke-rig prototype campaign.

**E4 — Calibrated demountable cartridge (Option B core).** A lead cartridge mates to
the cold station through a registration-controlled thermal interface (alignment
features plus a defined interstitial condition and fastening state). The factory
heat-leak certificate is issued **as-installed at that calibrated state**, so a field
demount/remount reproduces the certified thermal boundary within tolerance instead of
silently changing it. Optionally the cartridge also carries the E1 verification
structures and E2 controller-consumed record (the §5.2 bridge).

**E5 — Termination (cable-adjacent) variant.** The same verification-structure +
record + controller-consumption architecture applied to an HTS **termination** treated
as an integrated current-lead + joint + thermal-boundary assembly, for conduction-
cooled terminations. (Domain-distance note for counsel: cable-termination art exists —
IDS_pool T2-1 — but in the cable rather than magnet-coil domain.)

**Hall-sensing adjacency screen (Hard Rule 1, prong a):** the instrumented-lead
embodiments here use **voltage taps and thermometry only** — no magnetic-field / Hall
sensing anywhere. Per the gate record's recorded constraint, if any future embodiment
adds magnetic-field sensing to the lead, it must be re-screened against the user's
funded lane before drafting. No GaN in any embodiment (Hard Rule 2).
