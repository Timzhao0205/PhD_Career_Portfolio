# G06 — Quantum & Big-Physics Support Electronics (Room-Temperature)

## Landscape
Room-temp qubit control/readout racks, timing/sync distribution, precision bias sources, and FPGA feedback boxes sell as capital equipment to three buyers: QPU makers, big-physics facilities, and university/government QIS programs. The market is young and vendor-concentrated: full-stack control (Quantum Machines OPX1000, Zurich Instruments SHFQC+) [G06-03][G06-04], precision bias (QDAC-II, Keysight PZ2100A) [G06-05][G06-10], picosecond timing (CERN-derived White Rabbit via Safran) [G06-09][G06-02], and hardware decoding (Riverlane) [G06-12] each have one or two dominant suppliers. A cheaper open-source lane is emerging from DOE: Fermilab's QICK reached ~500 users at US national labs/academia/industry since 2021; Nov 2025 DOE signed intent with Fermilab and Dutch vendor Qblox to manufacture it commercially [G06-15][G06-16][G06-14]. China runs a parallel, faster, cheaper track: Origin Quantum's 天机4.0 (500+ qubits, May 2025) [G06-07] and Jiangsu-built ez-Q Engine 2.0 (5,000+ channels, thousand-qubit scale, delivered to USTC June 2025) [G06-17] are domestically sourced, not imported.

## Pains & buyers
QPU makers pay for channel density and $/qubit: Fermilab cites commercial control electronics at "tens of thousands of dollars per qubit," with QICK claiming ~10x reduction [G06-14]. Big-physics facilities buy timing/sync as capital equipment on grant cycles, prioritizing precision over price — White Rabbit sells sub-ns/picosecond sync into 14+ countries' facilities [G06-09][G06-02]. University/mid-scale programs are acutely price-sensitive, why QICK and China's low-cost 一体机 [G06-06] gained traction. QKD/quantum-network operators need entanglement-grade timing; CERN frames this as new partner-built infrastructure (Qunnect, Single Quantum), not a packaged product [G06-02]. Cryostat-side thermal budget matters to room-temp vendors too: amplifiers alone can eat up to half a dilution fridge's cooling budget, pushing cryo-side players (SemiQon, Qubic, Delft Circuits) toward integration that room-temp racks must interoperate with [G06-11]. Three structural pains recur: closed single-vendor control stacks forcing lock-in; no standalone vendor-agnostic decoder appliance (Riverlane's FPGA decoder ships inside Infleqtion/OQC/Oak Ridge/Rigetti stacks, not as a catalog instrument) [G06-12]; and timing hardware still shipped as a lab-bench switch, not hardened for unattended continuous field operation.

## Incumbents & gaps (price points)
OPX1000 (80 analog ch/3U, <160ns active reset, <150fs jitter@6GHz, supports superconducting/spin/neutral-atom/defect-center, scales past 1,000 qubits via QSync) [G06-03] and SHFQC+ (up to 6 superconducting qubits/unit, 8.5GHz, 350ns feedback) [G06-04] both price-on-request and lock customers into proprietary software; SHFQC+ targets superconducting only [G06-04]. QDAC-II (24ch, <20nV/√Hz, 25-bit DC resolution) is the de facto precision-bias standard, single-vendor [G06-05]; Keysight's PZ2100A/PZ2131A (1U 4-slot mainframe, 5ch/module SMU at 10pA, up to 100 ch) is general lab instrumentation, not qubit-sequencer-native [G06-10]. Fermilab/Qblox's QICK box (32 RF/DC ch, open-source, ~10x cheaper than commercial racks) is the disruptive low end but is manufactured by a Dutch firm under a DOE CRADA — no hardened/mil-spec variant exists [G06-15][G06-14]. On timing, Safran's WR Switch Low Jitter (18 SFP ports, >100km single-hop, 80W, -10 to 50°C) [G06-09] is effectively the market; nothing purpose-built exists for unattended quantum-network field nodes. On decoding, no vendor sells a standalone FPGA decoder appliance to QPU makers lacking in-house teams [G06-12]. In China, Origin Quantum cut integrated measurement-control cost >50% vs. >10M-yuan traditional builds [G06-06]; SpinQ frames per-qubit control cost at "tens of thousands of yuan" (~$2-5K) for sub-20-qubit systems [G06-13] — a 5-10x discount vs. the Western baseline, unproven yet at 500+ qubit scale.

## 2029 inflections
QICK's DOE-Qblox commercialization matures by 2027-28; by 2029 a hardened, ruggedized QICK-class module (vibration, wide temperature range, radiation-tolerant packaging) is a plausible standalone niche neither the open design nor Qblox's commercial line addresses [G06-15]. Hardware decoding shifts from bundled to appliance-based as QEC scales past small demos; Riverlane's roadmap (continuous "streaming logic") signals the category forming but stays QPU-vendor-tied through 2026 [G06-12]. Quantum-network/QKD field deployments and VLBI-style optical-clock links (six-telescope Europe-Korea network, already ~10^-15 s/s agreement) [G06-08] move from demo to semi-permanent infrastructure 2027-2030, needing telecom-grade timing hardware, not lab switches. QED-C/NIST SWaP work (denser RF interconnect, integrated flexlines) [G06-01] becomes standard practice by 2028-29, shifting competition toward $/channel and W/channel. Export-control scope — currently hitting Chinese cryogenics/optoelectronics suppliers, not control-electronics makers [G06-18] — plausibly widens by 2028-2031, forcing separate US- and China-facing product lines rather than one global SKU.

## China notes
Indigenization is fast: 天机4.0 (500+ qubits) [G06-07] and the 中微达信/Jiangsu-built, USTC-delivered ez-Q Engine 2.0 (5,000+ channels, thousand-qubit scale) [G06-17] are domestic, not imported. SpinQ's cost framing implies a 5-10x per-qubit price edge at the small-system tier vs. the Western baseline [G06-13][G06-14], unproven at 500+ qubit scale. BIS's March 2025 entity-list action hit Chinese cryogenics (Scikro, Physike) and optoelectronics/IC suppliers, not control-electronics or timing makers [G06-18] — this lane is currently less export-exposed than adjacent cryo/photonic hardware, a gap likely to narrow by 2028-2031. Best use of the founder's China network: timing/sync or FPGA-decoder co-development with USTC/CAS-adjacent labs (less export-sensitive categories) while keeping any US-national-lab-facing product on a fully separate compliance track from day one.

## Opportunity seeds

1. **Ruggedized, mil-spec QICK-class control module** for extreme-environment big-physics/defense QIS deployments, hardening the open Fermilab reference architecture (vibration, wide temperature range, conformal packaging) beyond what the Qblox commercial line targets [G06-15][G06-14]. Avoids C10 (precision magnet/facility power conversion) by staying strictly signal/control-level. Avoids N06/EXT-07/PWR-08 (cryogenic power electronics) and CL-24/EXT-17 (cryo-CMOS) by remaining a room-temperature box whose sequencing logic is identical whether driving a transmon, a trapped-ion RF electrode, or a neutral-atom AOM. Platform-agnosticism should be stated explicitly in the spec, not implied.

2. **Vendor-agnostic FPGA decoder/feedback appliance** sold as a catalog instrument to the long tail of QPU makers without in-house decoder teams, unlike Riverlane's vendor-bundled deployments [G06-12]. Avoids C34 (ultra-low-noise SMU) by staying in digital real-time decode/PID logic, not analog source-measurement. Avoids C33 (cryogenic coil QC instruments) by never entering the cryostat. Decoding logic for surface-code, color-code, or trapped-ion parity checks runs on identical room-temperature FPGA fabric, so value holds across platforms.

3. **Field-hardened, telecom-grade White-Rabbit-class timing appliance** for unattended quantum-network nodes and VLBI-style facilities, versus the lab-bench switch Safran sells today (-10 to 50°C, 80W) [G06-09]. This is pure timing/sync distribution, avoiding C10 (precision magnet & facility power converters) — the product carries clock/PPS/data only, never watts. Platform-agnostic by construction across trapped-ion network nodes, photonic QKD links, and radio-telescope arrays.

4. **Open, multi-vendor-interoperable precision bias/flux module** (QDAC-II-class 24-48ch low-noise DAC/ADC) undercutting single-vendor lock-in on $/channel. Avoids C34 (ultra-low-noise SMU) by staying a bias/flux/gate-drive OEM module sold to control-rack integrators, not a bench source-measure instrument. Platform-agnostic: the same module biases flux-tunable transmons, spin-qubit gates, or trapped-ion trim electrodes without cryogenic placement of the source itself.

5. **China-facing low-cost measurement-control line for the sub-50-qubit teaching/pilot tier**, co-developed with USTC/CAS-adjacent labs via the founder's China network, priced against SpinQ's ~$2-5K/qubit benchmark [G06-13] rather than the Western ~$10K+/qubit baseline [G06-14]. Avoids C10 by remaining signal-level only, and is firewalled from any US-national-lab-facing SKU given BIS's expanding quantum entity-list scope [G06-18]. Platform-agnosticism (superconducting or spin-qubit chips) is stated in spec even though initial pilots are superconducting-heavy.
