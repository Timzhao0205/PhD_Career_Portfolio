# ROADMAP 2026–2030 — Quarter-by-Quarter Preparation Plan (max-effort revision)

Aligned with: PhD finish ~mid-2029, company formation 2029/2030. **Primary bet = C12 cluster**
(winding cells + C33 QC module + C11 protection line + C15 vertical expansion, with C10 as the
electronics arm). **Fallback #1 = C06-pivot** (800 VDC protection intelligence). **Gated options
= C01/C03** (USV power bricks) **and C27** (250°C platform). **Emergency deep-fit fallback =
C23.** C08 is watch-only. Sources: deep dives in `40_PHASE4_DEEPDIVES/`, policy in
`50_PHASE5_POLICY/POLICY_BRIEF.md` (research, not legal advice). Decision gates in **bold**.
Changes vs. the prior roadmap are logged in `03_MAX_EFFORT_DELTA.md`.

## Design principles

1. Every quarter must advance both the PhD and the venture — no pure detours.
2. Validation before commitment: the C12 cluster is gated on named-buyer evidence, not
   enthusiasm. The observed cost of skipping stress-testing is −11.4 points (see
   `02_FULL_RANKING.md`) — never commit to an un-red-teamed idea.
3. Keep the Magnefy conflict wall: nothing in transformer condition monitoring, ever.
4. China = supply chain + competitive intelligence + relationships until counsel clears more
   (31 CFR 850 / COINS Act [P-01][P-03]; new regs due ~March 2027 — re-check then; Affiliates
   Rule reactivates 2026-11-09 and China's materials-truce expires Nov-2026 [P-06][P-36] —
   re-check both that quarter).
5. No Stanford IP entanglement: venture-relevant inventions developed personally, documented, on
   personal equipment and time; get a clean written understanding of what's thesis vs. yours.
6. The cluster is one company staged over time, not five companies: machine (C12) → QC module
   (C33) → protection line (C11) → electronics (C10) → test magnets (C15). Each stage attaches
   to the same customers; each has its own kill gate below.
