## 1. Problem & who has it

Magnet recyclers control product grade through feedstock composition, and today they manage it without instruments. Named archetypes at the 2029-2031 decision points:

- **Short-loop recycler feed manager** (HyProMag USA: hub + 2 spokes, 750 t/yr magnets + 291 t co-products, pre-processing budget **US$1.84/kg** for "sorting and initial processing," first revenue target Q1-2027 [DD-V12-14]). HyProMag's own method statement: waste "is segregated at site into different streams where there is a narrower range of compositional variation," and grade is adjusted **post-process** by powder blending and GBD [DD-V12-13]. The pain is real (composition variance), the current fix is provenance segregation, not sensing.
- **M2M feed buyer** (Noveon: accepts harvested EOL magnets "at least the size of HDD magnets or HEV traction-drive motor magnets"; <1% of REEs recycled today [DD-V12-15]) — incoming-QC/verification niche.
- **ITAD/HDD processor**: ~20M drives/yr retired from major US data centers (INL estimate) [DD-V12-16]; gating "magnet present / VCM intact / worth routing to harvest vs shred" at line speed.
- **EU ATF/dismantler** post-ELV: motor-removal duty applies ~24 months after entry into force (EP plenary 2026-06-18) [DD-V12-26] — thousands of sites needing a cheap pull/don't-pull decision, but at hand-tool budgets, not $100K instruments.
- **Chinese 综合利用 plant** (华宏科技: 15,000 t/yr magnet-material capacity, 12,000 t/yr recycled REO, 2024 output ~115 t Dy₂O₃ / ~25 t Tb₄O₇ [DD-V12-19]). Chinese practice prices scrap **per contained element after sampling assay** (Mysteel 2026-07-02: PrNd-basis 770-780 元/kg, Dy 1,250-1,280, Tb 5,600-5,800 元/kg contained [DD-V12-18]); the national standard 《钕铁硼生产加工回收料》 codifies classes (干粉/磁泥/块片料) and sampling rules (插钎取样), with REE total and 配分 as the pricing index [DD-V12-22]. Assay labor is cheap; no inline instrument in the loop.

Quantified stake: at 8-10 wt% Dy, one tonne of EH-grade magnets carries 80-100 kg Dy ≈ **¥100-128K (~$14-18K) of Dy value** above Dy-free grades [DD-V12-18] — mis-grading is expensive, which is exactly why the industry already samples and assays at every trade node. The open question is whether anyone pays for that answer **inline, pre-liberation** — §2 shows physics only permits part of the answer.

## 2. Product & extreme edge — PHYSICS ADJUDICATION (task a)

**What a passive external array CAN measure at conveyor speed.** Outside stray field is a functional of the magnetization distribution M(r) alone. Robust invertibles: total vector moment, coarse multipole structure (geometry/pole count), magnetized-vs-dead state, and — fused with 3D vision volume estimates — moment density ≈ Br class. Signal is not the problem: 2 g of magnetized NdFeB (Br≈1.2 T → m≈0.25 A·m²) produces ~6 µT at 20 cm, six orders above fluxgate noise floors; the binding constraint is clutter (moving steel, motors, superposed multi-magnet fields), i.e., an inversion/rejection problem — the founder's actual skill. Family separation by Br·geometry: hard ferrite (Br 0.2-0.45 T) vs RE metallic (0.85-1.48 T) is clean (3-6x moment-density gap); **NdFeB vs SmCo is only probabilistic** (N35 Br 1.17-1.22 T [DD-V12-01] vs Sm₂Co₁₇ ~1.02-1.16 T — adjacent bands, and unknown thermal knockdown aliases directly into the estimate).

**What Dy grading requires — red team CONFIRMED.** Dy/Tb set intrinsic coercivity HcJ, a hysteresis property with **zero external static observable**: N35 and N35EH share the identical Br band 1.17-1.22 T while HcJ jumps 955 → 2,388 kA/m [DD-V12-01]. Grain-boundary diffusion makes the degeneracy engineered, not accidental: a Tb₂O₃-GBD magnet measured HcJ 1,180 → 1,685 kA/m (+43%) with Br 1.407 → 1.395 T (−0.8%) [DD-V12-02]. Post-2012 EV traction feedstock is precisely this Br-decoupled type. Passive inversion of Br therefore cannot grade heavy-REE content. **The headline claim is retracted.**

**Is there an honest conveyor-speed applied-field hybrid?** Three channels adjudicated:
1. *Small-signal AC susceptibility*: recoil permeability ≈1.03-1.1 for both NdFeB and SmCo — no Dy signal, no family signal. Dead channel.
2. *Eddy response (0.1-10 kHz)*: sintered NdFeB σ ≈ 0.7 MS/m (measured, Sensors 2014 [DD-V12-05]); hard ferrite is effectively an insulator ("resistivity of ferrite is much larger than that of a single metal or alloy magnetic material" [DD-V12-30]). Eddy+static fusion separates ferrite/RE robustly and adds casing-conductivity context; NdFeB-vs-SmCo conductivity contrast (~1.5-2x) is second-order at scrap SNR. Honest but incremental — SGM-class eddy-current separators already exploit conductivity at every shredder, with no composition resolution [DD-V12-29].
3. *Sub-coercivity reverse-pulse classing* (drive coil + before/after array remanence): physically legitimate — sampling the demag knee measures HcJ class, which IS the Dy-relevant property. But the numbers kill conveyor deployment: probing an EH knee needs internal fields approaching µ₀HcJ ≈ 3 T; a 200-300 mm aperture at 1-3 T pulse is magnetizer-class stored energy with large forces on magnetized workpieces; and ms-scale pulses see ~0.4-0.5 mm skin depth in mild steel — **the pulse cannot penetrate the very housings the passive channel sees through**. It works only on liberated magnets in a fixture — which is exactly Hirst's shipped product (PFM08: 10.5 T, Br/HcJ/BHmax in <1 min, samples to 40/60 mm, enclosed fixture) [DD-V12-03], and at that point LIBS reads composition directly (CEA published quantitative Dy analysis of coated used NdFeB for sorting-prior-to-recycling; reported <10% relative uncertainty incl. a 1 m-standoff system — abstract-only verification [DD-V12-04]).

**Re-scoped product (what physics supports):** conveyor head = 64-256 ch TMR/fluxgate gradiometer array + low-frequency eddy coil + 3D camera; outputs per object at ≤2 m/s: RE-magnet present y/n, ferrite-vs-RE family (NdFeB/SmCo probabilistic), magnetized/dead, moment vector, and **estimated magnet mass + location inside closed casings** (DC field passes conductive housings; caveat: fields from magnets inside assembled motors are partially shunted by laminations — mass estimate degrades toward presence-only there). NOT in scope: Dy/Tb/HcJ grading. The honest differentiator vs LIBS/XRF (task b pressure test): LIBS needs an exposed, ablatable surface and sorts liberated pieces [DD-V12-09][DD-V12-10]; the magnetic head classifies **through housings, pre-liberation** — strongest on HDD/speaker/small-assembly streams, weakest on complete steel-housed motors, and the EV stream reaches recyclers post-extraction (liberated → LIBS-able) anyway. This is a competent instrument with one narrow superpower, not a 10x edge.

**PhD-lane honesty (task e):** array+inversion capability transfers and makes the re-scoped head cheap for this founder to attempt (weeks to breadboard, not quarters). But the binding constraints — HcJ observability (materials physics), scrap-hall clutter, capital-poor buyers — are untouched by inversion skill. Honest C1 = 4, not 5; the red team's anti-anchoring deduction stands. Capability leverage argues for building this as an **experiment/module**, not a company.

## 4. Competitive landscape — global AND Chinese

**Global.** A decade of EU public prior art sits exactly on this concept: FP7 REMANENCE (2013-2016, €3.72M EU: "Advanced sensing and mechanical separation techniques combined with... hydrogen decrepitation will recover the rare earth magnets in the WEEE"; Acreo sensor work, OptiSort, Stena, U. Birmingham) [DD-V12-08] → H2020 SUSMAGPRO (2019-2023, €14.7M total funding, Pforzheim-coordinated; "new sensing and robotic sorting lines," building on REMANENCE) [DD-V12-06][DD-V12-07]. Neither produced a merchant instrument — prior art AND a warning about demand. Commercial inline element-specific sorting exists: STEINERT LSS/PLASMAX LIBS chute systems (multi-spot, >95% purity, usually paired with XRT) [DD-V12-09]; TOMRA AUTOSORT PULSE dynamic LIBS (launched 2023-06-01; 3-7 t/h; >95% alloy purity) [DD-V12-10]. Unit prices are not public (both vendors quote-only); engineered LIBS lines are credibly mid-six-figure installs (**$300-800K estimate, unverified — flagged assumption**), vs handheld LIBS renting at $1,990/week / $5,990/month [DD-V12-11] for spot assay. Magnet-QC modality is productized by Magcam (>16,000-sensor Hall-array field cameras, bench portal/rotor scanners incl. inline Combi option — no HcJ) [DD-V12-12] and Hirst (coercivity, bench) [DD-V12-03]. Buyers self-solve: HPMS ingests magnetized whole assemblies ("can remove magnets from full rotor assemblies including those on the shaft") and demagnetizes in-process [DD-V12-13].

**Chinese (zh sourcing).** No inline magnet-triage instrument found in zh searches either — but the workflow gap is filled by cheap assay: GB-standard sampling classes + 配分计价 [DD-V12-22][DD-V12-18]. The likely cloners are integrated players: GEM self-develops recycling tech (57 REE patents incl. 电机拆解及回收利用; REE recovery >90%; led 2 national + 5 industry standards) [DD-V12-20]; Huahong is simultaneously China's largest magnet recycler and a recycling-equipment manufacturer [DD-V12-19]. Domestic magnetics-instrument base exists at lab grade (湖南联众磁学: coercivity meters, VSMs, Helmholtz systems — no inline/production-line magnet-sorting product listed) [DD-V12-23]. Read: whitespace formally exists in China, but the buyer set self-builds and the assay substitute costs near zero marginal labor.
