## 1. Problem & who has it

The urban mine of NdFeB is real and unharvested: "hundreds of thousands of tons of end-of-life NdFeB rare earth magnets continue to pile up in our landfills" and "less than 1% of the world's rare earth elements are ever recovered or recycled" (Noveon) [DD-V11-01]. Four named archetypes hold the pain in 2029-2031:

- **US magnet-maker feed buyer** (Noveon San Marcos, $215M Series C 1/2026, expanding "beyond 2,000 tons per year" [DD-V11-04]; Vulcan Elements + ReElement, $1.4B package incl. $620M DoW loan targeting 10,000 t/yr magnets "with focus on recycling end-of-life magnets and electronic waste" [DD-V11-06]). Their short-loop process needs "harvested end-of-life magnets... at least the size of HDD magnets or HEV traction-drive motor magnets" [DD-V11-01] — a feed class that barely exists as a merchant commodity. 10,000 t/yr of magnet output implies ~11-13 kt/yr of magnet-bearing feed; nobody today can harvest that intact.
- **ITAD/e-scrap operator** (Sims-class): ~20M HDDs/yr retired from major US data centers alone [DD-V11-09]; security policy sends them to shred (the WD-Microsoft program processed "~50,000 pounds of *shredded* end-of-life HDDs" [DD-V11-08]), destroying the short-loop option per drive.
- **EU dismantler (ATF)**: the ELV Regulation passed EP plenary 2026-06-18 (437-112) and applies 24 months after entry into force [DD-V11-15]; Article 30 "obliges authorised treatment facilities to remove the parts and components listed in Annex VII Part C before shredding," and the text singles out e-drive motors, "which contain important quantities of rare earth elements" [DD-V11-14]. Thousands of ATFs must soon pull motors with no economic way to go from 80 kg motor to 1.5 kg magnet.
- **OEM closed-loop programs**: LG-Noveon-Kangwon initiative reprocessing "end-of-life magnets recovered from LG Electronics products" into new magnets [DD-V11-05] — OEMs signing M2M loops need harvest capacity they don't own.

Quantified niche: US+EU merchant-harvest demand 2029-2032 ≈ 3-8 kt/yr of magnet mass across HDD/motor/appliance streams (derivation §5) — dozens-to-low-hundreds of cells, not thousands. This is a niche-equipment + service play, and must be priced as such.

## 2. Product & extreme edge

A SKU-recipe robotic cell: 2x 6-axis arms + force-torque control + 3D vision + programmable fixturing + induction/oven thermal stage (soften epoxy, optionally demagnetize above Curie ~310-340 C) + servo press/puller; output = intact (magnetized or thermally demagnetized) magnets sorted by SKU with digital provenance.

Specs vs SOTA (numbers):
- Manual baseline: ~5 min/HDD/worker (10-12 units/h) [DD-V11-10]; Hitachi's 2010 drum machine hit ~100-140 HDD/h (8x) but harvested only 26 t of magnets in 6 years — captive, ~10% of Hitachi's own magnet need, never merchant equipment [DD-V11-10].
- ORNL/UT-Battelle patented exactly this cell class: automated PM recovery from electric machines with a robot "training station" for unknown SKUs at ~160 motors/day [DD-V11-12], and HDD punch/water-jet magnet extraction from mixed 2.5"/3.5" streams with Curie-temperature demag [DD-V11-13]; ORNL's HDD pilot claimed up to 7,200 drives/day [DD-V11-09]. None became a product.
- Bulk mechanical SOTA: Cyclic's Mag-Xtract eats whole devices at "about 2,200 pounds... per hour" (~8 kt/yr/line) [DD-V11-03] but yields recycling feed, not reuse-grade known-SKU magnets; HPMS (HyProMag) strips magnets from whole rotors as demagnetized powder — no disassembly, hub AISC $19.6/kg product vs $55/kg market [DD-V11-02].
- V1 target: 30-60 motors/h (jig-assisted, 2-3 motor families) and 400-800 HDD/h module; magnet integrity ≥95%; pre-processing cost ≤$1.50/kg vs HyProMag's budgeted $1.84/kg spoke pre-processing line [DD-V11-02]. Honest edge statement: 3-10x labor productivity and SKU-recipe software + provenance (CRMA Art. 28 magnet labels/data carriers make recipes machine-readable [DD-V11-16]) — not a physics 10x. The red team's "three machines pretending to be one" stands: v1 = motors + HDD module; wind excluded (§5).

## 4. Competitive landscape — global and Chinese

Global: **HyProMag** (whole-rotor HPMS, Dallas hub 750 t/yr magnets + 291 t co-products, $125M capex, first revenue target Q1-2027, spokes doing "sorting and initial processing" [DD-V11-02]) — route that skips intact harvest. **Cyclic** (Mag-Xtract in-house, 5-6 planned sites [DD-V11-03]). **Noveon** (short-loop; holds OEM loops [DD-V11-04][DD-V11-05]). **Molg**: $5.5M seed (Closed Loop, Amazon Climate Pledge Fund, ABB Robotics & Automation Ventures, 10/2024), microfactories at Sims and hyperscaler ITADs, laptops/servers focus; +$5M DOE BIL award for laptop-battery harvest (50% faster, half cost) — *no magnet/motor scope today*; nearest-neighbor and fastest potential entrant [DD-V11-22]. **Fraunhofer ADIR** ended 2019 without commercialization [DD-V11-25]; iDEAR/DeMoBat/REASSERT produce licensable public IP (red team, accepted). **Apple Daisy**: 2 units, 1.2M iPhones/yr, free patents, zero licensees — evidence that recyclers do not adopt even free disassembly IP without a service wrapper [DD-V11-23].

Chinese (zh sourcing): **格林美 GEM** runs robotic automated dismantling lines (battery packs: 扫描-对准-松螺丝-搬运) in its Wuhan park — 250 kt/yr scrap vehicles, 50 kt/yr e-waste, 45 kt/yr power batteries — all captive, built through a decade of 电机拆解装备 R&D [DD-V11-19]. **华宏科技/鑫泰科技**: China's largest magnet recycler (15,000 t/yr magnet materials, 12,000 t/yr recycled REO; 2024 output ~115 t Dy2O3, ~25 t Tb4O7) — and Huahong is itself a recycling-equipment manufacturer; it will not buy US cells [DD-V11-18]. Commodity motor dismantlers (斩铜机/拉铜机, hammer-crush rotors, pull stator copper) sell at **¥34,000 (~$4.8K)** per set [DD-V11-21] — they destroy magnets but anchor Chinese willingness-to-pay near zero for "a robot that does what cheap hydraulics + labor do." Pricing signal: China's scrap market pays on contained PrNd — 钕铁硼废料 quoted 770-780 元/kg contained PrNd (tax-incl., 2026-07-02) with Dy 1,250-1,280 and Tb 5,600-5,800 元/kg [DD-V11-17]; at ~25% PrNd that is ~190-200 元/kg (~$27-28/kg) for *shredded/mixed* scrap — meaning China's hydromet complex already pays near-intact prices for any magnet scrap, compressing the intact premium to ~1-1.5x there. (Conflict note: Jan-2026 new-blank quotes 125-195 元/kg sit *below* that scrap parity — a post-Oct-2025-controls price inversion; both data points reported.) Expect Chinese integrator clones at ≤1/3 price by 2029-2030 *if* the category proves; the US counterweight is domestic-content money: DoD's $110/kg NdPr floor + $400M preferred at MP (10X facility, 2028) [DD-V11-07] and the $1.4B Vulcan/ReElement package [DD-V11-06] pay for onshore feed chains Chinese cells can't serve.

## 5. Market: bottom-up beachhead arithmetic + top-down

**(a) Value ladder ($/kg magnet-contained, 2026 evidence):**
1. Ferrous shred, magnet unseparated: magnet value ≈ steel scrap ($0.2-0.4/kg) — destroyed.
2. Shredded magnet fraction → hydromet: China parity ~$27-28/kg contained-PrNd basis [DD-V11-17]; US merchant feed realizations est. 40-70% of that ($11-19/kg; assumption, no published US quote).
3. Whole-assembly HPMS feed: value captured in-process; HyProMag spread = $55/kg product vs $19.6/kg AISC; pre-processing budget line $1.84/kg [DD-V11-02].
4. **Intact, known-SKU harvested magnets (Noveon spec ≥HDD-size [DD-V11-01])**: worth up to avoided-chemistry — plausible $20-35/kg delivered; ~2-3x shred parity, up to ~10x vs US mixed-shred realizations. Capturers today: integrated players only; the missing equipment is exactly the spoke/dismantler-level cell.
5. Component/direct reuse (VCM assemblies, reman rotors): $/unit, "significantly more value than scrap" (iNEMI/Google/Seagate) [DD-V11-09].

**(b) Feedstock 2027-2032 (honest):**
- **HDD**: 20M+/yr drives retired from major US data centers [DD-V11-09]; at ~12-18 g NdFeB/drive (analyst estimate) → 240-360+ t/yr US. Constraint: certified destruction (NIST SP 800-88 media-sanitization regime; Rev.2 superseded Rev.1 in Sept 2025 [DD-V11-24]) pushes hyperscalers to shred — the WD-Microsoft flow is shred-first [DD-V11-08]. A harvest cell must embed platter destruction to be adoptable; Cyclic-class bulk machines already serve the no-reuse case. HDD = secondary module, not the beachhead.
- **EV motors**: volume arrives late. Global EV fleet ~30M (2022) → ~250M (2030) [DD-V11-11] with 12-15 yr vehicle life → EOL EV motors stay <1 kt/yr NdFeB (US+EU) through ~2031 (0.6-1.7 kg/vehicle measured teardowns [DD-V11-11]); the *mandate* arrives early: ELV removal duty applies ~2028-2029 (EP text: +24 months post entry into force [DD-V11-15]; industry reads "most provisions from mid-2029"), covering every dismantled BEV/HEV incl. crash/warranty units.
- **Industrial/appliance PM motors, MRI**: HyProMag's own feed list; China's 大规模设备更新 program churns a motor stock consuming ~4.5 trillion kWh/yr [DD-V11-20] — but mostly induction (magnet-free); PM subset only.
- **Wind**: direct-drive nacelles carry tonne-class magnet sets in ~70 t assemblies — crane-and-torch work at hub sites, not a cell workpiece. **Excluded from v1; fixed-site service option ≥2032.**

**(c) Bottom-up beachhead (cells x throughput x ASP):** serviceable sites 2029-2032: US magnet-maker spokes (HyProMag 2, Cyclic 5-6, Vulcan/ReElement network, Noveon suppliers) ~10-20; US ITADs with >500K drives/yr ~20-40; EU ATF/dismantling hubs consolidating post-ELV ~50-150; OEM loops 5-15. Realistic capture: **30-120 cells cumulative by 2032** x $0.4-0.9M → **$12-108M cumulative equipment revenue** (mid ~$50M). One cell at 60-80K motors/yr x $2-4/motor RaaS fee = $150-300K/yr recurring; 60 cells → $9-18M/yr recurring by 2032. **(d) Top-down:** ~600 kt EOL magnet stock, <1% recycled (Noveon/RT); EU demand 10 kt (2020) → ~60 kt (2050), with 24-48% of EU NdFeB demand recyclable by 2040 [DD-V11-11]; if US+EU recycled magnet output reaches 12-15 kt/yr by 2032 (HyProMag 1 kt + Vulcan 10 kt + Noveon 2 kt+) and 30-50% flows through harvested/pre-processed streams at $1.5-2.0/kg service value → $6-15M/yr pre-processing value pool + equipment. Triangulation agrees: **tens of millions/yr, not hundreds** — venture-shaped only with recurring fees and software attach.

## 6. GTM: China-first AND US sequences — which leads

**US leads.** Reasoning against the founder's China-first default: (i) China's PrNd-basis scrap pricing [DD-V11-17] compresses the intact premium the cell monetizes; (ii) the buyers with equipment budgets self-build (GEM decade-long 拆解装备 program [DD-V11-19]; Huahong is an equipment maker [DD-V11-18]); (iii) ¥34K commodity dismantlers anchor price expectations [DD-V11-21]. US sequence: 2029 design-partner RaaS pilot inside one DoW-funded feed chain (Vulcan/ReElement network or Noveon supplier; their 10 kt/yr target needs ~11-13 kt/yr feed [DD-V11-06]) + one ITAD pilot with destruction-integrated HDD module; 2030 EU entry as ELV removal duty bites — sell/RaaS into German/French ATF hubs and OEM reman programs; 2031 recipe-library licensing to integrators. China sequence (parallel, asset-light): founder network sources 30-40% of BOM from day one; by 2030-31 license recipes to a Chinese integrator or JV for the 华宏-adjacent market rather than selling US-built cells — margin via software/recipes, not iron.

## 7. Regulatory & geopolitical exposure — **PROVISIONAL** (no POLICY_DELTA.md; POLICY_BRIEF background)

- Product class: industrial robot cell, plausibly EAR99; not an OISP-covered sector today. Caveats: OISP treats AI systems for certain robotics applications as notifiable for China transactions, and COINS implementing regs (~Mar-2027) can add sectors — the China licensing/JV leg needs counsel and a divestable structure (background: GEN3 POLICY_BRIEF).

- US pull: DoD $110/kg NdPr floor for 10 years + 10X offtake [DD-V11-07]; DoW/Commerce $1.4B recycling-centered magnet build [DD-V11-06] — feed provenance ("domestic, chain-of-custody") becomes a paid feature.
- EU pull: ELV Regulation adopted (EP 2026-06-18; Council formality pending; applies +24 months) with pre-shred removal of e-drive motors [DD-V11-14][DD-V11-15]; CRMA Art. 28 magnet labelling/data carriers across EVs, industrial robots, wind, appliances + Art. 29 recycled-content disclosure then delegated-act minimum shares; 2030 EU benchmark: recycling capacity ≥25% of strategic-raw-material consumption [DD-V11-16]. Labels/data carriers literally feed the recipe library.
- Data security: NIST 800-88-class sanitization mandates make *certified destruction* a design requirement for the HDD module, and favor onshore automation over offshore manual teardown [DD-V11-24][DD-V11-08].

## 9. Risks & kill criteria

1. **Route risk (top)**: HPMS-on-whole-rotors + hydromet-on-shred stay good enough; short-loop share stalls. *Kill if by mid-2028 harvested-intact feed commands <1.5x shred parity or short-loop is <15-20% of US recycled magnet output.*
2. **Captive-build reflex**: buyers copy Cyclic/GEM and self-build. *Kill if both 2029 design partners insist on ownership/exclusivity rather than fees.*
3. **Molg adjacency**: ABB-backed recipes extend from servers to motors. *Pivot trigger: Molg ships a motor/magnet recipe before our v1 → become their magnet-recipe supplier or kill.*
4. **IP**: ORNL patents (to 2041) unlicensable and design-around opinion negative → kill [DD-V11-12][DD-V11-13].
5. **Feedstock timing**: EV EOL slips past 2033 and ELV application delayed → freeze EU capex, hold US spokes only.
6. **Price risk**: REE truce collapse/flood; DoD floor insulates US NdPr but not EU/CN realizations [DD-V11-07].

## 10. Verdict

**CONDITIONAL PROCEED — conviction LOW-TO-MODERATE (kill ~55%, vs red team 60%).** The red team's core objection is confirmed by primary evidence (shred-first WD/Microsoft flow [DD-V11-08]; whole-rotor HPMS economics [DD-V11-02]; captive GEM/Huahong equipment culture [DD-V11-18][DD-V11-19]) — *as an equipment vendor V11 is not venture-scale*. What the red team under-weighted: the ELV removal mandate is now law with a 2028-29 application date [DD-V11-15], CRMA magnet labels create machine-readable recipes [DD-V11-16], and DoW-funded short-loop capacity (Noveon/Vulcan) has a genuine intact-feed spec [DD-V11-01][DD-V11-06]. The honest venture form is **RaaS + recipe library** (per-motor/per-kg fees, cells as deployed COGS), US-first, EU-second, China-as-supply-chain-and-licensee. Cheapest 2026-2028 validation experiments:
1. **Feed-spec price discovery (<$5K):** structured interviews/LOIs with Noveon, Vulcan/ReElement, HyProMag USA feed teams — the single number that decides everything: $/kg premium for intact known-SKU magnets vs shred parity, at what annual tonnage.
2. **Teardown benchmark (<$25K):** buy 15-20 EOL EDUs + 500 EOL HDDs; instrument manual vs jig-assisted extraction; publish $/kg-harvested and integrity curves vs the $1.84/kg HPMS pre-processing benchmark [DD-V11-02].
3. **EU ATF willingness-to-pay (<$10K):** survey 30-50 German/French dismantlers on post-ELV motor handling plans and $2-4/motor fee acceptance; convert 3+ into pilot MOUs.
