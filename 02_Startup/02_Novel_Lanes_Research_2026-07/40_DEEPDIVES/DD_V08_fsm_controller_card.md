## 1. Problem & who has it

The claimed problem: FSO/beam-control integrators without controls teams must either buy a bespoke, price-on-request drive+tracking stack or build FPGA control in-house. Named archetypes, checked against what they actually do:

- **OGS integrator engineer** (Cailabs, Astrolight, Safran, SSC/Contec-class). Real and growing: Cailabs raised €57M (2025-09-12), has **>10 OGS under contract**, and built a line assembling **5 OGS in parallel** [DD-V08-07]; ESA's Optical Nucleus Network (Tenerife/Almería/Nemea) explicitly uses a **COTS-component station design** [DD-V08-08]; Greece's Holomondas OGS opened 2026-05-21 with equipment from Astrolight (2.5 Gbps downlink) [DD-V08-09]. NASA's LCOT is the archetype blueprint: COTS 70 cm telescope, FSMs for fine tracking + uplink point-ahead [DD-V08-21, snippet]. **But**: these integrators either (i) buy the FSM *with its vendor controller* (PI/Newport/Cedrat bundles — LCOT philosophy), or (ii) are themselves selling the tracking know-how (Cailabs, Astrolight). The unmet need is PAT *software*, and they regard that as their own product.
- **Drone/HAPS FSO lead** (GA-ASI LAC-12 pod on MQ-1/MQ-9 [DD-V08-12]). Prime-integrated; GA-ASI builds beam control in-house; no merchant socket observed.
- **Directed-energy testbed engineer** (Navy N251-031 "Advanced Beam Control and Wave Slap Mitigation", 12-inch mast beam director, FSO/LIDAR/laser, jitter compensation; ≥1 Phase I ≈ $140K awarded) [DD-V08-20, snippet][DD-V08-22, snippet]. This is an SBIR performer channel, not a purchase order for a card.
- **Quantum-link/academic AO groups**: catalog buyers today of PI/Newport/Optotune — the incumbents' home turf [DD-V08-03][DD-V08-04][DD-V08-26].

Quantified niche (merchant tracking-electronics sockets, 2027-2031, ex-China): new OGS builds ~**200-600 cumulative** (anchor: $50M OGS market 2024 [DD-V08-06] ≈ 20-50 stations/yr at $1-3M ASP, growing 25-35%/yr inside Yole's $3B-by-2030 sector [DD-V08-06]; uncertainty dominated by hyperscaler direct-to-earth adoption); 1-3 FSM loops per station → **300-1,800 FSM control sockets**; drone/HAPS pods ~50-300 (prime-captive); DE testbeds ~10-30; quantum telescopes 20-60 (subset of OGS count). Of these, the share bought as a *third-party card rather than a mirror-vendor bundle or in-house build* is the contested sliver: observed behavior (LCOT, Nucleus, Cailabs) says **10-25%**.

## 2. Product & extreme edge

Proposed: <100 g / <10 W FPGA card, kHz closed loop, quaternion feed-forward, camera/quad-cell-in-the-loop PAT firmware, mirror-agnostic (VCM + piezo).

Specs vs SOTA (all fetched unless noted):

| Incumbent | What it is | Numbers |
|---|---|---|
| Cedrat CCBu20 | embedded 2-ch all-in-one piezo controller, SG conditioner, closed loop, GUI | 91×77×37 mm; -20…+150 V; OEM board class [DD-V08-01] |
| Cedrat CCBu20-NS | same, New-Space derivative, rad/vib-tolerant, TM/TC + fast SPI | status **prototype**, "various space grades" [DD-V08-02] |
| PI E-727 | 3/4-ch digital piezo controller; notch filters, 4th-order linearization, DDL, data recorder; sensors: capacitive/SG/piezoresistive | -30…+130 V; TCP-IP/USB/SPI/RS-232; quote-only pricing [DD-V08-03] |
| Newport FSM-300 | complete VCM FSM **system incl. controller** | ±1.5°, ≤1 µrad rms, **$12,667 catalog, in stock**; applications: "laser beam stabilization… tracking… free-space communication" [DD-V08-04] |
| Mirrorcle MEMS kits | mirror + USB MEMS controller (16-bit, 120 kS/s, ~0-200 V) | **$4,999-$8,490** catalog [DD-V08-05] |
| Optotune MR series | VCM FSM + OEM driver + reference design | catalog via Edmund [DD-V08-26, snippet] |

What the incumbents demonstrably **lack** (from their own fetched feature lists): integrated *target acquisition* (spiral scan, camera/quad-cell-in-the-loop), PAT state machines, platform-attitude (quaternion) feed-forward, disturbance-adaptive retuning, fleet calibration software. Cedrat/PI ship *position servos*; the customer writes the tracking loop. So a real gap exists — but it is a **software/firmware gap**, and the honest product is a **PAT subsystem (software stack + reference card)**, not a drive card. Two consequences: (i) the moat moves from electronics (weak — E-727-class hardware is mature) to control software that a terminal prime's controls engineer or a grad student can replicate per-program; (ii) the "10x procurement friction" claim collapses at the low end, where Newport/Mirrorcle already sell catalog, in-stock, published-spec systems at $5-13K [DD-V08-04][DD-V08-05]. RT's physics point stands unrebutted: galvo speed / gimbal compactness / zero-power holding is an **actuator** trilemma [DD-V08-23, snippet]; a card cannot fix it, and per-mirror notch/compensator tuning against mechanism resonances makes "mirror-agnostic" a services promise (my own control-engineering assessment, consistent with RT §4).

## 3. Feasibility & TRL path to sellable v1 (2029-2031)

Feasibility is not the gate. BOM sketch: Zynq-class FPGA SoM ($150-400); 2-4 ch 18-20-bit DAC/ADC + SG/capacitive AFE ($100-300); VCM H-bridge stage (tens of W) and/or ±150 V piezo stage ($150-400 — HV piezo power is C·V²·f-bound and breaks the <10 W claim for large mirrors, per RT, unverified but physically sound); event-camera/quad-cell input; chassis/conn ($100-250). **BOM $600-1,500; COGS ~$2-3K at 100-unit lots; must price ≤$15K against a $12,667 complete-system reference [DD-V08-04]** — the one-pager's $15-60K ASP is above reference class except as flight/rad-tolerant SKUs (where Cedrat's CCBu20-NS is already positioned [DD-V08-02]). Buy: SoM, AFE, optics, mirrors (never build actuators). Build: firmware/PAT stack, HIL rig (COTS FSM + disturbance table + sky/target simulator). Cleanroom: none. Timeline: card + firmware v1 in 12 mo (2029-2030); the gate is 18-36 mo aerospace design-in (RT C10), pushing first meaningful revenue to 2031-2032. TRL: 4→6 on internal HIL; 7 requires a host program — i.e., a customer who by §1 mostly does not exist as a merchant buyer.

## 4. Competitive landscape — global and Chinese

**Global.** Cedrat: catalog OEM embedded controllers (CCBu20/40) plus flight derivative (CCBu20-NS) and demo kits — the electronics layer is *not* missing [DD-V08-01][DD-V08-02]. PI: E-727 explicitly aimed at FSM/tracking applications, quote-priced [DD-V08-03]. Newport (MKS): complete FSM systems at published catalog prices [DD-V08-04]. Mirrorcle: MEMS mirrors + controllers $5-8.5K [DD-V08-05]. Optotune: VCM FSM + OEM driver + reference design [DD-V08-26]. FSO Instruments (Demcon/VDL/TNO): merchant fine-steering *mechanism* [RT-V08-08, prior]. Above the card: terminal primes internalize PAT — Mynaric makes CONDOR electronics **in-house in Hawthorne** (optical heads Munich; 370-terminal backlog; NG picked it for 56 T1 satellites) [DD-V08-10]; Tesat/CACI advertise beaconless PAT and in-house fine tracking as terminal features [DD-V08-25, snippet]; SpaceX is entering merchant *terminal* sales, compressing everything below it (8,000+ LCTs to launch this decade, 98% ISL) [DD-V08-11]. Motion-control analogy (parent's question c): Ingenia — Barcelona OEM servo-drive house — was absorbed by Novanta/Celera (2019-04-18) [DD-V08-14]; and Scanlab, the one great merchant beam-steering electronics business (~**40,000 scan systems/yr**, RTC control boards sold separately to laser-machine OEMs) [DD-V08-15], won because beam steering is **not** the laser-machine builder's differentiator. In FSO the tracking loop **is** the differentiator — the analogy inverts against V08.

**Chinese (zh, mandatory).** A complete domestic substitution stack already exists: **芯明天 Coremorrow** (Harbin): modular/board-level piezo controllers (E00/E01 families) *and* a dedicated 压电快反镜/偏转镜 (piezo FSM/deflector) product category with matched controllers, applications incl. adaptive optics and laser tech [DD-V08-16]. **三英精控 SYMC** (Tianjin): piezo FSMs with closed-loop feedback marketed for 卫星激光通信/ATP [DD-V08-17]. **上海隐冠 Yinguan** (est. 2019, semiconductor-motion lineage): piezo stacks (≤180 µm, 4,000 N, sub-ms) *plus self-developed piezo servo controllers* explicitly for 卫星激光通信 FSMs [DD-V08-18]. **同茂 Tongmao**: VCM FSM structures [DD-V08-24, snippet]. Research base: CAS 光电所/长春光机所 lineage dominates FSM/ATP literature [DD-V08-23, snippet]; current peer-reviewed Chinese work reports VCM-FSM tracking errors of 0.0036°/0.0131° on 60/120 Hz trajectories under 100 Hz disturbance (Chinese Optics, 2024) [DD-V08-19]. **Pricing signals:** none of the Chinese vendors publish prices (quote-only, standard for the class); the observable signal is category coverage — every layer V08 would sell (piezo stack, driver board, closed-loop controller, integrated FSM) has ≥2 domestic Chinese suppliers, so third-market competition arrives at Chinese cost structure while the US side is export-gated (§7). Report-mill FSM "market sizes" span $0.5B-$12B and should be discarded in favor of Yole's bottom-up: LCT systems $550M (2024) → $2.4B (2030), optical *devices* ~$60M → ~$400M, OGS $50M (2024) [DD-V08-06] — conflicting data shown; Yole preferred (method + segment granularity).

## 6. GTM: China-first AND US sequences

- **US-first (only viable sequence).** Year 1: SBIR channel (N251-031-class beam-control topics recur [DD-V08-20]) to fund a HIL-proven PAT stack; publish tracking specs against an E-727+in-house-loop baseline. Year 2: land 1-2 OGS integrators without controls depth (second-tier university/national-program stations building LCOT-derived designs [DD-V08-21][DD-V08-08]) as reference design wins; sell "PAT subsystem + SDK," not a card. Year 3+: attempt one drone-FSO or DE prime as sub-tier supplier. Leads: SBIR TPOC relationships + LCOT-blueprint adopters, because they are the only buyers whose *architecture* has an open slot.
- **China-first: not executable.** The card's defining specs (≥100 Hz control bandwidth at ≤10 µrad-class accuracy; ±26 mrad/500 Hz mirror-carrier thresholds) sit inside 6A004.d optical-control-equipment parameters [DD-V08-13]; space-qualified variants shadow into ITAR/9x515 (unchanged Aug-2025 USML revision left optical-comms terminals controlled) [PD-04]; Sept-2025 entity tranche added CAS-linked photonics/quantum procurement channels [PD-03]. A US-person founder selling PAT electronics into Chinese OGS/ATP programs is license-denial territory, and §1 shows China doesn't need it (Coremorrow/SYMC/Yinguan [DD-V08-16][DD-V08-17][DD-V08-18]). POLICY_DELTA G05: **CN ⛔ / US ✅ / PAR ⛔** — a Chinese sibling is disqualifying for the only real (defense/space-adjacent) customers. The founder's China network is worth ~zero in this lane.

## 7. Regulatory & geopolitical exposure (coordinated with POLICY_DELTA)

Item layer: below-threshold products (<100 Hz bandwidth, >10 µrad, ≤50 mm mirrors) can be EAR99 — but a kHz/µrad tracking card is *by definition* above 6A004.d thresholds ("steering… equipment" with ≥100 Hz bandwidths and 10 µrad-class accuracy; beam-steering-mirror carriers ±26 mrad travel / ≥500 Hz resonance / ≤10 µrad) [DD-V08-13]; de-speccing out of control = de-speccing out of the value proposition. NS-column licensing to D:1 destinations kills PRC sales; military/DE end-uses pull toward USML Cat XII "specially designed" territory (boundary fact-specific — CONSULT COUNSEL, per mission caveat). Space FSO remains ITAR/9x515-shadowed after the Aug-2025 targeted USML revisions [PD-04]. Team composition: deemed-export gating for PRC-national hires the moment tech classifies above EAR99 [PD-04→GEN3 P-08]. Customer screening burden rose with the Sept-2025 entity tranche (CAS channels) [PD-03]. Reactivation stack (Nov-2026 MOFCOM/Affiliates) is mostly irrelevant here — the lane is already one-sided. Net: **US/allied-only business with USP-heavy hiring; the geopolitical structure removes half the founder's edge.**

## 10. Verdict: conviction + cheapest validation experiments (2026-2028)

**NO-GO. The steelman (proliferating second-tier integrators need a PAT socket) is real but arithmetic-small (§5) and software-shaped, which strips the founder's hardware edge. **Strongest pivot-adjacency (parent q-e), with numbers:** PAT hardware-in-the-loop **test/EGSE benches** for terminal OEMs and interop labs — 15-40 buyers (terminal vendors [DD-V08-25], NRL-style interop labs [DD-V08-10], OGS factories running 5-parallel integration [DD-V08-07]), 1-4 benches each, ASP $150-400K → **$5-15M/yr peak**, sold to engineering orgs that *do* outsource test gear (unlike flight PAT); note mission's diagnostics/test cap applies at portfolio level. Industrial-laser beam control is Scanlab's fortress (40,000 systems/yr [DD-V08-15]) — do not attack.

Cheapest validation experiments 2026-2028 (run *before* any build):
1. **Buyer-of-record sweep (~$3-5K, 6 weeks, 2026):** 15 interviews (OGS integrators from the Nucleus/LCOT ecosystem, 2 drone-FSO programs, 2 DE labs): "show me the PO line for tracking electronics." Pass = ≥3 budgeted merchant line items ≥$25K. Predicted: fail.
2. **Benchtop PAT-stack bake-off (~$18-25K, one quarter, 2027):** Optotune/Newport FSM + own FPGA PAT firmware vs E-727+vendor servo; publish acquisition-time/RMS-tracking numbers under disturbance. Tests whether the software layer alone commands a price premium (pre-orders or eval-license revenue = pass).
3. **SBIR probe ($0 capital, 2026-2027):** submit to the next N251-031-class beam-control topic [DD-V08-20]; a Phase I award + TPOC pull = the only cheap way to test defense WTP; twice rejected = channel closed.
