# Scoring rubric — 100 points

Score each criterion 0–5, then multiply by its weight/5. Cite evidence for every score above 3.

| Criterion | Weight | Question |
|---|---:|---|
| Demonstrated demand | 16 | Are named buyers spending, tendering, piloting, or under a forced deadline? |
| Frontier/coolness and vision | 15 | Is the technical leap genuinely exciting with a large expansion narrative? |
| High-end niche quality | 10 | Is the beachhead narrow, valuable, reachable, and underserved? |
| Competition whitespace | 9 | Are incumbents few/slow/misaligned, with a defendable wedge? |
| Reachable validation budget | 9 | Can the key risk be tested for $25k–$250k and a sellable v1 plausibly reached before $5M cumulative capital? |
| Technical elegance/controllability | 11 | Is the system measurable, modellable, testable, and engineerable in bounded steps? |
| 10x technical edge | 7 | Is there a credible order-of-magnitude edge on a customer-valued dimension? |
| US–China dual-market leverage | 10 | Is there a credible independent beachhead in the US, China, or ideally both, supported country by country? |
| 2030 launch-window fit | 8 | Can preparation occur in 2026–2029, with a real 2030–2034 trigger and an opportunity window still open? |
| Expansion economics | 3 | Can the niche become a platform or adjacent product family? |
| Founder skill transfer | 2 | Do generic EE/CE skills shorten learning/build time? |

Hard gates before scoring:

- G1: at least two independent demand sources, including one primary buyer/procurement/filing.
- G2: no academic claim without affirmative peer-review status.
- G3: a bounded 6–18 month de-risk experiment and explicit budget.
- G4: named closest competitors and a non-cosmetic difference.
- G5: no dependence on nonexistent physics, unlimited capital, or one unverifiable market forecast.
- G6: safety/regulatory/export-control path is acknowledged; prohibited or structurally blocked
  cross-border routes cannot be the base case.
- G7: explicit 2030 timing thesis: bounded 2026–2029 pre-company de-risking, named 2030–2034
  demand trigger, expected TRL/adoption readiness at launch, and window-risk analysis. Reject if
  commoditization is likely before 2030 or commercial readiness is implausible by 2034.

Portfolio diversity gates for the final 24:

- at least 12 distinct lanes; no lane contributes more than 3 ideas;
- no more than 4 superconductivity/HTS ideas combined;
- no more than 6 diagnostic/test-only products;
- at least 14 products that directly deliver customer process/output value;
- at least 8 ideas with a <$100k decisive first experiment;
- at least 8 primarily sold to industrial customers, 4 to scientific/big-physics customers, and
  4 to infrastructure/utility/transport customers;
- at least 18 have credible US beachheads, at least 18 have credible China beachheads, and at
  least 12 have both (an idea may count in all three);
- no more than 4 may rely primarily on Japan, Taiwan, or South Korea; those markets may otherwise
  appear as optional expansion routes. India and Singapore must not appear as markets.
- all final 24 pass G7; at least 12 have a decisive pre-company experiment executable by 2028 and
  at least 8 have an identifiable design-in/procurement engagement path beginning by 2029.
