## 4. Certification and export constraints -- the central finding of this evidence file

**P3R2-F-01-S01** (Federal Register / govinfo.gov, directly fetched): effective **December 2, 2024**, BIS added **"Naura Technology Group Co., Ltd."** and **"Piotech"** (with regional subsidiaries) to the **Entity List**, license requirement "**for all items subject to the EAR**," review policy "**presumption of denial**." **AMEC is NOT on the Entity List** (per this research; it has had an unstable, separate history on the US Department of Defense's "Chinese Military Company" 1260H list, added and removed multiple times through 2024, which is a different instrument from BIS's Entity List and does not itself trigger EAR licensing).

**This means two of the idea's own three named CN buyers -- NAURA and Piotech -- are Entity-Listed with presumption-of-denial licensing for all EAR-subject items.** Only AMEC is a directly reachable named buyer without Entity List exposure.

**P3R2-F-01-S02** (Sidley Austin, directly fetched): BIS's "Affiliates Rule" (the "50% Rule"), effective **September 29, 2025**, extends Entity List license requirements to "any foreign entity owned 50% or more by listed companies," closing the prior loophole for unlisted subsidiaries, and -- critically -- **"liability under the EAR can attach regardless of actual or constructive knowledge"** (strict liability). This rule postdates the idea's likely last export-control review and is not reflected in the seed's `precompany_plan_2026_2029` text (which only specifies a "2027 export-control counsel gate").

**Read together, these findings materially sharpen the risk of F-01's CN chapter:**
1. The plan's own described mechanics -- an HK-structured IP entity, milestone-based license contracts, founder retaining "golden-reference subassemblies, calibration methodology, and factory test benches" offshore -- reads as a structure designed to keep the underlying technology outside direct EAR jurisdiction. Under the new strict-liability 50% rule, this is not obviously a safe harbor: if Hengyunchang or Injet (the disclosed intermediary supply-chain partners, per the idea's own text) sell components into NAURA- or Piotech-controlled tooling, or if any downstream entity is 50%+ owned by an Entity-Listed party, exposure could attach regardless of the licensor's actual knowledge.
2. Structuring specifically to route around export jurisdiction is itself a compliance red flag (evasion-adjacent), independent of whether the structure would technically succeed.
3. AMEC alone (not Entity-Listed) remains a legally cleaner direct target, meaningfully narrowing the buyer list the idea presents as evidence of CN demand.

## 5. US / CN verdicts

- **US: PASS-with-conditions.** Real underlying RF-power revenue base at the incumbent level (Advanced Energy); Comet's Synertia confirms the specific fast-matching white space is real and unclaimed (S03); demand for the exact <10-microsecond solid-state product remains unproven at this TRL4/precompany stage, consistent with (not contradicting) the idea's own 2028 evaluation milestones.
- **CN: UNCERTAIN, materially more constrained than the seed assumed.** Two of three named buyers (NAURA, Piotech) are BIS Entity-Listed with presumption-of-denial licensing, freshly reinforced by the September 2025 strict-liability 50% Affiliates Rule. AMEC alone remains a clean direct target. The idea's own "2027 export-control counsel gate" is necessary but should be treated as **binding and higher-stakes** than the original seed language suggests -- this is not a footnote-level compliance task but a threshold legal-structuring question that could determine whether the CN chapter is viable at all in its currently described form.

But the **CN leg's commercial/legal window is now conditioned on a threshold export-control-structuring question** (Section 4) that did not exist in this form when the idea was last scored -- the 2027 export-control counsel gate should be pulled forward and treated as a hard go/no-go checkpoint, not a routine compliance step, before further capital or team commitment to the CN chapter.
