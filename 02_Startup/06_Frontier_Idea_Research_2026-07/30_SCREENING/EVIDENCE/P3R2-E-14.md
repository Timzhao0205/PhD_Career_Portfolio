## 4. Certification and export constraints

US: NERC PRC-029-1 / FERC Order 909 (atlas L08-043) is a real, near-term compliance requirement and a plausible moat if the relay is positioned as compliance tooling. No BIS Entity List exposure found for State Grid Corporation of China or XJ Electric in this research (they do not appear to be Entity-Listed), so the CN license-only channel is not blocked by Entity List status -- but any HIL-bench/firmware technology transfer to China would still require standard EAR classification review (likely a lower-tier ECCN or EAR99 for grid-protection relay logic, materially lower risk than the quantum/semiconductor-equipment items in the other four ideas in this batch, but not zero-risk). This is consistent with, and supports, the idea's own conservative "license-only, no CN entity" scoping.

## 5. US / CN verdicts

- **US: PASS.** This is the strongest demand case of the five ideas assigned: three independent developers (Pattern Energy, Invenergy, Grid United), multiple named multi-terminal projects with disclosed capital ($2.6B, $1.7B+ in contractor awards, and a four-project pipeline), plus a structural federal regulatory driver (FERC Order 1920) pushing toward interregional/multi-terminal topologies that specifically need vendor-neutral DC protection. Buyers (developers, converter vendors, TSOs) are reachable via public project pipelines.
- **CN: license-only by design, not a beachhead -- appropriately scoped.** Real CN demand exists (State Grid UHV tender activity, atlas L08-034) but NR Electric's entrenched incumbent position (S04) makes a merchant relay/discrimination beachhead unrealistic; the license-only test-suite channel remains legally viable (no Entity List block found) but should be read as a minor supplementary revenue stream, not a growth engine.

## 6. 2030 timing (two independent sources, window verdict)

1. **Primary/official:** DOE Transmission Facilitation Program funding for Southern Spirit ($360M, atlas L08-041), dated 2026 construction start / 2029 in-service.
2. **Independent official (separate federal instrument):** FERC Order No. 1920 (May 13, 2024; amended Nov 21, 2024) -- **P3R2-E-14-S03** -- mandating interregional transmission-planning coordination, with MISO's own compliance deadline extended to June 12, 2026, per public reporting.

**Window verdict:** Opens 2026 (multiple projects breaking ground: Southern Spirit, Grain Belt Express) through 2029-2032 (in-service dates), and persists into the early 2030s as Grid United's pipeline (North Plains Connector construction from 2028) continues the buildout. **New risk flagged:** **P3R2-E-14-S06** (Grist, 2024) documents real state-level and incumbent-utility opposition to Southern Spirit specifically (Louisiana SB108 eminent-domain restriction; Entergy Mississippi's regulatory challenge) -- a project-level risk not mentioned in the idea's own timing_window_risk text, though it does not appear to threaten the broader multi-project national pipeline (Grain Belt Express and Grid United's projects are unaffected by Louisiana law).
