# P3R2-B-14 — Demand/Competitor Evidence

Concept: Dual-standard megawatt charging/battery-swap interface power conversion bridging Chinese
GB/T swap ecosystems and IEC TS 63379 MCS static charging, for exported mining/port fleets. Primary
market: CN (contingent/standards-arbitrage thesis).

**Binding pre-promotion condition (fix_applied_notes, 2026-07-13 FIX):** "documented evidence that
one named export program (XCMG-Fortescue delivery chain or an SPIC swap-export follow-on) will
procure dual-standard interface hardware; without it the standalone thesis ends." Demand is already
explicitly classified in the seed as "contingent (an option on standards non-convergence), not a
base-demand seed." Evaluated first because it governs the gate result.

## 1. Demand

**XCMG-Fortescue channel (checked directly):**
- International Mining (im-mining.com), 2025-09-26 (`P3R2-B-14-S01`, fetched): XCMG will supply "up
  to half" of Fortescue's planned fleet of 300-400 zero-emission 240t haul trucks, phased deliveries
  2028-2030. No charging-standard, connector, or dual-standard-hardware procurement is mentioned.
- The Driven (thedriven.io), 2026-05-25 (`P3R2-B-14-S02`, fetched): Fortescue's first 240t electric
  haul truck (Liebherr T264, 2.6MWh battery) is being commissioned alongside Fortescue's own
  **in-house-developed** 6MW fast charger ("Fortescue Zero production series power system"),
  fixed (not pantograph) charging, ~30-minute full charge. **The article does not reference IEC TS
  63379/MCS compliance or any third-party dual-standard hardware purchase — Fortescue built its own
  proprietary charging system rather than procuring a merchant dual-standard converter.**

**SPIC Qiyuan channel (checked directly):**
- Rio Tinto official press release, 2025-10-27 (`P3R2-B-14-S03`, fetched): Rio Tinto and China's
  State Power Investment Corporation (SPIC) Qiyuan launched a battery-swap trial at Oyu Tolgoi —
  8 Tonly 91t trucks, 13x800kWh batteries, one swap station, one static charger, explicitly labeled a
  **trial** running through end-2026, not a commercial deployment. No export program, no MCS/dual-
  standard hardware, and no follow-on commercial order is mentioned in the release.

**Verdict: the FIX gate is NOT satisfied.** Neither named channel shows evidence of procuring
dual-standard interface hardware. Fortescue's proprietary in-house charger and SPIC's still-running
trial both point away from, not toward, a near-term merchant dual-standard converter buy. This is
consistent with the seed's own "contingent, not base-demand" self-classification, which the fresh
evidence supports rather than resolves.

## 2. Competitors / standards landscape

- CharIN (global consortium), IEC TS 63379 official publication, 2026-02-09 (`P3R2-B-14-S04`,
  charin.global, fetched): confirms the MCS connector/cable/interlock specification is now formally
  published (a "landmark achievement" per CharIN's chairman), covering conductive DC charging at
  megawatt power levels; no commercial-rollout date is stated in the release itself (separate trade
  reporting places broad commercial MCS rollout at 2027-2028).
- XCMG — in-house power-electronics team building its own truck-side charging interface (implied by
  Fortescue building its own charger rather than sourcing a merchant interface).
- CATL swap ecosystem and SPIC Qiyuan — China's dominant battery-swap technology suppliers for heavy
  equipment; both are pursuing their own vertically-integrated swap-station/static-charger combos
  (Rio Tinto release explicitly credits "China's widely used and leading battery swap technology"),
  reducing the need for a third-party bridging converter within their own ecosystem.
- Western MCS charger vendors (ABB E-mobility, Siemens, ChargePoint-class heavy-duty entrants) —
  not independently re-verified this session; treated as the existing MCS-only competitive set per
  the seed's own framing.
