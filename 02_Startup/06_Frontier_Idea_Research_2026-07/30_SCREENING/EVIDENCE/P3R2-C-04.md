## 1. Demand (per market leg)

### US leg
- **Primary (regulatory trigger):** Atlas L14-033 (US EPA, AIM Act HFC phasedown FAQ, `regulator`, T1).
  Independently re-confirmed this session via search of EPA.gov and the Federal Register: allowance
  step-down to 30% of baseline for 2029-2033, 20% for 2034-2035, 15% (85% total cut) from 2036 --
  consistent with the atlas figure, no change found.
- **Independent second:** Atlas L14-034 (EU F-gas Regulation 2024/573, `standard`, T1) -- re-confirmed:
  2025 quota cut to 24.3% of baseline, 12.3% from 2027; GWP>150 stationary-refrigeration placing-on-market
  ban from 2030. Both are real, dated (2024-2025), binding regulatory triggers independent of each other
  (US vs EU) and independent of any single vendor's claims.
- **Verdict: demand is real** -- these are enacted rules with hard compliance dates (2029, 2030), not
  aspirational targets, and they directly remove the incumbent PFAS-based two-phase fluid option from the
  legal market on a schedule that lands inside C-04's 2030 launch window.

### CN leg
- **Primary:** Atlas L14-035 (GB 40879, China's mandatory data center energy-efficiency standard,
  `standard`, T1) and new source **P3R2-C-04-S02** (NDRC/MIIT Data Center Green and Low-Carbon Development
  Special Action Plan, July 2024, fetched, government, T1): PUE ≤ 1.25 for new/expanded large data centers
  by end-2025, PUE ≤ 1.2 for national-hub-node projects (figures corroborated via independent secondary
  citations of the same document; the PDF itself was not machine-OCR-extractable this session -- see ledger
  notes).
- **Independent second:** Atlas L14-039 (T/CIEP 0263-2025, China liquid-cooling technical specification,
  `standard`, T2).
- **Verdict: demand is real and PUE-driven**, but note the binding caution above: China's PUE/liquid-cooling
  push is a genuine efficiency mandate, not evidence of a specific "PFAS-free" or "two-phase" requirement --
  air-cooled and single-phase water solutions can also satisfy GB 40879. The fluid-chemistry-specific demand
  driver in China is weaker than in the US/EU (where HFC/PFAS regulation is chemistry-specific by
  definition).

**G1: PASS (US leg, regulatory-driven); PASS with caveat (CN leg -- efficiency-driven, not chemistry-driven).**

## 4. Certification and export constraints

- EPA AIM Act Technology Transitions rules (atlas L14-033) directly restrict which refrigerants/fluids can
  be used in new equipment by sector, with a May-2026 final rule extending some compliance deadlines
  (confirmed via search this session, EPA.gov) -- this is the single most important regulatory gate for
  C-04 and should be tracked continuously, not treated as fixed.
- EU F-gas Regulation (L14-034): GWP>150 stationary-refrigeration equipment placing-on-market ban from 2030
  -- a hard EU compliance date landing exactly at C-04's launch year.
- Export separability: thermal hardware (cold plates, pumps, condensers) is not export-controlled; the
  fluid itself could be subject to chemical-specific trade rules depending on final chemistry choice --
  not independently verified this session, flagged as a pre-launch diligence item.

## 5. Primary-market verdicts

- **US:** Demand real and regulation-anchored (AIM Act), buyer reachable via ODM/CDU vendor design-in;
  window open through the 2029-2033 HFC step-down period. Competitive field is well-funded (ZutaCore
  $100M+, Accelsius $65M) but none confirmed as explicitly PFAS-free -- a genuine, if narrow, wedge remains
  if C-04 can demonstrate a qualified non-PFAS fluid at datacenter scale before 2031 (its own kill trigger).
- **CN:** Demand real but efficiency-driven (GB 40879 PUE mandate) rather than chemistry-driven; buyer
  reachable via CN CDU vendors (Envicool) but the specific "PFAS-free" differentiator carries less force in
  China absent an HFC-equivalent mandate. Window open but weaker than the US leg.

## 6. 2030 timing (two independent sources)

1. **Primary/official:** EPA AIM Act allowance step-down (atlas L14-033, `regulator`, T1): 30% of baseline
   allowance 2029-2033 -- a primary, official, dated (2029) US regulatory trigger squarely inside the
   2028-2035 window this task requires.
2. **Primary/official (second, independent):** EU F-gas Regulation 2024/573 (atlas L14-034, `standard`,
   T1): GWP>150 stationary-refrigeration placing-on-market ban from **2030** -- an independent (different
   jurisdiction, different legal instrument) primary trigger landing exactly at the launch year.

**Verdict:** the window **opens as regulation bites in 2029-2030** and should **persist** through at least
the mid-2030s given the AIM Act's multi-step schedule (85% cut not reached until 2036) -- this is one of the
better-anchored 2030 timing cases in this batch, because both triggers are enacted law with fixed dates, not
forecasts. The binding 2031 fluid-availability kill trigger remains the correct check: if no non-PFAS
two-phase fluid reaches datacenter-scale qualification by then, single-phase water is likely to win the
cycle instead, as the seed's own risk note already states.
