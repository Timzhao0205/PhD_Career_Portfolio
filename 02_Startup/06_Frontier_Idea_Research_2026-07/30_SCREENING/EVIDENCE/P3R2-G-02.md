## 1. Demand

**CN accelerator/irradiation market context (real, growing, but not consumables-specific):** CGN Dasheng's listed
parent, CGN Nuclear Technology Development Co. (中广核核技术发展, CGNNT, 000881.SZ), reported in its FY2025 annual
report (dated 2026-04-27) that its accelerator-and-irradiation-processing segment generated **RMB 601M revenue
(+15.88% YoY)** at 28.45% gross margin, with **overseas accelerator sales up 264.3% YoY (a record)** and irradiation-
sterilization revenue **up 52% YoY**, plus 400+ new irradiation customers and a newly-introduced 12-inch-wafer
irradiation process (**P3R2-G-02-S03**). This is real, dated, quantified evidence that China's industrial e-beam
accelerator and irradiation-service market is large and growing -- the addressable context G-02's consumables thesis
depends on. **Honest limitation:** this is CGN Dasheng's own disclosure about its own accelerator/irradiation-service
business, not direct evidence that it or its customers are procuring THIRD-PARTY merchant beam-exit-window/target
consumables; no named operator purchase order for windows/targets specifically was found this session, consistent with
the idea's own admission that "component-level pain is INFERRED... no atlas source yet documents window/target failure
economics." The isotope-cyclotron leg (China Isotope & Radiation Corp / Atom Hi-Tech centralized procurement, atlas
L05-036) was not independently re-verified this session (time-budget triage) and is carried forward from the existing
atlas citation.

**Permissive-ISO correction (binding condition -- CONFIRMED as written, gate remains UNMET):** ISO 11137-1:2025,
published 2025-04-08, raised allowable sterilization energy limits from 10 to 11 MeV (e-beam) and 5 to 7.5 MeV (X-ray)
(**P3R2-G-02-S04**) -- this is correctly framed by the idea as a *permissive* change (enabling, not mandating,
uprating). No named operator or OEM announcement of an actual uprate to the new limits was found this session. Per the
idea's own binding rule, **this means the ISO trigger should be treated NOW as de-weighted to "CN consumables +
localization demand only,"** not carried as an active fleet-wide re-qualification driver -- this de-weighting should be
applied as the current base case, not merely flagged as a future contingency.

## 2. Competitors -- merchant-targetry incumbency map (BINDING, PRE-PROMOTION)

- **ARTMS** (Vancouver, Canada) -- **acquired by Telix Pharmaceuticals in April 2024** for US$57.5M upfront plus
  contingent earn-outs (**P3R2-G-02-S05**); ARTMS is no longer an independent merchant supplier, now operating as a
  captive technology arm inside Telix's vertically-integrated radiopharma supply chain.
- **IBA** (Belgium) -- explicitly "the world leader in PET and SPECT cyclotrons," and confirmed to sell **target systems
  (Nirta liquid/solid/gas targets, Fluidomica) directly alongside its cyclotrons**, not just the cyclotrons themselves
  (**P3R2-G-02-S06**) -- i.e., IBA is already a bundled cyclotron-plus-target merchant supplier.
- **Comecer** (Italy, an ATS company) -- established solid-target-processing system vendor (ALCEO).
- **Conclusion, per the idea's own pre-agreed rule:** merchant cyclotron-targetry **is** already served by entrenched,
  well-resourced incumbents (IBA, Comecer) and is consolidating further (Telix/ARTMS). **The cyclotron-target leg
  should therefore be re-scoped away from**, leaving the e-beam/X-ray window+converter leg as the only leg with a
  plausible remaining white-space claim.
- **E-beam/X-ray window+converter leg:** Varex Imaging is confirmed as a vertically-integrated Linatron X-ray-
  accelerator manufacturer for cargo/security screening, with its own products described as serving "most cargo
  screening systems worldwide" (**P3R2-G-02-S07**); no public disclosure was found confirming or denying whether Varex
  sources its beam-exit windows/conversion targets in-house or from third parties -- an honest gap that neither
  confirms nor refutes white space on this specific leg.

## 4. Certification and export constraints -- central new finding

CGN Dasheng (中广核达胜加速器技术有限公司) operates within the corporate family of China General Nuclear Power Group
(中国广核集团, "CGN") -- multiple convergent secondary sources (company registry aggregators, CGNNT's own site
architecture) describe it as a subsidiary of CGNNT (000881.SZ), whose confirmed "actual controller" (实际控制人) is
CGN Group, though the precise ownership-percentage chain was **not independently re-verified via direct document fetch
this session** (baike.baidu.com, qixin.com, and cgnnt.com.cn each returned fetch errors -- 403/405/404 -- on repeated
attempts) and should be treated as a search-derived lead pending confirmation. What **is** independently confirmed via
OpenSanctions (fetched, 2026-07-13): **China General Nuclear Power Corporation is an export-controlled, sanctioned
entity** (BIS Entity List, added August 2019, for attempted illegal acquisition of US nuclear technology for military
diversion) (**P3R2-G-02-S02**), while "CGN Dasheng" itself returns no direct match in OpenSanctions' aggregated
datasets (**P3R2-G-02-S01**) -- i.e., not itself separately named, but sitting inside a sanctioned corporate family.
Given the BIS "50% Affiliates Rule" (effective Sept 2025, strict-liability, extends Entity List license requirements to
entities owned 50%+ by listed parties -- see the parallel finding for NAURA/Piotech in this cohort's F-01 evidence),
**a US-founder company contracting with CGN Dasheng would need dedicated export-counsel review of the exact ownership
chain before any sale of beam-exit-window/target hardware** -- this is a materially higher-stakes compliance question
than the idea's current text acknowledges, and should be added as a named risk alongside the existing JV/IP-leakage
concern.
