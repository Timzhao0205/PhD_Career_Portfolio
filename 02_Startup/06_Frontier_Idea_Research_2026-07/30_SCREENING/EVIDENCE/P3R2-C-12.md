# P3R2-C-12 — Demand/Competitor Evidence

Concept: kW-class 20-80K turbo-Brayton cryocoolers — gas-bearing modular cryogenic engines filling
the gap between lab cryocoolers and bespoke helium plants. **Binding pre-promotion condition**: the
elegance judge downgraded this idea from primary_market US+CN to **US-only, with CN as license-only
(no CN beachhead entity)** — `primary_market: "US"`, `china_beachhead: false`,
`elegance_verdict: FIX_APPLIED`. Per instructions, this evidence file validates the **US case only**
and assesses the **licensing-route realism** for any future CN sales; China is not evaluated as a
demand market. Two more binding conditions from `fix_applied_notes`: (1) a **full competitor map**
of the 100W-2kW @ 20K band (Air Liquide/Linde turbo-Brayton, Stirling vendors, Chinese cryocooler
makers) is required before promotion — delivered below; (2) staged capex — no full-skid integration
spend before a named buyer LOI — a company-building discipline this file cannot verify directly but
notes is unchanged.

## 1. Demand (US only)

**US leg (primary, company/program-level):**
- **Commonwealth Fusion Systems (CFS) SPARC** — direct, concrete match to the product spec: SPARC's
  18 toroidal-field magnets operate at **-253°C (20K)** (TechCrunch, `P3R2-C-12-S01`, fetched,
  2026-01-06) — exactly the seed's target temperature. CFS's cryoplant (magnet-cooling system) was
  already procured in an earlier funding round after ~3 years of engineering/procurement lead time
  (search-confirmed); magnet installation is on track for completion by "end of summer 2026" with
  reactor turn-on targeted for 2027. **This is real, dated, primary-adjacent evidence that kW-class
  20K cooling is an active, near-term US fusion-industry requirement**, though CFS's own SPARC unit
  is already sourced (not an open near-term sales opportunity) — the demand signal is for the
  *class* of buyer (fusion magnet factories scaling past SPARC to commercial ARC-class plants
  post-2030), not a confirmed open RFP from CFS itself.
- Bluefors (Finland-headquartered, with a confirmed US manufacturing presence per reused atlas
  `L13-041`) — quantum-computing dilution-refrigerator maker; relevant as an adjacent-market
  precedent (US-based manufacturing buildout for a related cryogenic-plant-scaling problem) rather
  than a direct 20-80K buyer, since dilution refrigerators target mK, not 20-80K.
- DOE fusion milestone-based funding (reused atlas `L03-032/035`) — no new confirmation this session
  beyond the existing atlas citation; not independently re-verified.

**Verdict (US only): demand is real and dated** — CFS's own SPARC magnets already run at exactly
20K, and the fusion-industry buildout (SPARC 2027 -> commercial follow-ons) is a genuine, primary-
adjacent US demand signal. No equivalent HTS-grid-operator-level primary source was found or
re-verified this session for the US leg beyond the fusion anchor; the seed's "Bluefors-era quantum
facilities" claim is directional context, not a demonstrated 20-80K buyer.

## 2. Competitors — full 100W-2kW @ 20K band map (binding P4 requirement)

- **Air Liquide (Turbo-Brayton line)** — confirmed via direct fetch (`P3R2-C-12-S05`, biogasworld.com)
  as a **commercially available product line today**, covering **25K-200K**, up to 70 bar, with
  documented 10kW-class HTS-cable cooling reference applications (search-confirmed separately, CERN
  conference material, not independently fetched to full text) and maintenance-free operation
  claims exceeding 300,000 hours (all-active-elements-on-one-shaft design, directly analogous to the
  seed's gas-bearing turboalternator approach). **This is the single most important competitive
  finding this session: Air Liquide already sells a mature, commercial turbo-Brayton product
  overlapping most of the seed's 20-80K target band** — the "mid-band gap" framing in the seed is
  materially weakened for the upper part of the band (25-80K); the seed's differentiation must rest
  on packaging (catalog pricing, 6-month lead time, rack-integrable form factor), not on raw
  temperature/power-band access.
- **Linde** — named by the seed as a large-scale turbo-Brayton incumbent; not independently
  re-verified this session (no new fetch), retained from the seed's own framing.
- **Sumitomo (SHI) Cryogenics** — confirmed via direct fetch (`P3R2-C-12-S04`, shicryogenics.com) as
  a Japan-parented (Sumitomo Heavy Industries), globally-operating GM-cycle cryocooler maker with a
  single-stage GM line spanning **20K-77K**, newest model (CH-160D3) rated "100W @ 20K and 725W @
  77K" — this is GM-cycle (reciprocating, oil-free but not gas-bearing turbo-Brayton), lower power
  than the seed's 200W-2kW target but directly adjacent and already at the bottom of the seed's
  power range.
- **Cryomech** — AL630 GM cryocooler delivers 100W @ 20K, described as "the most powerful GM
  cryocooler in Cryomech's portfolio" (search-confirmed); like Sumitomo, this is GM-cycle, sub-seed-
  power-class, not turbo-Brayton.
- **Absolut System** (France) — Cryo-Cool 1000 product, 1kW @ 20K turbo-Brayton (search-confirmed,
  not independently fetched) — a third turbo-Brayton competitor at exactly the seed's lower power
  band, previously not named in the seed record at all.
- **Chinese makers (context only — CN is not a target market per the binding downgrade, but
  competitive-technology context matters for a US/global company assessing moat durability):**
  CAS Institute of Physics (中科院理化技术研究所) has independently developed gas-bearing helium
  turbo-expander cryoplants across a broad range — including **2kW @ 20K helium refrigeration units
  already deployed domestically and internationally, notably to South Korea's KSTAR fusion facility**
  (`P3R2-C-12-S03`, cas.cn, fetched) — and spun off a commercialization vehicle, Beijing Zhongke
  Fuhai Cryogenic Technology Co. (北京中科富海低温科技有限公司, founded 2016 with ~20+ partner
  enterprises). **This is a materially new finding**: China already has a domestically-developed,
  internationally-exported (to a fusion facility, no less), gas-bearing-turboexpander cryoplant
  capability in a directly overlapping power/temperature class — meaning the underlying technology
  is not a Western-exclusive frontier, even though the seed does not compete for CN customers.

**Competitor-map conclusion (satisfies the binding P4 gate): the 100W-2kW @ 20K band is not empty.**
Air Liquide and Absolut System already sell commercial turbo-Brayton units in this exact band;
Sumitomo and Cryomech sell adjacent GM-cycle units at the bottom of the power range; CAS-IET/Zhongke
Fuhai has a proven, exported gas-bearing turbo-expander capability in China. The seed's premise of an
uncontested "gap" is not supported at the technology-class level — the defensible claim narrows to
productization (catalog SKU, 6-month lead time, N+1 redundancy, remote monitoring) rather than
technical whitespace.

## 4. Certification and export constraints (the binding downgrade rationale — checked directly)

- **ECCN 3A904** ("cryogenic cooling systems and specified components"), added to the Commerce
  Control List effective 2024-09-06, controls systems rated to provide cooling
  power >=600 microwatts at or below **0.1 Kelvin** for periods >48 hours (search-confirmed across
  multiple law-firm advisories; one advisory, Arnold & Porter, fetched directly (`P3R2-C-12-S06`),
  confirms the ECCN number and category label but not the numeric threshold in the fetched
  text itself — the 0.1K/600µW threshold is corroborated across Covington & Burling, Mayer Brown,
  Morgan Lewis, and Gibson Dunn summaries found via search, not independently fetched in full for
  the precise number). **This threshold (0.1K) is nearly three orders of magnitude colder than the
  seed's 20-80K target band.** On the plain text of the control as reported, ECCN 3A904 as currently
  defined **does not appear to directly capture the seed's 20-80K turbo-Brayton product** — it is
  aimed at dilution-refrigerator-class systems supporting quantum-computer qubit cooling, not
  industrial/fusion/HTS-grid cooling at 20-80K.
- **However, the broader China-cryogenics export-scrutiny environment is real and escalating**: BIS
  added Scikro Instrument (Shanghai/Hong Kong) and Physike Technology (Beijing) to the Entity List in
  March 2025 specifically for supplying "dilution refrigerators...to Chinese parties on the Entity
  List and defense entities" (`P3R2-C-12-S02`, chinaquantum.info, fetched) — confirming the seed's
  flagged claim that 2025 Entity-List activity targeted Chinese cryo suppliers is **directionally
  correct but product-specific to dilution refrigerators (quantum), not 20-80K industrial systems.**
- **Net assessment on the CN licensing-route question (per the binding downgrade)**: a future
  license application to export a 20-80K industrial/fusion/HTS-grid cryocooler to a Chinese civilian
  buyer would likely fall under general EAR provisions (Commerce Control List review, possible
  China military end-user/end-use review under Section 744.21 given fusion/HTS dual-use adjacency)
  rather than the specific ECCN 3A904 quantum-cryogenics control — meaning a CN license is plausible
  in principle for a clearly civilian buyer, but not free of scrutiny, and the "unconfirmed, flagged"
  language in the seed's own record undersold the fact that a *different*, quantum-specific control
  (3A904) is confirmed real, even though it likely doesn't reach this product. **The judge's
  US-only/license-only-CN downgrade is the correct conservative posture given this ambiguity**; this
  session's research neither fully clears nor fully confirms a CN block, and a formal EAR
  classification request (CCATS) to BIS is the only way to resolve it with certainty.

## 5. US/CN primary-market verdicts

- **US: demand real (CFS/SPARC's own 20K magnet-cooling requirement is a concrete, dated anchor),
  buyer-reachable in principle (fusion sector is small, well-mapped, and the seed's own plan
  correctly gates spend on a named buyer LOI before full-skid investment), window open through the
  SPARC-to-commercial-fusion timeline (2027 first plasma -> 2030s commercial scaling).**
- **CN: not evaluated as a market per the binding downgrade.** Licensing-route assessment: plausible
  but unresolved; the specific quantum-cryogenics ECCN (3A904) likely does not reach this product's
  20-80K band, but China military end-user scrutiny and general diligence remain live
  considerations. No CN buyer, tender, or beachhead claim is made or should be made for this idea.

## 6. 2030 timing

1. **Primary/official-adjacent, US:** CFS SPARC magnet-installation completion targeted "end of
   summer 2026," reactor turn-on/first plasma targeted 2027 (TechCrunch, `P3R2-C-12-S01`, fetched) —
   a dated, near-term US fusion-industry milestone that anchors the 2027-2034 scaling window this
   seed depends on.
2. **Regulator/export-control, dated:** ECCN 3A904 cryogenic-cooling control effective 2024-09-06
   (Arnold & Porter, fetched, corroborated across multiple law-firm trackers) plus March 2025 and
   September 2025 Entity List additions targeting China's quantum-cryogenics supply chain
   (`P3R2-C-12-S02`, fetched) — this is a *constraint*-side timing anchor (confirms the export-
   control environment is actively tightening on cryogenics broadly, through at least late 2025),
   supporting the seed's own conservative US-only structuring rather than a demand-side trigger.

**Verdict: the US window is open and dated (SPARC 2027, commercial fusion scaling into the 2030s);
this idea's viability now rests more on productization/moat (given Air Liquide, Absolut System,
Sumitomo, and Cryomech all already compete in adjacent bands) than on demand existence, which is
real. CN remains correctly excluded as a market per the binding downgrade; the licensing question is
narrowed but not closed by this session's research.**
