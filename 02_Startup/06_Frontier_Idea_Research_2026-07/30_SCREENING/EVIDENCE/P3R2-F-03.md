# P3R2-F-03 — Demand/Competitor Evidence

Concept: High-speed permanent-magnet turbo-generator + power-converter "electrical cartridge"
(0.3-20 MW) for waste-heat sCO2 and geothermal/ORC power blocks. Primary market: **US+CN**.
`elegance_verdict: FIX_APPLIED` with binding conditions: (1) a P4 incumbent map centered on
Calnetix-class merchant PM+converter specialists and turbine-OEM in-house lines (Turboden/Hanwha)

## 1. Demand

**US leg:**
- **Primary, direct-customer (company press releases, both sides of the transaction):**
  `P3R2-F-03-S01` (Fervo Energy, 2026-04-07, fetched) and `P3R2-F-03-S02`/corroborating Turboden
  release confirm a three-year framework agreement for Turboden to "supply Organic Rankine cycle
  (ORC) units for up to 35 GeoBlocks totaling 1,750 MW" — an expansion from a prior 150MW/500MW Cape
  Station order. Cape Station Phase I is "in the advanced commissioning stage... with startup
  expected later this year" (2026).
- **Independent second source:** `P3R2-F-03-S03` (Utility Dive, 2026-04-09, fetched) independently
  reports the same 1.75GW deal and adds industry-momentum context: "investment in next-generation
  geothermal has accelerated rapidly — increasing more than fivefold since 2022" and "bookings for
  geothermal products increased more than threefold in 2025." The deal explicitly aims to "expand
  the domestic supply chain for Turboden" and "shorten lead times" — consistent with the idea's
  "turbine supply is the bottleneck as EGS scales" painful-job framing, sourced from the buyer's own
  stated rationale rather than assumed.
- **Verdict, US demand: real, dated, dual-sourced (two independent outlets covering the same primary
  transaction, plus the buyer's own stated supply-constraint rationale).** This is turbine-level
  demand (Fervo buys complete ORC units from Turboden), not yet electrical-cartridge-level demand —
  F-03's product (generator+converter+bearing-drive sub-assembly sold to a machine builder) has no
  named buyer; Turboden remains vertically integrated as far as these sources show.

**CN leg:**
- **Primary, official/state-media:** `P3R2-F-03-S04` (Xinhua/english.news.cn, 2025-12-21, fetched)
  confirms Chaotan One — "a demonstration project for supercritical CO2 power generation technology
  utilizing sinter waste heat," developed by CNNC's Nuclear Power Institute of China, at Shougang
  Shuicheng Iron and Steel in Guizhou's Liupanshui City — "commenced commercial operation" 2025-12-20,
  raising power-generation efficiency "over 85 percent" and net output "over 50 percent" versus
  conventional steam waste-heat recovery, generating "over 70 million kilowatt-hours... annually."
- **Independent second source:** `P3R2-F-03-S05` (Global Times, fetched) independently corroborates
  the same project (location, operator, 15MW/unit capacity, Dec 2025-2026-05-31 second-unit grid
  connection reported elsewhere) and frames it within China's "dual carbon goals."
- **Verdict, CN demand: real and dual-sourced for the underlying sCO2/steel-sintering retrofit wave**
  this idea's CN leg depends on. However, this is a **single named project** (Chaotan One/Shougang
  Shuicheng); the idea's own text already flags the Xinjiang first-set retrofit TAM (`L04-051`) as
  single-source and requiring pre-scale triangulation — this session's fetches corroborate that
  Chaotan One is real and operating, but do **not** independently triangulate a broader sintering-
  retrofit *market size* beyond this one plant. **The pre-scale triangulation gate remains open.**

## 2. Competitors

**P4-required incumbent map:**
- **Calnetix Technologies / Access Energy** (Cerritos, CA, US) — `P3R2-F-03-S06` (fetched): the
  Carefree Integrated Power Module (IPM) combines Calnetix's Magnaforce (PM generator), Powerflux
  (magnetic bearings) + Insight controller, and Vericycle 300-AE bidirectional power electronics into
  one hermetic, seal-less unit delivering "up to 167 hp (125 kW)" from 82°C+ heat sources — this is
  **exactly** the "electrical cartridge" product F-03 proposes, already shipping commercially, sold
  directly to industrial/marine/oil-and-gas customers, not just to machine-builder OEMs.
- **Turboden** (Italy, Mitsubishi Heavy Industries subsidiary) — the counterparty in the Fervo deal;
  vertically integrated (builds its own turbine+generator+electrical package), per `S01`/`S02`/`S03`.
- **Ormat Technologies** (Reno, NV, US) — `P3R2-F-03-S07` (fetched): describes itself as "the only
  company capable of offering this range of services, thanks to our vertically integrated structure,"
  offering both individual equipment and turnkey solutions — another vertically integrated geothermal
  ORC incumbent, though the page did not disclose whether components are sold standalone to third
  parties.
- **Hanwha Power Systems** (South Korea, named KR side-market buyer in the idea record) —
  `P3R2-F-03-S08` (fetched): markets Turbo Expander Generator (TEG) systems up to 30,000 kW across
  gas-pressure-letdown, petrochemical, and 10-35MW-class gas-turbine waste-heat recovery — confirming
  Hanwha as a real, active competitor/integrator in the same heat-to-power space, not just a
  hypothetical KR buyer as the idea record frames it.
- **Assessment against the merchant white-space claim:** Calnetix already sells a merchant electrical
  cartridge (generator+bearings+converter as one hermetic unit) directly into waste-heat/ORC/marine
  markets. This is the single most direct incumbent found and **materially narrows** F-03's claimed
  white space — the wedge cannot be "a standardized electrical cartridge sold to integrators" in the
  abstract, since Calnetix/Access Energy already does close to that; F-03's differentiation would need
  to be scale (0.3-20MW vs. Calnetix's ~125kW-class IPM), duty class (sCO2/high-temperature vs.
  low-temp ORC), or an open multi-vendor interface standard Calnetix does not publish.
- **CN:** no third-party CN merchant electrical-cartridge vendor was identified this session; the
  idea's own citations (CAS-IET/SMDERI ecosystem, `L04-102/106`, not re-verified this session) remain
  the best available CN competitor context.

## 3. Price signals and arithmetic

- No public per-unit price for a 0.3-20MW electrical cartridge was found (bespoke/OEM-embedded
  component, not separately listed).
- **Assumption anchors (labeled, wide uncertainty):**
  - Calnetix's ~125kW IPM units are priced as OEM sub-assemblies, not publicly listed; **assumption:**
    scaling roughly with power rating for a first-order estimate would put a 5MW-class cartridge
    (40x the Calnetix reference unit's output) in a **very wide $2-15M band** — this is a directional,
    unsourced scaling assumption only, not a verified quote.
  - Fervo-Turboden: 1,750MW / 35 GeoBlocks = 50MW average GeoBlock size; if the electrical-cartridge
    content is assumed at **10-20% of total turbine-package cost (assumption, no source)**, and a
    50MW ORC turbine package costs on the order of $30-60M installed (assumption, industrial ORC capex
    band, no direct source this session), cartridge content per GeoBlock could be **$3-12M —
    directional only.**
  - Chaotan One: 2x15MW units generating "over 70 million kWh annually" combined and "~30 million yuan"
    (~$4.1M) incremental annual revenue — this is plant-level revenue, not electrical-equipment cost,
    and should not be read as a cartridge price signal.
- **Installed-base scaling (assumption):** if the 2030-2034 window sees Fervo's 35-GeoBlock buildout
  (US) plus a CN sintering-retrofit wave scaling from the single Chaotan One plant to, optimistically,
  a "few dozen" steel-sector retrofits by 2034 (**assumption — no source confirms this scale; the
  idea's own pre-scale gate is unresolved**), a merchant cartridge capturing even 20% of that turbine
  population implies **single-digit-to-low-tens of units per year by 2032-2034** — an illustrative,
  not validated, estimate.

## 5. US/CN primary-market verdicts

- **US: demand is real and dual-sourced** (Fervo-Turboden 1.75GW framework, independently reported by
  Utility Dive). Buyer (Fervo) is named and reachable, but the *product* — a merchant electrical
  cartridge sold to machine builders — has no named buyer; Turboden remains vertically integrated in
  this transaction. Calnetix is a direct, already-shipping competitor that narrows the claimed white
  space. **Verdict: PASS on underlying demand signal; UNCERTAIN on the merchant-cartridge business
  model given Calnetix's existing product and Turboden's vertical integration — the 2029 binding
  machine-builder co-development gate is the correct discipline and remains open.**
- **CN: demand is real for one named, operating plant** (Chaotan One, dual-sourced). TAM triangulation
  beyond that single plant remains unresolved (pre-scale gate not cleared this session). **Verdict:
  UNCERTAIN — single-project demand confirmed; broader retrofit-wave market size not independently
  triangulated.**

## 6. 2030 timing

1. **Primary/official-adjacent, US, near-term-to-2030 trigger:** `P3R2-F-03-S01`/`S03` — Fervo's
   1.75GW/35-GeoBlock framework runs on a three-year delivery framework from 2026, with Cape Station
   Phase I startup expected in 2026 and the broader GeoBlock buildout continuing through the
   framework's multi-year term — placing serial ORC-turbine (and by extension electrical-cartridge)
   demand growth through 2028-2029 and into the 2030-2034 window as the framework scales.
2. **Independent, CN, dated primary/state-media trigger:** `P3R2-F-03-S04` — Chaotan One commenced
   commercial operation 2025-12-20, with a second unit grid-connected 2026-05-30 (per broader CN
   coverage), explicitly framed as a national first-of-its-kind demonstration expected to precede
   wider steel-sector retrofit deployment; the idea's own `L04-051` places the next major milestone
   (Xinjiang first-set completion) at ~2028, which would be the trigger for follow-on CN retrofit
   procurement in the 2030-2034 window — this 2028 date is the idea's own atlas-sourced figure, not
   independently re-verified this session, and is flagged accordingly.

**Verdict: window plausibly opens in the 2030-2034 range on both legs**, but the US leg's opportunity
is narrowed by Calnetix's existing merchant cartridge and Turboden's vertical integration, and the CN
leg's scale beyond one demonstration plant remains unverified. Net: OPENS, conditionally, pending the
2029 US co-development gate and CN retrofit-scale triangulation — consistent with treating this as a
FIX_APPLIED idea whose conditions are not yet fully satisfied.
