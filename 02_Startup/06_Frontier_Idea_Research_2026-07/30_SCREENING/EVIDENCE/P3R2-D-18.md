# P3R2-D-18 — Demand/Competitor Evidence

Concept: Affordable Ka-band metamaterial electronically-scanned radar tiles hitting the Army's
published $300k/unit cost target for test ranges and counter-UAS sensing. Primary market: US
(defense_prime). Primary lane L16, secondary L13/L12.

**Binding pre-promotion condition (fix_applied_notes, 2026-07-13 FIX):** a **2028 second-demand-
anchor gate** -- "another program office, a prime teaming agreement, or a Phase II award" -- because
one SBIR topic cannot carry the thesis; absent it, kill or fold into a prime-supplier position. Also
a P4 requirement to map Echodyne-class metamaterial ESA vendors and prime GaN AESA cost curves.
Evaluated first because it governs the gate result.

## 1. Demand

**Primary (government solicitation, directly verified):** Army SBIR|STTR topic page for A254-049,
"Affordable Ka-Band Metamaterial-Based Electronically Scanned Array Radar for Test and Training"
(`P3R2-D-18-S01`, fetched, armysbir.army.mil): confirmed real and currently **open** -- Solicitation
25.4, opened 2026-04-15, closed 2026-05-13, Phase I funding up to $250,000 for a 1-6 month period,
target production cost **$300,000/unit**, 30-40GHz operation, beam-switching 10-20us, 20km range.
**As of this session (2026-07-13), no Phase I award has yet been announced** for this topic -- the
close date was two months ago and awards were not found in a follow-up search. This means the
demand signal is real but **still at the solicitation stage, not yet a contract**.

**Second demand anchor (2028 FIX gate) -- NOT satisfied as of this session:** a targeted search for
a second program office or prime teaming agreement found only one related-but-distinct precedent:
Army topic A214-029, "Metamaterial Based Antenna" (`P3R2-D-18-S02`, fetched, armysbir.army.mil) --
closed in 2021, a **different** program (Army Aviation SATCOM tri-band antennas for
AH-64E/CH-47F/UH-60M, not radar) using metamaterials for Ku/K/Ka-band communications, not
beam-steering ESA radar. This shows the Army has repeatedly funded metamaterial RF SBIR work across
multiple offices over 5 years, which is *supportive context* for institutional appetite, but it does
**not** satisfy the seed's own 2028 gate (a second Ka-band-radar-specific demand anchor), since it
is a different application, different program office, and already closed/historical. **No named
prime-teaming agreement or Phase II award for A254-049 specifically was found.**

**Verdict: G1 gate condition is honestly unmet today.** Demand exists (a real, open, funded Army
SBIR topic with an explicit $300k cost target) but remains single-threaded exactly as the seed's own
FIX anticipated; the 2028 checkpoint has not yet been reached, so continued study/Phase-I-only
posture (not full venture-scale commitment) is the correct read.

## 2. Competitors

- **Echodyne** (Kirkland, WA) is a far larger and faster-moving incumbent than the seed's "K/Ku-band
  incumbent -- judgment" framing suggests. suasnews.com, 2026-07 (`P3R2-D-18-S03`, fetched): Echodyne
  just opened a new $40M, 86,350 sq ft Washington-state manufacturing facility with capacity for
  **30,000+ radars/year**, driven by counter-UAS/C-UAS, force protection, and BVLOS demand, and is
  integrated into products from Anduril, Axon, Moog, and Northrop Grumman. UASweekly, 2026-04-23
  (`P3R2-D-18-S04`, fetched): Echodyne's EchoShield radar was selected as the primary radar for
  Trust Automation's Small-Unmanned-Aircraft-Detection-System (SUADS) family under a **$490M USAF
  IDIQ contract**. **However, independently confirmed frequency data (via WebSearch on
  radartutorial.eu/everythingrf listings, not independently fetched this session; flagged for
  re-verification) indicates Echodyne's EchoFlight/EchoShield products operate at K-band
  (24.45-24.65GHz), not Ka-band (30-40GHz).** This is consistent with the seed's claim that the
  Ka-band niche is not yet directly occupied by Echodyne's fielded products, but Echodyne's scale,
  capital, manufacturing capacity, and metamaterial IP make it the single most credible fast-follower
  threat if it chooses to extend upward in frequency.
- **Guangqi Technology (光启技术, Kuang-Chi, SZSE:002625)**: chnfund.com, 2025-09-08
  (`P3R2-D-18-S05`, fetched, zh): confirmed a real, large, and growing Chinese metamaterial defense
  hardware business -- signed **RMB 1.278 billion** in metamaterial "batch production contracts"
  with five customers (aerospace structural components) for delivery by 2026-06-30. This
  corroborates the seed's "multi-billion-RMB recurring category" claim at the company-revenue level,
  though the specific products described (aerospace structural/stealth components) are not confirmed
  to be Ka-band beam-steering radar tiles specifically -- Guangqi is a broader metamaterial-defense
  conglomerate, not a like-for-like Ka-band ESA competitor.
- **KRISS-to-KER (Korea)** (`P3R2-D-18-S06`, fetched, eurekalert.org, published 2025-08, research
  dated 2025-07-18 IEEE TMTT): **material correction to the seed** -- this transfer is about
  Frequency-Selective-Surface (FSS) radome design and an electromagnetic-wave stealth-evaluation
  system (radar cross-section reduction / stealth), transferred to Korea Electrotechnology Research
  (KER) for KRW 500 million. It is **not** beam-steering ESA/phased-array radar technology. The
  seed's own framing ("upside only, excluded from base case") is the right level of caution --
  this session's fetch confirms the technology is only tangentially related to D-18's core product,
  reinforcing rather than weakening that exclusion.

## 3. Price signals and arithmetic

- **$300,000/unit** is the Army's own stated production-cost target (`P3R2-D-18-S01`, primary,
  fetched) -- the clearest single price anchor available.
- Conventional GaN/GaAs AESA cost comparison: general industry sourcing found only relative cost
  ratios (GaN MMIC ~50-66% of cost-per-RF-watt vs. GaAs) rather than an absolute $/unit figure for a
  Ka-band test-range AESA; **[assumption, flagged]:** given the SBIR topic itself frames current
  Ka-band AESA options as "unaffordable" against a $300k target, a plausible incumbent price range
  for an equivalent-aperture conventional AESA is on the order of **$1M-$3M+** (order-of-magnitude
  inference from the topic's own affordability framing, not independently sourced this session).
- **Installed-base arithmetic [assumption, wide uncertainty]:** Phase I is a single $250k R&D award,
  not units. If Phase II/III materializes and the Army buys for its own test/training ranges
  (a modest, single-digit-to-low-dozens range/site population plausible for Army-wide test and
  training infrastructure) at the $300k target price, an initial production run might be on the
  order of **$3M-$15M** (10-50 units) -- before any counter-UAS volume upside, which would depend on
  winning against Echodyne-class incumbents in a different (K-band-occupied, Ka-band-open) product
  category.
