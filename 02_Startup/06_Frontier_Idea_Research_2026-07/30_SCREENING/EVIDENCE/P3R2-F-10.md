## 1. Demand

### L16-028 refresh (binding requirement)
- **Original (2022, atlas):** Jiaocheng Ultrasonic (骄成超声, 688392.SH) 2021 data: CATL RMB130M,
  Corun RMB68M, BYD RMB36M against RMB196M segment revenue — CATL+BYD >50% of comparable
  new-line equipment purchases.
- **Refreshed this session (T2, fetched, Nov 2025-dated analyst note via Eastmoney):** Jiaocheng's
  2025 fundamentals: total company revenue **RMB774.04M (+32.41% YoY)**, net profit RMB117.55M
  (+36.89% YoY); the company remains explicitly "bound to" (绑定) CATL and BYD as leading
  customers for electrode/tab-welding equipment; battery-equipment segment forecast RMB200-250M
  for 2025. Critically, Jiaocheng is **diversifying into adjacent inspection/QC technology**: it
  has already deployed "2.5D/3D ultrasonic scanning microscopes" for advanced-packaging QC to
  "domestic leading customers," and supplies composite-current-collector ultrasonic rolling-weld
  equipment to CATL. [F-10-S01]
- **Assessment:** The buyer-concentration finding (CATL/BYD-dominated equipment demand) is
  **confirmed still current in 2025**, not stale. But this is demand evidence for **welding**
  equipment, not **inspection** equipment — the seed's own honest framing that "inline volumetric
  weld QC demand is inferential" stands. The new finding that Jiaocheng itself is moving into
  ultrasonic **scanning/QC** tools is a **material incumbent-response risk**: the entrenched
  equipment supplier is not standing still on adjacent inspection technology.

### CN leg (battery Al-Cu tab welds)
- No named battery OEM RFP, pilot, or design-in LOI for **laser-ultrasonic** inline inspection was
  found this session (consistent with the seed's own admission that this bridge is inferential).
  **G1 — CN leg: UNCERTAIN.** Real, refreshed equipment-buyer base; no confirmed inspection-specific
  buyer signal.

### US leg (pipeline/pressure-vessel welds)
- No named pipeline operator or NDT-service company evaluation of laser-ultrasonic inspection was
  found this session. A relevant **adjacent** program exists: EWI (Edison Welding Institute, a US
  industrial R&D consortium, Columbus OH) has run a "laser-based pipeline weld inspection system"
  for over 15 years, but on direct inspection this is **laser topography/profilometry** (pig-based
  scanning for alignment mismatch, bead height, internal geometry) — **not** laser-generated
  ultrasound for volumetric flaw detection. [F-10-S04] A federal DOT/PHMSA-titled research record,
  "Automated Laser Ultrasonic Testing (ALUT) of Hybrid Arc Welds for Pipeline Construction, #272,"
  was located via search (rosap.ntl.bts.gov) but returned HTTP 403 on two fetch attempts — its
  existence is a discovery lead only and is **not used as evidence** here per claim-discipline
  rules (a title/snippet is not a load-bearing source).
- **G1 — US leg: FAIL, plainly.** No named operator/NDT-service buyer evidence exists, consistent
  with and reinforcing the seed's own binding de-weighting decision. The pipeline standards cycle
  (ISO 13588, ASTM E2700, API 1104) is a **compliance floor**, not a procurement trigger, exactly
  as the seed already concluded.

**G1 verdict — overall: FAIL (US leg, plainly) / UNCERTAIN (CN leg).** Neither leg has a
buyer/procurement-grade demand source; the CN leg has a refreshed, real equipment-buyer base but
no inspection-specific signal, while the US leg has no named-evaluation signal at all.

## 2. Competitors

- **US/global (pipeline, contact PAUT incumbent baseline):** **Evident (formerly Olympus
  Scientific)** — PipeWizard iX: phased-array UT + TOFD + conventional UT, **contact-based**
  (couples directly to pipe surface), validated to ASTM E-1961/API 1104/DNV-OS-F101 — confirms
  the seed's framing that today's dominant technology is contact/couplant-based. [F-10-S03]
- **Laser-ultrasonic incumbent (adjacent, real fast-follow risk):** **Tecnar** (Quebec, Canada) —
  already sells "Weldspector," an in-line laser-ultrasonic weld-quality sensor for resistive
  overlap seam welds and laser butt welds in continuous galvanizing/annealing lines, <10-second
  diagnosis, non-contact, works on moving/hot targets. Tecnar's own applications page lists six
  verticals (hot-metal inspection, microstructure analysis, additive manufacturing, linear-weld
  evaluation, thin coatings, composite/aerospace inspection) and **does not currently list battery,
  busbar, or pipeline applications** — confirming F-10's "genuine no-man's-land" framing for those
  two specific verticals, but Tecnar is the closest existing laser-UT platform and the most likely
  fast-follower if either vertical proves out. [F-10-S02]
- **Laser-based (non-ultrasonic) adjacent competitor:** **EWI** — 15-year laser pipeline-inspection
  track record, but geometric/topographic, not volumetric-flaw detection; a credible pivot
  candidate given its laser-optics and pipeline-industry relationships. [F-10-S04]
- **CN:** Jiaocheng Ultrasonic (incumbent welding-equipment supplier now moving into scanning/QC,
  see above) [F-10-S01]. SIUI (Shantou Institute of Ultrasonic Instruments) is named in the seed
  as a CN NDT maker but was not independently re-verified this session (judgment/carry-forward
  only). Baker Hughes (production NDT, global) not independently re-verified this session.

## 5. Primary-market verdicts

- **US verdict: demand not proven (FAIL, plainly).** No named pipeline operator/NDT-service
  evaluation exists. Buyer reachable: unclear — the standards bodies (ASTM, ISO) are not
  procurement decision-makers. Window: not open on demonstrated evidence; the seed's own
  de-weighting is reaffirmed and should remain binding.
- **CN verdict: demand inferential (UNCERTAIN).** The equipment-buyer base is real and refreshed
  (Jiaocheng/CATL/BYD), but no inspection-specific design-in signal exists, and the incumbent
  equipment supplier is itself moving into adjacent scanning/QC technology — a real
  incumbent-response risk that narrows the window rather than leaving it open and uncontested.
  Buyer reachable: plausible via a battery-equipment-OEM co-development channel, but unconfirmed.
- **Window verdict:** Neither leg has a demonstrated open window as of mid-2026. The 2028 binding
  gates (CN OEM design-in LOI; US named-operator evaluation) and the end-2029 POD/speed kill
  remain the correct control points — this session's research does not clear either gate, and
  surfaces a new incumbent-response risk (Jiaocheng's own QC diversification) that should be
  weighed against the CN leg specifically.

## 6. 2030 timing verdict

- **Timing source 1 (T2, fetched, dated Nov 2025 / current):** Jiaocheng's 2025 growth and
  diversification into battery-adjacent scanning/QC and solid-state-battery equipment shows the
  underlying CN battery-equipment replacement cycle continuing to accelerate through 2025-2026,
  supporting a plausible (but unconfirmed) demand window opening within 2027-2030 **if** a
  design-in bridge is secured. [F-10-S01]

  next-edition date located this session — the seed's assumed 2026-2031 revision window is
  **not independently corroborated** and should be treated as an unconfirmed inference, weakening

- **Verdict:** The CN window's opening is **plausible but not confirmed on a dated trigger** — it
  depends entirely on securing the binding 2028 OEM design-in LOI, which remains unproven. The US
  window has **no confirmed 2028-2035 primary/official trigger** located this session; the
  standards-cycle framing should be treated as soft, exactly as the seed's own fix already
  concluded. Recommend the US pipeline leg remain de-weighted/effectively parked, and the CN leg
  be treated as a 2027-2028 go/no-go checkpoint rather than a scored 2030 demand driver.
