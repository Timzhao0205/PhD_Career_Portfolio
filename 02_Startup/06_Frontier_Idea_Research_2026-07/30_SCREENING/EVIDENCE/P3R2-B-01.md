# P3R2-B-01 — Demand/Competitor Evidence

Concept: Sealed negative-pressure (subatmospheric) water/low-GWP two-phase cold-plate loop for >2kW/chip
Chinese AI racks, leak-safe by physics, PFAS-free by design. Primary market: CN.

## 1. Demand

**Regulatory/procurement trigger (primary, government):**
- NDRC/MIIT/NEA/National Data Administration joint notice 发改环资〔2024〕970号, "Data Center Green and
  Low-Carbon Development Special Action Plan" (issued 2024-07-03, published 2024-07-23,
  `P3R2-B-01-S01`). Confirms: new/retrofitted large and super-large data centers must hit PUE <=1.25
  by end-2025; national-hub-node data centers <=1.2; East-Data-West-Computing hub-node clusters get
  rigid-constraint priority for new large-DC siting; by end-2030 China targets "international
  advanced levels" in average PUE. This is the regulatory demand driver the seed cites (matches
  atlas `L14-035`/`L14-036` framing) and independently confirms the 1.25/1.2 caps with an explicit
  official document number and date.
- SAMR National Standard Information Public Service Platform, GB 40879 revision status page
  (fetched 2026-07-13, `P3R2-B-01-S02`): confirms the standard is under active revision (public
  comment 2025-03-17 to 2025-04-16), that the current text's scope is limited to "air cooling with
  electric air conditioning," and that the revision explicitly proposes removing that limitation to
  cover all cooling methods including liquid cooling, alongside tightening the PUE limit values.
  This is the primary confirmation that a liquid-cooling-inclusive, tighter PUE standard is coming,
  not yet finalized as of 2026-07.

**Buyer/procurement source (primary, direct):**
- Southern Power Grid Computing Technology Digital Engineering (Guangdong) Co., Ltd. (a China
  Southern Power Grid subsidiary), "2026 Data Center Liquid Cooling Technology and Energy Efficiency
  Optimization" public tender, project code CG2100022002264708, announced/awarded April 2026
  (`P3R2-B-01-S03`, hangnian.com bid-tracking mirror of the official notice). Scope: "integrated
  high-efficiency heat-dissipation cold-source equipment" and "data center liquid cooling and energy
  efficiency optimization engineering." This is a real, dated, named-buyer public tender for the
  general product category (liquid-cooling + energy-efficiency retrofit) the seed targets. It is
  **not** two-phase/negative-pressure specific — the tender document (as mirrored) does not name a
  cooling architecture — so it demonstrates buyer spend on the painful job (PUE compliance via
  liquid cooling) but not yet on this specific mechanism.

**Independent second demand source:**
- China Telecom Guangdong Branch, "2026 Guangdong Company Domestically-Made 10,000-card Intelligent
  Computing Pool Phase 2 Construction Project," tender opened 2026-02-11 (budget ceiling ~RMB 216M
  ex-tax), winning-candidate announcement 2026-03-09 naming Alibaba Cloud (~RMB 207M) as first
  candidate and Inspur (~RMB 232M) as second (`P3R2-B-01-S04`, news.qq.com/myzaker reporting a
  procurement-award notice). This is a named, dated, budgeted procurement award confirming the scale
  of domestic AI-compute buildout that drives the thermal painful job, but it is a compute-cluster
  procurement, not a cooling-technology procurement — treat as buildout-scale corroboration, not
  direct two-phase-cooling demand.

**Verdict: Demand for liquid-cooling/PUE-compliance spend is proven (real, dated, named-buyer
tenders exist). Demand for this idea's specific negative-pressure two-phase mechanism is NOT
independently proven by a named buyer tender — no tender or buyer document found names two-phase or
vacuum/negative-pressure cooling. This is consistent with the seed's own framing (non-converged,
emerging architecture) but means the mechanism-specific demand is inferential, resting on the
regulatory PUE trigger plus TRL4 lab data, not a signed customer commitment.**

## 2. Competitors

**China (single-phase incumbents, the mechanism the seed must beat):**
- Envicool (英维克, Shenzhen) — full-chain cold-plate/CDU vendor, one of the vendors building to
  Google's "Deschutes" 2MW CDU spec (atlas `L14-043`); product catalogue (envicool.com, accessed via
  search) confirms current shipping lines are single-phase cabinet/rack CDUs, not negative-pressure
  two-phase.
- Sugon Digital (曙光数创, Shenzhen Stock Exchange-listed subsidiary of Dawning/Zhongke Shuguang,
  ticker 872808.BJ), "megawatt-class phase-change immersion liquid-cooling cabinet C8000 V3.0"
  (`P3R2-B-01-S05`, 36kr.com, fetched): single-cabinet power >900kW, claimed heat-dissipation
  capability >200W/cm2, proprietary domestic coolant for "year-round natural cooling," diamond-copper
  thermal-conduction material. This is a **direct two-phase competitor already shipping in China**
  — via immersion, not cold-plate negative-pressure, but the closest domestic architecture rival.
  Vendor-claimed specs, not independently verified.
- Gaolan Energy-Saving Technology (高澜节能, Guangzhou) — cold-plate liquid-cooling supplier to
  Alibaba Cloud and Huawei Cloud per trade-press reporting (search-only, not independently fetched
  this session — treat as a discovery lead, not confirmed evidence).

**Taiwan:**
- ITRI (Industrial Technology Research Institute) — low-pressure two-phase cold-plate work reaching
  2.4kW/chip, publicized by Taiwan's Ministry of Economic Affairs (MOEA) in Feb 2025 (atlas
  `L14-053`; Taiwan Today headline confirms MOEA public reveal, but full technical detail was not
  retrievable this session — locator limited to headline/date). This is the closest technical rival
  architecture (also negative/low-pressure two-phase cold-plate) and is government-backed research,
  not yet a commercial product.

**Domestic-substitution speed (CN):** Single-phase cold-plate/CDU localization is already advanced
(Envicool, Gaolan, Inspur, Sugon all shipping); two-phase/immersion is the newer wave with at least
one domestic player (Sugon) already at megawatt scale. This narrows, but does not close, the
negative-pressure-specific niche: no Chinese vendor found is shipping a negative-pressure (sub-
atmospheric) cold-plate product as of 2026-07.

## 4. Certification and export constraints

- T/CIEP 0263-2025 "Data Center Liquid Cooling System Technical Specification" (group standard,
  released/implemented 2025-04, confirmed via National Digital Standards Library; atlas `L14-039`)
  is the interface standard the product claims compatibility with — confirmed still current and
  distinct from the GB 40879 mandatory-efficiency standard (`P3R2-B-01-S02`).
  China Ceramics Electronics-parallel: no export-control issue specific to water/dielectric-fluid
  two-phase cooling was identified (this is not a controlled substance/technology channel);
  low-GWP-fluid sourcing and any vacuum-hardware components would need ordinary customs/import
  handling, not export-control licensing, based on available evidence.
- No US export-control exposure was identified for this product (cooling hardware, not a controlled
  dual-use item), so the constraint is entirely on the CN-market-access side: a foreign founder
  cannot sell direct-to-hyperscaler in China and must route through licensing/JV/ODM per the seed's
  own access-route framing — confirmed still the only realistic path in 2026.

## 6. 2030 timing

1. **Primary/official:** NDRC/MIIT/NEA/NDA 发改环资〔2024〕970号 (2024-07-23, `P3R2-B-01-S01`) —
   explicit end-2025 PUE<=1.25/1.2 caps and an explicit "by end of 2030, international advanced
   levels" target for average PUE/energy efficiency/carbon efficiency in Chinese data centers. This
   is a dated, official 2024-issued document with an explicit 2030 horizon.
2. **Primary/official, independent agency:** SAMR GB 40879 revision (public-comment 2025-03/04,
   `P3R2-B-01-S02`) — a second, independent official standards-body process (distinct from NDRC)
   confirming the mandatory standard itself is being rewritten to include liquid cooling and tighter
   PUE limits, expected to land before the seed's 2028-2029 field-demonstration milestones.

**Verdict: the regulatory window (PUE compliance driving liquid-cooling capex) is open now and
persists through 2030 per the NDRC plan's own 2030 horizon language. The window for a negative-
pressure two-phase entrant specifically is narrower than the seed assumes: at least one Chinese
two-phase competitor (Sugon, immersion-based) already ships at megawatt scale in 2026, so "non-
convergence" is true across architectures but not equivalent to "no competitor."**
