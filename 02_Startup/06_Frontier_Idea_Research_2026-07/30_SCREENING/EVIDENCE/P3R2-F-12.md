# P3R2-F-12 — Evidence Report (P4 Demand/Competitor Analysis)

**Idea:** Classification-certified DC power and protection stacks for battery-electric/
battery-swap inland/coastal vessels — CCS Rules-native string protection, insulation monitoring,
containerized-pack power interface. CN-primary; the wave's strongest CN-primary seed
(judge-designated).
**Analyst:** demand-competitor-analyst, Sonnet 5 / high effort.

## 1. Demand

- **Primary regulatory trigger (standard, T1, atlas + refreshed this session):** China
  Classification Society's "Rules for Battery-Powered Ships" 2025 edition, effective **31 Dec
  2025** (L10-045, atlas) — adds containerized mobile power-source inspection, shore-based
  monitoring, and online data-exchange requirements. This session additionally located and fetched
  CCS's companion **"Pure Battery-Powered Ship Technology and Inspection Interim Rules 2025,"
  published 21 Feb 2025** [F-12-S02], confirming CCS issued *two* related 2025 rule packages —
  strengthening confidence this is a real, active, multi-instrument regulatory push, not a single
  isolated rule.
- **Independent second demand source (government, T1, fetched, dated May 2026):** Fujian
  provincial government's industry bureau reports China's electric-vessel market at **RMB 36.75
  billion for 2026**, and confirms **multiple battery majors have entered the marine-electrification
  market beyond CATL**: CATL itself (dedicated marine subsidiary since 2017, ~900 vessels equipped
  by end-2025, team doubling to ~500 people in 2026); **CALB (中创新航)** — "Zhiyuan" marine
  battery series **already certified by CCS, DNV, ABS, RINA, and BV**; **BYD** — strategic
  partnership with Norway's Corvus Energy for high-rate LFP marine batteries; and a
  Sunwoda/EVE-Energy-branded "SAIL" marine battery line (268Ah/314Ah cells) powering two 3,000-ton
  methanol-hybrid container ships under construction in Qingyuan, Guangdong. [F-12-S05]
- **G1 verdict: PASS, with a major competitive-intensity caveat.** Demand is real, dated, and
  scaling (CCS rule + RMB36.75bn 2026 market). But the market is **far more crowded and
  multi-classification-certified than the seed's "nascent specialist supply base" framing
  suggested** — see Competitors.

## 2. Competitors

- **CALB (中创新航)** — already holds CCS **and** DNV/ABS/RINA/BV type approval for its own
  marine battery series. [F-12-S05] This is the single most important competitive finding: F-12's
  proposed "DNV variant scoped for export hedge" as a differentiator is **already achieved by an
  existing non-CATL CN battery major**, materially narrowing F-12's claimed white space.
- **Danfoss Editron** — obtained CCS type approval for its synchronous-reluctance-assisted
  permanent-magnet motors and frequency converters on **22 June 2020** (not 2025-26 — an
  established, half-decade-old certification, not a new entrant), has a Nanjing manufacturing
  plant, and an active partnership with **CSSC Shanghai Marine Diesel Engine Research Institute**
  supplying a DC power system (two propulsion motors/inverters, bidirectional DC/DC converters,
  microgrid controllers) for Chinese electric vessels. [F-12-S01] Danfoss's certified scope is
  motors/converters, **not** battery string-protection/IMD specifically — so it is an adjacent
  rather than direct competitor to F-12's exact product, but its long-standing CCS certification
  and CSSC relationship undercut the seed's "no specialist supply base" premise for the marine
  DC-electrification stack broadly.
- **Non-CATL shipyards/integrators (addressing the seed's own P4 requirement to "confirm
  non-CATL integrator/shipyard share"):** 山东新能船业 (Shandong New Energy Shipbuilding) reported
  (per trade-press search synthesis, not independently fetched this session — flagged **T3,
  unconfirmed**) a 37-day build cycle and 180+ new-energy-vessel order book for 2026 spanning
  LNG/electric/methanol; 湖北安赛思新能源 (Hubei AnSaiSi New Energy) claims (same caveat) a
  "world's first 3,000-degree containerized power source" with 15+ vessels equipped and a 40+
  vessel 2026 target. **Both are discovery leads only this session** (search-snippet level,
  not independently fetched/verified) and should be independently confirmed via primary filings
  before being scored as confirmed non-CATL demand evidence.
- **CSSC-affiliated suppliers:** confirmed present in the market via the Danfoss/CSSC Shanghai
  Marine Diesel Engine Research Institute partnership above — see Section 4 for the export-control
  implications of any JV/licensing route through a CSSC-affiliated partner.

## 4. Certification and export constraints

- **CCS type approval** is the binding certification gate (2025-edition rules, L10-045 +
  F-12-S02); **DNV/ABS** are the named export-hedge targets — but CALB has already achieved
  CCS+DNV+ABS+RINA+BV simultaneously, meaning multi-society certification is achievable and
  already achieved by a competitor, not a unique moat.
- **Export-control screening — the most significant finding across the whole group, load-bearing
  for F-12's go-to-market structure:**
  - **China State Shipbuilding Corporation (CSSC)** was added to the **US BIS Entity List on 18
    December 2020** [F-12-S03, fetched] and remains flagged as a Chinese-military-affiliated
    entity in the June 2026 DoD 1260H update (cross-referenced, not independently re-fetched from
    a primary DoD source this session for the 2026 date specifically). Its 703rd and 704th
    Research Institutes independently appear on the Consolidated Screening List.
  - **BIS's "50 Percent Rule," effective 29 September 2025, extends Entity List export
    restrictions automatically to any foreign affiliate that is 50%+ owned, directly or
    indirectly, by an Entity-Listed party** — meaning any JV or licensed-manufacturing partner
    that is majority-owned by CSSC **inherits CSSC's export restrictions automatically**, under
    strict liability (violations can occur even without knowledge of the ownership chain).
    [F-12-S04, fetched]
  - **Practical implication for F-12's own stated go-to-market plan** ("JV or licensed
    manufacturing with a CN marine-electric supplier"): before selecting a JV/licensing partner,
    the company **must screen the candidate's ownership chain for CSSC (or other Entity-Listed
    party) affiliation above the 50% threshold**. If the partner is CSSC-affiliated, any
    US-origin technology, software, or component used in the joint product would require a BIS
    export license, and the JV entity itself could become subject to Entity List-equivalent
    restrictions. This is a **hard, previously unflagged compliance constraint** that should be
    added as an explicit pre-JV screening gate, not an afterthought.
  - **CATL** remains on the US DoD's Section 1260H list (confirmed current as of the June 2026
    update; effective 30 June 2026 DoD is barred from procuring from 1260H-listed entities) — a
    procurement/reputational overhang if F-12's market strategy runs through CATL-affiliated
    vessel/shipyard programs specifically, distinct from (and less severe than) CSSC's BIS Entity
    List status.
  - No BIS/VEU hits found this session for CALB, Danfoss Editron, or Sunwoda/EVE Energy.

## 5. Primary-market verdicts

- **US verdict: not applicable.** The seed explicitly scopes F-12 as CN-primary
  (`us_beachhead: false`); the DNV/ABS export-hedge angle is a future secondary-market
  consideration, not a US beachhead, and is addressed under Competitors/Certification above.
- **CN verdict: demand real and buyer reachable, but far more competitively crowded than
  assumed, with a serious new export-control constraint on partner selection.** The CCS
  regulatory trigger is real and dated; the market is large and growing (RMB36.75bn 2026); named
  non-CATL buyers/integrators exist (CALB, Sunwoda/EVE, BYD-Corvus, and discovery-lead-level
  shipyards). But CALB has already achieved the multi-class-society certification F-12 frames as
  its wedge, and Danfoss is an established, CCS-certified, CSSC-partnered incumbent in the
  adjacent motor/converter niche. The JV/licensing route central to F-12's foreign-founder access
  plan must now explicitly screen for CSSC (BIS Entity List) ownership exposure before partner
  selection — a new, binding pre-condition this session surfaces for the first time.
- **Window verdict:** Opens now (CCS rules already effective 31 Dec 2025) and plausibly persists
  through the early 2030s given the ~2-year CCS revision cadence and the compliance-driven
  refit/newbuild wave — but the window is **more contested** than the seed's "best CN-primary
  seed" framing implied; F-12's differentiated protection/IMD/interface subsystem niche (as
  opposed to full battery packs or motors/converters) may still be genuinely open, but this was
  not independently confirmed against a named non-CATL, non-CALB buyer this session.

## 6. 2030 timing verdict

- **Timing source 1 (primary/official, T1, atlas + refreshed this session):** CCS Rules for
  Battery-Powered Ships, 2025 edition, effective **31 December 2025** [L10-045], plus the
  companion Interim Rules published **21 February 2025** [F-12-S02] — both official CCS
  publications, both dated, both within the 2028-2035 relevance window as the compliance floor
  for the newbuild/refit wave.
- **Timing source 2 (independent, T1, government, fetched, dated May 2026):** Fujian provincial
  government's confirmation of an accelerating, multi-player electric-vessel market (RMB36.75bn
  2026, CATL's marine team doubling in 2026, CALB/BYD/Sunwoda-EVE all actively building/certifying
  in 2026) [F-12-S05] — shows the underlying demand is scaling in real time, not merely
  regulatory-paper demand.
- **Verdict: window OPENS now and persists at least through the early 2030s** given the dated CCS
  trigger and the confirmed, accelerating market scale. This is the strongest-timed idea in the
  group. The caveat is competitive, not temporal: the window is open, but it is **not empty** —
  CALB and Danfoss are already inside it with certified products, and any JV path must clear the
  new CSSC/BIS ownership-screening gate identified this session before partner selection.
