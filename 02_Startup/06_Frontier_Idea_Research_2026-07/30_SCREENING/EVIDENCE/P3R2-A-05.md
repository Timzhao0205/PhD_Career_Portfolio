**Verdict:** Demand is strong and multiply-sourced across independent US big-science programs (EIC, PIP-II, QIS centers) plus the international ITER tender stream. This comfortably passes the primary + independent-second-source bar.

## 2. Competitors
- **Sole global merchant incumbent (confirmed, no second found):** SAES Getters S.p.A. (Lainate, Italy) -- IntegraTorr NEG coating, CERN-origin technology, >20-year track record (atlas L07-037). A dedicated search this session for a second merchant NEG-coating vendor (Japan's ULVAC, Chinese vacuum houses, or any other named competitor) did not surface one; ULVAC and other general vacuum-equipment houses were referenced only in the context of not having NEG-specific process capability. This reinforces the seed's "effectively single-sourced globally" claim, though it should be read as "no second merchant vendor found in this session's search," not as an exhaustive negative-space proof.
- **In-house/non-merchant alternative:** CERN-affiliated labs coat their own beam pipes in-house (per SAES's own origin story -- IntegraTorr was "originally developed at CERN"); this is a substitute-in-kind for large European facilities but not a US-based option.
- **Adjacent vacuum-component vendors (not NEG-capable):** Kurt J. Lesker Company (Pittsburgh, PA) -- high-voltage feedthroughs, published list pricing $169.50-$5,365 across ~25 models (atlas L07-036) -- useful price-signal anchor for adjacent UHV hardware, not a NEG competitor.
**Verdict:** The single-source (SAES, Italy) claim holds up under fresh search; a US-domestic merchant entrant would be a genuine second source, which is the core of the idea's value proposition.
