# P3R2-E-11 -- CPO thermal-stabilization module (package lid + micro-loop + micro-TEC + TIM stack)

Analyst: demand-competitor-analyst (Sonnet 5, high effort). Primary market: US (`us_beachhead: true`, `china_beachhead: false`). Secondary: TW. Non-advocacy analysis; citations to atlas IDs and new `P3R2-E-11-S0x` IDs.

## 1. Demand

**Primary-ish (company disclosure + real deployment):** Coherent Corp 10-K FY2025 and 2025 Analyst/Investor Day (atlas L12-035/036) -- Coherent's own $4B datacenter-interconnect TAM targeting. **Caveat, flagged explicitly:** Coherent is simultaneously a potential buyer and a potential competitor (per the idea's own text), so its investor-day TAM figures should be read as a vendor's market framing, not independent customer-side demand evidence.

Stronger buyer-side confirmation: **P3R2-E-11-S01** (The Next Platform, Oct 2025) -- Broadcom's Bailly CPO switches already deployed at named hyperscalers: "Meta Platforms" plus "a couple of hyperscalers" (implicitly including Tencent); Davisson-generation chips "shipping to early access customers" as of Oct 2025. This confirms CPO adoption is real and underway now, not merely projected.

**Independent second:** **P3R2-E-11-S03** (Lightmatter/OCP reference-architecture initiative, March 2026) -- a multi-vendor consortium (Celestica, Corning, Dell, Flex, Foxconn, Hyve, Keysight, Qualcomm, Quanta) committing to open CPO interoperability standards. This is genuine, independent, dated industry commitment -- but it is standards/interoperability-focused, **not a specific thermal-module purchase signal**.

**Honest gap:** no hyperscaler procurement filing or RFP specifically for a merchant package-level thermal-stabilization module was found. Demand exists for CPO overall; demand for THIS specific merchant subsystem is unproven, and (see Section 2) increasingly being answered in-house by incumbents before a merchant vendor could compete for it.

## 2. Competitors

- **US:** Broadcom (buyer + in-house competitor: per S01, its own thermal fix is relocating laser modules to the faceplate, not package-level stabilization), Coherent (buyer + competitor, per idea's own framing), Marvell (**P3R2-E-11-S04**: partnered with **Mikros Technologies** -- a new named US precision-cooling competitor -- for "targeted cooling of hot spots"; Marvell's own architectural response is also laser-faceplate relocation, following its ~$3.25B Celestial AI/Photonic Fabric acquisition, per prior public reporting), Ayar Labs (different architecture: remote laser placement reduces on-package thermal load rather than solving in-package stabilization -- a structurally different, competing approach).
- **EU:** **No EU-based CPO-package-thermal competitor was identified.** This is a genuine, flagged gap rather than an oversight -- European photonics players (e.g., component-level silicon-photonics foundries) were not found operating at the package-thermal layer.
- **TW/CN-adjacent supply chain:** **P3R2-E-11-S05** (TechNews Taiwan, March 2026) -- **material finding:** ASE (日月光) is ramping its own in-house **FOEB (fan-out embedded bridge)** thermal-warpage solution into **volume production in H2 2026** for 1.6T FOCoS-CPO packages; TSMC's COUPE platform integrates optical engine + CoWoS ASIC on-substrate at 6.4Tbps in 2026. Both are OSAT/foundry in-house thermal-adjacent solutions, already shipping.
- **JP/KR:** no named CPO-package-thermal player identified (flagged gap).

- **Bottom-up, ASSUMPTION-labeled:** if a merchant thermal module captures 10-30% of total CPO-switch-ASIC packages by 2030 (assumption -- reflecting a scenario where OSATs/switch vendors capture the rest in-house, which the ASE/TSMC/Marvell evidence above suggests is the more likely outcome) applied to 200,000 units (S05) => 20,000-60,000 units; at an assumed per-unit thermal-module price of $300-$1,200 (assumption, informed by advanced-packaging precision cold-plate/TEC BOM comparators, no direct market quote found) => **~$6M-$72M** addressable 2030 revenue for the merchant niche. Given the in-house capture evidence below, the realistic outcome likely sits at or below the low end of this range.

## 5. US / CN verdicts

- **US: UNCERTAIN, leaning toward a compressed/closing window.** CPO adoption itself is real and dated (Broadcom shipping now, S01; OCP consortium forming, S03). But the specific white space the idea targets -- "no thermal architecture has converged... no vendor owns this" -- is being filled **faster than the idea's own 2028-29 co-development-agreement timeline assumed**: ASE is already ramping an in-house thermal-warpage solution into volume in **H2 2026**, TSMC's COUPE is substrate-integrating in 2026, and both Broadcom and Marvell are pursuing an architectural workaround (move the laser away) rather than buying a package-level stabilization module. This is a genuinely new finding that should shorten the previously assumed runway; the idea's own FIX gate (2028: CPO volume-ramp evidence PLUS one switch-vendor/OSAT co-development agreement) is the right test, but evidence gathered here suggests the gate may need to be pulled forward or the window may already be narrowing.
- **CN: N/A.** No CN beachhead claimed; Taiwan (not mainland China) is the manufacturing-supply-chain secondary market and is already covered above.

## 6. 2030 timing (two independent sources, window verdict)

1. **Official/company-disclosed:** Coherent Corp 10-K FY2025 / 2025 Analyst Day (atlas L12-035/036) -- company-disclosed $4B datacenter-interconnect TAM target tied to the CPO generation ramp.
2. **Independent market-research:** **P3R2-E-11-S02** (Yole Group, Paris) and **P3R2-E-11-S05** (Morgan Stanley, via TechNews) -- both independently project a 2028-2031 CPO volume inflection (200,000 unit shipments by 2030; $8.1B-$14.4B market by 2030-2031).

**Window verdict:** Opens 2027-2028 and reaches real volume by 2028-2030 per multiple independent forecasts -- the CPO transition itself is not in doubt. However, the narrower window that matters for E-11 specifically (a *merchant* package-thermal module, as opposed to CPO generally) is **already being contested and substantially pre-empted by incumbents (ASE, TSMC, Broadcom, Marvell) years before 2030**, not just at the 2033 kill-date the idea's record specifies. This is the single most important updated finding for this idea.
