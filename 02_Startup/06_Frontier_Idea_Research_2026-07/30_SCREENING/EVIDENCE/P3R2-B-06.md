# P3R2-B-06 — Demand/Competitor Evidence

Concept: Electrostatic-chuck (ESC) power/control electronics subsystem — HV clamp supply, JR
leakage compensation, fast declamp, multi-zone temperature-uniformity electronics — for Chinese
semiconductor tool OEMs. Primary market: CN.

**Binding pre-promotion condition (fix_applied_notes, 2026-07-13 FIX):** "P4 must produce a named
ESC-localization program/tender or a ceramics-maker co-development LOI before this seed can be
promoted." This condition is evaluated first because it governs the gate result.

## 1. Demand

**Searched for:** a named ESC-localization tender, program, or ceramics-maker co-development LOI
specifically for the clamp-power/control-electronics module (the seed's actual product — not the
ceramic puck itself).

**Found (does not satisfy the gate):**
- Jiangfeng Electronics (江丰电子, Shenzhen/Shanghai-listed, ticker 300666) signed a "Cooperation
  Framework Agreement on Electrostatic Chuck Project" with KSTE INC. (South Korea) on 2025-01-17
  (`P3R2-B-06-S01`, sina.com.cn company-disclosure reporting, fetched). KSTE will provide "full-
  process technical support" and "design and delivery of production lines" so Jiangfeng can achieve
  "independent mass production of electrostatic chucks in mainland China." Jiangfeng's July-2025
  private-placement plan allocates ~RMB 998M of a ~RMB 1.948B raise to a 5,100-unit/year ESC
  production line. **This is a full-stack ceramic-ESC technology transfer from an existing Korean
  ESC producer to Jiangfeng — it is not a tender or LOI for the electronics/clamp-power module the
  seed proposes to sell, and it suggests Jiangfeng's electronics needs may be filled by the KSTE
  partnership rather than by an independent merchant electronics supplier.**
- China Ceramics Electronics (中瓷电子) stated at a 2022 investor briefing that it is "laying out
  precision ceramic components for semiconductors to support domestication of semiconductor
  equipment parts" and aims to "achieve domestication of high-difficulty precision components like
  heating discs and electrostatic chucks" (`P3R2-B-06-S02`, cnpowder.com.cn, fetched). **This is a
  strategic-intent statement with no investment figure, capacity target, or dated commitment — it is
  not a tender or LOI.**
- No named ESC-localization government tender (中国政府采购网-style) specific to clamp-power
  electronics was found in this session's searches.

**Verdict: the binding P4 gate is NOT satisfied.** No named ESC-localization program/tender or
ceramics-maker co-development LOI for the electronics module was found. What exists instead
(Jiangfeng-KSTE full-stack tech transfer; China Ceramics Electronics' in-house strategic intent)
weakens rather than strengthens the "merchant electronics partner" thesis, since both signals point
toward domestic ceramics players acquiring or building full-stack (ceramic + electronics) capability
rather than seeking an external electronics-only supplier.

## 2. Competitors

- **Advanced Energy (US)** — Trek 646 Series "Software-Driven Electrostatic Chuck Supply," 0 to
  +/-3kV output, supports both Coulombic and Johnsen-Rahbek clamping, wafer-present/clamped
  detection, programmable/uploadable clamp profiles (`P3R2-B-06-S03`, advancedenergy.com, fetched).
  **Confirmed: AE already sells a merchant, standalone HV clamp-power-supply module through normal
  distribution channels** — this is materially the same product category the seed proposes, already
  commercially available globally (not China-qualified, and subject to the seed's own flagged
  AE/MKS-bundling risk, which the fetched product page does not resolve either way — the Trek 646 is
  sold standalone here, but AE's chamber-integrated bundled offerings were not checked this session).
- Chinese ceramic-ESC entrants (potential future buyers, not yet competitors on electronics):
  Huazhuo Precision (华卓精科), Haituo Innovation (海拓创新), Jiangfeng Electronics (300666),
  Fujian Fortune Precision (富创精密), Kema Technology (珂玛科技) — all named in Chinese trade
  reporting as ceramic-ESC developers at early/small-batch stages (search-only synthesis, not
  independently fetched per-company; treat as discovery leads).
- CETC-system entrants: CETC 12th Institute (sample completed 2019, small-batch production) and
  CETC North Intelligent Electric (2022 announced RMB 1.5B investment, 10,000-unit/year target per
  search-only reporting, not independently fetched — discovery lead, not confirmed this session).

## 3. Price signals and arithmetic

- China ESC (whole-assembly, ceramic+electronics) market size cited at RMB 5.838B in 2025 (Chinese
  trade-press aggregation, search-only, not independently fetched — treat as directional, not
  verified) growing from a 2021 base of RMB 2.112B per a separate report. Global ESC market cited at
  US$13.71B (2024) to US$19.42B (2030) per the Jiangfeng-KSTE reporting (`P3R2-B-06-S01`) — note
  these figures come from company-disclosure press coverage, not an independently audited market
  report, and no India-flagged consultancy was used.
- **Electronics-only share is an unstated assumption.** No source found decomposes the ESC bill of
  materials into ceramic-puck vs. clamp-electronics value. Assumption for bottom-up arithmetic: if
  electronics is 15-30% of ESC system value (wide, labeled assumption, no source), and the China ESC
  market is ~RMB 5.8B (2025, unverified trade figure), the addressable electronics slice is roughly
  RMB 0.9-1.7B (~US$125-235M) — a directional order-of-magnitude figure only, not a validated TAM.

## 4. Certification and export constraints

- The FIX-scoped product (mature-node >=28nm and panel/display tools only) is deliberately outside
  advanced-node export-control scope; this session found no new evidence changing that judgment.
  No US export-control document specific to ESC clamp-power electronics was located (these are
  general industrial HV power-supply/control electronics, not enumerated on control lists found this
  session) — but this was not exhaustively re-verified against the latest Entity List/EAR updates.
- China-side: no localization mandate or certification specific to ESC electronics was found beyond
  the general ~35-50% domestic-equipment-adoption push reported for etch/deposition tools in 2026
  (TrendForce reporting on NAURA/AMEC domestic adoption exceeding targets) — this is tool-level
  localization, not electronics-subsystem-level, and does not directly evidence demand for a merchant
  ESC electronics module.

## 5. US/CN primary-market verdicts

- **US: not applicable** (`us_beachhead: false`; CN-primary seed, KR secondary).
- **CN: demand is inferential, and the P4 gate explicitly required to promote this seed was not met.
  No named ESC-localization tender or ceramics-maker co-development LOI for the electronics module
  was found. Buyer reachability is uncertain: the clearest near-term buyer path (Jiangfeng) already
  has a full-stack technology partner (KSTE, South Korea) as of January 2025, which may close rather
  than open the merchant-electronics niche this seed targets.**

## 6. 2030 timing

1. **Primary/official:** State Council, "十五五规划纲要" (PRC 15th Five-Year Plan Outline,
   2026-2030), authorized release 2026-03-13 via Xinhua (`P3R2-B-06-S04`/general, shared with other
   G03 ideas), Chapter 8 explicitly names integrated circuits among sectors requiring "decisive
   breakthroughs" in core technology by 2030 via "extraordinary measures" and "full-chain
   advancement" — no ESC-specific quantified target found in the fetched excerpt.
2. **Industry/trade (secondary):** TrendForce reporting (2026-01-12) that China's domestic chip-
   equipment adoption rate beat its 2025 target at 35%, led by NAURA and AMEC — a dated, named
   industry-tracking data point on the broader localization wave this seed's demand trigger depends
   on, though it is tool-level, not ESC-electronics-level.

**Verdict: the general China semiconductor-localization window is open and reinforced by an
explicit, dated 2030 official target. However, this does not substitute for the seed's own required
gate (a named ESC-localization tender or ceramics-maker electronics LOI), which remains unmet.**
