Prepared 2026-07-13. Binding pre-promotion condition: none formal (elegance_verdict PROMOTE); adjudication flags "MVDC adoption pace and $1.5M pre-company experiment" and "per-project incumbents may bundle protection into converter stations" as the honest risks to red-team.

## 1. Demand
**Primary (official_project_award, T1):** Southern Spirit Transmission (atlas L08-041) -- Pattern Energy's ~320-mile, +/-525kV, ~3,000MW VSC-HVDC ERCOT-to-Eastern-Interconnection line, ~$2.6bn privately funded, ~$360m DOE Transmission Facilitation Program support, construction start 2029, in-service 2032. This is HVDC transmission, not an MVDC campus breaker order -- it evidences the underlying DC-infrastructure buildout wave the idea rides, not a direct MVDC-breaker purchase order.
**Independent second source:** GE Vernova Q1 2026 earnings call (atlas L08-033, company_filing T1): "significant growth in substations, HVDC, switchgear, and transformers," datacenter Electrification orders $2.4bn in Q1 2026 alone (exceeding all of FY2025), HVDC backlog ~$10bn.
**Government program trigger:** ARPA-E DC-GRIDS (atlas L02-034, T2 trade press on a government program): $35M across 12 projects developing MT-HVDC converter tech, explicitly targeting datacenter-driven demand.
**Verdict on demand:** Real and dated at the transmission/datacenter-electrification level, but no primary buyer (hyperscaler, utility, or OEM) has yet issued a tender, spec, or LOI naming "MVDC solid-state circuit breaker" as a purchased line item. Demand for the *category the product would occupy* (MVDC protection) is inferred from the DC-buildout trigger, not directly evidenced by a breaker-specific procurement document. This is a real but one-step-removed demand chain -- standard for a pre-commercial protection subsystem, not a G1 failure, but not yet a named buyer order either.

In fact at least two specialized, venture-funded MV solid-state-power-electronics companies (DG Matrix, and the Singapore-flagged Amperesand) are already shipping commercial pilot volumes to hyperscale datacenter customers in 2026 -- two to four years ahead of this idea's first-experiment/launch timeline. The "no product category exists" framing is weakening faster than the seed assumed, even though none of these competitors is confirmed to have solved DC fault interruption at the 1-35kV/1-4kA breaker level specifically (they are solid-state transformers/converters, a related but distinct function from a standalone breaker).

## 3. Price signals / installed base / bottom-up arithmetic
No public price list exists for MV hybrid SSCBs (Hitachi Energy's page has no quantitative specs or pricing -- atlas L08-052 note). Bottom-up niche sizing, every input labeled:
- US datacenter-campus MVDC pilot count 2030-2033: **assumption**, 5-15 pilot campuses (range), based on the 800VDC/MVDC adoption-phasing evidence (SemiAnalysis: Phase 3 facility-level 800VDC viable late 2028/2029, ~39GW incremental 800VDC capacity by 2030 [P3R2-A-02-S04]) scaled down from rack-level DC to campus-level MVDC distribution, which is a slower-adopting, higher-voltage tier than rack-level 800VDC -- **uncertainty: high, +/-2x**.
- Breakers per campus: **assumption**, 20-80 units per MVDC campus distribution ring (based on typical MV switchgear-bay counts for a multi-hundred-MW campus) -- **uncertainty: high**.
- Price per unit: **assumption**, $150k-$400k per 1-35kV/1-4kA hybrid module (interpolated from adjacent HVDC hybrid-breaker cost literature and MV switchgear-bay pricing; no direct vendor quote found) -- **uncertainty: very high, no source**.
- Implied 2030-2033 addressable order value: 5-15 campuses x 20-80 units x $150k-$400k = **$15M-$480M cumulative**, an extremely wide, assumption-dominated range that should not be treated as a market-sizing number until a real vendor quote or pilot RFP is found.
- HVDC-transmission-scale reference point (not this product, but a scale anchor): GE Vernova's ~$10bn HVDC backlog (atlas L08-033) and Southern Spirit's ~$2.6bn single-project cost (atlas L08-041) show the adjacent HVDC capital-equipment market is already large; MVDC campus breakers are a much smaller, emergent sub-segment within it.

## 4. Certification and export constraints
- Established AC MV switchgear certification pathways exist (IEC 62271 series for AC circuit breakers/switchgear; UL 1066 power circuit breakers; UL 489 for lower-voltage breakers) but **no finalized IEC, IEEE, or UL standard specifically for MVDC solid-state circuit breakers was found** in this session's search -- this is a genuine, unresolved certification gap that a new entrant would need to either wait for or drive standards development around (a real timing risk, not covered in the seed).
- IEC 61803 (atlas L08-044) covers HVDC converter-station loss determination, not breaker interruption performance -- adjacent but not directly applicable.
- No China/EAR export-control flag identified for MV breaker hardware itself (power equipment, not on the semiconductor-tool control lists reviewed for A-10); standard ITAR/EAR screening for any SiC power-semiconductor content would still apply generically.

**Verdict:** Window opens circa 2029-2030 and plausibly persists through the 2032-2034 commercial-readiness-kill-date horizon, consistent with the seed.
