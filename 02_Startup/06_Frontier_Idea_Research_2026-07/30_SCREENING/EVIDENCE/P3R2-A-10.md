## Gate evaluation (decisive for the CN leg)
The idea names three CN customers/partners for its mature-node-only chapter: **AMEC, NAURA, and Piotech**. Fresh research this session finds all three now carry adverse US export-control status as of the Dec 2024 BIS rule (89 FR 96830, effective Dec 2 2024) [P3R2-A-10-S01, corroborated by P3R2-A-10-S02]:
- **NAURA Technology Group and named subsidiaries** (Beijing Naura Microelectronics Equipment, Beijing Naura Semiconductor Equipment, and regional units) -- **added to the BIS Entity List**.
- **Piotech and named subsidiaries** (Piotech (Beijing), Piotech (Shanghai), Piotech Chuangyi (Shenyang), Piotech Jianke (Haining)) -- **added to the BIS Entity List**.
- **AMEC (Advanced Micro-Fabrication Equipment)** -- **removed from the Validated End-User (VEU) program** (along with CSMC and HHGrace) in the same rule.
Entity-Listed status generally requires a specific BIS license (presumption of denial for many advanced-semiconductor-relevant items) before any EAR-subject US-origin item, software, or technology can be exported/reexported to the listed entity or its named subsidiaries -- **regardless of whether the served fab node is "mature" (>=28nm) or "leading-edge."** Node-based licensing carve-outs exist for some ECCN categories but do not categorically exempt Entity-Listed customers. Separately, a September 2025 rule continued tightening VEU treatment for China-based fabs generally (TSMC/Samsung/SK hynix VEU revocations) [P3R2-A-10-S03], showing the trend between Dec 2024 and mid-2026 is toward *more* restriction, not less.
**Conclusion: the 2027 export-control-counsel gate is not a routine formality -- it is very likely to find that the specific named CN customers this idea's own text identifies are Entity-Listed or VEU-lapsed, making the "mature-node, partitioned-entity" CN leg legally fraught even before considering the two-entity corporate-structure and IP-firewall complexity.** This does not necessarily kill the US-only leg of the idea, but it substantially undermines the case for treating primary_market as "US+CN" and china_beachhead as "true" at this time. I flag this as the single most important red-team finding for A-10.

## 4. Certification and export constraints
- **This is the central constraint for the idea.** EAR ECCN controls on semiconductor manufacturing equipment (etch, deposition, metrology/inspection, among others) were expanded in the Dec 2024 BIS rule specifically to cover items "needed to produce advanced-node integrated circuits" [P3R2-A-10-S01/S02]. Whether a standalone IEDF sensor/waveform-bias-control retrofit module (as opposed to a full etch tool) is itself EAR-controlled at a level requiring a license depends on its specific ECCN classification, which was not determined this session and requires the counsel review the idea itself schedules for 2027 -- but the customer-side finding (NAURA/Piotech Entity-Listed, AMEC VEU-lapsed) is independently disqualifying for the named customers regardless of the component's own classification.
- SEMI-standards engagement (mentioned in the idea's precompany plan) is not itself an export or certification barrier.
- No FTO (freedom-to-operate) analysis was performed this session; the idea's own text flags this as a prerequisite gate for the separately merged B-21 event-detection layer, which this analysis did not evaluate further.
