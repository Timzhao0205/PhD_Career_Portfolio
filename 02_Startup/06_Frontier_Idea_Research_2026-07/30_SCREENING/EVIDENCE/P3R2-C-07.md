- **Copper electrowinning (policy-independent beachhead):** No new primary tender/procurement source for a

  technical/vendor-context source already in the ledger.
- **Verdict: US hydrogen-linked demand is real but now time-compressed** by the 45V deadline; the
  electrowinning beachhead is not independently re-verified this session and should be treated as

- **US/Europe:** **ABB** -- UNIREC H2, a thyristor-based rectifier delivering up to 25kA at 1500VDC for

  the ABB product page timed out repeatedly and could not be independently re-verified this session --
  **treat the ABB product-spec figures as discovery-only, not ledger-accepted, pending a successful direct
  fetch**). **Ingeteam (Spain)** -- new source **P3R2-C-07-S02** (fetched, T2): INGECON H2 FSK E12000, an
  **IGBT-based active-front-end rectifier already shipping** (first units delivered September 2023 to
  German/Spanish green-hydrogen plants), 12,000A nominal (up to 15,860A max), harmonic distortion <3% with
  no added filters/STATCOM needed. **This is a direct, currently-shipping incumbent occupying the exact
  AFE/IGBT-vs-thyristor technology space C-07 proposes** -- but for new-build plants, not retrofit of an
  installed thyristor fleet, which narrows (rather than eliminates) C-07's differentiation to (a) retrofit
  of the existing installed base and (b) stack-neutral, measurement-and-verification-backed performance
  contracts, neither of which Ingeteam's product-page marketing addresses.
- **China:** **Sungrow (阳光电源)** -- confirmed via search this session (not independently fetched) as
  both a top electrolyzer-tender winner (2023: Peric 135MW, Sungrow 131MW, LONGi 95MW of China's
  competitive awards) and a vertically-integrated supplier of its own IGBT-based hydrogen power supplies
  (制氢电源), ALK/PEM electrolyzers, and BOP systems -- i.e., **Sungrow is simultaneously a potential
  customer and the single most credible verticalized competitor**, exactly as the idea's own
  `competition_outlook_2030` states. This is the CN kill condition the adjudication note names, and this
  session's evidence reinforces rather than dispels that risk.
- **Price/technology context (discovery-only, not independently fetched to primary-source standard this

## 3. Price signals and bottom-up arithmetic

- Thyristor vs. IGBT price ratio: **ASSUMPTION** ~5x per the unverified ne21.com figure above (RMB
  200-300k vs. 1.2-1.5M per 5MW set) -- if directionally correct, this is the single biggest headwind to
  C-07's retrofit economics: the AFE upgrade must recover its ~4x incremental capital cost purely from the
  claimed 5-14% specific-energy savings (atlas L11-016/017/019/020/021) within a bankable payback period.
- Bottom-up payback sanity check (all inputs labeled): for a 50MW electrolyzer plant, **ASSUMPTION**
  electricity cost $0.04/kWh, ~90% capacity factor, 8,760 h/yr => ~394 GWh/yr consumed;
  **ASSUMPTION** 8% specific-energy saving (midpoint of the 5-14% literature range) => ~31.5 GWh/yr saved
  => **~$1.26M/yr opex savings** at $0.04/kWh. Retrofit capex for a 50MW AFE skid: **ASSUMPTION**
  $150-300/kW incremental cost over thyristor baseline => $7.5-15M incremental capex, implying a
  **6-12 year simple payback** at these assumptions -- a marginal, not obviously compelling, case unless
  electricity prices, capacity factor, or the specific-energy saving are higher than these conservative
  midpoints, or unless a performance-contract (opex-only) structure removes the capex hurdle from the
  buyer's balance sheet (which is exactly what C-07's product proposes). This arithmetic is illustrative
  only; every input is an explicit assumption, not a fetched figure, and should be replaced with real plant
  data before scoring.

## 4. Certification and export constraints

- 45V FEOC exemption (S01): unlike most other OBBBA-surviving energy credits, Section 45V is explicitly
  **exempted** from new "foreign entity of concern" restrictions -- relevant if C-07's CN-entity technology
  or components could otherwise have triggered US-side compliance risk for hydrogen-linked US customers;
  this session found no such restriction applies to 45V-eligible projects.
- Export separability: industrial rectifiers (thyristor or IGBT-based) are broadly uncontrolled dual-use
  industrial equipment; no export-control-specific finding this session contradicts the idea's own "good"
  separability claim, though (as with C-02/C-03) this was not verified against primary BIS text this
  session and should be re-checked before final capital commitment.

## 5. Primary-market verdicts

- **US:** Demand real but now time-compressed on the hydrogen side (45V 2027 construction-start deadline);
  the electrowinning beachhead is the more policy-durable leg but lacks an independently re-verified named

  customer commitment before final scoring.
- **CN:** Demand real (LONGi/Peric/Sungrow tender volumes, Kuqa retrofit case) but the single largest CN
  competitor (Sungrow) is simultaneously the most likely large buyer -- **verdict: demand real, but the
  Sungrow verticalization kill condition is not hypothetical; it is the named CN market leader's current,
  observed business model.**

## 6. 2030 timing (two independent sources)

1. **Primary/official:** New source S01 (Baker Botts on OBBBA, T2, analyzing Public Law 119-21 / H.R.1,
   2025) -- Section 45V construction-start deadline of **December 31, 2027** is a primary legislative
   trigger squarely inside the 2028-2035 window, though it is a *narrowing*, not *opening*, trigger for
   new-build US hydrogen capacity.
2. **Market/industry:** Atlas L11-035 (IEA Global Hydrogen Review 2025, government-adjacent international
   body) -- documents persistent global electrolyzer overcapacity (China ~2x demand), an independent
   industry-analysis signal that stack commoditization (and thus a shift of competitive focus toward power
   quality/electricity cost, which is C-07's stated thesis) is already underway rather than a 2030-era
   event.

**Verdict:** the US hydrogen-linked portion of the window is **narrowing before 2030** (45V deadline effect)
while the CN portion and the policy-independent electrowinning beachhead remain **open through 2030 and
beyond**, consistent with the idea's own beachhead-sequencing logic (electrowinning first, H2Hubs second).
The overall 2030 opportunity is real but now more CN- and electrowinning-weighted than the original US
hydrogen-first framing implied, and the Sungrow verticalization risk on the CN side is corroborated, not
resolved, by this session's evidence.
