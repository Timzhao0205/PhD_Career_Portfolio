# P3R2-C-01 — Demand/Competitor Evidence

Concept: 800VDC rack power-path protection — integrated solid-state breaker + precharge/inrush
control + insulation monitoring + hot-swap sequencing unit — for AI racks and power sidecars.
Primary market: US+CN (dual, independent legs). Absorbed A-01/B-03/B-04/E-01 content per merge notes.

## 1. Demand

**US leg (primary, company/technical roadmap):**
- NVIDIA developer blog, "NVIDIA 800 VDC Architecture Will Power the Next Generation of AI
  Factories," published 2025-05-20 (atlas `L02-043`, re-fetched this session for confirmation):
  full-scale 800VDC production is timed to the Kyber rack-scale platform / Rubin Ultra generation in
  2027; the article explicitly states overcurrent protection is handled today by "fuse-and-disconnect
  combinations or emerging solid-state devices or safety breakers" and flags "fault detection and
  serviceability in VDC systems" as "a key area for innovation" — i.e., NVIDIA's own primary
  technical material names the exact gap this seed fills.
- Open Compute Project "Diablo 400" (Mount Diablo) rack-and-power specification, backed by Google,
  Meta, and Microsoft (atlas `L02-044`, DCD reporting) — defines disaggregated 800VDC power sidecars
  for 100kW-1MW IT racks with front-accessible, hot-swappable, galvanically-isolated PSU modules,
  i.e., the exact hot-swap/precharge/protection interface this seed's product targets, published by
  the hyperscaler-run open-standard body that is the closest thing to a buyer specification in this
  market.
- NVIDIA GPU roadmap reaffirmed at GTC 2025/2026: Rubin (H2 2026) -> Rubin Ultra/Kyber NVL576
  (H2 2027) -> Feynman (2028), per TrendForce, 2025-03-19 (`P3R2-C-01-S06`, fetched) — confirms an
  annual-cadence company roadmap with an explicit 2028 generation, extending the 800VDC buildout
  window well past the initial 2027 rollout.

**CN leg (primary/near-primary, buyer-side industry forum):**
- GCC-Open AI Infra community (国内产学研 "Open AI Infra") 2026 OAII Summit, "800V Power Supply"
  themed forum, held 2026-04-23, co-chaired by Baidu Intelligent Cloud and ByteDance, with Huawei,
  Vertiv, Schneider Electric, Wanguo Data, Inspur, Silan Microelectronics and others participating
  (`P3R2-C-01-S03`, comentropy.org, fetched). The forum's own stated findings: "800V direct current
  protection logic differs completely from 240V HVDC" due to short-circuit current direction
  variability and energy constraints; explicit gaps named in unified fault-output standards,
  grounding/arcing/insulation-monitoring specifications, "lack of 800V safety certification standards
  within China," non-unified connector standards, and "scarcity of high-voltage eFuse components."
  **This is buyer-side (Baidu, ByteDance are hyperscaler buyers) confirmation, dated and named, that
  the exact painful job (unresolved 800V DC protection/grounding/certification) is live in China as
  of April 2026** — the closest available primary-adjacent demand evidence for the CN leg.
- China's historically low HVDC penetration (~12% vs. 78% UPS, atlas `L02-048`) means the 800V
  conversion wave is still ahead in China, not behind — independently reinforced by SuperX/Zhongheng
  Electric's 2025-11-02 800VDC product launch (`P3R2-B-22-S01`, reused here) confirming active,
  named, dated commercial buildout.

**Verdict: demand is proven on both legs with primary/near-primary sources — a hyperscaler-run open
standard (OCP Diablo 400) plus NVIDIA's own technical material on the US side, and a named,
buyer-co-chaired industry forum explicitly stating the protection/certification gap on the CN side.
This is the strongest-evidenced idea in this batch.**

## 2. Competitors

  H2 2026 (trade-press reporting, search-only this session).
- Eaton — acquired Resilient Power Systems (Aug 2025) for solid-state-transformer expertise
  (`P3R2-C-01-S04`, SemiAnalysis, fetched); also Boyd Thermal (liquid cooling, March 2026,
  ~$1.7B 2026 revenue contribution per trade press).
- Schneider Electric — named participant in the China 800V forum and a general incumbent shelf
  vendor; not independently re-verified for a specific 800V protection product this session.
- ABB — SACE Infinitus solid-state circuit breaker (1000V/2500A), datacenter adaptation announced
  with NVIDIA involvement, Oct 2025 (`P3R2-C-01-S04`).
- LS Electric (Korea) — first UL-certified DC molded-case circuit breaker at 1500V, listed for
  datacenter applications (`P3R2-C-01-S04`).
- Funded SSCB/SST startups: DG Matrix (ABB-backed, Infineon SiC supply agreement, targeting UL
  pre-certification by Q2 2026, included in NVIDIA's MGX reference architecture), Amperesand
  (targeting 30MW of commercial deployments in 2026), Heron Power (building a 40GW-capacity US
  manufacturing facility for 4.2MW "Heron Link" units), Novos Power (claims a direct MV-to-800VDC

  startups in the twelve months ending March 2026 per the same source.

**China incumbents:**

  vertically-integrated in-house solution, not independently re-verified as a shipping product this
  session.
- Delta China, Zhongheng Electric, SuperX/Zhongheng JV (`P3R2-B-22-S01`) — active 800VDC product
  vendors (reused).
- Innoscience — the only full-stack GaN IDM in NVIDIA's 800VDC/Kyber ecosystem, providing an
  all-GaN 800V-to-1V conversion chain (`P3R2-C-01-S01`, Innoscience press release, 2025-10-13,
  fetched); confirmed independently via Sina Finance reporting that Innoscience is "the only Chinese
  chip company selected" for NVIDIA's supplier list (atlas `L02-050`). This is a device-level (GaN
  semiconductor), not protection-shelf-level, competitor/enabler — relevant to the seed's supply
  chain, not a direct shelf competitor.

**Domestic-substitution speed (CN):** the China 800V protection-electronics space is still
pre-standardized as of April 2026 per the OAII forum's own admission (no unified fault-output
standard, no China 800V safety-certification standard yet) — i.e., domestic substitution has not yet
converged on a dominant design, consistent with the seed's "component vacuum" thesis, though Huawei's
in-house posture is a real risk the seed itself flags.

## 4. Certification and export constraints

- IEC 62477-1 (safety requirements for power electronic converter systems, up to 1000V AC/1500V DC)
  is confirmed as the relevant general safety standard (webstore.ansi.org/IEC records, search-only
  this session, consistent with atlas `L02-112`); it applies broadly (industrial/commercial/
  residential PECS) and would govern this shelf's baseline certification path.
- China: no dedicated 800V DC safety-certification standard exists yet as of April 2026 per the OAII
  forum's own statement (`P3R2-C-01-S03`) — this is simultaneously a demand signal (gap to fill) and
  a market-entry risk (no clear certification path to qualify against yet).
- Export control: the underlying protection electronics (breakers, precharge controllers, IMD) are
  conventional industrial power electronics and were not found on any control list this session
  (consistent with the seed's own EAR99 framing). The supply-chain-relevant export-control item is
  **China's gallium/germanium export-control regime** (MOFCOM/General Administration of Customs
  Announcement No. 23 of 2023, effective 2023-08-01, `P3R2-C-01-S02`, fetched from the official
  MOFCOM export-control portal): gallium nitride (GaN) is named as "one of the most representative
  third-generation semiconductor materials" subject to this licensing (not prohibition) regime. This
  matters for the China-variant supply chain if it uses Innoscience-class domestic GaN devices, and
  is a genuine cross-border consideration for a dual-entity (US+CN) structure, though it is a
  licensing gate, not an outright ban, per the notice's own text.

## 5. US/CN primary-market verdicts

- **US: demand real and buyer-reachable.** NVIDIA/OCP have published primary technical material
  naming this exact protection gap; Vertiv/Eaton/ABB/funded startups are already moving, meaning the
  window is real but competitive and time-sensitive (the seed's own "spec absorption by NVIDIA/OCP"
  kill trigger is well-grounded — DG Matrix's inclusion in NVIDIA's own MGX reference architecture
  shows the platform owner can bring competitors inside the reference design). Window: open now,
  contestable through 2027-2028 as the Kyber generation ships, plausibly persisting through Feynman
  (2028) given the confirmed annual-cadence roadmap.
- **CN: demand real, buyer-side confirmed via a named, dated, co-chaired (Baidu/ByteDance) industry
  forum explicitly stating the protection/certification gap.** Buyer reachability is constrained to
  the standard CN-market playbook (licensed CN entity, OEM/ODM design-in) — no direct-sale evidence
  either way was sought this session beyond the forum's existence. Window: open and arguably earlier-
  stage than the US leg, since China lacks even a certification standard yet (per the forum's own
  admission), which is both an opportunity (component vacuum) and a risk (no path to certify against
  until one is written).

## 6. 2030 timing

1. **Primary/official (company technical roadmap, reaffirmed at GTC 2025 and GTC 2026):** NVIDIA
   Rubin -> Rubin Ultra (H2 2027, Kyber NVL576) -> Feynman (2028) annual-cadence roadmap
   (`P3R2-C-01-S06`, TrendForce, 2025-03-19, fetched) — an explicit, dated, company-primary 2028
   generation confirms the 800VDC buildout is a multi-generation program, not a one-off, extending
   the opportunity window past the initial 2027 rollout.
2. **Primary/official (government, CN leg):** State Council "十五五规划纲要" (15th Five-Year Plan
   Outline, 2026-2030), Xinhua-distributed 2026-03-13 (shared source, recorded once as
   `P3R2-B-06-S04`) — explicit 2030-horizon target for "decisive breakthroughs" in integrated
   circuits and related core technology, the umbrella policy driving China's AI-compute and
   power-electronics buildout that this idea's CN leg depends on.

**Verdict: the window opens in 2027 (US, NVIDIA Kyber) and is already active as an unresolved
buyer-side problem in China (April 2026 OAII forum), and persists at least through the Feynman
generation (2028) and the 15th Five-Year Plan horizon (2030) — i.e., the window opens before 2030
and plausibly persists through it, consistent with the seed's own "component vacuum, not late" thesis.
The standing risk (confirmed, not just theorized) is platform-owner spec absorption: NVIDIA's own
MGX reference architecture already includes at least one merchant SST/SSCB competitor (DG Matrix),
showing the absorption risk is concrete, not speculative.**
