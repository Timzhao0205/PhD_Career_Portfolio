# P3R2-F-04 — Demand/Competitor Evidence

Concept: Merchant active-magnetic-bearing (AMB) drive electronics + rotor-health platform, entering
via semiconductor turbomolecular pumps and expanding to sCO2 compressors/blowers/flywheels. Primary
market listed as **US+CN**, but `fix_applied_notes` makes the CN leg explicitly **CONDITIONAL**: "a
named CN pump/tool-OEM design-in channel must be evidenced in P4 or primary_market downgrades to US
(INFICON APAC revenue attribution... is not buyer evidence)." A P4 teardown of SKF S2M/Calnetix/
Waukesha against the semiconductor-grade-duty claim is also required. This file delivers both and
concludes the CN leg fails the idea's own pre-set bar.

## 1. Demand

**US leg:**
- No named paid OEM evaluation or purchase order was found this session (consistent with the idea's
  own `current_trl: 5` and 2028 binding-gate framing — the "OEM will externalize AMB electronics"
  thesis is explicitly the idea's own "stated key uncertainty," not yet resolved).
- **Context demand signal (industry-wide, not company-specific):** semiconductor capital spending and
  fab buildout continue to grow (per the idea's own atlas citations `L07-050/052/053`, not
  re-verified this session), and turbopump/vacuum-equipment replacement cycles are a recurring,
  structural demand driver in fabs — this supports the *addressable market exists* claim but is not
  buyer-level evidence.
- **Verdict, US demand: NOT proven at the buyer level this session — the core externalization thesis
  remains untested, matching the idea's own low confidence rating and 2028 binding paid-evaluation
  gate.** This should be scored as pre-demand / thesis-stage, not confirmed.

**CN leg (conditional per the idea's own fix):**
- **No named CN pump/tool-OEM design-in channel was found.** Searches for a Chinese semiconductor
  tool-OEM (AMEC, NAURA, or others) sourcing AMB drive electronics from a third-party/merchant
  supplier returned no such arrangement. Instead, this session found **multiple Chinese domestic
  in-house/vertically-integrated maglev-turbopump competitors already serving CN fabs**: `P3R2-F-04-S06`
  (Hangzhou Daily, 2025-06-05, fetched) profiles Kuntai (昆泰磁悬浮), which claims to be "the only
  domestic enterprise that has broken the foreign technology monopoly on semiconductor-grade magnetic
  levitation molecular pumps," with its KTP-304 model priced "70%-80% of imported equipment" and
  "orders already scheduled through the latter half of 2025." Additional named domestic players
  (Zhongke Instruments/中科科仪, Feixuan Technology/飞旋科技) were identified via broader search but not
  independently fetched this session.
- This is the opposite of a design-in channel for a merchant (especially non-Chinese) AMB supplier —
  it is direct evidence of fast CN domestic substitution exactly in the AMB/maglev-turbopump niche
  F-04 targets, consistent with the idea's own `competition_outlook_2030` warning that "domestic
  substitution speed is the main CN risk."
- **Per the idea's own binding fix condition, absent a named design-in channel, `primary_market`
  downgrades from US+CN to US-only.** This session's research applies that downgrade.

## 2. Competitors

**P4-required teardown (US/Europe):**
- **SKF Magnetic Mechatronics (S2M)** (Vernon, France; SKF subsidiary since 2007) — per
  `P3R2-F-04-S01` (Wikipedia "Magnetic bearing," fetched): S2M (est. 1976) "was the first to
  commercially market active magnetic bearings," and while Leybold AG (not S2M) is credited with the
  first commercial maglev turbomolecular pumps (1975/1989), S2M is confirmed among the historically
  central AMB suppliers to semiconductor and industrial turbomachinery markets; SKF Magnetic
  Mechatronics has ~250 employees and ~€50M annual turnover (per broader WebSearch context, not
  independently re-verified this session).
- **Calnetix Technologies** — also named in `S01`, confirmed as an industrial-turbomachinery AMB
  developer (see also its overlapping role as F-03's competitor).
- **Waukesha Magnetic Bearings** (Dover Corporation subsidiary) — per `S01`, positioned for oil/gas
  compressors and large industrial turbomachinery; the idea's own precompany plan names Waukesha as
  one of three incumbents its 2027 teardown must survive.
- **Synchrony Magnetic Bearings** (Johnson Controls International subsidiary) — additional named
  incumbent surfaced in `S01`, not in the idea's original competitor list but directly relevant.
- **Assessment against the "semiconductor-grade duty" wedge:** the teardown suggests SKF S2M has the
  deepest historical semiconductor/turbopump lineage of the three named incumbents, while Calnetix and
  Waukesha skew toward general industrial/oilfield/marine turbomachinery (per F-03's Calnetix findings
  and Waukesha's compressor-focused product pages). This partially supports F-04's wedge (a gap
  between generic merchant AMB and semiconductor-specific harsh-duty controllers) but does not clear
  it — S2M's own history shows merchant semiconductor-grade AMB supply is not a greenfield category.
- **Turbopump OEMs (potential US/JP buyers, largely vertically integrated):** `P3R2-F-04-S02` (Ebara
  Technologies, fetched) shows Ebara's own EMT/MK/MK-R turbomolecular pump lines use "5-axis MagLev"
  bearings with "integrated onboard controllers" — the page does not disclose whether the controller
  electronics are built in-house or sourced from a merchant AMB vendor, leaving the idea's central
  "will OEMs externalize" question unresolved from Ebara's public materials. Edwards Vacuum (UK, Atlas
  Copco) also markets "STP Maglev Turbomolecular Pumps" (surfaced via search, not independently
  fetched this session) as another vertically-postured turbopump OEM.
- **CN competitors (confirms domestic-substitution risk, not a design-in channel):** Kuntai/昆泰磁悬浮
  (`S06`), plus Zhongke Instruments/中科科仪 and Feixuan Technology/飞旋科技 (named via broader search,
  reportedly leading domestic magnetic-levitation molecular-pump vendors) are themselves competitors
  to any merchant AMB platform in China, not potential channel partners.

## 3. Certification and export constraints

- **BIS Entity List check (critical finding):** `P3R2-F-04-S03` (TechNode, 2024-12-03, fetched) and
  `P3R2-F-04-S05` (TrendForce, 2026-01-12, fetched) confirm **NAURA Technology Group was added to the
  BIS Entity List in the December 2024 semiconductor-equipment rule** ("140 Chinese semiconductor-
  related companies" added, with NAURA named among firms "now barred from purchasing US-made
  components"). `P3R2-F-04-S04` (OpenSanctions, fetched) corroborates NAURA entities appear in the
  "US Trade Consolidated Screening List (CSL)" and a Taiwan strategic high-tech commodities list,
  though the OpenSanctions UI did not surface a clean BIS-Entity-List-specific tag in this fetch.
  **AMEC was removed from the VEU program at its own request but was not added to the Entity List**
  per the same December 2024 rule (per WebSearch context on the Federal Register rule; the Federal
  Register page itself returned a CAPTCHA wall on direct fetch this session and is NOT counted as
  independently fetched).
- **Implication:** NAURA — one of the two named CN buyer-ecosystem anchors in the idea's own
  `named_buyer_examples` ("Chinese tool-OEM supply chains (AMEC/NAURA ecosystem)") — is Entity-Listed,
  meaning any US-origin merchant AMB electronics sold toward a NAURA-affiliated tool would require a
  BIS license under a restrictive review standard. Combined with §1 above (no design-in channel found;
  fast domestic AMB substitution already underway), this is a second, independent reason the CN leg
  should not be counted as an open, addressable market for a US-based entrant.
- `P3R2-F-04-S05` (TrendForce) also confirms China's overall domestic semiconductor-equipment adoption
  rate reached "35%" in 2025 (up from 25% in 2024, beating the official 30% target), with NAURA's own
  furnaces at ">60%" share on SMIC's 28nm lines — independent, dated (2026-01-12) confirmation that CN
  fab-tool localization is accelerating broadly, reinforcing the domestic-substitution risk finding.

## 4. Price signals and arithmetic

- `P3R2-F-04-S06` gives one concrete CN price signal: Kuntai's semiconductor-grade maglev turbopump is
  priced at "70%-80% of imported equipment" — a direct data point on how aggressively CN domestic
  substitutes are already undercutting Western/merchant pricing in this exact product category
  (relevant context for the CN-downgrade decision, not a US price signal).
- No public per-axis or per-controller price was found for merchant AMB electronics (SKF S2M,
  Calnetix, Waukesha all sell as engineered components, not listed retail items).
- **Assumption (labeled, wide uncertainty):** if a 5-axis semiconductor-turbopump AMB retrofit
  controller costs on the order of $20,000-80,000 per unit (assumption — generic industrial AMB
  controller cost band, no direct source this session), and the addressable US semiconductor-fab
  turbopump fleet undergoing controller refresh in the 2030-2034 window numbers in the low thousands
  of units (assumption, no source), a merchant supplier capturing 5-15% share implies **roughly
  $1.5-10M cumulative revenue potential across the window — a highly uncertain, illustrative estimate
  only**, not a validated TAM.

## 5. US/CN primary-market verdicts

- **US: demand NOT proven at buyer level.** No named paid OEM evaluation exists yet; the core
  thesis (will pump/machine OEMs externalize AMB electronics) is explicitly unresolved per the idea's
  own framing, and this session found no external evidence resolving it either way. Competitive
  teardown (§2) shows the "semiconductor-grade duty" wedge is narrower than a greenfield gap — SKF
  S2M has real semiconductor/turbopump history. **Verdict: UNCERTAIN — thesis-stage, pending the
  idea's own 2028 binding paid-evaluation gate; do not score as proven demand.**
- **CN: FAIL as an independently evidenced beachhead.** No named design-in channel exists; the
  clearest CN-side finding is fast, aggressive domestic AMB/maglev-turbopump substitution (Kuntai,
  and by broader-search context Zhongke/Feixuan) already undercutting import pricing, plus one of the
  idea's two named CN buyer-ecosystem anchors (NAURA) is BIS Entity-Listed. **Per the idea's own
  binding fix condition, this session's finding requires the primary_market designation to downgrade
  from US+CN to US-only, consistent with the F-06 precedent in this same batch.**

## 6. 2030 timing

1. **Primary/official-adjacent, near-term-to-2030 trigger:** `P3R2-F-04-S05` (TrendForce, 2026-01-12)
   — CN domestic chip-equipment adoption at 35% in 2025 versus a 30% official target, with etching/
   thin-film deposition already over 40% — an industry-data-service-sourced, dated trigger showing the
   CN fab-tool localization wave (which drives the domestic-AMB-substitution risk) is running ahead of
   its own official schedule, into the 2026-2030 window.
2. **Independent, context-level trigger:** the idea's own atlas citations (`L07-050/052`, semiconductor
   vacuum/turbopump fleet growth; not re-verified this session) plus this session's confirmation that
   Ebara and other turbopump OEMs continue shipping integrated (not obviously externalized) maglev
   controller products — both point to steady-but-not-accelerating US-side demand growth through 2030,
   without a single dated 2028-2035 official trigger equivalent to the CN localization figures.

**Verdict: on the US leg, the window's opening depends entirely on the unresolved externalization
thesis and is not independently datable to a specific 2028-2035 trigger this session — treat as an
open, unproven timing case. On the CN leg, the window is effectively closing for a merchant (non-
Chinese) entrant now, given accelerating domestic substitution and an Entity-Listed named buyer-
ecosystem anchor — consistent with the CN downgrade above.**
