# P3R2-C-08 — Demand/Competitor Evidence

Concept: Thermal-shock-tolerant printed-circuit heat exchangers (PCHE) — merchant diffusion-bonded
recuperators for sCO2 power, heat pumps, and hydrogen precooling. Primary market: US+CN (dual,
independent legs). Structural pairing (binding): develop and pitch jointly with **P3R2-C-19**
(gas-foil bearing/seal cartridges) as one high-temperature-components company — evaluated as a
paired claim below. Binding pre-promotion condition carried in `adjudication_note`: the CN RMB100bn
TAM figure is flagged single-source (state media) and requires independent triangulation before
being treated as validated.

## 1. Demand

**US leg (primary/near-primary, DOE-program + regulatory):**
- STEP Demo (Supercritical Transformational Electric Power), the SwRI/GTI Energy/GE
  Vernova/DOE 10 MWe sCO2 pilot, is now operational: Phase 1 testing completed (27,000 rpm,
  500°C turbine inlet, synchronized to grid at ~4 MW net), reconfirmed operational as of March 2026
  (reused atlas `L04-039`, re-verified this session via search — status current). This is the
  flagship US demand anchor: PCHE recuperators account for a documented ~80% of sCO2 cycle capex
  (peer-reviewed literature, search-confirmed this session), making the recuperator the
  cost-dominant qualified-vendor bottleneck the seed targets.
- NRC 10 CFR Part 53 (technology-inclusive advanced-reactor licensing framework) is finalized and
  in force: American Nuclear Society "Nuclear Newswire" report (`P3R2-C-08-S01`, fetched) confirms
  NRC approval of "a comprehensive new approach to license advanced reactors...across their life
  cycles," with multiple industry law-firm trackers (Orrick, BDLaw, Morgan Lewis; search-corroborated,
  not independently fetched) dating Federal Register publication 2026-03-30 and an effective date of
  2026-04-29 (30 days later) — i.e., the seed's claimed "2026-04" effective date is confirmed
  directionally correct (effective late April, not early April). This is the primary/official
  regulatory trigger unlocking advanced-reactor BOP procurement (incl. sCO2/heat-exchanger balance
  of plant) that the seed's `demand_trigger_2030_2034` field cites.
- TerraPower Natrium BOP vendor-contract-award announcement (reused atlas `L04-045`) confirms an
  active, named advanced-reactor BOP supply chain already forming.

**CN leg (primary, state-media-reported but with independently-confirmed underlying facts):**
- Chaotan One ("超碳一号"), the CNNC/Nuclear Power Institute of China steel-sintering waste-heat
  sCO2 project at Shougang Shuicheng (Guizhou), is **already commercially operating, not merely a
  future trigger**: Unit 1 entered commercial operation 2025-12-20 (reused atlas `L04-048/051`);
  Unit 2 grid-connected 2026-05-30, bringing the pair to a confirmed 30 MW stable installed capacity
  and ~230 million kWh/year (`P3R2-C-08-S03`, Sina Finance, fetched, dated 2026-05-31). This
  independently updates the seed's 2026 baseline: the CN commercial anchor project is now fully
  online, ahead of a "2030-2034 trigger" framing — the retrofit-fleet wave this seed depends on is
  not speculative, it has a running reference plant.
- **Separately from Chaotan One, a genuine CNNC "Molten-Salt Energy Storage + Supercritical CO2
  Power Generation" demonstration project is confirmed targeting 2028 completion** (Power
  Magazine, `P3R2-C-08-S02`, fetched: "CNNC now reportedly plans to scale up applications through a
  molten-salt energy storage plus sCO2 generation pilot — as a selected Chinese national
  'first-of-a-kind' demonstration project — envisioning completion in 2028"), corroborated by
  Chinese-language aggregated reporting (search-only, not independently fetched) placing this
  project in Xinjiang with construction starting H1 2026. **Correction to the seed record**: the
  longlist's `demand_trigger_2030_2034` field cites atlas source `L04-051` for this "Xinjiang
  molten-salt+sCO2 2028" claim, but `L04-051` is actually the CCTV article about the *Guizhou*
  Chaotan One project (confirmed by title/content match) — a citation mismatch. The underlying claim
  (a distinct 2028 Xinjiang molten-salt+sCO2 project exists) is independently corroborated this
  session, but the atlas citation needs correcting to a new source, not L04-051.
- **CN RMB100bn (千亿元) TAM claim — remains single-source-flagged, as adjudicated.** The figure
  ("如果传统烧结机余热设备超过300套的可改造规模全部进行改造，市场规模大概在1000亿元左右") appears in
  the CCTV article already in the atlas (`L04-051`) and is explicitly framed there as an industry
  forecast ("据业内预测"), not a CCTV-measured figure. This session's search for an independent
  triangulation (China Iron and Steel Association filings, sintering-machine inventory counts) found
  only process-technology guidance documents (chinaisa.org.cn energy-efficiency benchmarking
  circulars) with no independent sintering-machine-count or retrofit-TAM figure. **The RMB100bn
  figure stays flagged single-source pending further P5/P6 triangulation** — treat as a scenario
  ceiling, not a validated TAM.

**Verdict: demand is real and independently evidenced on both legs.** US: a national regulatory
unlock (NRC Part 53, in force) plus an operating $155M multi-partner pilot plant. CN: a fully
operational commercial reference plant (Chaotan One, 30MW, both units online) plus a distinct,
nationally-designated 2028 follow-on demonstration (Xinjiang molten-salt+sCO2). The CN leg's TAM
sizing remains unvalidated (single-source) even though the underlying commercial activity is real.

## 2. Competitors

**US/global PCHE incumbents (P4 gap-fill — flagged "not in atlas" by the idea record):**
- **Heatric** (Boyd/Meggitt-lineage) — confirmed via peer-reviewed literature search as "the most
  widely adopted supercritical CO2 recuperator" vendor; 30+ years of PCHE experience, used in
  Sandia's own sCO2 test loop (search-confirmed, academic secondary sources, not independently
  fetched this session — company identity/market position is well-established public knowledge and
  cross-confirmed across multiple peer-reviewed articles found this session).
- **Vacuum Process Engineering (VPE)** — confirmed via direct fetch (`P3R2-C-08-S04`) as a
  **US-based** (Sacramento, CA) diffusion-bonded microchannel/PCHE manufacturer with a named recent
  "hybrid dry cooler system for sCO2 power cycles" build, serving "laser and lithography equipment
  manufacturers, defense contractors, government agencies" historically. This directly fills the
  seed's "flag for P4" gap: VPE is a real, US-domiciled, DOE-linked direct competitor already active
  in this exact product category.
- Chart Industries — search-only signal of expanded PCHE/cryogenic-heat-exchanger manufacturing
  investment in 2025-2026 alongside Heatric and VPE (industry capacity-expansion reporting,
  not independently fetched).

**China incumbents/fabricators:**
- CAS-IET (Institute of Engineering Thermophysics, Chinese Academy of Sciences) — named in the
  seed as actively researching thermal-cycling-tolerant PCHEs (reused atlas `L04-029`); institute-
  affiliated, not a merchant catalog vendor as of this session's research.
- CNNC/Nuclear Power Institute of China (NPIC) — the Chaotan One and Xinjiang-project developer;
  vertically integrated (designs and operates its own sCO2 plant), a "build not buy" risk parallel
  to the seed's own flagged "Chinese institutes verticalize" kill trigger.

**Differentiation assessment:** the seed's wedge (qualified >20°C/min thermal-transient tolerance +
code documentation) is not yet contested by either Heatric or VPE on the public record found this
session — both are established diffusion-bonded PCHE makers but neither was found (this session) to
publish a qualified rapid-thermal-transient product line specifically. This keeps the differentiation
claim plausible but unproven; a P5/P6 check of Heatric's and VPE's published transient-tolerance specs
is recommended before final scoring.

## 4. Certification and export constraints

- **ASME code case status**: ASME BPVC Section III Division 5 (High-Temperature Reactors) exists as
  a published code (2025 edition, NRC endorsement-review process ongoing per Federal Register
  search results), but **a standalone, finalized PCHE-specific nuclear code case was not found as
  completed** this session — "nuclear code case development of printed-circuit heat exchangers" is
  documented in the literature as an active R&D/qualification thread (2018-vintage conference paper
  still the most concrete public reference found), consistent with the seed's own 2028
  "begin ASME code-case conversations" plan being genuinely still-open, not already closed by a
  competitor.
- **Export control**: non-nuclear industrial/waste-heat PCHEs (the CN-market variant) were not found
  on any control list this session — low-sensitivity, consistent with the seed's own framing.
  Nuclear-qualified US product lines (advanced-reactor BOP) should stay in the US entity per the
  seed's own separability plan; no new contrary evidence found.
- **CNNC/China National Nuclear Corporation counterparty note**: CNNC (parent) is **not** on the
  BIS Entity List (distinct from China General Nuclear/CGN, which is — see `P3R2-C-09` evidence for
  detail), but CNNC and an affiliate (CNECC) appear on the US Department of Defense's Section 1260H
  "Chinese military company" list and OFAC's NS-CMIC list (US-persons investment-ban lists, not
  export-license lists) per search-only corroboration this session. This does not block component
  sales to CNNC-affiliated buyers under the EAR but is a relevant compliance-diligence flag for any
  US investor/founder considering equity ties to CNNC-linked entities — not applicable to a pure
  component-sales business model, but worth noting for deal-structuring counsel.

## 5. US/CN primary-market verdicts

- **US: demand real, buyer-reachable, window open.** NRC Part 53 is now in force (2026-04-29) and
  the STEP pilot is operating; TerraPower Natrium BOP contracting is active. VPE is a real US
  merchant competitor already in-market, meaning the opportunity is contestable, not virgin
  territory, but the specific rapid-transient-qualified niche remains unproven as occupied.
- **CN: demand real (Chaotan One is operating today, not just a 2030 trigger), buyer reachability
  constrained to licensed-entity/OEM channel (standard CN playbook), TAM unvalidated.** CNNC is
  vertically integrated on its own flagship projects, which is a real "build not buy" risk for a
  merchant entrant; the Xinjiang follow-on project (2028) is a second, independently-confirmed
  demand data point but its equipment-sourcing model (in-house vs. merchant) was not established
  this session.

## 6. 2030 timing

1. **Primary/official, US:** NRC 10 CFR Part 53 final rule, effective 2026-04-29 (ans.org/Nuclear
   Newswire, `P3R2-C-08-S01`, fetched; date corroborated by multiple law-firm trackers, search-only) —
   a 2026-dated regulatory trigger unlocking advanced-reactor BOP procurement through the 2030s.
2. **Primary/official-adjacent, CN:** CNNC's nationally-designated "Molten-Salt Energy Storage +
   Supercritical CO2 Power Generation" demonstration project, explicit 2028 completion target
   (Power Magazine, `P3R2-C-08-S02`, fetched) — a distinct, dated 2028 CN trigger, independent of
   the already-operating Chaotan One plant.

**Verdict: the window is open now in China (Chaotan One already commercially generating power) and
opens formally in the US in April 2026 (Part 53 in force) with a second CN trigger in 2028 — both
legs clear the "opens/persists through 2030" bar. Standing risks: China TAM sizing remains
single-source, CNNC's own vertical-integration posture on its flagship projects, and neither Heatric
nor VPE's transient-tolerance specs were checked against the seed's differentiation claim.**
