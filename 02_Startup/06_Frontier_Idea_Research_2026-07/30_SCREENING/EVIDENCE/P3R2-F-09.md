# P3R2-F-09 — Evidence Report (P4 Demand/Competitor Analysis)

**Idea:** Merchant high-power RF vacuum windows and output couplers (brazed-ceramic assemblies
with integrated diagnostics) for klystrons, gyrotrons, and solid-state RF chains.
**Analyst:** demand-competitor-analyst, Sonnet 5 / high effort.
**Binding condition honored:** 2026-07-13 R2 fix — 2028 evaluation-window supply into one NAMED
rebuild program made a binding gate; P4 must produce evidence on whether tube primes buy
reliability-critical components merchant (if negative, re-scope honestly).

## 1. Demand

### US/EU leg (accelerator/fusion RF)
- **Primary (buyer_procurement, T1, atlas):** Fusion for Energy (F4E) signed a ~EUR20M contract
  with Thales in 2023 to manufacture 6 of ITER's 1 MW/170 GHz gyrotrons (L05-029, atlas).
- **Independent second source (T2 trade press, fetched, refreshed this session):** ITER's gyrotron
  requirement has **doubled** under the updated 2024 baseline — from 24 to **48 gyrotrons at start
  of operations**, plus another 24 for the deuterium-tritium phase. Japan and Russia were each
  separately contracted in 2025 for up to 20 (option for 4 more = 24) additional gyrotrons, with
  deliveries extending to **2032**. [F-09-S02] This is a *positive* refresh: gyrotron (and their
  windows/couplers) demand is larger and extends later than the seed assumed.
- **Government funding (atlas, T1):** DOE HEP Accelerator Stewardship: 14 FY2024 awards across
  national labs, universities, and companies (General Atomics, Varian Medical, Axiom Optics, BAE
  Systems, RadiaBeam) (L05-031, atlas). SLAC/CPI "GREEN-RF" ~$2M DOE grant to boost klystron
  efficiency 45%→70%+ via retrofit, explicitly framed for existing accelerators (L05-032, atlas).
- **G1 — US/EU leg: PASS.** Demand is real, named, and growing (ITER doubling), with an existing
  DOE funding channel (ARDAP) that is the seed's own named 2028 binding gate.

### CN leg (CEPC / CGN Dasheng industrial accelerators)
- **CRITICAL REFRESH — negative update (T1/T2 scientific trade press, fetched):** CEPC (China's
  proposed Higgs-factory collider, the seed's named big-science CN demand trigger) **will NOT be
  included in China's 15th Five-Year Plan (2026–2030)**, despite completing full Technical Design
  Reports (accelerator TDR 2023, detector TDR Oct 2025). IHEP study leader Wang Yifang: "We plan
  to submit CEPC for consideration again in **2030**, unless FCC is officially approved before
  then, in which case we will seek to join FCC, and give up CEPC." [F-09-S01] This directly
  contradicts/delays the seed's `demand_trigger_2030_2034` ("CEPC construction decision... reached
  via big-science procurement routes") — the decision point has moved to 2030 at the earliest,
  with a real chance CEPC is abandoned entirely in favor of CERN's FCC.
- **Industrial e-beam leg (separate from CEPC, still standing):** CGN Dasheng Accelerator
  Technology's H1 2025 half-year report exists and is retrievable (L05-035, atlas, company
  filing, T1) though quantitative figures were not text-extractable; a related environmental
  filing for a new sterilization accelerator project is corroborated but not independently
  fetched (L05-038, atlas). IHEP's own high-efficiency klystron R&D (>80% efficiency target,
  803 kW/78.5% demonstrated 2024) continues independent of the CEPC construction decision
  (L05-012/L05-013, atlas) — this is R&D funding, not a procurement signal.
- **G1 — CN leg: UNCERTAIN, downgraded this session.** The seed's primary named CN trigger
  (CEPC) is now confirmed delayed to a 2030 re-decision with real cancellation risk. The
  industrial e-beam/CGN Dasheng leg is a real, separate, smaller demand channel not dependent on
  CEPC, but it was not the seed's primary CN framing.

## 2. Competitors

- **Merchant RF-window/ceramic suppliers already exist — this is the single most important
  finding for F-09's wedge.** **Kyocera** sells a catalog "SN287" silicon-nitride RF window
  product explicitly marketed for gyrotrons and klystrons, rated for >150 GHz millimeter-wave
  performance, using Kyocera's proprietary ceramic-to-metal bonding technology (fetched directly
  from Kyocera's own product page). [F-09-S03] **CoorsTek** and **Morgan Advanced Materials** are
  named (per general ceramics-industry context) as suppliers of custom ceramic-to-metal
  feedthrough/window assemblies for high-power microwave systems. This means F-09's premise that
  "every tube maker treats windows/couplers as internal art" with "no merchant qualified-spares
  supplier" is **only partially true**: standalone ceramic RF windows ARE already sold merchant by
  large, well-resourced ceramics houses. F-09's defensible wedge narrows to the **integrated
  diagnostics + qualification/acceptance-test protocol** layer, not window fabrication itself —
  consistent with the seed's own "Wedge: merchant qualification + diagnostics" framing, but with
  harder evidence that a capable incumbent (Kyocera) already occupies the base-product niche.
- **CPI (Communications & Power Industries):** the historically unified vendor (klystrons/TWTs/
  gyrotrons/magnetrons/SSPA) has **corporately split** — the vacuum-electron-device business
  (klystrons/TWTs/gyrotrons) now operates as a separate entity, "CPI Electron Device Business"
  (cpi-edb.com), while cpii.com retains antennas/SSPA/medical-imaging (atlas L05-050, refreshed
  2026-07-12). cpi-edb.com itself returned HTTP 403 and could not be independently verified this
  session — flagged for a future wave.
- **Vitzro Nextec (Korea):** commercialized a domestically localized 80 MW klystron under a
  Korean Ministry of Science/ICT-funded program with Pohang Accelerator Lab since 2015 (atlas
  L05-037) — a KR secondary-market competitor/reference point, not US/CN.
- **Excluded (India-origin, binding rule 17):** IMAT (a CVD-diamond RF-window maker marketing
  "least reflection," "high power handling" windows for klystron/gyrotron linear accelerators) was
  found and fetched, but its own website confirms headquarters at "G-696 B, RIICO Industrial Area,
  Sitapura, **Jaipur, India**." Per the binding India-origin rule, this source **cannot** be used
  to support competitor, technical, or demand claims for F-09 and is excluded from this report and
  the source ledger entirely (not even carried as discovery-only, since it is not needed for any
  claim here).

## 3. Price signals, installed base, bottom-up arithmetic

- ITER gyrotron unit scale reference: F4E-Thales contract ≈ EUR20M for 6 units → **~EUR3.3M per
  gyrotron system** (not windows alone) [L05-029, atlas] — sourced, but this is whole-tube pricing,
  not window/coupler component pricing.
- **Assumption (uncertainty range):** an RF window+coupler assembly with integrated diagnostics
  is typically **1–3% of a klystron/gyrotron system's total cost** (industry rule-of-thumb,
  judgment — no direct source found this session; flagged **assumption, ±50% uncertainty**) →
  implied per-unit window/coupler value of **~EUR35k–100k** for an ITER-class gyrotron.
- **Bottom-up TAM sketch (assumption-heavy):** ITER's expanded need (48+24=72 gyrotrons total,
  ~24 more per Japan/Russia by 2032, Europe's 6 fixed) plus DOE ARDAP-funded accelerator upgrades
  (14 awards/yr, assumption: 2–4 touch RF-source hardware) suggests a **low tens of units/year**
  global window/coupler replacement-and-new-build market in the ITER/DOE-adjacent niche through
  2032 — at EUR35k–100k/unit this is **EUR0.7M–2.4M/yr addressable**, before CEPC (now uncertain)
  or broader industrial-accelerator volume. This matches the seed's own honest "small TAM"
  framing and its kill-date logic (2 paying customers by end-2032).
- CGN Dasheng's segment scale is confirmed to exist (half-year report retrievable) but specific
  RMB figures were not independently extractable this session — treat prior "RMB340M new
  contracts" figure as **unverified, not re-confirmed.**

## 4. Certification and export constraints

- **IEC 60060-1:2025** (high-voltage test techniques, 4th edition, published 17 Apr 2025) governs
  HV test/acceptance protocols relevant to window acceptance testing (L05-043, atlas).
- **Export-control screening (binding instruction) — significant finding:** **China Academy of
  Engineering Physics (CAEP)**, named in the seed's own `competition_outlook_2030` as a potential
  CN internal-supply competitor ("CAEP/CAS internal supply"), has been on the **US Commerce
  Department's BIS Entity List since 1997** for WMD/military end-use concerns (confirmed via
  multiple independent government/tracker sources this session, though not re-fetched from a
  single primary BIS page). Any technology, software, or component of US origin cannot be
  supplied to CAEP or its listed sub-institutes without a BIS license. This is a **hard
  compliance wall**, not a mere reputational risk, for any US-origin IP in this lane that might
  otherwise flow toward CAEP-linked programs.
- **IHEP** (Institute of High Energy Physics, the CEPC/klystron R&D institute) appears on at
  least one restricted-party aggregator (OpenSanctions) as of a July 2025 reference, but this
  session could not confirm the specific list (BIS Entity List vs. a narrower research-security
  list) via a primary-source fetch. **Recommend formal restricted-party screening of IHEP before
  any technical collaboration** rather than treating it as cleared.
- No BIS/VEU hits found this session for CGN Dasheng specifically (distinct from CGN Group's
  broader nuclear-fuel-cycle entities, some of which have separately faced US scrutiny in other
  contexts) — absence of evidence, not evidence of absence.

## 5. Primary-market verdicts

- **US verdict:** Demand real, named, and growing (ITER doubling, DOE ARDAP, SLAC/CPI GREEN-RF).
  Buyer reachable via the DOE ARDAP channel, consistent with the seed's own binding 2028 gate.
  Window: open, and *widening* given ITER's doubled gyrotron requirement through 2032.
- **CN verdict: materially weaker than the seed assumed.** The named big-science trigger (CEPC)
  is now confirmed delayed to at least a 2030 re-decision with real cancellation risk (ceding to
  FCC). The remaining CN demand (CGN Dasheng industrial e-beam) is real but smaller and
  independent of the seed's primary CN framing. Buyer reachability for CAEP-adjacent programs is
  additionally constrained by a hard BIS Entity List wall. **Recommend the CN leg be treated as
  UNCERTAIN/de-weighted pending a 2027-2028 checkpoint on both CEPC's 2030 resubmission and IHEP's
  restricted-party status**, not as a co-equal demand trigger with the US/EU leg.
- **Window verdict:** US/EU window OPENS and extends to 2032 (positive refresh vs. the seed's
  2029-centric framing). CN window is UNCERTAIN — plausibly does not open on the seed's assumed
  timeline at all if CEPC is not approved in 2030 and is instead folded into FCC (a European,
  not Chinese, program).

## 6. 2030 timing verdict

- **Timing source 1 (independent, T2, fetched, 2028–2032 trigger):** ITER's Baseline-2024
  gyrotron requirement expansion (48 units at start + 24 more for D-T phase; Japan/Russia
  contracted for up to 24 additional units each, deliveries to 2032). [F-09-S02]
- **Timing source 2 (primary/official, T1/T2, fetched, explicit 2030 date):** IHEP/Wang Yifang's
  on-record statement that CEPC will be resubmitted for China's plan in **2030**, with FCC as a
  named fallback/competing claim on the same technology. [F-09-S01]
- **Verdict:** The US/EU-side window **persists and extends** through 2030–2032 (ITER ramp). The
  CN-side window **does not open on the seed's assumed 2030–2034 schedule** — it is now gated on
  a 2030 political/scientific decision that could go either way, and even a "yes" in 2030 would
  push CEPC construction well past 2030. This is a load-bearing negative refresh: F-09's CN leg
  should not be scored as if CEPC demand is a 2030-era certainty.
