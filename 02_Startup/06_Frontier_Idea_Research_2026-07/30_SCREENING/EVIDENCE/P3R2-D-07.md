# P3R2-D-07 — Demand/Competitor Evidence

Concept: RF energy recovery for klystron/gyrotron fleets — multi-stage depressed collector (MSDC)
modules plus adaptive drive electronics closing the simulated-vs-hardware RF efficiency gap.
Primary market: **US only**; CN (CEPC) is explicitly "access-limited" demand logic, not a beachhead.
Binding FIX conditions from `fix_applied_notes` (all addressed below): (1) named-facility fleet/
$-per-efficiency-point arithmetic, (2) 2028 OEM/rebuilder channel-agreement-in-principle milestone
tracked as an open gate, (3) explicit 2029 license-vs-fold-into-P3R2-C-09 decision point noted as
unresolved. Frontier wildcard: TRL 3, judged on timing-contract and licensing-shape honesty, not
inflated into a standalone-company case.

## 1. Demand

**US leg (primary official source + independent second source):**
- Atlas `L05-032` (U.S. DOE, 2023, fetched, accepted, verified_non_india_origin,
  `official_project_award`) — DOE's ~$2M GREEN-RF grant to Communications & Power Industries (CPI),
  targeting CPI's 5.5MW tube and SLAC's 65MW 5045 tube, aiming to raise klystron efficiency from ~45%
  to 70%+. **Re-fetched this session (same URL, no new source ID needed):** the DOE article also
  states "approximately 30,000 particle accelerators operate worldwide" across medicine, industry,
  energy, environment, and national security — a macro fleet-size figure not previously captured in
  the atlas's recorded claim, now added here as a session refresh.
- Independent second source: atlas `L05-031` (DOE Office of Science, HEP Accelerator Stewardship
  Awards, fetched, accepted, `official_project_award`) — 14 FY2024 awards spanning national labs
  (LLNL, Argonne, Brookhaven, SLAC, Oak Ridge, TJNAF, Los Alamos), universities, and private companies
  (including General Atomics, BAE Systems) — confirms an active, multi-institution federal RF/
  accelerator R&D funding channel independent of the single GREEN-RF grant.
- Atlas `L05-029` (Fusion for Energy, 2023, fetched, accepted, `official_project_award`) — F4E-Thales
  ~EUR20M contract for 6 of ITER's 24 required 1MW/170GHz gyrotrons (Europe 6, Russia 8, Japan 8,
  India 2) confirms the gyrotron half of the fleet has a live, named, multi-party procurement channel
  (non-US but directly relevant to the "ITER-class gyrotrons" buyer named in the idea record).

**Verdict, US: demand is real** — a live DOE-funded efficiency-recovery program with a named industry
partner (CPI) and a named target-tube fleet (CPI 5.5MW, SLAC 65MW), reinforced by a broader 14-award
federal RF-research funding program. **No named retrofit purchase order exists yet** (expected at
TRL 3) — this is R&D-stage demand with an identified path to a first paying customer (a DOE lab or
CPI itself as channel), not a booked commercial sale.

**CN leg (access-limited demand logic only, not scored as a market):**
- `P3R2-D-07-S02` (Chinese Physics B, IHEP/CAS + KEK, 2022-07-18, fetched, peer-reviewed) confirms a
  real, named, academically documented Chinese klystron-efficiency R&D program for CEPC (804kW peak,
  65.3% efficiency achieved in 2022); WebSearch-only corroboration (not independently fetched this
  session) suggests subsequent progress to ~77% at 800kW against an 80% design goal, with cited
  savings of RMB 90-130M/year at CEPC's ~60MW total RF load versus a 55% conventional baseline. **This
  is used strictly as CN technical/arithmetic context, consistent with the idea's own `china_beachhead:
  false` and "CN demand logic exists... access-limited" framing — CEPC itself is not yet an approved,
  funded, constructed collider, so this is not scored as CN demand.**

## 2. Competitors

- **CPI (Communications & Power Industries)** — confirmed via `P3R2-D-07-S01` (fetched) as a real,
  commercially shipping MSDC-klystron maker, but its **public MSDC product line (GEN IV, up to
  3.35kW) targets satellite DTH/broadcast uplink, not the multi-megawatt scientific/accelerator
  klystron class** (CPI's SLAC-class tubes are a separate, non-public-catalog product family). CPI is
  simultaneously D-07's most obvious channel partner (already DOE's chosen GREEN-RF partner per
  `L05-032`) and its most obvious competitive-absorption risk (the idea's own 2029 license-or-fold
  decision point implicitly acknowledges this).
- Thales (atlas `L05-029`) — ITER gyrotron incumbent, ~50% efficiency/98% reliability spec on its
  170GHz tubes; a scientific/fusion-RF-tube maker with its own efficiency roadmap, not confirmed to
  have a merchant MSDC retrofit product for third-party tubes.
- **Vitzro Nextec** (Korea) — atlas `L05-037` (Edaily, 2025, fetched, accepted,
  verified_non_india_origin) confirms this is a **real, verified competitor**, not a speculative
  local-language lead: Vitzro Nextec commercialized a domestically localized 2.856GHz/80MW klystron
  under a Korean Ministry of Science and ICT program run with Pohang Accelerator Laboratory (PAL)
  since 2015; PAL operates ~70 such units. This is klystron manufacturing localization, not
  specifically an MSDC-efficiency retrofit product — a real but partially adjacent competitor.
- CEPC's own high-efficiency klystron R&D (IHEP/CAS + KEK, `P3R2-D-07-S02`) is itself a potential
  future competitor/substitute if China's collider program proceeds and China's own labs internalize
  MSDC-class efficiency gains rather than buying a Western retrofit product — consistent with the
  idea's own "if OEMs internalize MSDC quickly the wedge narrows" risk framing.

## 3. Price signals and arithmetic (FIX-condition bottom-up requirement)

- **Named-facility fleet-size anchors (per the FIX condition):**
  - SLAC's original 2-mile linac klystron gallery: historically "240 klystrons" / "more than 150"
    per WebSearch-corroborated symmetry-magazine/SLAC-group reporting this session (not independently
    fetched as clean text; the symmetrymagazine.org fetch attempt returned only a partial, non-price
    excerpt) — **treat as an order-of-magnitude single-facility anchor (~150-240 tubes), not a
    precisely sourced count.**
  - ITER's ECH gyrotron fleet: 24 gyrotrons under the original baseline (Europe 6, Russia 8, Japan 8,
    India 2, per atlas `L05-029`), rising to 48 at start of operations and a further 24 for D-T
    operations under the new baseline (72 total, WebSearch-corroborated, not independently fetched) —
    at 1MW/tube this is 24-72MW of installed gyrotron RF power in one facility alone.
  - DOE's own framing (`L05-032`, re-fetched this session): ~30,000 particle accelerators operate
    worldwide across all applications — the vast majority are lower-power industrial/medical tubes
    outside D-07's target class, but this anchors the outer bound of the addressable universe before
    narrowing to multi-MW scientific/accelerator/fusion-ECH tubes specifically.
- **Cost anchors:** klystrons and modulators can cost "up to half a million dollars apiece" (WebSearch-
  corroborated trade reporting this session, not independently fetched as clean text) — if efficiency
  retrofit hardware (MSDC collector + drive electronics) is priced at an **assumption** of 20-40% of a
  new-tube cost (i.e., roughly $100-200K per retrofit), and the addressable near-term fleet (SLAC-class
  large accelerators, ITER-class gyrotron stations, DOE HEP-funded labs) is on the order of a **few
  hundred to low-thousands of tubes globally through the early 2030s** (directional, combining the
  SLAC and ITER anchors above with the broader ~14-lab DOE Accelerator Stewardship footprint,
  `L05-031`), the **directional near-term retrofit TAM is plausibly in the $30-200M range — wide
  uncertainty, no per-unit retrofit price exists yet since no product has shipped (TRL 3).**
- **Savings-side arithmetic (CN context only, not a US TAM input):** CEPC's own cited figures (WebSearch
  this session, not independently fetched beyond the 2022 peer-reviewed paper) claim RMB 90-130M/year
  savings at CEPC's ~60MW RF load for a 55%->70-80% efficiency improvement — useful as a **savings-per-
  installed-MW benchmark** (roughly RMB 1.5-2.2M per MW-year per 15-25 efficiency points) that could be
  applied, with appropriate uncertainty, to size US facility-level electricity-cost savings, but this
  session did not locate a US-specific $/MW-year figure to cross-check it against.

## 4. Certification and export constraints

- No dedicated safety/performance certification standard specific to MSDC retrofit modules was
  identified this session; klystron/gyrotron tubes themselves are typically qualified against
  facility-specific acceptance protocols (e.g., ITER's own 98%-reliability/50%-efficiency spec per
  F4E-Thales, atlas `L05-029`), not a general external standard.
- **Export control:** RF power tubes and associated high-voltage pulsed-power electronics for
  particle-accelerator and fusion-heating applications sit in a sensitive dual-use-adjacent category
  (accelerator technology has historically been ITAR/EAR-relevant in some configurations), but this
  session found no evidence that MSDC collector/drive-electronics retrofit hardware specifically is
  on the BIS Entity List or Commerce Control List by name. Given `china_beachhead: false` and the
  CEPC content being used only as access-limited context (not a sales target), no CN-specific
  Entity-List check was performed for a named CN customer in this idea — correctly, since none is
  proposed as a buyer.

## 5. US/CN primary-market verdicts

- **US: demand directionally real, buyer ecosystem reachable (DOE labs, CPI as channel, ITER-linked
  European buyers as a secondary/side channel), but no named retrofit purchase order exists yet.**
  UNCERTAIN-leaning-PASS: appropriate for TRL 3 with a credible 2026-2029 de-risking path (the idea's
  own 2027 lab test-stand demonstration), contingent on the 2028 OEM/rebuilder channel-agreement-in-
  principle milestone (CPI-class) actually being reached — this has NOT yet been demonstrated and

- **CN: correctly not scored as a market.** CEPC demand logic is real technical context (a genuine,

  construction collider, and the idea record's own framing (access-limited) is honest and should not
  be inflated into a CN beachhead.

## 6. 2030 timing

1. **Primary/official, US:** atlas `L05-032` (DOE GREEN-RF grant) plus `L05-031` (DOE HEP Accelerator
   Stewardship, 14 FY2024 awards) confirm ongoing, currently-funded federal RF-efficiency R&D; the
   idea's own commercial_readiness_kill_date (2034-06) and the FIX-mandated 2029 license-or-fold
   decision point are the concrete near-term (2028-2029) triggers rather than any single dated federal
   deadline — this is the weakest timing case in the batch precisely because there is no single dated
   2028-2035 official trigger analogous to D-01's DOE Milestone Program FY2027 renewal or D-02's
   signed tape contracts.
2. **Independent, named-facility, near-term trigger:** atlas `L05-029` (F4E-Thales ITER gyrotron
   contract) — an active ~6-year (2023-2029) manufacturing/delivery program for ITER's ECH system,
   with the broader ITER baseline extending gyrotron procurement into the early 2030s (48-72 total
   units) — an independent, dated, multi-party (non-US but directly fleet-relevant) trigger.

**Verdict: the window is plausibly open through 2030-2034 but is the least contractually anchored
of this batch's five ideas.** Unlike D-01 (DOE Milestone Program FY2027 renewal) or D-02 (signed,
quantified tape contracts), D-07 has no single dated 2028-2035 official trigger of its own — its
timing case rests on (a) an ongoing, real but modest DOE R&D grant, (b) ITER's multi-year gyrotron
delivery program as an adjacent facility-fleet anchor, and (c) the idea's own explicit 2028/2029
internal decision gates. This is consistent with — and reinforces — the elegance judge's own
"licensing-shaped" adjudication: the honest read is that D-07 is a real technical opportunity with a
plausible but thin and largely self-imposed (not externally dated) timing case, appropriate for a
license-or-fold decision rather than a standalone-company commitment before 2029.
