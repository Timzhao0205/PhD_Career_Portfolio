# P3R2-F-02 — Demand/Competitor Evidence

Concept: Superconducting-magnet electrical balance-of-plant platform — standardized precision
drive-and-dump skids (10 ppm-class high-current DC converters + energy-extraction/dump units +
current leads + busbars), entry SKU = catalog binary current leads/feedthroughs (absorbed from
F-21). Primary market: **US+CN**, `elegance_verdict: PROMOTE` with a binding P4 pre-promotion
condition: an OCEM/Danfysik-class incumbency map. This file delivers that map and tests the named
buyers (CFS/Realta/WHAM, DOE Milestone program, ASIPP) and the CFS-bundling risk flagged as the
named US ceiling.

## 1. Demand

**US leg:**
- **Primary, direct-customer:** `P3R2-F-02-S03` (PRNewswire, CFS/Realta Fusion joint release,
  2026-04-02, fetched) — CFS and Realta Fusion signed a long-term strategic HTS-magnet partnership
  with "the potential to reach a multi-billion dollar value"; CFS will design/build magnets for
  Realta's Anvil prototype and its Hammir commercial-scale successor and "first-of-a-kind power
  plants" targeted for "the mid-2030s." This is real, named, dated merchant-magnet commerce (not a
  BOP-skid order specifically — CFS sells magnets, not yet the drive-and-dump electrical island —
  but it proves the magnet-buyer market the BOP platform would sit behind is real and growing).
- **Independent second source:** `P3R2-F-02-S04` (DOE/energy.gov, fetched) confirms the Milestone-
  Based Fusion Development Program is authorized for "$415 million through fiscal year 2027" under
  the CHIPS and Science Act, with $46M obligated for the first 18-month period across 8 selectees
  (Realta Fusion, Thea Energy, Focused Energy, etc.), and the most aggressive awardees targeting
  "FPP preliminary-design reviews by the late 2020s" toward "an operating FPP by the mid-2030s." This
  is a primary federal program-funding signal, independent of the CFS/Realta commercial deal.
- **Verdict, US demand:** real and dual-sourced — one named commercial magnet contract (potential
  multi-billion-dollar value) plus a funded federal program with 8 named companies on parallel
  tracks. Both signals are about magnets/pilot-plant programs, not yet a signed BOP-skid order —
  F-02's own product (drive-and-dump + leads + busbars) has no named buyer yet, which is consistent
  with `current_trl: 6` and a 2027-29 precompany plan, not an existing sale.

**CN leg:**
- The idea's named buyer "ASIPP (PF converter control modules / RMP power-supply tenders)" could
  **not** be independently confirmed as a specific tender this session. What was found: `P3R2-F-02-S07`
  (Hefei Institutes of Physical Science/CAS, 2026-06-27, fetched) reports ASIPP completed and
  parameter-tested a 582-tonne toroidal-field magnet (14.5T, 120GJ stored energy, 98kA operating
  current, "1.3x the volume of ITER's TF magnet, 3x the stored energy") and a high-Tc central-solenoid
  coil, both stated "100%国产化" (100% domestically produced) — this confirms ASIPP is actively
  building large-scale, high-current superconducting magnet systems that structurally require
  precision drive/dump/lead/busbar hardware, but the article contains **no mention of power-supply
  or converter procurement**, and a targeted search for ASIPP PF-converter/RMP power-supply tender
  notices returned only broader ASIPP tritium-system tenders (unrelated equipment class) and general
  ITER-China conductor/power-supply/diagnostics task assignments (context, not a dated tender).
- **Verdict, CN demand: NOT independently confirmed as buyer evidence this session.** The idea's
  named CN buyer claim is plausible given ASIPP's demonstrated big-science magnet-building scale and
  100%-domestic-content push, but no dated procurement/tender record for drive-and-dump-class power
  electronics was located. This should be treated as an **unverified named-buyer claim**, not
  confirmed demand — a gap the orchestrator should flag before scoring the CN leg as equally strong
  as the US leg.

## 2. Competitors

**P4-required incumbency map (binding pre-promotion condition):**
- **OCEM Power Electronics** (Valsamoggia, Bologna, Italy) — `P3R2-F-02-S01` (fetched): magnet power
  supplies "up to 50,000 Amperes" with stability/resolution "up to 5ppm," serving CERN (LHC dipoles/
  quadrupoles), Diamond Light Source, hadron-therapy centers, and fusion (JT-60SA switching-network
  units interrupting 20kA in <1ms; DTT tokamak "safety units," ~€8M/3 units per prior WebSearch
  context). ">3,500 custom power converters" delivered over 35+ years. This is a live, well-
  resourced, decades-established competitor in exactly the converter-catalog space.
- **Danfysik** (Denmark) — `P3R2-F-02-S02` (fetched): "high-stability power supplies... from a few
  watts to megawatts and stability from 0.1ppm" — i.e., tighter stability than F-02's own 10ppm-class
  target — plus a large custom-magnet portfolio, serving accelerators/research facilities globally.
  Page did not surface energy-extraction/dump-unit specifics, but the core precision-converter
  business directly overlaps F-02's claimed converter component.
- **Assessment against the idea's own white-space claim:** both incumbents confirm precision magnet
  converters ARE catalog items (matching the idea record's own framing: "precision magnet converters
  are catalog items, so the white space is the integrated extraction/dump + leads + acceptance-
  protocol island, not converter novelty"). Neither company's public materials describe a
  standardized, catalog *drive-and-dump-plus-leads-plus-acceptance-protocol* bundle — the narrower
  white-space claim survives this fetch-level check, but only barely: both firms have 30+ years of
  fusion/accelerator delivery experience and could bundle a competing offer quickly if a buyer asked.
- **CFS vertical-integration/bundling risk (named US ceiling):** `P3R2-F-02-S06` (TechCrunch,
  2026-04-02, fetched) confirms CFS sells HTS magnets **standalone** to Realta, WHAM, and Type One
  Fusion — "no mention of bundled power electronics or balance-of-plant systems" in CFS's current
  commercial motion. This is a meaningful finding: as of this session, CFS is **not** yet bundling
  BOP/drive-and-dump electronics with its magnet sales, so the "named US ceiling" risk is not yet
  realized — but CFS's scale ($6.85B raised, 1,000+ employees per search context) means it could
  vertically integrate at any time, and the risk should stay flagged, not cleared.
- **CN:** ASIPP itself is the in-house builder/user of its own magnet systems (100%-domestic-content
  framing per S07); no third-party CN merchant BOP-skid vendor was identified this session, consistent
  with a CN market served (if at all) through big-science institutional procurement rather than an
  open commercial channel.
