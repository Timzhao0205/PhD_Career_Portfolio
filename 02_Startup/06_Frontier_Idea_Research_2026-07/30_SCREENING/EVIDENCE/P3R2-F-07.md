# P3R2-F-07 — Evidence Report (P4 Demand/Competitor Analysis)

**Idea:** High-cycle, high-current charging-interface thermal subsystems — liquid-cooled MCS
cable/connector assemblies (US) + high-cycle swap-coupler/buffer-battery thermal modules for
battery-swap infrastructure (CN).
**Analyst:** demand-competitor-analyst, Sonnet 5 / high effort.
**Binding condition honored:** 2026-07-13 R2 fix — CN-leg BINDING GATE (P4 must confirm CN
swap-equipment component procurement is open to licensed outside suppliers before any CN
investment); 2028 charger-OEM co-development gate; P4 connector-incumbent map required.

## 1. Demand

### US leg (MCS conductive charging)
- **Primary-ish (fleet capital deployment, T2 trade press, fetched):** WattEV ordered **370 Tesla
  Semi** Class-8 electric trucks (announced May 9 2026) — "largest single electric truck
  deployment in California" — with >300 units deployed under a **joint program with the Port of
  Oakland**; first 50 units 2026, remaining 320 by end-2027. New MCS-equipped charging stations
  are opening at Port of Oakland, Fresno, Stockton (2026) and breaking ground in Sacramento
  (2026). [F-07-S01]
- **Independent second source (T2, fetched):** Kempower, Windrose and EV Realty jointly validated
  MCS interoperability at EV Realty's San Bernardino truck-charging hub (announced 25 Mar 2026),
  delivering up to 1.2 MW ahead of an April 2026 site opening — a concrete step toward commercial
  MCS availability for fleet operators. [F-07-S02]
- **Government trigger (atlas, T1):** EPA Clean Ports Program — $3B IRA-funded, 53 grants across
  26 states/territories for zero-emission port equipment (L10-048, atlas).
- **Standard (atlas, T1, official 2026 trigger):** IEC TS 63379 (MCS) published Feb 2026,
  1,250V/3,000A DC, >3.75 MW peak (L10-050, atlas).
- **Assessment:** Demand for MCS *charging infrastructure* is now real and actively capitalized
  (not merely a standard on paper) — WattEV+Port of Oakland is a genuine buyer/procurement-grade
  signal (a named operator deploying capital against a named site and truck order). However, this
  is demand for MCS **charging service**, not proven demand for a **merchant thermal-interface
  subsystem** sold to OEMs: Phoenix Contact already ships a liquid-cooled 1 MW-class CCS2
  connector today (1,000 A boost, water-glycol cooling, in-house thermal engineering) — see
  Competitors. The specific "sell thermal subsystems to charger/connector OEMs" business model
  therefore has **real end-market demand but unproven merchant-supplier demand**.

### CN leg (battery-swap thermal/contact hardware)
- **Primary (buyer_procurement, T1, fetched):** Sinochem New Energy's 2023–2024 open framework
  tender for complete heavy-truck battery-swap-station equipment (charging module + swap module +
  monitoring room + backup batteries) explicitly **does not accept dealer/agent bids** — only
  manufacturers with production facilities and a track record of **≥15 delivered battery-swap
  units and ≥2 heavy-truck swap-station projects since 2019** may bid; cap price RMB 2.5M/set
  incl. tax; max 4 suppliers selected. This *confirms* the CN swap-equipment channel uses
  competitive framework tenders, not sole-source CATL procurement — partially satisfying the
  binding gate. [F-07-S03]
- **Independent second source (buyer_procurement, T2, fetched):** Guangdong Tower Energy's
  2025–2027 tender for ~4,000 battery-swap charging cabinets (31,800 chambers, RMB 40.68M incl.
  tax) — same manufacturer-only, no-dealer-bids structure, 6-year warranty, 15-day delivery SLA.
  Confirms the open-tender pattern is not a one-off. [F-07-S04]
- **Installed-base/duty-cycle evidence (T1/T2, fetched):** CATL Choco-Swap: 1,650 stations across
  127 cities as of May 2026, target >3,000 stations/190 cities by end-2026, 99-second swap cycle
  [F-07-S05]. NIO: 100 million cumulative battery swaps reached 6 Feb 2026 (NIO's own release)
  [F-07-S06]; 3,839 national swap stations and 171,600 single-day swaps (1 May 2026 holiday peak)
  per China Securities Journal [F-07-S07].
- **Gate-relevant negative finding:** the swap **connector** sub-niche specifically (not the whole
  station) already has an entrenched CN merchant supplier: Recodeal (瑞可达) has been NIO's
  primary swap-connector supplier since 2016/2017, IATF16949/UL94V-0 certified, HVC series
  800A/1500V connectors, ≥5,000 mate-cycle life. [F-07-S08] CATL's own swap system is described
  in the seed as vertically integrated in-house. So while *station-level* equipment procurement is
  open-tender, the *connector/thermal-interface* layer specifically already has a qualified
  domestic incumbent for the largest non-CATL swap network (NIO).
- **G1 verdict — CN leg: UNCERTAIN.** Real, large, high-cycle installed base and open
  station-level tenders exist (gate partially satisfied), but the specific connector/thermal
  sub-assembly niche is not demonstrably open merchant territory — it is already served
  (NIO/Recodeal) or vertically captured (CATL in-house).

**G1 verdict — overall: UNCERTAIN on both legs.** End-market demand (MCS trucking, CN battery
swap) is real and growing fast in 2026, but demand for the *specific merchant thermal-subsystem
product* F-07 proposes is not independently confirmed on either leg.

## 2. Competitors

- **US/Europe (cooled MCS/HPC cable-connector incumbents):**
  - **Phoenix Contact** — ships a liquid-cooled CCS2 connector today: 1,000A boost/800A
    continuous with active water-glycol cooling (leak detection, temp sensors), IP67; MCS cables
    expected "early 2026," MCS inlets "medium term," 2.25 MW cables (1,500A/1,500V) from Q4 2026.
    [F-07-S09]
  - **Stäubli**, **Huber+Suhner** — both market MCS/HPC connector product lines (per atlas-adjacent
    search context; Huber+Suhner covers 150 kW–800 kW/800 A continuous).
  - These incumbents are solving cooled-connector thermal engineering **in-house**, which is the
    central competitive risk to F-07's thesis (a merchant thermal-subsystem supplier selling *into*
    connector OEMs who already build this themselves).
- **CN (swap connectors/thermal hardware):**
  - **Recodeal (瑞可达, 688800.SH)** — NIO's primary swap-connector supplier since 2016. [F-07-S08]
  - CATL's Choco-Swap system: in-house/vertically integrated (per seed; not independently
    contradicted this session).
- **US timing/infrastructure context (not competitors):** EV Realty, Kempower, WattEV are
  charging-network operators/integrators, not thermal-subsystem competitors — they are potential
  **customers**, reinforcing rather than displacing the demand read above.

## 4. Certification and export constraints

- **IEC TS 63379** (MCS) is the binding global interoperability standard (Feb 2026) [L10-050,
  atlas]; **GB/T 45926-2025** governs CN excavator battery-swap systems (effective 1 Jan 2026)
  [L10-046, atlas] — a *different* vertical (construction equipment) from EV/truck swap, useful
  only as evidence of CN standards-body formalization of swap-system engineering generally.
- **Export-control screening (binding instruction):** **CATL** was added to the US DoD's Section
  1260H "Chinese Military Companies" list in January 2025 and remained listed in the June 2026
  update; effective **30 June 2026, DoD is prohibited from procuring from 1260H-listed
  entities** [F-12-S03/S04 context, cross-checked]. This is a **DoD-procurement and reputational**
  constraint, **not** a BIS Entity List export-license requirement — no evidence found this session
  that CATL itself is on the BIS Entity List. Any US-market go-to-market plan that depends on
  CATL's swap ecosystem specifically (rather than NIO/Recodeal's non-CATL network) should account
  for this reputational/procurement overhang, especially if the company later seeks DoD-adjacent
  or federally-funded US customers.
- No BIS Entity List or VEU hits found this session for Recodeal (688800.SH) or the CN swap-tender
  counterparties (Sinochem New Energy, Guangdong Tower Energy) — absence of evidence, not
  evidence of absence; recommend formal restricted-party screening before any JV/licensing
  commitment.

## 5. Primary-market verdicts

- **US verdict:** Demand for MCS charging infrastructure is real, dated, and actively capitalized
  (WattEV/Port of Oakland, EV Realty/Kempower, EPA funding). Buyer reachability for a **merchant
  thermal-subsystem supplier** is uncertain — incumbent connector OEMs (Phoenix Contact) are
  already vertically solving the thermal problem in-house and shipping product in 2026. Window:
  open now (2026) but **narrowing faster than the seed assumed** — the seed's 2029–2033 volume
  thesis is too late; the technical differentiation must land by 2027–2028 or the OEMs close the
  gap themselves.
- **CN verdict:** Demand is real and enormous in scale (NIO 100M swaps, CATL 3,000+ stations by
  end-2026), and station-level procurement is open-tender (not CATL-exclusive). But the specific
  connector/thermal sub-niche is already served by an entrenched domestic supplier (Recodeal for
  NIO) or captured in-house (CATL). A foreign-founder entrant would need a JV/licensing route and
  would face steep tender eligibility bars (2+ years of delivery track record). Buyer reachable:
  difficult, not impossible, via a licensed CN manufacturing partner.
- **Window verdict:** Opens now (2026); the underlying end-markets (MCS trucking, CN battery
  swap) are scaling faster in 2026 than the seed's timing thesis implied, which **compresses**
  rather than extends the merchant-entry window — incumbents (Phoenix Contact, Recodeal) are
  closing the technical gap concurrently.

## 6. 2030 timing verdict

- **Timing source 1 (primary/official, atlas L10-050):** IEC TS 63379 (MCS) published Feb 2026 —
  official global standard; sets the interoperability floor for 2026–2030+ deployment.
- **Timing source 2 (independent, T2, fetched, dated 2026):** WattEV's 370-Tesla-Semi order and
  multi-site 2026 MCS depot buildout (Port of Oakland, Fresno, Stockton, Sacramento) [F-07-S01] —
  shows the demand ramp is already underway in 2026, not deferred to 2029–2033 as the seed's
  `launch_2030_timing_thesis` assumed.
- **Verdict: window OPENS now and is ALREADY PARTLY CAPTURED by incumbents' in-house thermal
  engineering (Phoenix Contact, Recodeal) before 2030**, rather than opening fresh in 2029–2033.
  This is a material, negative refresh of the seed's timing thesis: the "unsolved thermal
  engineering" premise is eroding in real time on both legs.
