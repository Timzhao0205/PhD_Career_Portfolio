## 1. Demand

**PRIMARY buyer/procurement source:** Baker Hughes' own press release announcing a strategic collaboration with XGS
Energy (March 25, 2026) to advance a 150MW geothermal project in New Mexico, supplying the PNM grid in support of
Meta's data-center operations, targeting full operation by 2030 [new:P3R2-A-14-S01]. This is a named oilfield-service
buyer (one of the idea's own named buyer examples) committing engineering/subsurface work to a dated commercial EGS
project that sits squarely inside the idea's 2030-2034 demand window.

**Independent second demand source:** ARPA-E/DOE's own SUPERHOT program page confirms $30M in government funding
targeting reservoirs >375C/>22MPa, with a goal of enabling 10-20GW of baseload power access [new:P3R2-A-14-S02];
Utility Dive's reporting fills in the named awardee — Mazama Energy, a 3-year demonstration project near Newberry
Volcano, Bend, OR (~10GW site potential) — confirming this is a real, funded, government-anchored program, not just a
funding announcement [new:P3R2-A-14-S03].

**Reinforcing evidence:** SLB-Ormat's October 2025 partnership explicitly targets "independent power producers,
utilities, and data center operators" for EGS pilot-to-commercial scale-up [new:P3R2-A-14-S06] — a third named
US oilfield-service major committing capital to the same demand thesis, independent of Baker Hughes/XGS.

**Supply-gap confirmation:** CISSOID's own last-time-buy notice confirms its 175-225C HT product line is not merely
winding down but has fully closed its ordering window (mid-2025) due to X-FAB foundry-process termination, and the
company is now "managing the sale of remaining inventories" only [new:P3R2-A-14-S04] — this independently confirms
(rather than merely repeats) the idea's central supply-gap premise, with a harder, now-realized date.

**Verdict: G1 — PASS.** Real, named, dated (targeting 2030) commercial buyer commitments exist from all three
oilfield-service majors named in the idea record (Baker Hughes, SLB, and — per the idea's own list — Ormat as
developer), plus an active, separately-funded government R&D program (ARPA-E SUPERHOT) and DARPA THERMAL technical
roadmap. This is the strongest-evidenced demand case of the five ideas in this group.

## 2. Competitors

**United States:**

  confirming the market gap [new:P3R2-A-14-S04].
- **Texas Instruments** — HT silicon line tops out at 175-210C, per idea record; unchanged this session.
- **Honeywell** — legacy HT microelectronics line exiting, per idea record; CISSOID's own page explicitly offers
  cross-reference support "for customers replacing Honeywell's obsoleted High Temperature Microelectronics Products,"
  independently corroborating the Honeywell exit.
- **In-house teams at SLB/Halliburton/Baker Hughes** (200C-class), per idea record — these majors are simultaneously
  demand signals (§1) and potential vertical-integration competitors; worth flagging as a dual-nature risk.
- **DARPA THERMAL performers** — a government-funded technical roadmap toward 800C mixed-signal ICs is active
  (solicitation DPA26BZ02-NV008 closed June 24, 2026) [new:P3R2-A-14-S05], meaning DARPA — not a merchant vendor — is
  currently the most advanced entity pushing past 300C; a founder-led venture should expect to either partner into or
  compete against DARPA-performer IP.

**China:** No named commercial 300C+ HT electronics vendor or procurement found this session (searched directly in
Chinese). Chinese oilfield literature shows continued reliance on imported HT chips (historically Honeywell) for
downhole/geothermal logging, with no evidence of a domestic 300C+ substitute. CN correctly remains outside this
idea's demand base.

**Japan:** Fukushima-decommissioning robotics ecosystem remains a valid qualified-electronics side market per atlas
[atlas:L15-011], unchanged this session.

## 3. Price signals, installed base, and bottom-up arithmetic

- **Cost reference (component-level, weak/soft):** Dewar flasks are "the single highest cost item in most geothermal
  logging tools" at ~$10,000 each (industry technical-conference source, not independently re-fetched this session —
  treat as indicative only, not load-bearing).
- **Bottom-up arithmetic (labeled assumptions):**
  - **Fact:** SUPERHOT targets 10-20GW of baseload power access from reservoirs >375C [new:P3R2-A-14-S02]; DOE
    estimates suggest next-generation geothermal broadly (EGS) could reach 90GW by 2050, up to 300GW potential in the
    US (per SLB/Ormat partnership framing, secondary citation from search synthesis, not independently re-fetched —
    treat as directional).
  - **Assumption:** each commercial EGS/superhot well requires 1-3 downhole HT instrumentation modules (logging,
    monitoring, telemetry) over its drilling-and-completion lifecycle, at an assumed $50k-$250k per qualified 300C
    module (analogy to existing 225C-class logging-tool electronics packages; no direct 300C module price was found
    this session). Uncertainty: wide, easily +/-3x, since no commercial 300C product yet exists to price against.
  - At an illustrative 100-300 superhot/EGS wells drilled per year by the early-to-mid 2030s (a plausible but
    **unverified extrapolation**, not sourced to any single document) and 1-3 modules/well at $50k-$250k, the
    addressable market would be roughly **$5M-$225M/year** at that point — an extremely wide range reflecting how
    early-stage this specific niche (300C instrumentation, as opposed to geothermal drilling broadly) still is.
  - No aggregator market-size report was used for this figure; several "geothermal drilling services market" reports
    returned in search were not fetched or cited given the sourcing caution on generic market-research vendors.

## 4. Certification and export constraints for a 2030 launch

- No significant export-control regime specific to HT downhole electronics was identified (unlike A-13's space/ITAR
  case); this is a domestically-consumed industrial/energy product, not a controlled dual-use space or defense item
  by default.
- Qualification is technical/industrial rather than regulatory: 1,000-hour continuous 300C operation with <1% drift,
  and packaging thermal-cycling to 500 cycles, are the idea's own stated internal qualification targets — no external
  certification body (e.g., API, IECEx) was found to gate this specific niche this session; API/oilfield tool
  qualification norms would apply generically but were not separately verified.
