# P3R2-A-21 — Ruggedized multi-megawatt charging power systems (MCS/IEC TS 63379) for US ports, rail yards, mines

Analyst: demand-competitor-analyst, P4 group G02. Model/effort: Sonnet 5, high reasoning effort.
Geography rule applied: India and Singapore excluded from all market/buyer/competitor claims below. Australia
(Fortescue) appears only as competitive/technical context, never as a market or demand claim.

## 0. Pre-promotion condition check

No explicit judge-mandated evidence gate beyond the standing merge note that C-15's CN demand leg was "NOT imported —
demand-without-access vs vertically integrated domestic stacks." This session's research (§2, XCMG/Huawei/Huaneng
mining-truck deployment) independently confirms and strengthens that reasoning: real Chinese MW-charging demand
exists but is captured by a domestic vertically-integrated consortium, reaffirming — not merely inheriting — the
decision to keep CN out of this idea's addressable market.

## 1. Demand

**PRIMARY buyer/procurement source:** EPA's own Clean Ports Program Awards page: $2.94B across 53 grants in 26
states/territories; the Zero-Emission Technology competition alone is $2.8B/25 awards funding 1,500+ cargo-handling
units, 1,000 drayage trucks, 10 locomotives, 20 vessels, and charging/BESS infrastructure; the two largest awards are
Port of NY/NJ ($451.6M) and Port of LA ($411.7M) [new:P3R2-A-21-S01]. This is a named, dollar-quantified federal grant
program directly funding the charging-infrastructure category this idea targets.

**Independent second demand source (with a material caveat):** E&E News/POLITICO reporting confirms three EPA
programs (LA Port $412M, AQMD CPRG $500M, GGRF/Climate United $250M) were subject to a 2025 funding freeze under the
Trump administration; LA Port received its first post-freeze allocation Feb 24, 2025; AQMD access was described as
"in flux"; the EPA Administrator reportedly sought to cancel the GGRF program entirely [new:P3R2-A-21-S03]. EPA's own
program page shows the initiative back in "implementation phase" as of June 2026 [new:P3R2-A-21-S02] — so the funding
is real and (as of this check) flowing again, but it survived a genuine cancellation attempt, not a hypothetical one.

**Additional named-buyer color:** Union Pacific ordered 10 Wabtec FLXdrive battery-electric locomotives (~2.5MWh
each) in 2022, delivering from late 2023 for yard service [new:P3R2-A-21-S07] — real but still pilot-scale; no
subsequent fleet-expansion order was found this session, so treat the rail leg of demand as directionally real but
not yet proven at fleet scale (a downgrade from the idea's "FLXdrive fleet" framing, which implies more scale than
the evidence currently supports).

**Verdict: G1 — PASS, with a flagged political-fragility risk.** The core EPA funding is real, obligated, and (per
the freshest check) implementing as of mid-2026, but it visibly survived a 2025 cancellation attempt and remains
politically exposed through the 2026-2030 window this idea depends on. The rail leg of demand is thinner than the
idea's framing suggests (one 10-unit pilot order, no confirmed scale-up).

## 2. Competitors

**United States:** ABB, Siemens, Kempower (truck-focused EVSE majors), Wabtec/Cat (proprietary bundled systems) — all
per idea record, unchanged this session. **Fortescue** (a mining major, not a US company, but the most advanced named
mining-electrification buyer globally) is building its own 6MW DC chargers in-house (30-minute full charge for a
Liebherr T264, 240t/2.6MWh), with 300+ electric haul trucks planned for the Pilbara — trucks supplied by **Liebherr
and China's XCMG** [new:P3R2-A-21-S05]. This independently confirms the idea's own named risk ("Fortescue-style
in-house builds") and shows a Chinese OEM (XCMG) is already competing for mining-truck supply in a non-China market —
a signal that Chinese heavy-equipment players are not purely domestic-market-bound.

**China:** A real, operating, vertically-integrated MW-charging + battery-swap mining ecosystem already exists: 100
XCMG ZNK95 electric autonomous mining trucks at China Huaneng's Yimin coal mine (Inner Mongolia, deployed July 2025),
using Huawei megawatt superchargers (2,400A continuous, fully liquid-cooled) and 6-minute automated battery swaps,
with State Grid Smart Vehicle-Network as the charging-infrastructure partner [new:P3R2-A-21-S06]. This is **more
commercially mature** (100-unit fleet, in production operation) than the equivalent US picture (still prototype/pilot
stage per the idea's own precompany plan), but it is captured entirely by a domestic SOE/OEM consortium
(XCMG+Huawei+State Grid+Huaneng) with no evidence of third-party merchant-vendor access — reinforcing the
"demand-without-access" framing already applied to CN in this idea's merge notes.

**Japan/Taiwan/Korea:** JP listed as a secondary market in the idea record; no new named competitor or buyer found
this session.

## 3. Price signals, installed base, and bottom-up arithmetic

- **Standard-driven cert baseline:** IEC TS 63379 (MCS connectors, inlets, cable assemblies for megawatt DC charging)
  was formally published February 2026 [new:P3R2-A-21-S04] — the standard the product is built around is now real,
  not merely pending, though the companion EVSE-side standard IEC 61851-23-3 remains in development.
- **Charger price/spec reference:** Fortescue's 6MW charger fully recharges a 2.6MWh battery in ~30 minutes
  [new:P3R2-A-21-S05] — confirms the power/time envelope the idea targets is achievable today (de-risks the physics,
  though Fortescue built its own rather than buying a merchant unit).
- **Bottom-up arithmetic (labeled assumptions):**
  - **Fact:** EPA Clean Ports funds ~1,000 drayage trucks and 10 locomotives directly, plus unspecified charging
    infrastructure, across 53 US ports [new:P3R2-A-21-S01].
  - **Assumption:** a ruggedized 1.5-6MW modular charging system (per the idea's own product spec) costs
    $1.5M-$4M per site-ready installation (analogy to the idea's own $8-20M V1 capital range implying a handful of
    pilot deployments; no direct vendor quote for a harsh-duty MW charger was found this session — treat as a wide,
    labeled assumption, uncertainty +/-2x).
  - If even 50-150 of the EPA-funded port/rail/mine sites eventually require a harsh-duty MW-class charging system by
    2030-2033 (an **unverified extrapolation** from the 53-port/1,000-truck program footprint, not sourced to a
    single document), the addressable niche would be roughly **$75M-$600M** in cumulative equipment value across the
    initial US wave — a wide range that should be treated as illustrative, not a validated TAM.

## 4. Certification and export constraints for a 2030 launch

- IEC TS 63379 (published Feb 2026) is the primary connector/cable standard; UL/NEC listing would be required for
  US grid-tied installations (standard industrial-electrical practice, not separately re-verified this session).
- No significant export-control constraint applies (this is a domestically-deployed industrial power system, not a
  controlled dual-use item).
- Political/regulatory risk is the dominant "certification-adjacent" constraint here: the idea's own named risk (CARB
  repeal precedent) is corroborated by the broader 2025 EPA funding-freeze pattern found in §1 — regulatory reversal
  risk is real and has already partially materialized once in this program's short life.

## 5. Primary-market verdicts

- **US verdict:** Demand is REAL and buyer-reachable (EPA is a public grant-making federal agency; named port/rail/
  mine buyers exist). The window is OPEN but politically fragile — funding has already survived one freeze/
  cancellation attempt and remains exposed through 2030.
- **CN verdict:** Not a market for this idea (china_beachhead: false, correctly assigned) and freshly reaffirmed:
  real CN demand exists (Huaneng/XCMG/Huawei) but is fully captured by a domestic vertically-integrated consortium,
  consistent with "demand-without-access."

## 6. 2030 timing

**Timing source 1 (primary/official, inside 2028-2035 window):** EPA's own Clean Ports Program implementation
timeline runs 3-4 years from each award (finalized Dec 2024-Jan 2025), meaning equipment deployment continues through
approximately 2028-2029 [new:P3R2-A-21-S02] — squarely feeding into a 2030 commercial-launch window for a merchant
charging-system vendor entering after the initial grant-funded wave.

**Timing source 2 (independent, standards-based):** IEC TS 63379's February 2026 publication, with CharIN's own
framing of "accelerat[ing] the transition to zero-emission mobility" [new:P3R2-A-21-S04], is an independent,
standards-body-confirmed trigger that de-risks the certification path specifically, on a similar 2026-2028 timescale.

**Verdict: the window OPENS now (2026-2028, grant-funded pilot wave) and is positioned to PERSIST through 2030-2033**
as fleets scale, but two real risks could compress it: (a) political/funding reversal (already partially realized
once), and (b) OEM/mining-major in-house bundling foreclosing the merchant niche (Fortescue/XCMG evidence shows this
is already happening at the largest scale globally, even if not yet in the specific US port/rail/mine segments this
idea targets).
