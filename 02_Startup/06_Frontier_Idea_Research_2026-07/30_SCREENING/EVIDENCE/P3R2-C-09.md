# P3R2-C-09 — Demand/Competitor Evidence

Concept: Standardized solid-state pulsed-power platform — stackable Marx/modulator modules with
digital droop control for industrial e-beam, X-ray, and accelerator RF drive. Primary market: US+CN
(dual, independent legs). No explicit FIX gate in the longlist record (`elegance_verdict: PROMOTE`),
but the record's own `timing_window_risk` field flags export separability as a live, unresolved
question requiring "legal review required by 2028" — evaluated below with a specific counterparty
finding.

## 4. Certification and export constraints

- **IEC 60060-1:2025** (high-voltage test techniques) remains the relevant acceptance-test standard
  referenced by the seed; no contrary finding this session.
- **Export control — the critical new finding this session**: CGN Dasheng's ultimate parent,
  **China General Nuclear Power Group (CGN)**, together with three named affiliates — China General
  Nuclear Power Corporation, China Nuclear Power Technology Research Institute Co. Ltd., and Suzhou
  Nuclear Power Research Institute Co. Ltd. — were added to the BIS Entity List on 2019-08-14 for
  "efforts to acquire advanced U.S. nuclear technology and material for military uses in China"
  (Pillsbury Law, `P3R2-C-09-S01`, fetched; license policy is presumption of denial). **CGN Dasheng
  Electron Accelerator Technology Co., Ltd. is not itself one of the four named entries** — as a
  formal EAR matter, BIS Entity List restrictions are entity-specific, not automatically inherited by
  every subsidiary of a named parent group, so CGN Dasheng is not confirmed Entity-listed in its own
  right. **However, CGN Dasheng is an admitted wholly-owned subsidiary of the very group named as
  entry #1 ("China General Nuclear Power Group")**, which creates a real counterparty-diligence
  ambiguity (ownership/control proximity to a named Entity-List parent) that the seed's own
  "legal review required by 2028" flag correctly anticipates. **This should be resolved by export
  counsel before any CGN Dasheng sales discussion, not assumed clear.** China Isotope & Radiation
  Corporation and other named CN buyers were not found on the BIS Entity List this session
  (search-only check; a full consolidated-screening-list lookup was not performed).
- Marx-generator/pulsed-power hardware is confirmed dual-use-adjacent (radar, EMP, directed-energy
  applications) per general ECCN/ITAR context found this session, but no specific ECCN number was
  identified for stackable solid-state Marx/modulator modules as a class — consistent with the
  seed's own framing that this needs a 2028 legal-structuring review, not a settled classification.
