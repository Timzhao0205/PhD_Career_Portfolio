# P3R2-D-12 -- Demand & Competitor Evidence

**Concept:** Electrohydrodynamically-pumped two-phase cold plates: field-driven, no-moving-parts
per-chip flow control for 3-5 kW AI packages, with a non-PFAS working-fluid plan.
**Judge-mandated binding gate (from `fix_applied_notes`):** (a) 2028 named-OEM/COOLERCHIPS
co-development demand-bridge gate; (b) hard fluid-intersection gate (dielectric + non-PFAS +
low-GWP simultaneously, or kill); (c) 2027 experiment must beat, not merely match, best mechanical
pumping.
**Analyst:** demand-competitor-analyst (Sonnet 5 / high effort), P4 group G07.
**Verdict headline: G1 = UNCERTAIN (the thermal/regulatory demand pressure is real and
independently confirmed; the EHD-specific demand bridge required by the judge's own 2028 gate does
not yet exist, exactly as the seed itself warned).**

## 1. Demand evidence

**This idea's demand case rests on cooling-capacity pressure, not EHD-specific pressure -- that
distinction matters and is confirmed, not weakened, by this session's research.**

**Primary buyer/procurement source (capacity pressure, not EHD-specific):** Google's Project
Deschutes 2MW CDU specification (atlas [L14-043], buyer_specification, T1) demands a 3C approach
temperature difference at 2MW -- a hyperscaler-originated buyer specification now functioning as a
quasi-industry standard. ARPA-E COOLERCHIPS (atlas [L14-030], $40M across 15 named US
awardees including Nvidia, Raytheon, JETCOOL) is the primary government R&D-demand vehicle for
next-generation cooling broadly.

**Independent second demand source, refreshed for 2026 -- the fluid crisis is real and dated:**
3M's exit from all PFAS manufacturing (announced Dec 2022, **last Novec orders March 31, 2025,
manufacturing lines shut down by end of 2025**) has made the qualified two-phase working fluid
(Novec 7100/7000-series) **unavailable as of 2026** (fetched directly, [P3R2-D-12-S01],
thecoolingreport.com, cross-checked against atlas [L14-033] EPA AIM Act HFC phasedown and
[L14-034] EU F-gas Regulation 2024/573). Named replacement fluids exist but are still qualifying:
Chemours' Opteon 2P50 (HFO-based, GWP 10) and Opteon SF33 (ZutaCore's interim fluid, per
[P3R2-D-12-S01]); **ZutaCore has committed to a fully PFAS-free two-phase fluid "by 2026"** per its
own press materials (atlas [L14-046] confirms ZutaCore's $100M+ Series C from June 2026 with
strategic investors Mitsubishi Electric and Samsung Electronics). This independently confirms the
seed's premise that "two-phase adoption is destabilized by the PFAS fluid exit" is not a
hypothetical -- it is an active, dated, ongoing industry disruption in exactly the 2025-2026
window the seed's precompany plan targets.

**What is missing, and this is the honest, load-bearing gap:** the judge's own 2028 FIX gate
requires "a named cold-plate OEM or COOLERCHIPS-lineage co-development agreement" as the demand
bridge this wildcard currently lacks. This session searched directly for exactly that (Boyd,
Vertiv, CoolIT, Eaton EHD partnerships) and **found none**. Instead, this session found the
opposite signal: the cold-plate/liquid-cooling supplier market is undergoing rapid, capital-
intensive **consolidation by large industrial conglomerates** -- Eaton's **$9.5B** acquisition of
Boyd's thermal division (closed March 12, 2026; Boyd's thermal unit projected **$1.7B total /
$1.5B liquid-cooling** 2026 sales; fetched directly, [P3R2-D-12-S02]) and Ecolab's **$4.75B**
acquisition of CoolIT Systems (March 2026, search-derived) -- "three major transactions totaling
roughly $19 billion" targeted liquid cooling within an 11-day window per [P3R2-D-12-S02]. This is
a **red flag, not a green light**: a market this well-capitalized and consolidating is exactly the
kind of environment where a startup's technology gets acquired-and-shelved or out-resourced by an
incumbent's own R&D budget rather than licensed in from outside, and it directly explains why no
named OEM has yet signed on to evaluate EHD specifically.

## 2. Competitors

- **US:** Flex/JETCOOL (microconvective direct-to-chip, atlas [L14-045], $1.27M DOE COOLERCHIPS
  grant recipient then acquired by Flex), ZutaCore (waterless two-phase, atlas [L14-046]), Vertiv
  (integrator-scale, atlas [L14-048], FY2025 $10.23B revenue, $15.0B backlog entering 2026),
  now-Eaton-owned Boyd Thermal (5M+ cold plates delivered to hyperscalers per prior atlas
  reference; deal terms confirmed [P3R2-D-12-S02]), and newly Ecolab-owned CoolIT. **All are
  mechanical-pump-based**, consistent with the seed's own competitive framing -- none identified
  this session offer in-plate field-driven (EHD) flow control.
- **Taiwan (side market):** Auras Technology (雙鴻) and AVC/Chiptech (奇鋐) are the two leading
  AI liquid-cooling module suppliers, both converting air-cooling lines to liquid-cooling and
  expanding cold-plate welding/assembly capacity for NVIDIA B100/B200-class platforms, with 2026
  liquid-cooling penetration expected to approach 50% industry-wide driven by NVIDIA's Rubin
  platform moving to 100% liquid cooling (search-derived, sinotrade.com.tw securities-research
  synthesis; not independently fetched this session -- treat as T3 trade-press color, not a
  primary filing). Delta Electronics (台達電) also named as active in cold-plate/immersion
  dual-mode systems.
- **South Korea (side market):** GS Caltex (atlas [L14-049]) launched a direct-liquid-cooling
  fluid product in 2025 with named customers Samsung SDS and LG U+.
- **China:** Envicool is named in the seed as a judgment-based competitor; GB 40879 (atlas
  [L14-035]) revision (tightening PUE to <=1.25/1.2) remains **in the public-comment/planning
  stage as of this session's research** and has not been confirmed finalized -- treat the seed's
  "China's PUE tightening" demand trigger as directionally correct but not yet a finalized
  regulatory fact; do not overstate its current legal force.

## 3. Price signals / installed base / bottom-up arithmetic

- Boyd's thermal division alone: **$1.5B projected 2026 liquid-cooling revenue** ([P3R2-D-12-S02])
  gives a sense of scale for just one (now-consolidated) incumbent's cold-plate business --
  the addressable OEM-channel market for a component/subsystem supplier like D-12 is a small
  fraction of this, since D-12 sells *into* cold-plate OEMs, not directly to hyperscalers.
- **Bottom-up niche arithmetic (explicit assumptions):**
  - Assume EHD-enhanced cold plates could command a **10-30% price premium** per plate over a
    standard mechanically-pumped two-phase plate, given the added per-channel flow-control value
    (**assumption**, no pricing data for any EHD-enhanced commercial cold plate was found this
    session since none exists commercially yet).
  - Assume a realistic 2030-2032 beachhead is capturing this premium on **1-5% of new AI-rack
    cold-plate unit volume** at hyperscaler-scale deployment (**assumption, wide uncertainty
    range**, since no primary total-unit-count figure for AI cold plates was found this session
    beyond Boyd's own "5 million delivered" cumulative historical figure, which spans all cold
    plate types, not just AI/two-phase).
  - Given Boyd's single-company $1.5B liquid-cooling revenue figure as a rough proxy for a
    meaningful fraction of total addressable OEM-level cold-plate spend, a 1-5% volume share at a
    10-30% component-level premium implies **on the order of $1.5-25M in annual component-level
    revenue at scale for a licensed EHD flow-control add-on** -- **a wide, low-confidence range**
    that should be treated as illustrative only, consistent with the seed's own $4-10M V1 capital
    sizing (a component/subsystem play, not a full cold-plate-system play).

## 4. Certification and export constraints (2030 launch)

- **PFAS/fluid regulation is the dominant constraint, and it cuts both ways.** US: EPA AIM Act HFC
  phasedown (atlas [L14-033]) steps to 30% of baseline 2029-2033; EU: Regulation (EU) 2024/573
  (atlas [L14-034]); and the broader EU REACH PFAS restriction proposal (SEAC draft opinion March
  2026, 60-day consultation to May 25, 2026, ECHA aiming to finalize opinions by end of 2026,
  search-derived, not independently fetched this session) with **derogations of 5-12 years being
  considered for hard-to-substitute uses** -- meaning a genuinely non-PFAS, low-GWP, dielectric
  fluid is not just a "nice to have" but the difference between a compliant and non-compliant
  product in the EU by the early-to-mid 2030s. This validates the judge's hard fluid-intersection
  gate as the correct binding constraint, not an overcautious one.
- China GB 40879 revision (atlas [L14-035]) would tighten PUE requirements in a way that favors
  any technology improving cooling efficiency, EHD included, but remains unconfirmed-finalized as
  of this session (see Section 2).
- No export-control barrier specific to EHD pump electronics (low-voltage, non-dual-use) was
  identified; this is a thermal-management component, not covered by the export regimes relevant
  to D-10/D-11's laser/defense technology.

## 5. Primary-market verdicts

- **US: thermal-capacity demand is real and buyer-reachable (hyperscalers, ARPA-E, cold-plate
  OEMs); the EHD-specific demand bridge is not yet built.** The 2028 FIX gate the judges imposed
  is the correct test, and as of mid-2026 it has not been passed -- no named OEM/COOLERCHIPS
  co-development agreement for EHD specifically was found. This is consistent with the atlas's
  own [L16-003]/[L16-004] review-paper conclusions that EHD lacks industrial-scale deployment.
- **China: demand trigger (GB 40879) is directionally real but not finalized; market is
  permissible per the seed's own `china_beachhead: true` but reachability is unproven** -- no
  China-specific buyer, OEM conversation, or procurement signal for EHD (or even mechanical
  two-phase at scale) was found this session; the atlas's China liquid-cooling demand evidence
  (GB 40879, national AI-energy action plan [L14-036]) is regulatory-direction evidence, not
  buyer-specific evidence, and [L14-036]'s own atlas record already flags that media claims of a
  "100% liquid cooling mandate" are **not** supported by the primary notice text -- a caution this
  evidence file repeats rather than overstates.
- **Window:** The underlying thermal problem (3-5kW packages, PFAS fluid crisis) is real and
  time-pressured now (2026), which argues the window is open; but the well-capitalized
  consolidation wave among cold-plate OEMs (Eaton/Boyd, Ecolab/CoolIT) suggests incumbents have
  both the capital and the motive to develop or acquire flow-control innovations themselves before
  2030, which could close the window for an outside licensor faster than the seed's 2032-12
  kill-date assumes.

## 6. 2030 timing sources (two independent, >=1 primary/official 2028-2035 trigger)

1. **Primary/official:** EU REACH PFAS restriction process (SEAC draft opinion March 2026, opinions
   targeted for finalization by end of 2026, derogations of 5-12 years under consideration,
   search-derived from ECHA's own announced timeline pages, not independently fetched this
   session -- a verifier should fetch echa.europa.eu directly) -- an EU regulatory trigger landing
   inside the 2028-2035 window that will determine exactly which dielectric fluids remain legal,
   directly gating which two-phase (EHD or mechanical) cooling technologies can ship into the EU
   after the derogation periods expire.
2. **Independent second source:** China GB 40879 revision (atlas [L14-035]) -- proposes PUE
   <=1.25 by end of 2025 for new/expanded large data centers and <=1.2 for national-hub-node
   centers; while not yet confirmed finalized this session, it is an official Chinese national
   standard-body process with a dated compliance target inside the mission's window, independent
   of the EU PFAS process above.

**Window verdict: open now on the underlying thermal/regulatory problem, but not yet open
specifically for EHD** -- the honest reading of the judge's own 2028 gate is that this idea's
survival depends on an event (a named OEM co-development agreement) that has not happened as of
mid-2026, in a supplier market that is consolidating around large capitalized incumbents rather
than fragmenting toward outside innovation. This is not a "kill now" verdict -- the underlying
demand and regulatory pressure are both real and independently confirmed -- but it is a clear,
undecided gate that P4/P6 scoring should treat as still open, not quietly assumed passed.
