# P3R2-D-01 — Demand/Competitor Evidence

Concept: Merchant quench-detection-and-protection subsystem for REBCO/HTS fusion magnets
(co-wound fiber-optic sensing + sub-ms active energy extraction, sold with a qualification dossier).
Primary market: **US only**. Binding condition (merge_import_notes / adjudication_note): CN chapter
(ASIPP/CFETR) is documented as **license-out-only, counsel-gated** — `china_beachhead: false` is
correct and is not re-opened here; this file verifies the US case and treats CN strictly as an
upside/licensing footnote, per the idea record's own framing. Frontier wildcard: TRL 3, current_trl
noted explicitly per assignment instructions — judged on timing-contract and buyer-path honesty, not
inflated.

## 1. Demand

**US leg (primary official source + independent second, buyer-side, source):**
- Atlas `L03-032` (U.S. DOE, 2023-05, fetched, accepted, verified_non_india_origin,
  `official_project_award`): DOE's Milestone-Based Fusion Development Program, authorized $415M
  through FY2027 ($46M initially obligated), selected 8 companies including **Commonwealth Fusion
  Systems and Tokamak Energy**; awardees collectively raised >$350M in private capital against $46M
  federal seed. This is a primary, official, named-awardee US government program directly tied to
  HTS-magnet milestones.
- Atlas `L03-030` (US ITER / ORNL, 2026-04-27, fetched, accepted, `official_project_award`): US ITER
  completed final central-solenoid deliveries (18m, six-module magnet) — confirms a second, distinct,
  live US superconducting-magnet procurement channel (national-lab side) beyond the private-company
  milestone program.
- Independent second/buyer-side source: atlas `L03-035` (TechCrunch, 2026-04-02, fetched, accepted,
  `direct_customer_documentation`) — CFS's own Chief Commercial Officer states CFS closed "the
  largest deal of this kind to date," **selling HTS magnets to Realta Fusion**, following an earlier
  magnet sale to the University of Wisconsin WHAM experiment (2024) and a technology license to Type
  One Fusion. This is named-customer, quoted-executive evidence that a **merchant market for HTS
  magnets already exists** — the exact buyer category (magnet builders/integrators) D-01's protection
  subsystem would sell into.
- Refresh, this session: `P3R2-D-01-S01` (Prism News, 2026-03-11, fetched) confirms CFS installed the
  first of 18 SPARC magnets at Devens in a fortnightly-delivery buildout, targeting first plasma in
  2026 and net-energy demonstration in 2027, with ARC (grid-scale commercial device) targeted for
  "the early 2030s" — i.e., the buyer-facility buildout is physically underway now, not a future
  abstraction.

**Verdict: demand is proven** — a primary federal program with named awardees (CFS, Tokamak Energy),
a second live national-lab procurement channel (US ITER), and named-customer commercial magnet sales
(Realta Fusion, University of Wisconsin) all independently confirm both R&D-stage funding and an
emerging merchant-magnet market. The specific quench-protection subsystem itself has **no direct
demand evidence (no named RFP/tender for quench protection was found)** — demand is inferred from
the magnet-buyer ecosystem's stated painful job (no-insulation-winding tradeoffs), not from a
protection-specific procurement document. This is consistent with TRL 3 / pre-product status and
should be read as "buyer ecosystem proven, specific-product demand still to be created," not as a
booked order.

## 2. Competitors

- In-house teams at CFS and TE Magnetics (atlas `L03-035`, `L03-044`) are the closest analog
  competitors — both are building magnets and, by extension, their own protection/quench-management
  approaches (no-insulation windings avoid needing fast external detection at the cost of ramp/control
  performance, per the idea's own `painful_job` framing).
- CERN's CLIQ (Coupling-Loss Induced Quench) system is the reference LTS-tuned quench-protection
  lineage (idea record's own citation; not independently re-verified this session, treated as
  established prior art context, not a merchant fusion-market competitor).
- Instrumentation vendors (Lake Shore Cryotronics-class Hall/temperature sensors) supply components,
  not integrated detection+dump systems — **no merchant vendor of an integrated HTS quench
  detection+protection product was identified in the atlas or in this session's research**, consistent
  with the idea record's own claim.
- CFS's own public materials (per `P3R2-D-01-S01` and atlas `L03-035`) emphasize magnet sales and
  licensing as a near-term revenue line — this means CFS is simultaneously a **potential customer and
  a potential vertically-integrated competitor** if it builds in-house protection rather than buying
  it, the same absorption risk flagged generically elsewhere in this batch (C-01/C-21 for power
  electronics).

## 3. Price signals and arithmetic

- No direct price was found for a merchant quench-detection-and-protection package (expected, given
  TRL 3 / no product exists yet).
- **Bottom-up arithmetic (assumptions labeled):** DOE's Milestone Program funds 8 companies; CFS alone
  is building an 18-magnet SPARC machine now and has already sold/licensed magnets to at least 3 named
  external counterparties (Realta Fusion, U. Wisconsin WHAM, Type One Fusion license) within ~2 years.
  **Assumption:** if each of the 8 milestone-program companies builds 1-2 magnet systems (10-30
  individual HTS coils each, a generic fusion-magnet-system scale assumption, not sourced per-company)
  through 2030, and each system requires one protection-subsystem sale in the **assumption** range of
  $2-8M (anchored loosely on the idea's own `first_experiment_budget_usd` of $250K for a lab-scale
  demonstrator, scaled up by an unverified multiple for a full production-grade multi-coil system),
  the near-term (2028-2032) US TAM is plausibly in the **$20-100M range across the milestone-program
  cohort alone — highly uncertain, no per-unit price source exists, and this excludes ITER/national-lab
  channels entirely.**

## 4. Certification and export constraints

- No dedicated safety-certification standard specific to HTS quench-protection electronics was
  independently confirmed this session (the idea's own framing — protection/standards immaturity is
  simultaneously the moat and the schedule risk — is not contradicted by anything found this session).
- **Export-control assessment:** quench-protection electronics for HTS magnets are described in the
  idea record as "dual-use-adjacent." This session found no evidence the specific technology class
  (fiber-optic strain/temperature sensing, FPGA trigger logic, kV-class energy-extraction circuits) is
  itself on the BIS Entity List or Commerce Control List by name — these are generally
  instrumentation/power-electronics categories subject to standard EAR99-or-adjacent classification,
  not a named controlled technology. However, **the CN chapter (ASIPP/CFETR) is correctly gated on
  counsel clearance, not on a confirmed license requirement** — this session did not find ASIPP or
  CFETR on the BIS Entity List (no direct check was performed this session against the current
  Supplement No. 4 text, which returned an oversized/unreadable fetch both attempts made; this should
  be independently re-verified before any CN engagement is contemplated, consistent with the idea
  record's own "ONLY if export counsel clears" framing).

## 5. US/CN primary-market verdicts

- **US: demand real, buyer ecosystem reachable.** DOE milestone-program awardees (CFS, Tokamak
  Energy/TE Magnetics) and US ITER/ORNL are identifiable, named, currently-active US counterparties;
  CFS has already demonstrated it will buy/sell/license magnet-adjacent technology from/to external
  parties (Realta Fusion, U. Wisconsin, Type One Fusion). PASS as a buyer-reachable market, with the
  caveat that no protection-specific demand signal (RFP/tender) exists yet — this is a "sell the
  ecosystem before the product exists" situation, appropriate to TRL 3.
- **CN: not evaluated as a market** per the binding pre-promotion condition (license-out-only,
  counsel-gated, `china_beachhead: false`). This file does not attempt to establish CN demand and
  treats the ASIPP/CFETR 35.1T magnet program (atlas `L03-029`/`L03-037`, both fetched, accepted,
  verified_non_india_origin) purely as evidence that a credible future licensing counterparty exists
  in China, not as market entry.

## 6. 2030 timing

1. **Primary/official, US, trigger within/adjacent to the 2028-2035 window:** atlas `L03-032` — DOE
   Milestone-Based Fusion Development Program authorized through **FY2027** ($415M authorized, only
   ~$98M appropriated to date per WebSearch this session, not independently fetched) — the FY2027
   authorization-renewal decision is the concrete near-term trigger event whose outcome directly gates
   2028-2032 pilot-plant-scale funding cadence for CFS, Tokamak Energy, and peers.
2. **Independent, company-level, explicit early-2030s trigger:** `P3R2-D-01-S01` (Prism News,
   2026-03-11) and atlas `L03-035` (TechCrunch, 2026-04-02) both independently report CFS's own stated
   target of ARC (grid-scale commercial device) for "the early 2030s," following SPARC first-plasma
   (2026) and net-energy demonstration (2027).

**Verdict: the window opens by 2030 and plausibly persists through 2033-2034** (the idea's own
`commercial_readiness_kill_date`). The physical buildout (SPARC magnet installation now, ARC targeted
early 2030s) and the funding-program renewal decision (FY2027) both land inside the founder's 2030
launch horizon, giving a credible 2026-2029 pre-company learning path (per-idea `first_experiment`,
2027 university-lab quench campaign) that lines up with CFS's own hardware timeline. Risk, honestly
stated: if no-insulation windings prove fully sufficient at pilot-plant scale (the idea's own
`timing_window_risk`), or if the FY2027 authorization is not renewed at meaningful appropriation
levels, the window narrows materially — this is a real, not hypothetical, risk given the confirmed
gap between $415M authorized and ~$98M appropriated to date.
