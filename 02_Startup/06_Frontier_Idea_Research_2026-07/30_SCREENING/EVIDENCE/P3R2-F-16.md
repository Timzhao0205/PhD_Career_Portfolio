## 3. Price signals and arithmetic

- No public unit price for CN-market PCB/packaging plasma cleaners was found (bespoke/quote-based
  category; CN tender-document purchase fees of RMB500/USD100 in both `L01-114` and
  `P3R2-F-16-S01` are administrative fees, not equipment prices).
  - **Commodity-tier anchor (assumption, wide uncertainty):** general industrial plasma-cleaning
    systems range roughly **$4,000-$70,000** at the low/entry end per Western vendor catalog listings
    (Plasma Etch Inc. product pages, discovery-only context, not a CN-market equivalent) — a directional
    floor only; inline production-scale PCB/packaging tools are very likely priced well above this
    (assumption, no direct CN price source located this session).
  - **Premium-thesis arithmetic (the idea's own >=30% premium target):** if a baseline CN inline
    plasma-cleaning line costs an assumption of **$150,000-$400,000 per line** (assumption, no direct
    source), a 30% metrology premium implies **$45,000-$120,000 incremental revenue per line sold**
    — before consumables/service pull-through. No independent source validates either the baseline
    or the premium-realization rate; this is the idea's own untested assumption, repeated here for
    traceability only.
  - **Market-scale anchor:** atlas `L01-044` (NAURA/Piotech/ACM Research China-vendor Q1-2026 revenue
    growth of 26-57% YoY) supports a general thesis of a fast-growing CN process-equipment market
    into which a premium plasma tool could sell, but is not specific to plasma-cleaning-machine
    revenue.
