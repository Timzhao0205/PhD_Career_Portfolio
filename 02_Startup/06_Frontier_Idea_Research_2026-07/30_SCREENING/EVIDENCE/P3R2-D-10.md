# P3R2-D-10 -- Demand & Competitor Evidence

**Concept:** Coherent-beam-combining phase-control engine (controller ASIC/FPGA + modulator
front-ends), a channel-scalable "conductor" subsystem sold to directed-energy primes and
ultrafast-laser OEMs.
**Analyst:** demand-competitor-analyst (Sonnet 5 / high effort), P4 group G07.
**Verdict headline: G1 = PASS (strongest demand case of the five assigned ideas -- named, dated,
dollar-denominated US government buyers exist now, with a specific beam-control procurement
line item).**

## 1. Demand evidence

**Primary buyer/procurement source:** US Navy Joint Beam Control System procurement, reported via
laserwars.net's direct reporting on JLWS contract documents (fetched, [P3R2-D-10-S01]): **"the
Navy plans on awarding $31.7 million in contracts for Joint Beam Control System development as
soon as the fourth quarter of 2026, with another $30 million in contracts for procurement and
testing of containerized hardware expected by March 2027."** This is a **named, dated,
dollar-denominated beam-control-specific line item** -- i.e. the exact subsystem category D-10
proposes (not just "a laser weapon program" but the beam-control piece of it) has a near-term
(Q4 2026-Q1 2027) government procurement date attached. Combined with the already-atlas'd JLWS
prime awards (nLIGHT $44M/$627M ceiling, Lockheed Martin Aculight; atlas [L12-031], [L12-032]) and
Army/Navy JLWS R&D totalling **$675.93M through FY2031** ([P3R2-D-10-S01]; also independently
reported via tomshardware.com's citation of DoD FY2027 budget documents), this is the strongest,
most specific procurement evidence found across the five assigned ideas.

**Independent second demand source:** DoD FY2027 budget request, independently reported: **>$2
billion in directed-energy RDT&E** requested for FY2027 (search-synthesized from multiple defense-
budget trade press covering the same primary budget documents; the "High Energy Laser Advanced
Component Development & Prototype" program element requested **$44.5M (up from $5.5M in FY2026)**
and "High Energy Laser Advanced Technology Program" **$201M** -- not independently re-fetched from
the primary comptroller.war.gov PDF this session due to a failed direct fetch, treat these two
specific dollar figures as T2/search-corroborated pending a verifier re-fetch of
comptroller.war.gov/Portals/45/Documents/defbudget/FY2027/...). This is independent of, and
consistent in direction with, the Navy beam-control line item above -- both point to a real,
growing, near-term (FY2026-2027) DoD RDT&E budget for exactly this technology stack. DARPA AMPED
and MELT (atlas [L12-040], [L12-041]) are additional already-verified government program-level
demand signals for the underlying photonic/laser-tile building blocks a phase-control engine would
serve.

## 2. Competitors

- **US:** Prime in-house control teams are the main competitive threat, not merchant vendors --
  nLIGHT (its own JLWS/CBC work traces to HELSI 2019, per [P3R2-D-10-S01]) and Lockheed Martin
  Aculight both do beam control in-house (atlas [L12-033], [L12-041]); no merchant phase-control
  vendor was identified in the atlas or this session's searches, consistent with the seed's own
  claim. Coherent Corp. has a separate $30M DoD deal for a 400kW-class laser (optics.org,
  search-derived, defence-blog.com corroborating -- not independently fetched this session,
  discovery lead).
- **Israel (context, not a target market):** Civan Lasers (Jerusalem) -- proprietary "Dynamic Beam
  Laser" CBC technology; Elbit Systems is developing airborne high-power laser weapons building on
  Rafael's ground-based Iron Beam (fetched directly, [P3R2-D-10-S03], defensenews.com) which the
  same article's underlying reporting states operates at **10-100 kW** (a different, lower power
  class than JLWS's 150-500 kW target, and a discrepancy vs. an earlier search snippet citing
  "200-300 kW" for Iron Beam -- **conflict noted, not averaged**: the directly-fetched
  defensenews.com figure of 10-100 kW is treated as more reliable than the unfetched
  nextbigfuture.com "100 kW fully operational" claim). Israel is not a target market under the
  binding US/China geography rule but is directly relevant competitive/technical context since
  Civan's CBC IP could plausibly be offered into the same US primes.
- **South Korea (side market):** DAPA (Defense Acquisition Program Administration) announced,
  June 1, 2026 (fetched directly, [P3R2-D-10-S02], korea.kr -- official government source), that
  it successfully **indigenized the laser oscillator** for the "Cheongwang" (천광) laser anti-air
  weapon (Block-1), the world's first laser anti-air weapon to reach operational status
  (December 2024), with the domestic oscillator delivering **>=50% performance improvement** over
  the previously imported component and domestic-content ratio rising from 76% to 90%. This is a
  **fielded, operational** system (unlike JLWS's still-prototype status), giving Korea a real
  domestic laser-weapons industrial base -- but the article does not confirm whether Cheongwang
  uses coherent beam combining specifically vs. a single high-power fiber laser, so this should
  **not** be read as a confirmed CBC competitor; it is confirmed as a fielded competing
  laser-weapon program that a US-based CBC subsystem vendor would need to out-perform to win any
  future Korea-side business.
- **China:** Harbin Xianguang/Xinguang Photoelectric's "Lijian" (利剑) portable laser-weapon series
  (fetched directly, [P3R2-D-10-S04], military.china.com) is **2-10 kW class**, ~500m-1,200m
  range, ~2 million yuan/unit, targeted at drones -- a materially **lower power class** than
  JLWS's 150-500 kW cruise-missile-defense target, and the article gives no indication of
  coherent beam combining (a single-emitter or simple fiber-laser architecture is more consistent
  with the described weight/cost/power profile). China's separately-reported "LY-1" high-power
  laser system (search-only, not independently fetched -- discovery lead) may be a closer power
  class but was not verified this session. **Honest read: no confirmed China-based
  high-power (100kW+) CBC competitor was found this session**, though China is investing broadly
  in laser weapons at multiple power tiers.

## 3. Price signals / installed base / bottom-up arithmetic

- No public per-channel price exists for complete phase-lock controller systems (search-confirmed
  absence); the closest component-level price anchor found is fiber-optic combiner hardware
  (passive components, not phase-lock electronics) at **$385-$924/unit** (Agiltron, search-
  derived) and commercial precision phase-measurement instruments like the Liquid Instruments Moku
  Phasemeter (sub-6-microradian precision, search-derived) as a reference for what commercial
  phase-metrology hardware costs -- neither is a direct comparable for a purpose-built
  channel-scalable CBC controller ASIC/FPGA stack, so this remains a **build-up-from-BOM
  estimate, not a market price**, consistent with the seed's own admission that this subsystem is
  currently "bespoke, unsolved engineering rebuilt per program."
- **Bottom-up niche arithmetic (explicit assumptions):**
  - JLWS alone implies **2 known prime integrators** (nLIGHT/Lockheed Martin Aculight) each
    needing a beam-control solution for systems scaling from 16 to 1,000+ channels (per the
    seed's own first-experiment framing) -- **assumption: if a merchant controller captured
    even 1 of the 2 JLWS primes' beam-control subsystem spend, at the Navy's own disclosed $31.7M
    Joint Beam Control System line item scale, first-contract revenue could be $10-30M**,
    uncertainty driven by whether primes ultimately buy vs. build (the seed's own
    `timing_window_risk` flags this exact risk).
  - DARPA AMPED ($39M/$14M Phase 1/2 per a single-source aggregator, atlas [L12-040] flags this
    figure as unconfirmed on the primary darpa.mil page) and MELT programs add further,
    smaller-scale near-term government R&D contract opportunities (**assumption**, dollar
    figures not independently confirmed for MELT).
  - Total near-term (2026-2031) addressable US DoD beam-control-adjacent RDT&E, combining the
    Navy's $31.7M+$30M near-term contracts, the $675.93M JLWS 5-year R&D total, and the >$2B
    FY2027 DE RDT&E figure, suggests a **realistic 2028-2031 subsystem-contract opportunity in
    the $10-50M range for a merchant entrant that lands one prime relationship**, with the total
    program-level spend (hundreds of millions to billions) mostly flowing to primes and
    remaining out of reach for a subsystem startup absent a design-win. This is consistent with
    the seed's own $3-8M V1 capital range.

## 4. Certification and export constraints (2030 launch)

- IEC 60825 (laser product safety, atlas [L12-048]) and ISO 11553-1/-2 (industrial laser
  machinery safety, atlas [L12-049], [L12-050]) govern any dual-use industrial CBC product line.
- **ITAR is the dominant constraint**, not a hypothetical one: the seed's own mitigation
  ("dual-use industrial sales and ITAR-segregated product lines") is necessary because a
  phase-control engine sold into JLWS/DARPA programs is unambiguously a defense article; a 2030
  go-to-market plan must budget for ITAR registration, technology-control-plan overhead, and
  restricted foreign-national access from the outset -- this was not independently re-verified
  with a primary DDTC source this session (discovery-level statement based on the well-established
  nature of US laser-weapons ITAR classification), and should be confirmed with a primary DDTC/
  USML Category XII citation before final scoring.
- Export to China of any weapons-adjacent CBC IP is not a realistic consideration; this is
  consistent with the seed's `china_beachhead: false`.

## 5. Primary-market verdicts

- **US: demand real, buyer reachable, window open now.** This is the clearest primary-market case
  of the five ideas: named prime integrators (nLIGHT, Lockheed Martin Aculight), a named
  program office (DoD SCADE CTA, atlas [L12-031]), and a **specific, dated, dollar-denominated
  beam-control procurement line item landing in Q4 2026-Q1 2027** ([P3R2-D-10-S01]) all exist
  today, not as a 2030-2034 hypothetical.
- **China: not a beachhead, and not a confirmed high-power CBC competitor either.** Consistent
  with the seed's `china_beachhead: false`; China's fielded laser weapons found this session are
  a lower power class (kW-scale portable systems) than JLWS's 150-500 kW target.
- **Window:** The window is open now and the key risk is not "does demand exist" but "does a
  merchant vendor win a design slot before primes finish internalizing beam control" -- exactly
  the risk the seed's own `timing_window_risk` field already names. The Navy's own Q4 2026/Q1
  2027 procurement dates mean this risk resolves within 12-18 months of the current date, well
  before 2030, making near-term execution (not 2030 timing) the binding constraint for this idea.

## 6. 2030 timing sources (two independent, >=1 primary/official 2028-2035 trigger)

1. **Primary/official:** DoD/Navy Joint Beam Control System and containerized-hardware
   procurement dates ([P3R2-D-10-S01], reporting on official budget-justification documents) --
   Q4 2026 ($31.7M) and Q1 2027 ($30M) contract dates, with Army+Navy JLWS R&D totaling $675.93M
   through **FY2031**, squarely inside the mission's 2028-2035 window.
2. **Independent second source:** NNSA/LLNL Enhanced Fusion Yield Capability laser upgrade (atlas
   [L12-042]) -- Critical Decision-1 approved April 13, 2026, $26M FY2026 appropriation, providing
   an independent (non-DE-weapons) large-laser-system government demand signal that corroborates
   continued US federal appetite for large coherent/high-power laser system procurement into the
   early 2030s, diversifying the demand base beyond DE weapons alone.

**Window verdict: open now, and likely to persist through the early 2030s given JLWS's FY2031
R&D horizon and 300-500 kW fielding target** -- but the practical decision point (prime buy-vs-
build) resolves on a 2026-2028 timescale, not a 2030 one, so a 2030 company launch date risks
missing the design-win window this evidence shows opening now.
