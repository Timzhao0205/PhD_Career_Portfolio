## 1. Demand

**US leg:** NVIDIA's own technical blog (2025-05-20) confirms **"full-scale production of 800 VDC data centers" is
planned to start in 2027**, coinciding with the Kyber rack-scale architecture (1 MW+ IT racks, up to 576 Rubin Ultra
GPUs/rack), with "20-plus industry partners" publicly supporting the transition (**P3R2-G-03-S01**) -- corroborating and
refreshing atlas L02-043/044. **Honest gap:** no NEW primary named-buyer procurement/commissioning-contract source was
found this session confirming a specific US commissioning firm or hyperscaler has budgeted for THIRD-PARTY acceptance
instrumentation specifically (as opposed to internal/vendor self-certification) -- consistent with the idea's own
framing that this is anticipatory demand gated on a 2029 US frame-agreement.

**CN leg -- re-sourced penetration figure (binding condition -- SATISFIED):** MIR睿工业 (a Beijing-headquartered
industrial market-research firm, established 2008, with offices in Beijing/Shanghai/Wuhan/Japan/Canada -- confirmed
non-India-origin), reporting via Huxiu (dated 2026-06-10), states China's data-center power market in 2026 is **"80%+"
AC UPS versus approximately "10%" for 800V HVDC**, with HVDC not expected to become mainstream until "after 2027-2028,"
driven by NVIDIA's roadmap (**P3R2-G-03-S02**). **This replaces the idea's own flagged-unverified "~12% HVDC/78% UPS
(~2021)" figure** with a current, dated (2026), sourced estimate that is directionally consistent (large UPS majority,
single-digit-to-low-teens HVDC minority) but now properly attributed and dated -- satisfying the binding P4 re-sourcing
requirement. Recommend the idea adopt this 2026 MIR-sourced figure in place of the previously-flagged unverified one,
with an appropriate single-research-firm precision caveat.

## 2. Competitors -- instrument-major/CN-institute map (BINDING, PRE-PROMOTION)

- **Keysight:** launched new high-power regenerative DC test products (RP5900/EL4900 series, 1.5-12 kW) in November
  2025 (**P3R2-G-03-S03**, fetched directly). These are explicitly **lab-based automated test equipment (ATE) for
  component/device validation** ("engineers validating complex, multi-kilowatt devices" in "controlled lab
  environments"), not live-site, multi-hundred-kilowatt bus commissioning/acceptance tools, and the announcement makes
  no mention of 800VDC datacenter site-acceptance applications. This **confirms** the idea's own framing: instrument
  majors are investing in adjacent high-power test capability but have not yet built a system-level site-acceptance
  protocol product.
- **Hioki:** active messaging around "continuous power validation" for datacenters (2026 seminar), directionally
  competitive but no specific site-acceptance/stability-margin product confirmed this session.
- **CQC (China Quality Certification Center):** confirmed via its own site to offer "数据中心认证及评价" (Data Center
  Certification and Evaluation) as a listed national-level service line (**P3R2-G-03-S04**, fetched directly), built on
  GB 50174/CQC 1324-2018 covering power quality, grounding, batteries, and site infrastructure broadly -- but no
  DC-bus impedance-stability or unearthed-IT-network insulation/ground-fault characterization service was found
  advertised, consistent with (does not contradict) the idea's claimed white space.
- **CEPREI:** general commissioned product/safety/EMC testing across electrical-equipment categories under
  national/international/industry standards on request; no specific data-center DC-power-system or site-acceptance
  service is advertised on its own site as fetched this session -- again consistent with, not contradicting, the
  claimed white space, though CEPREI's broad "any standard on request" mandate means it could plausibly extend into
  this space.
- **Conclusion:** the binding competitor map is populated and, on the evidence gathered this session, does **not** show
  an existing direct product clone at either the instrument-major or CN-institute level -- the "third-party-acceptance
  willingness thesis" survives this pass, though Keysight's continued high-power-ATE investment is a genuine multi-year
  absorption risk, consistent with the idea's own kill-trigger design (OCP publishing a complete acceptance-test
  standard implemented by instrument majors before 2031 -> fold to niche/license).
