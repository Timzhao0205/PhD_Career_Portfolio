# P3R2-F-17 — Demand/Competitor Evidence

Concept: Active safety-interlock modules (sub-ms beam shutoff, workpiece return-path + body-proximity
sensing) for handheld laser welding/cleaning tools, design-in for CN OEM export compliance. Primary
market: **CN only** (`us_beachhead: false`). `elegance_verdict: FIX_APPLIED (option)` — HELD AS A
PRICED OPTION, pre-company spend capped at $150k until a dated regulatory signal exists. Binding 2028
gate: one of (a) documented ISO/IEC revision work-item, (b) EU/US market-surveillance action on
handheld Class-4 welders, or (c) insurer/OEM requirement — else kill at 2030. P4 discovery lead:
chase European welding-association handheld-laser safety materials for an eligible dated anchor.

## 1. Demand

This idea is explicitly NOT yet demand-proven by design (a priced option). The task is to test
whether the 2026-07 evidence base moves any of the three 2028 gate conditions closer to satisfied.

- **Gate condition (a) — documented ISO/IEC revision work-item: SUBSTANTIALLY SATISFIED, dated.**
  `P3R2-F-17-S01` (ADH Machine Tool industry article, fetched, Anhui-CN-headquartered machine-tool
  maker's technical blog) confirms "a preliminary draft of the updated ISO/DIS 11553-2 is now in
  circulation, with the finalized version anticipated for release in 2025," describing it as "the
  first major technical overhaul in more than 15 years" of the 2007 edition (atlas `L12-050`). A
  follow-up WebSearch (not independently page-fetched this session due to repeated iso.org HTTP 403
  blocking — a known access pattern, flagged rather than fabricated) shows the standard has since
  progressed to **"ISO/FDIS 11553-2″ (Final Draft International Standard)** status at iso.org/
  standard/82411.html, i.e., beyond DIS toward final publication. Treat the FDIS-stage finding as a
  **discovery lead, not an independently fetched source** (see Section 6 caveat).
- **A second, independent, and directly fetched standards-body signal:** `P3R2-F-17-S02` (American
  Welding Society, "Setting the Standard for Handheld Laser Welding Technology," Welding Digest, Dec
  2024, fetched) — the **AWS D17 Committee has already begun drafting handheld-laser-welding
  criteria into D17.1/D17.1M:2024** (Specification for Fusion Welding for Aerospace Applications),
  explicitly to "account for the use of handheld laser welding units," expected to take "several
  years" to finalize, alongside a new AWS Safety and Health Fact Sheet No. 46 on handheld laser
  welding safety. This is a second, independently-sourced, dated (2024, ongoing into 2026+) standards
  work-item — a different body (AWS, US) and different standard than ISO 11553-2, corroborating that
  regulatory/standards tightening is a real, multi-body trend, not a single isolated draft.
- **A related but distinct US regulatory tightening (context, not gate condition (b) directly):**
  `P3R2-F-17-S01` also confirms the FDA's Laser Notice transition: **"Beginning January 1, 2025, all
  newly manufactured laser products entering the U.S. market must be certified according to the 2014
  version" of IEC 60825-1**, replacing the older FDA Laser Notice 50. This is a real, dated (Jan 2025),
  enforceable US regulatory action tightening the general laser-product safety framework — it is NOT
  specific to handheld Class-4 welders or market-surveillance enforcement against them, so it only
  partially and indirectly supports gate condition (b); it should be read as directional pressure,
  not a clean satisfaction of the named condition.
- **Gate condition (b) — EU/US market-surveillance enforcement action on handheld Class-4 welders
  specifically: STILL NOT FOUND.** A search of the EU Safety Gate (formerly RAPEX) system and general
  enforcement-news queries returned no specific recall or enforcement action against a handheld
  Class-4 laser welder/cleaner this session (negative finding, consistent with the prior judge's
  external check).
- **P4 discovery lead (European welding-association materials) — CHASED AND FOUND, but confirms the
  prior judge's characterization.** `P3R2-F-17-S03` (IIW/EWF/EWA "Considerations about Handheld Laser
  Beam Welding," brochure, european-welding.org, published July 2025) exists and is real, but its
  content is **voluntary best-practice guidance** ("general recommendations and best practice
  examples for laser safety, education, qualification"), explicitly stating "local regulations...
  must always be complied with" rather than itself creating a new dated mandate. This is a genuine,
  dated (July 2025) industry-association safety-guidance document — useful as further evidence the
  handheld-laser-safety topic is actively being worked by multiple bodies (IIW, EWF, EWA, AWS, ISO)
  simultaneously — but it does NOT independently satisfy gate condition (b) or (c) on its own.
- **Gate condition (c) — insurer/OEM requirement or CN OEM design-in LOI: NOT FOUND** this session.
  No Raycus/Han's Laser safety-interlock product announcement, partnership, or LOI was located.
- **Net demand verdict:** **the option thesis has strengthened, not weakened, this session** — TWO
  independent, dated (2024-2025), ongoing standards-committee work-items (ISO 11553-2 revision at
  FDIS stage per search evidence, and AWS D17.1 handheld-laser criteria drafting, directly fetched)
  substantively address gate condition (a), and the Jan-2025 FDA IEC 60825-1:2014 transition adds
  real (if indirect) regulatory-tightening context. Conditions (b) and (c) remain unmet. This is
  **not yet demand-proven customer evidence** (no buyer, no LOI, no enforcement action) — say
  **FAIL G1 on customer/buyer demand**, while noting the option's own trigger logic is trending
  toward satisfaction, which is a distinct and more favorable finding than a simple FAIL implies.

## 3. Price signals and arithmetic

- No public price exists for a certified sub-ms beam-disable safety module (bespoke/pre-market
  category, consistent with TRL 4 and the option structure). All figures are **explicit assumptions**:
  - **Prototype cost (idea's own figure, not independently re-sourced):** $150,000 capped pre-company
    spend, consistent with the priced-option structure.
  - **Per-unit design-in economics (assumption, wide uncertainty):** if a certified interlock module
    costs an assumption of **$200-$800 per unit** in volume (analogous to industrial safety-sensor/
    interlock BOM costs, not independently sourced this session) and CN handheld-welder unit shipments
    are in the tens of thousands per year (Raycus alone shipped "5.19万台" handheld units in 2025 per
    `L12-038`, +66.22% YoY), a fully-penetrated CN handheld-welder-market design-in could represent an
    **order-of-magnitude $10-40M/year revenue ceiling if 100% attach (assumption; realistic near-term
    attach rate is likely far lower, single-digit percent, given voluntary-only demand today)** —
    directional only, not validated by any procurement or OEM commitment.

## 4. Certification and export constraints

- **ISO 11553-2:2007** (atlas `L12-050`) remains the active base standard; a revision is in progress
  (Section 1) but not yet published — the certification target is a moving, not yet fixed, baseline.
- **IEC 60825 series** (atlas `L12-048`) — laser-product safety, Ed.3.0 2014 base + 2025 SER package,
  independently reconfirmed via the FDA's Jan-2025 IEC 60825-1:2014 adoption (`P3R2-F-17-S01`).
- **ISO 11553-1:2020** (atlas `L12-049`) covers fixed (non-handheld) laser processing machines — the
  Part 2 (handheld) gap the idea targets is real and independently reconfirmed this session.
- No export-control (EAR/ITAR) trigger identified for a sensing/interlock safety module itself;
  the constraint is regulatory/standards-compliance risk, not export licensing.

## 6. 2030 timing

1. **Dated standards-body signal #1 (search-confirmed, caveat on fetch status):** ISO/FDIS 11553-2
   (iso.org/standard/82411.html) — WebSearch results consistently describe this as the Final Draft
   International Standard stage superseding ISO 11553-2:2007, but direct WebFetch to iso.org returned
   HTTP 403 on four attempts this session (a known ISO.org bot-blocking pattern). **This is reported
   as a discovery lead corroborated by multiple independent search snippets, not as an independently
   page-fetched primary source** — it is NOT included in the source ledger as an accepted, fetched
   record, per this mission's evidentiary standard. Directionally, FDIS-stage standards typically
   publish within 1-2 years, which would land inside the idea's 2028-2032 window.
2. **Dated standards-body signal #2 (directly fetched, primary):** `P3R2-F-17-S02` (AWS Welding
   Digest, Dec 2024) — AWS D17.1 handheld-laser-welding criteria drafting "already begun," expected
   to take "several years," placing a plausible completion inside the 2027-2030 window.

**Verdict: the regulatory/standards-tightening window plausibly opens in the 2027-2030 range**,
consistent with the idea's own 2028-2032 thesis, supported now by two independent, dated
standards-body work-items (one directly fetched, one search-confirmed) rather than zero as of the
prior adjudication. This still falls short of a dated ENFORCEMENT trigger (gate condition (b)) or
a signed OEM/insurer requirement (gate condition (c)) — the option should remain priced and capped,
not promoted to a funded demand case, pending either of those two harder triggers.
