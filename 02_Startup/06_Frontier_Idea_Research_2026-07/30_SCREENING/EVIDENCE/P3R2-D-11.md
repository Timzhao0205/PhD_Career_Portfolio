# P3R2-D-11 -- Demand & Competitor Evidence

**Concept:** 2-micron drive-laser modules for next-generation EUV sources, targeting a modeled
~5% conversion-efficiency architecture that could obsolete the CO2 drive chain.
**Judge-mandated binding gate (from `fix_applied_notes`):** kill unless tin-droplet CE >=4% **at
relevant rep-rate** is demonstrated by end-2033.
**Analyst:** demand-competitor-analyst (Sonnet 5 / high effort), P4 group G07.
**Verdict headline: G1 = FAIL on the demand-trigger framing as written; the physics gate is
PARTIALLY evidenced (CE has exceeded the 4-5% bar, but only at a repetition rate ~1,000x below
what "relevant" means industrially) and the incumbent's own public roadmap currently argues
against needing this technology by 2030.**

## 1. Demand evidence -- and why this is the weakest of the five ideas

**No primary buyer/procurement source was found.** ASML/Cymer (the sole named buyer) has not
issued a tender, RFP, or procurement commitment for 2-micron drive-laser modules; there is no
company filing, budget line, or program award analogous to what was found for D-08/D-09/D-10.
The seed's own `demand_source_ids` (L12-034, L12-053) do not actually support the demand claim on
inspection: **L12-034 is IPG Photonics' FY2025 10-K** (a fiber-laser competitive-dynamics filing
unrelated to EUV source roadmaps), and **L12-053 is a Gigaphoton product-line/vendor-datasheet
page** (source_type `vendor_datasheet`, demand_evidence_type `none` in the atlas record itself) --
neither is buyer/procurement evidence. **This is exactly the kind of vendor-claim-mistaken-for-
demand pattern CLAUDE.md rule 5 and the source standards warn against; flagging it plainly rather
than repeating the seed's citation.**

**What this session found instead, refreshed for 2026, argues against urgency:** ASML's own lead
EUV-source technologist (Michael Purvis, quoted via Electronics Weekly, fetched directly,
[P3R2-D-11-S01]) states ASML has already **demonstrated a 1,000W EUV source using the existing
CO2-driven architecture** (doubling droplet rate to 100,000/s with a two/three-pulse shaping
scheme) for delivery by ~2030, and sees **"a reasonably clear path toward 1,500 watts, and no
fundamental reason why we couldn't get to 2,000 watts"** -- all without switching drive
wavelength. This directly contradicts the seed's `demand_trigger_2030_2034` framing that "EUV
source power roadmap pressure beyond the 1,000W milestone" creates urgency for a 2-micron switch:
**the incumbent's own stated near-term roadmap solves the power problem with CO2, not 2-micron.**

**The one real (non-vendor) demand-adjacent signal:** DOE Office of Science's Microelectronics
Science Research Centers (MSRC) program, part of the CHIPS and Science Act, is funding a
**$12M/4-year** LLNL-led project (fetched directly, [P3R2-D-11-S02], llnl.gov) explicitly to test
whether the "BAT laser" (thulium-doped YLF, ~2-micron) can raise EUV source efficiency ~10x vs.
CO2 -- **with ASML San Diego named as a collaborating partner**, alongside SLAC and ARCNL
(Netherlands). This is real: a national lab, government-funded, multi-year program with the actual
buyer as a named technical partner. It is **not, however, a procurement commitment** -- it is
government-funded exploratory R&D that ASML is participating in as a hedge, consistent with a
large incumbent keeping options open, not evidence that ASML intends to buy a 2-micron drive
system by any date. This is the honest, correctly-labeled version of the demand case: **a funded
government research hedge exists; a buyer commitment does not.**

## 2. The physics gate, evaluated explicitly and honestly

The judge-mandated gate requires **CE >=4% at relevant rep-rate by end-2033**. This session
located the underlying peer-reviewed literature directly:

- **Mostafa, Behnke, Engels, Bouza, Sheil, Ubachs, Versolato (ARCNL/Vrije Universiteit Amsterdam),
  "Production of 13.5 nm light with 5% conversion efficiency from 2 um laser-driven tin
  microdroplet plasma," Applied Physics Letters 123, 234101 (2023), with a 2024 erratum**
  (PDF retrieved directly this session, [P3R2-D-11-S03]; peer-reviewed, DOI 10.1063/5.0173303
  per AIP indexing found via search) -- **already demonstrates 5.0% in-band EUV conversion
  efficiency from a 2-micron-driven, preshaped tin-microdroplet target**, i.e. above both the
  seed's original 2028 kill-gate (CE>=3%) and the judge's stricter CE>=4% bar.
- **The critical caveat, found via targeted search cross-referencing this exact paper: the 2-micron
  main pulse in this experiment ran at 10 Hz repetition rate.** Industrial EUV sources (ASML's
  own CO2 system) run tin-droplet generators at **~50,000-100,000 droplets/second** ([P3R2-D-11-S01]
  confirms the 100,000/s droplet rate for the 1,000W CO2 system). **10 Hz is roughly three to four
  orders of magnitude below "relevant rep-rate" for a kilowatt-class industrial source.** The
  judge's gate wording ("at relevant rep-rate") is precisely targeting this gap, and on the
  evidence available this session, **it has not been met**: the CE number is real and
  peer-reviewed, but it was measured in a regime (single-shot-adjacent, thermally unloaded target)
  that does not resolve whether CE holds up at the rep-rate, average power, and debris/target-
  replenishment conditions an industrial source requires. A separate IOPscience paper found this
  session ("Modeling the hundreds-of-nanoseconds-long irradiation of tin droplets with a 2
  um-wavelength laser for future EUV lithography," search-identified but not independently
  fetched this session -- discovery lead) suggests the field itself is still at the modeling stage
  for extending these results toward relevant conditions.

**Honest verdict on the gate: PARTIALLY MET.** The CE>=4% *number* has been exceeded in a
peer-reviewed lab result, which is more physics progress than a skeptic might assume going in --
but the "at relevant rep-rate" qualifier, which is doing real work in the judge's gate language,
is not satisfied by any source found this session. The gate should be read as **open but
unresolved**, not passed and not conclusively failed; the 2026-2029 precompany plan's own
"droplet-interaction test service quantifying CE and debris" is correctly targeting exactly this
unresolved rep-rate question, which is appropriate given what this session found.

## 3. Competitors

- **US/incumbent drive-chain supplier:** TRUMPF (Germany, atlas [L12-039]) supplies the CO2 drive
  lasers ASML/Cymer currently uses; TRUMPF's FY2024/25 revenue fell ~17% to EUR 4.3B with Laser
  Technology division down to EUR 1.2B, a soft year but not existential distress.
- **National-lab/government 2-micron research competitor-collaborator:** LLNL's BAT laser program
  ([P3R2-D-11-S02]) is simultaneously the closest thing to a "competitor" (a well-funded, ASML-
  partnered alternative R&D path a startup would compete against for mindshare/resources) and a
  potential co-development partner or exit path, consistent with the seed's own "acqui-license"
  framing.
- **Japan (side market):** Gigaphoton (Komatsu Group) -- atlas [L12-053] -- continues DUV/EUV
  light-source R&D and presented at SPIE Advanced Lithography + Patterning 2026 on hydrogen-
  etching/collector-mirror-reflectance management (search-derived patent-filing coverage,
  [P3R2-D-11 context], not independently fetched this session) -- Gigaphoton remains a plausible
  second buyer but, like ASML, has issued no procurement commitment for 2-micron drive modules.
- **No China-based EUV source competitor is relevant** because of export controls (see Section 4)
  -- China cannot legally buy EUV tools at all, so there is no China-side competitor or buyer to
  assess for this specific product category.

## 4. Certification and export constraints (2030 launch) -- decisive for the China verdict

- EUV lithography equipment has been **export-restricted from China since the first Trump
  administration**; ASML has never shipped an EUV machine to China (confirmed via direct fetch of
  a Motley Fool article covering ASML's own Q1 2026 disclosures, [P3R2-D-11-S04]). The proposed
  **MATCH Act** (April 2026) would extend restrictions to DUV immersion systems and even
  **servicing of already-installed equipment in China**, and ASML guided **China to ~20% of 2026
  revenue** (down from 33% in 2025; Q1 2026 China system sales fell to 19% from 36% in Q4 2025 per
  the same source). Most analysts cited estimate China is "at least a decade away from independent
  EUV capability."
- **Implication for this idea specifically:** a 2-micron EUV drive-laser module is, by definition,
  a component of an export-controlled EUV lithography system. There is effectively **no legal
  China market for this product at all**, now or through 2030-2034 -- this is not a "China
  beachhead: false" judgment call like the other ideas, it is a hard export-control wall.

## 5. Primary-market verdicts

- **US: demand NOT proven at the commitment level; only a government-funded research hedge
  exists.** ASML/Cymer is reachable (it is a known, identifiable buyer with a US operating site,
  ASML San Diego, named in the LLNL partnership), but there is no tender, LOI, or procurement
  signal, and the incumbent's own public roadmap statement argues the near-term power problem is
  already solved without switching drive wavelength. **G1 verdict for the demand leg specifically:
  FAIL** -- this should not be scored as having met the primary-buyer-evidence bar the mission
  requires; the honest evidence is a technical option, not a demand signal.
- **China: not reachable, not applicable.** Export controls foreclose this market entirely; do not
  score China as even a theoretical secondary opportunity for this specific idea.
- **Window:** The seed's own framing ("if the CO2 chain saturates, the incumbent must buy or build
  2-micron capability on exactly our timeline") is now contradicted by the incumbent's own 2026
  public statements about a clear CO2 path to 2,000W. The window may not open by 2030 at all; it
  is contingent on CO2 scaling hitting a wall that ASML's own technologist currently says he does
  not see.

## 6. 2030 timing sources (two independent, >=1 primary/official 2028-2035 trigger)

1. **Primary/official:** DOE MSRC/LLNL BAT-laser award ([P3R2-D-11-S02]) -- a federally funded,
   4-year (through ~2028-2029) research program with ASML San Diego as a named partner, testing
   the 2-micron hypothesis. This is the one legitimate official-source trigger inside the mission's
   window, but note it is a **research-timeline** trigger (does the physics work by ~2029), not a
   **procurement-timeline** trigger (will ASML buy a commercial system by 2030-2034) -- the two
   should not be conflated.
2. **Independent second source:** ASML's own public roadmap statement of a 1,500W-2,000W CO2 path
   ([P3R2-D-11-S01]) is itself a "timing source" in the sense that it dates when the incumbent
   currently expects to need (or not need) an architecture change -- and it currently reads as a
   **negative** trigger for this idea's timing thesis, i.e. it is evidence the window may **not**
   open by the 2030-2034 range the mission requires, not evidence that it will.

**Window verdict: uncertain-to-closed by 2030, contingent on a CO2 scaling failure that has not
occurred and is not currently forecast by the incumbent itself.** This is consistent with the
seed's own honest self-description as "the best-disciplined moonshot... an option purchase, not a
company plan" and with `current_trl: 2`. The evidence gathered this session supports treating
this idea exactly as the seed's fix_applied_notes intended: a capped-capital, gate-limited
research bet, not a 2030 company-launch candidate, and the demand leg specifically should be
marked FAIL pending an actual ASML/Gigaphoton procurement or LOI signal that does not currently
exist.
