# P3R2-F-15 — Demand/Competitor Evidence

Concept: Multi-stack DC distribution and per-stack management skids (isolation, current balancing,
hot-swap, stray-current control) for 100+ MW alkaline electrolyzer parks in China — the layer
between the rectifier and dozens of stacks. Primary market: **CN only** (`us_beachhead: false`).
`elegance_verdict: FIX_APPLIED` with a binding P4 requirement: evidence that a licensed JV can win
park-management retrofit scope at one named SOE park (the adjudication note states no such tender
exists yet and the SOE-audit-pressure demand trigger is inferential). This file tests that
requirement, refreshes the Sungrow/LONGi verticalization kill signal, and checks CN 15th-FYP timing.

## 1. Demand

**CN leg (the only claimed leg):**
- **Named buyers re-checked:** Sinopec Kuqa (`L11-049`), China Energy Construction's 125-set 2023
  procurement (`L11-051`), LONGi/Peric overseas contracts (`L11-050`) — all already atlas-verified,
  `verified_non_india_origin`, and still describe electrolyzer-stack procurement, not a per-stack
  DC-distribution/isolation/hot-swap product line.
- **Primary market-structure finding (new, this session):** `P3R2-F-15-S05` (jgvogel.cn trade-press
  aggregator of CN electrolyzer tenders, fetched) — a March 7, 2025 China Energy Construction
  (中能建) tender at Shijiazhuang Luquan District (7,000 Nm3/h, 44.15 MW PV-coupled) bundles
  "碱性电解槽制氢装置、**电源**、球形储罐、往复式压缩机..." (alkaline electrolyzer units, **power
  supplies**, spherical tanks, compressors) as ONE procurement package under the EPC/owner, not as
  a separable BOP-electrical line item. Cross-checked against seven other 2024-25 tenders in the
  same aggregator (State Power Investment, CNPC, Sinopec, Dalian Institute of Chemical Physics):
  only this one explicitly bundles power/distribution equipment with the stacks; the rest procure
  stacks alone. **This directly confirms the idea's own gap analysis** (no dedicated third-party
  DC-distribution-skid tender exists) but also shows the default market structure routes power
  electronics through the same EPC/rectifier-vendor procurement lot, which is a structural headwind
  to an independent BOP vendor winning scope, not just an absence of demand signal.
- **P4-required evidence check (licensed JV winning retrofit scope at a named SOE park): NOT
  FOUND this session.** No tender, RFP, or JV announcement for park-level per-stack DC-distribution,
  isolation, hot-swap, or stray-current retrofit work at Kuqa, the CEC 125-set sites, or any other
  named CN park was located. `P3R2-F-15-S04` (Xinhua, fetched, 2024-08-27) instead shows Sinopec
  built its OWN in-house "green-electricity flexible hydrogen production advanced control system"
  for "full-factory intelligent control" at Kuqa — i.e., the SOE's stated preference so far is
  proprietary in-house systems integration, not third-party BOP vendors, which is a demand-access
  risk beyond the "no tender yet" framing already in the record.
- **Verdict: FAIL G1 (CN leg) as independently evidenced buyer demand this session.** The painful
  job (utilization loss, stray current, park-level electrical management) remains plausible and
  technically grounded (atlas `L11-012/013`), and the named buyers are real, large-scale, growing
  procurers of electrolyzer *stacks* — but no primary buyer/procurement source for the specific
  per-stack DC-distribution/management product exists, and the one directly relevant new finding
  (bundled EPC procurement + in-house SOE control-system preference) points toward a harder, not
  easier, market-entry path than the idea record's framing assumed.

## 2. Competitors

  `P3R2-F-15-S01` (Sungrow official news, fetched, 2024-12-26) — Sungrow has already shipped the
  **SMS1000 electrolyzer digital management system** ("全景监控、安全防控、五维健康管理" — panoramic

## 3. Price signals and arithmetic

- No public per-unit price exists for a dedicated per-stack DC-distribution/management skid (bespoke
  category, consistent with Section 1's finding that it is not yet a standalone procurement line).
  All figures below are **explicit assumptions with wide uncertainty**:
  - **Bundled power/distribution-equipment share of a park build (assumption):** if power/distribution
    equipment (transformer, rectifier, distribution cabinets, controls) is historically ~10-20% of an
    electrolyzer-park capex stack (industry-typical rough split for renewable-coupled BOP, not sourced
    this session — assumption), and a park at Kuqa scale (260 MW, `L11-049`) costs on the order of
    several hundred million USD all-in (assumption, not independently priced this session), the
    power-electronics-adjacent scope per large park could be **$10-50M — wide uncertainty, no direct
    source**; the isolation/balancing/hot-swap SKU specifically (not the whole rectifier package)
    would be a fraction of that, plausibly **$1-5M per 100+ MW park (assumption)**.
  - **Utilization-gain economics (the idea's own binding gate):** the idea requires >=3% utilization/
    specific-energy gain to justify skid cost. `L11-049`/this session's refresh both confirm Kuqa-
    scale parks run well below nameplate at times (safe-operation range constraints), so the physical
    basis for a utilization gain is plausible, but no independent study quantifying the specific
    dollar value of a 3% gain at a named park was located this session — remains an assumption.
  - **Installed-base scaling (assumption):** CN alkaline electrolyzer nameline capacity may exceed
    50 GW/year by 2026 per general industry commentary (not independently fetched this session,
    discovery-only); if even a low single-digit percentage of new large (>=100MW) parks adopted a
    third-party DC-management retrofit at the $1-5M assumption above, the addressable TAM is
    **order-of-magnitude $10-100M/year by the early 2030s — directional only, not validated.**

## 4. Certification and export constraints

- No laser/nuclear/military dual-use export-control trigger identified for DC distribution/switchgear
  hardware itself; this is industrial power-electronics equipment, not inherently EAR/ITAR-controlled.
- The real constraint is **market-access structure, not export control**: CN big-science/SOE hydrogen
  parks procure through bundled EPC lots (Section 1) and, per `P3R2-F-15-S04`, the flagship SOE site
  favors in-house systems-integration control software — a foreign or JV vendor's practical entry
  route is licensed technology transfer to a domestic manufacturing partner, not direct sale, which
  the idea's own precompany plan already assumes ("licensed JV").
- No IEC/IEEE standard specific to per-stack DC-distribution/isolation skids for electrolyzer parks
  was identified this session (consistent with the idea's white-space framing).

## 6. 2030 timing

1. **Primary/official, CN, 2021-2035 trigger:** `P3R2-F-15-S09` (National Development and Reform
   Commission / National Energy Administration, "Hydrogen Industry Development Medium-and-Long-Term
   Plan (2021-2035)," ndrc.gov.cn, fetched) — a directly government-issued planning document (issued
   2022-03-23 by NDRC's Advanced Technology Division) whose title and scope confirm China's hydrogen
   industry is under an official, phased national plan extending through 2035; the landing page
   confirms issuing authority and date but this session could not extract the granular numeric
   2025/2030/2035 targets from the linked PDF/OFD (repeated PDF-binary extraction failures this
   session) — cited for its authoritative existence/date/scope, not for specific figures.
2. **Independent, CN, trade-press corroboration:** `P3R2-F-15-S02` (H2TECH/Gulf Energy Information,
   Houston TX-headquartered, fetched) plus `P3R2-F-15-S03` (Sina Finance, fetched, published
   2026-03-16) both independently confirm the subsequently-released 15th Five-Year Plan (2026-2030,
   released 2026-03-13) explicitly commits to "布局发展绿色氢氨醇" (layout and develop green
   hydrogen/ammonia/methanol) as part of new-type energy-system construction — general on hydrogen
   specifics but a second, independent, dated confirmation that the 2022 long-term plan's hydrogen
   commitment is being carried forward into the current five-year cycle.

**Verdict: the policy window for CN green-hydrogen fleet build-out opens/persists through 2030-2032
consistent with the idea's own thesis (mid-life underperformance reviews on the 2023-27 fleet)**, but
this session found no independent evidence that a park-management retrofit *procurement* window has
opened yet — the timing thesis rests on inference from the broader policy commitment, not a dated
SOE audit-and-retrofit program. Continue tracking as the idea's own 2028 EPC-partnership gate
requires, rather than treating the 2030-32 window as already open for this specific product.
