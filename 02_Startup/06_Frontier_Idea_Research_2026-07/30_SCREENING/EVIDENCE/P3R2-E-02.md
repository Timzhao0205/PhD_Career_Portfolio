## 0. Framing note on demand at TRL3/precompany stage

This idea is TRL3 and precompany. No one has bought the specific product (a fluid-agnostic vapor-quality sensor + MPC controller) because it does not exist yet. ## 1. Demand

**Painful job / regulatory trigger (primary, official):** EPA AIM Act phasedown (atlas L14-033), refreshed via **P3R2-E-02-S01** (Hunton Andrews Kurth, 2026): consumption allowance drops from 181,522,990 MTEVe (2026-2028) to 90,761,495 MTEVe (2029-2033) -- confirming the ~50% 2029 step-down is still scheduled. **New risk, not in the seed:** industry litigation against the Technology Transitions Rule is "pending in the D.C. Circuit," and EPA proposed an October 2025 reconsideration adjusting GWP thresholds effective Jan 1, 2026 -- the regulatory trigger this idea leans on is under active legal contest, not settled law.

**Named-buyer/second demand source:** Vertiv 10-K liquid-cooling backlog, $15B (atlas L14-048, company filing) -- real capital commitment to liquid cooling broadly. **P3R2-E-02-S04** (Data Center Dynamics, Meta/OCP): confirms Meta is expanding liquid cooling via Open Rack V3 / AALC, but **this source does NOT confirm two-phase specifically** -- Meta's disclosed near-term path is single-phase/AALC. This is an honest gap: I found no hyperscaler procurement document naming two-phase cooling (as opposed to liquid cooling generally) as a locked-in 2026-2028 buy.

**Market-timing corroboration:** **P3R2-E-02-S03** (Dell'Oro Group): liquid-cooling manufacturer revenue ~$3B (2025) -> ~$7B (2029); two-phase "expected to expand gradually, with adoption accelerating once chip-level TDPs and thermal flux exceed the practical limits of single-phase systems" -- directly matches the idea's own painful-job framing, and implies two-phase remains a minority, still-emerging segment through the late 2020s.

**Verdict on demand:** the underlying problem (PFAS/HFC fluid transition colliding with rising heat flux) is real and dated. Demand for the specific merchant control product is **unproven** -- consistent with, not contradicting, the idea's own 2028 FIX gate.

## 4. Certification and export constraints

Low relative to the other four ideas in this batch. No BIS Entity List exposure (no CN customers targeted). Likely certification path is UL/ETL electrical safety and ASHRAE/OCP interoperability specs (cold-plate, UQD connector) rather than export control. EPA SNAP-program fluid-listing requirements apply to fluid producers, not directly to a controls-only vendor, but the control system would need to be qualified against whichever SNAP-listed low-GWP fluids customers select -- a design-validation burden, not a legal blocker.

## 6. 2030 timing (two independent sources, window verdict)

1. **Primary/official:** EPA AIM Act phasedown schedule (atlas L14-033, refreshed by **S01**) -- 2029 step-down from 60% to 30% of HFC baseline, dated 2029-2033, but now flagged as under litigation/reconsideration risk.
2. **Independent official:** EU F-gas Regulation (EU) 2024/573 (atlas L14-034) -- separate jurisdiction, separate instrument, with its own 2027-2030 HFC restriction milestones.

**Window verdict:** Opens ~2028-2029 if both regulatory schedules hold, converging with rising chip-level heat flux (per Dell'Oro, S03). **Genuinely at risk of slipping** if the AIM Act litigation succeeds or EPA's reconsideration softens the 2029 step (new finding, not in the original seed). Even if the regulatory trigger holds, the more decisive uncertainty is commercial (buy vs. build), gated by the idea's own 2028 checkpoint -- this file finds no new evidence resolving that question either way.
