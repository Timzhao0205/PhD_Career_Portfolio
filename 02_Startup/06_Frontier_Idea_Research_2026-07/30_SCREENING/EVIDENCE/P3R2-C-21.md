# P3R2-C-21 — Demand/Competitor Evidence

Concept: Direct-DC renewable-to-datacenter coupling converters (multiport MVDC blocks, PV/BESS
straight into 800VDC campuses). Primary market: **US only** — elegance judge downgraded the CN leg
to license-out-only (Sungrow/Huawei-class incumbents make direct CN entry non-credible;
`china_beachhead: false`). This file verifies (a) the US case on its own merits and (b) whether the
CN licensing-out route is realistic, per the assignment's binding condition. It does not treat CN as
a beachhead, market-size input, or buyer path.

## 1. Demand

**US leg (primary + independent second source):**
- ARPA-E DC-GRIDS program (atlas `L02-034`, Ohio State ECE reporting, fetched, accepted,
  verified_non_india_origin): $35M across 12 projects developing MT-HVDC converter technology,
  explicitly framed around data-center-driven demand; a March 2026 $2.5M award to Virginia Tech
  (WebSearch-corroborated only, not independently re-fetched this session) is the most recent
  tranche. This is an ARPA-E (DOE) program — a primary federal R&D-demand signal, not a customer
  order — appropriately typed as R&D-stage demand, not procurement.
- **Independent second source, buyer-side and dated 2025-2026:** `P3R2-C-21-S01` (Data Center
  Frontier, 2025-10-15, fetched) confirms Eaton unveiled an 800VDC reference architecture at OCP
  Global Summit 2025 (Q1 2026 white paper planned) and ABB announced an NVIDIA collaboration
  targeting "1-megawatt racks and multi-gigawatt campuses," with NVIDIA's own roadmap targeting
  "~2027" for 800V DC deployment. This is vendor/ecosystem-side confirmation that MVDC/800VDC
  conversion hardware is an active, funded buildout, not a speculative future market — though **no
  named datacenter-operator deployment commitment was found in this article**, so it corroborates
  ecosystem momentum, not a signed customer PO.
- Context (not counted as primary demand, but supports the painful-job framing): Google/Fervo 24/7
  clean-power partnership (atlas `L04-050`, fetched, accepted) establishes the hyperscaler pattern of
  directly financing/co-locating generation with data-center load that C-21's product sits inside of.
  Delta/Alibaba's 2019 HVDC "Panama Power" precedent (atlas `L02-048`) shows the rack-to-facility HVDC
  architecture lineage is a decade old in China, though that specific deployment is AC-fed HVDC to a
  240/400VDC bus, not renewable-DC-to-campus coupling.

**Verdict, US: demand is real (funded federal R&D program + two independent 2025-2026 vendor/
ecosystem confirmations) but still pre-order — no named US campus/IPP has committed to a
DC-coupling pilot as of this session. This matches the idea record's own FIX gate ("2028: a named
US campus/IPP DC-coupling pilot commitment; absent it, re-batch the seed") — the gate is not yet
cleared and should be tracked as an open condition, not assumed satisfied.**

**CN leg (license-out only — verifying realism, not beachhead demand):**
- Two independent, dated, primary CN government documents confirm a real policy lane: atlas
  `L14-036` (国能发科技〔2026〕34号, AI-energy action plan, 2026-04-08, fetched, accepted) and
  `P3R2-C-21-S02` (发改能源〔2026〕688号, green-power direct-connection notice, 2026-05-14, fetched
  this session) — the latter explicitly prioritizes "算力设施" (computing facilities) for green-power
  direct connection and sets a dated compliance ramp (self-consumption ≥35% of total electricity
  "2030年前", i.e., before 2030). This is genuine, current, official CN demand-side policy, not a
  stale precedent.
- However, this CN demand is **structurally captured by incumbents**, not addressable by a new
  entrant: Sungrow and Huawei are the dominant Chinese power-electronics/inverter majors positioned to
  fill exactly this policy lane (idea record's own framing), and per `P3R2-C-21-S03` (Utility Dive,
  2025-05-15, fetched) both Huawei and Sungrow are simultaneously the subject of active US national-
  security scrutiny (undocumented "rogue" communication devices found in Chinese-made inverters/
  battery systems; Huawei has been under a US federal equipment prohibition since 2019). This cuts
  two ways: it confirms the CN incumbent moat is real and hard (license-out is the only credible
  route), while also confirming a US-side tailwind (de-risking from Chinese inverter/BESS suppliers)
  that favors a US-native competitor in the US leg.

**Verdict, CN: demand-side policy is real and current, but the licensing-out framing is the correct
call — direct CN beachhead entry would compete head-on with Sungrow/Huawei-class vertically
integrated giants inside their home market and their most policy-favored segment. Realistic outcome
is, at best, a technology-license deal with a CN partner for the protection-coordination IP, not a
standalone CN sales motion.**

  general-purpose standard that would apply, per prior-session atlas context in this lane; not
  re-fetched this session).
- **Export/compliance check performed per mission rule (BIS Entity List / VEU status of named CN
  customers):**
  - Huawei: **confirmed on the BIS Entity List** since 2019, presumption-of-denial license policy,
    actively enforced as of June 2026 (Bosch settlement over unlicensed Huawei-affiliate shipments,
    per WebSearch this session, not independently fetched) — `P3R2-C-21-S03`.
  - Sungrow: **no match found** in an OpenSanctions BIS-Entity-List-dataset search this session
    (`P3R2-C-21-S04`) — inconclusive, not a clearance, and in active US congressional/DHS scrutiny
    (Republican Study Committee press release on Chinese solar-inverter security risk, referenced via
    WebSearch this session but the RSC page itself returned HTTP 403 on direct fetch and is therefore
    NOT counted as fetched evidence here).
  - **Conclusion: the CN licensing-out structure is the only defensible route.** Any structure
    touching Huawei-affiliated entities requires a BIS license under a presumption-of-denial policy;
    Sungrow's status is unresolved but the underlying inverter/BESS hardware category it and Huawei
    both sell into is under active US national-security review, which is exactly the kind of
    counterparty risk that should gate any CN engagement through export counsel before signing,
    consistent with the idea record's own "flags lowered accordingly" framing.

## 5. US/CN primary-market verdicts

- **US: demand is directionally real (funded ARPA-E program + two independent 2025-2026 vendor/
  ecosystem confirmations naming NVIDIA/Eaton/ABB momentum) but not yet proven at the customer level
  — no named US campus/IPP pilot commitment exists yet. Buyer reachable in principle (EPCs,
  datacenter developers, renewable IPPs are identifiable, addressable US counterparties) but the
  idea's own 2028 FIX gate is the correct discipline: treat as UNCERTAIN pending that named pilot,
  not PASS.**
- **CN: demand-side policy is real and current (two independent 2026 government documents), but
  buyer access is not real for a new entrant — the segment is structurally owned by Sungrow/Huawei-
  class vertically integrated incumbents, reinforced by active US-side security scrutiny of exactly
  those two companies. License-out-only is the correct, and only credible, structure. FAIL as a CN
  beachhead; PASS only as a licensing chapter, contingent on export-counsel clearance.**

## 6. 2030 timing

1. **Primary/official, CN, explicit 2030 trigger:** `P3R2-C-21-S02` (发改能源〔2026〕688号,
   2026-05-14) sets a dated compliance ramp requiring self-generated/self-consumed electricity to
   reach ≥35% of total consumption "2030年前" (before 2030) for green-power-direct-connection
   projects, with computing facilities named as a priority category — an explicit, official,
   government-set 2030 deadline.
2. **Independent, US, near-term trigger:** `P3R2-C-21-S01` (Data Center Frontier, 2025-10-15) reports
   NVIDIA's own roadmap targeting "~2027" for 800V DC deployment, with Eaton's detailed white paper
   due Q1 2026 and ABB/NVIDIA collaboration already underway — an independent, dated, near-term
   trigger that opens the window before 2028.

**Verdict: the window opens before 2030 on both legs (US ecosystem buildout ~2027, CN policy
compliance deadline 2030) but the US leg's opportunity is time-limited by incumbent shelf-vendor
speed (Eaton/ABB/GE Vernova/Delta are all already moving) and gated on the 2028 named-pilot FIX
condition; the CN leg's window is real for incumbents but effectively closed to a new US entrant
except via licensing. Net: window OPENS before 2030 for a US-native player if the 2028 pilot gate is
cleared; otherwise this idea should be re-batched per its own fix_applied_notes.**
