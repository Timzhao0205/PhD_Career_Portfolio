# P4 Evidence: P3R2-C-03 -- Medium-voltage solid-state transformer power block (2.4MW, 10-35kV to 800VDC)

Analyst: demand-competitor-analyst | Model/effort: claude-sonnet-5 / high | Date: 2026-07-13

Binding condition carried from P3 (fix_applied_notes): **2028 HARD GATE** -- demonstrable IEC 60076-24-class
standard progress (committee draft or later) PLUS an OCP SST procurement signal (spec revision or
hyperscaler RFI naming SST power units); if either is absent, re-batch to the wildcard pool and do not raise

## 1. Demand (per market leg)

### US leg
- **Primary:** Atlas L02-044 (OCP Mount Diablo working group, Google/Meta/Microsoft + Eaton/ABB/Siemens,
  `trade_press`, T2) -- confirms buyer-side (hyperscaler) co-authorship of the DC-power roadmap this idea
  targets. New source **P3R2-C-03-S03** (Enphase/OCP platinum-member press release, fetched, T1
  `company_filing`): Enphase joined OCP on 2026-06-30 specifically "to help advance AI data center power
  standards" for ±400VDC/800VDC architectures, contributing its own IQ SST product -- confirms the OCP SST
  standards track is live and drawing public-company commitment.
- **Independent second:** Atlas L02-034 (ARPA-E DC-GRIDS program, government-adjacent) plus the competitor
  evidence in Section 2 below (Heron Power's 40GW customer pipeline, ~1/3 datacenter) is itself strong
  *demand* evidence even though Heron is a competitor -- a buyer base that size would not form for a
  fictitious need.
- **Verdict: demand is real** for MV-to-800VDC conversion generally. **However, the specific *procurement
  signal* the binding gate requires -- "spec revision or hyperscaler RFI naming SST power units" -- was
  NOT independently confirmed this session.** Enphase's OCP membership is vendor-side participation, not a
  hyperscaler RFI. Half of the 2028 hard gate's demand-side condition is therefore **not yet met** as of
  this evidence pass.

### CN leg

## 2. Competitors (critical finding: the field is far more crowded than the idea record states)

The idea's `competition_outlook_2030` names only "1-2 ARPA-E-spun startups." This session found at least
**four** funded, named competitors already building MV-to-LVDC/800VDC solid-state transformer products for
datacenters, ahead of any IEC-certification-first strategy:

- **Heron Power (US)** -- new source **P3R2-C-03-S01** (TechCrunch, fetched): Heron Link, 5MW/unit,
  34.5kV AC direct to 800VDC at >98.5% efficiency (per search-corroborated spec), 30s onboard battery
  backup; **$178M total raised** ($38M Series A 2025 + $140M Series B Feb 2026); **>40GW customer pipeline**
  (~1/3 datacenter, rest solar/BESS); planned 40GW/yr factory, pilot production early 2027. This is a
  near-exact match to C-03's proposed 2.4MW-class MV-to-800VDC block, moving on a commercial (not
  certification-gated) timeline roughly two years ahead of C-03's earliest possible product (2029-2031 per
  its own precompany plan).
- **Hyperscale Power (CH/DE)** -- new source **P3R2-C-03-S02** (TechCrunch, fetched): €5M seed (March
  2026), ETH Zürich-derived HF-SST technology, explicitly targeting datacenter MV-to-LVDC conversion.
- **Enphase Energy (US)** -- S03 above: IQ SST product line, now inside the OCP standards process itself.
- **SolarEdge and Navitas** -- named in search results (not independently fetched this session; discovery
  only) as also preparing 2-5MW-class SST products for 2027-2028 delivery.
- Named incumbents: GE Vernova, Eaton, Schneider, ABB, Siemens (systems-integration scale, per NVIDIA
  ecosystem post and OCP working group); Huawei/Delta China domestically in CN.

**This is a material red-team finding**: C-03's core differentiation thesis ("certification is the
blocker, not physics") is directly challenged by Heron Power's commercial pace, which is not waiting for
IEC certification and already has a customer pipeline an order of magnitude larger than C-03's $25-60M v1
plan implies is achievable pre-certification.

## 4. Certification and export constraints

- **IEC 60076-24 status (critical finding):** New source **P3R2-C-03-S04** (IEC Webstore, fetched):
  IEC 60076-24:2020 is a **published** standard (Edition 1.0, 2020-07-09) with a **stability date of 2029**
  (i.e., not scheduled for revision), and its scope is **voltage-regulating distribution transformers**
  (25kVA-3,150kVA, up to 36kV) -- a different device class from the MV solid-state transformer /
  high-frequency insulation-coordination problem the idea describes. A targeted search this session found
  **no IEC TC14 (or other) new work item** specifically addressing SST high-frequency insulation
  coordination. **As written, the binding 2028 hard gate's first named condition ("demonstrable IEC
  60076-24-class standard progress") has no qualifying instrument to show progress on, because the named
  standard is not under revision and does not cover the technical problem.** This should be corrected before
  2028 -- either by identifying the actual relevant standards effort (if one exists, e.g., an IEC/IEEE
  SST-specific committee) or by acknowledging that the certification-gap thesis rests on atlas source
  L02-036's characterization, which this session's independent IEC-registry check does not confirm.
- Export separability: MV SiC power stacks to China may face licensing review under general power-
  electronics/dual-use rules; not independently verified against current BIS text this session (same caveat
  as C-02, Section 4). The idea's own plan to build a CN entity using domestic SiC is a reasonable mitigant
  if the concern materializes, but should be verified before the 2029 CN LOI conversation.

## 5. Primary-market verdicts

- **US:** Demand for MV-to-800VDC conversion is real, but the specific standards+RFI signal the 2028 gate
  requires is **not yet observed**, and the competitive field is materially more advanced than the idea
  record assumes (Heron Power alone: $178M raised, 40GW pipeline, pilot production 2027). **Recommend
  treating the 2028 hard gate as likely to fail on current evidence** unless a hyperscaler RFI specifically
  naming SST power units appears before then -- which would argue for re-batching to the wildcard pool per
  the seed's own rule, or for radically re-scoping the differentiation away from "certification-first" and
  toward something Heron/Hyperscale/Enphase are not yet doing.
- **CN:** Demand is directionally real (800V HVDC adoption is happening, per ByteDance/Alibaba evidence) but
  no CN-specific MV-SST product category or buyer signal distinct from the general 800V trend was found
  this session.

## 6. 2030 timing (two independent sources)

1. **Primary/official:** Atlas L14-036 / NEA notice (April 2026, government, T1): 2030 target for
   "world-leading" clean-energy supply capacity for AI computing facilities in China -- general, not
   SST-specific.
2. **Industry:** New source S01 (Heron Power, TechCrunch, Feb 2026): Heron's own commercial timeline --
   pilot production early 2027, ramping over "the following two years," i.e., volume production by
   **~2029** -- a competitor's timeline functions here as an independent timing signal for when the market
   window is likely to be substantially occupied.

**Verdict:** the underlying MV-to-800VDC market window is real and opens in the 2027-2029 period, but on
current evidence it looks more likely to be **captured by already-funded competitors before 2030** than to
remain open for a certification-first entrant following C-03's precompany plan (first field pilot not until
2028-2029, product not until 2029-2031). This does not necessarily kill the seed's HF-insulation/
standards-seat research track (which is cheap and publishable regardless), but it substantially weakens the
case for committing the $25-60M v1 capital plan on the current thesis, and reinforces rather than
contradicts the 2028 hard-gate logic the seed already carries.
