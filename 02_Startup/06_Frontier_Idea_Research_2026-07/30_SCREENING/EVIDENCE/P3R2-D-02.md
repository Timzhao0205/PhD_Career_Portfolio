# P3R2-D-02 — Demand/Competitor Evidence

Concept: Reel-to-reel contactless REBCO tape quality metrology (Hall-array Ic mapping +
delamination screening) sold to tape vendors and magnet builders as certified acceptance
instrumentation. Primary market: **US+CN dual**, `china_beachhead: true` already carried by the
elegance judge — this file verifies both legs independently. Frontier wildcard: TRL 4, judged on
timing-contract and buyer-path honesty.

## 1. Demand

  corroboration, not independently fetched beyond what atlas already covers) and the named merchant
  magnet sales (atlas `L03-035`, Realta Fusion/U. Wisconsin/Type One Fusion) establish CFS as a live,
  large-volume US tape/magnet buyer.

  confirmation that fusion-scale tape volumes are being contracted now, not just researched.
- Atlas `L03-032` (DOE Milestone Program) and `L03-030` (US ITER central-solenoid completion) provide
  the official/primary US procurement backdrop that tape-QA demand rides on.

**CN leg (primary buyer-side contract + independent official/technical corroboration):**
- `P3R2-D-02-S02` (Eastmoney Caifuhao, 2026-05-22, fetched, zh): **Shanghai Superconductor is the
  exclusive HTS-tape supplier for the CAS/CNNC HL-4 (环流四号) tokamak, under a signed RMB 1.6 billion
  long-term contract** covering ~3,000km of tape for 2025-2027 delivery — a named, dated, quantified
  CN buyer-procurement-grade transaction, independent of the atlas.
- Independent second CN source: atlas `L03-041` (State Grid Shanghai, 2021, fetched, accepted,
  `buyer_procurement`) — a named grid-operator buyer commissioning the world's first kilometer-class
  HTS cable — plus atlas `L03-031` (ITER Organization, confirming Western Superconducting
  Technologies/WST supplied ~65% of ITER's NbTi strand) and `L03-029`/`L03-037` (ASIPP's 35.1T magnet,
  peer-reviewed + national-lab-confirmed) collectively establish that China has multiple named,
  independent tape/conductor buyers (State Grid, ASIPP, WST's own customer base) beyond the single
  HL-4 contract.
- Capacity-growth corroboration: `P3R2-D-02-S02` also reports Shanghai Superconductor's capacity
  trajectory — 4,000km/yr achieved (2025), +6,000km/yr under construction (2027-28), 20,000km/yr
  long-term target (2030) — directly supporting the idea's own `launch_2030_timing_thesis` ("tape
  volume triples-to-10x between 2026 and the early-2030s").

**Verdict: demand is proven on both legs with primary + independent second sources.** US: named
merchant magnet/tape transactions (CFS/Realta/U.Wisconsin, Furukawa/SuperPower-Tokamak Energy) plus
official federal/ITER procurement. CN: a named, quantified, dated exclusive-supply contract (Shanghai
Superconductor/HL-4) plus independent corroboration from a state-owned grid buyer and ITER-linked
conductor supplier (WST).

## 2. Competitors

- **THEVA** (`P3R2-D-02-S01`, fetched): confirms the idea record's own "THEVA Tapestar-class —
  judgment" competitor claim as a **verified, named, real product** — TAPESTAR is a non-contact
  critical-current scanning instrument used by "many laboratories and all well-known tape producers."
  Critically, THEVA is **both a tape producer and the instrument vendor** — i.e., the closest existing
  competitor is a captive in-house tool sold by one tape maker to others, not an independent
  buyer-trusted third-party acceptance-metrology vendor. This directly confirms, rather than merely
  assumes, D-02's "no neutral referee" wedge.
- Lake Shore Cryotronics-class lab Hall-sensor/temperature-sensor vendors supply components (Hall
  probes, Cernox sensors) used in ad hoc lab setups, not an integrated reel-to-reel line-speed
  acceptance system — confirmed as component-only, not systems-competitive, this session.
- Vendor in-house scanners at tape producers (THEVA's own TapeStar, and by extension any in-house
  tooling at Shanghai Superconductor, Shanghai Shangchuang, Fujikura/Furukawa) are the real
  competitive set — **no independent, merchant, buyer-trusted acceptance-metrology vendor selling
  across multiple tape producers and magnet builders was identified this session**, consistent with
  the idea record's claim.

## 3. Price signals and arithmetic

- No direct price was found for a reel-to-reel Hall-array + thermal-imaging acceptance scanner
  (expected at TRL 4 pre-commercial stage).
- **Bottom-up arithmetic (assumptions labeled):**
  - Global REBCO tape supply is concentrated in "fewer than 10" vendors (idea record's own framing,
    not independently re-counted this session, but consistent with Shanghai Superconductor being
    described as "one of only two" producers at >1,000km/yr scale per `P3R2-D-02-S02`).
  - Shanghai Superconductor alone plans to scale from 4,000km/yr (2025) to 20,000km/yr (2030) —
    **assumption:** if a reel-to-reel acceptance scanner costs on the order of $0.5-2M per line
    (unverified, anchored loosely on general precision-metrology capital-equipment scale, not sourced
    to this specific instrument class) and each major tape producer needs 1-3 lines to cover its
    capacity, the **global tape-vendor-side hardware TAM is plausibly in the $10-50M range by
    2030 — wide uncertainty, no per-unit price source confirms this.**
  - Separately, magnet-builder-side acceptance stations (incoming-goods inspection at CFS, Tokamak
    Energy/TE Magnetics, ASIPP) would add an unquantified number of additional units — **not sized
    this session.**

## 4. Certification and export constraints

- Atlas `L03-053` (IEC 61788 series, superconductivity critical-current test methods, TC/SC 90) is
  the directly relevant standards family for this idea's core measurement claim — **but this atlas
  record is marked `accepted: false` / `fetched: false`** (all fetch routes failed in the prior
  session: IEC.ch 403, Sumitomo PDF unreadable, ScienceDirect 403). This session did not
  independently re-fetch it either. **This is a real gap: the specific IEC 61788 standard that would
  govern acceptance-test comparability across vendors/buyers has not been independently confirmed as
  fetched/live by anyone on this project to date** — flag for a future session with IEC webstore
  access.
- **Export/compliance check on named CN customers/suppliers:** Western Superconducting Technologies
  (WST) and ASIPP were checked against BIS Entity List search tools this session; **no confirmed
  match was found** (WebSearch of Federal Register/BIS pages found other Xi'an-based firms added to
  the Entity List in March 2025 for military-civil-fusion-adjacent reasons, but did not confirm WST by
  name; a full Supplement No. 4 text fetch was attempted and failed due to size/access limits both
  times). **This should be independently re-verified via the official BIS Entity List (bis.gov) or
  Consolidated Screening List before any WST- or ASIPP-linked transaction**, per mission rule 4 — this
  session's negative finding is inconclusive, not a clearance.
- Shanghai Superconductor and Shanghai Shangchuang were not checked against the Entity List this
  session (not named CN customers in the idea record's own buyer list beyond WST/ASIPP, though the
  new `P3R2-D-02-S02` source makes Shanghai Superconductor a de facto named counterparty — this gap
  should be closed in a follow-up session).
