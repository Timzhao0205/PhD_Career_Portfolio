# P3R2-F-22 -- Second-source vacuum variable capacitors and fast RF tuning components (Comet-monopoly layer), US+CN

Analyst: demand-competitor-analyst (Sonnet 5, high effort). Primary market: **US+CN dual** claimed (`us_beachhead: true`,
`china_beachhead: true`). Secondary: JP. Non-advocacy analysis; citations to atlas IDs and new `P3R2-F-22-S0x` IDs.
**Headline finding: the CN leg's "own the tuning-component layer" white-space claim is directly contradicted by an
already-scaled, fast-growing, named domestic competitor (Guoli, 688103.SH) selling into the exact same buyer set.**

## 1. Demand

**CN leg:** Real, dated, quantified demand for vacuum capacitors in Chinese semiconductor RF matching exists -- but it is
already substantially captured by a domestic incumbent, not open white space. Guoli Electronics (Kunshan Guoli Electronics
Technology Co., 688103.SH) reports semiconductor vacuum-capacitor segment revenue of **RMB 73.0M in H1 2025 (+81.18% YoY)**
at **41.88% gross margin (2024 figure)** (**P3R2-F-22-S02**), and its own disclosure states its vacuum capacitors for
etch/thin-film-deposition RF matchers are "directly comparable to Swiss Comet and Japanese Meidensha," already integrated
into Huachuang (北方华创/NAURA) and Zhongwei (中微/AMEC)-adjacent lines, with **"supply insufficient for demand"**
(供不应求) (**P3R2-F-22-S03**). Guoli also markets vacuum capacitors, hydrogen thyratrons, and klystrons directly into
China's controlled-fusion program (**P3R2-F-22-S01**), showing a second, adjacent CN demand channel.

Independent second CN source: Hengyunchang's own STAR-Market IPO filing coverage (**P3R2-F-22-S04**) confirms it supplies
RF power systems to Tuojing, Zhongwei, NAURA, Weidao Nano, and ACM Shanghai, with China's semiconductor RF-power-system
localization rate **below 7% in 2023** (rising toward the seed's own cited <12% in 2024, atlas L06-054) -- but this figure
is for RF power systems broadly, not vacuum capacitors specifically, and Guoli's growth shows the capacitor sub-layer is
being domestically substituted *faster* than the RF-system layer as a whole.

**Honest gap:** no customer-side (NAURA/AMEC-issued) procurement document naming vacuum-capacitor purchases was found;
all CN demand evidence here is vendor-disclosed (Guoli's, Hengyunchang's own regulatory filings), which is a stronger
evidentiary class than marketing copy (audited/regulator-reviewed revenue disclosure) but still one step removed from a
buyer's own procurement record.

**US leg: FAIL on primary buyer evidence.** No named US/EU OEM procurement document, RFQ, or qualification program
committing to a second vacuum-capacitor source was found. Generic sole/limited-source supplier risk-factor language
exists in RF-equipment-maker 10-Ks (MKS Instruments, Advanced Energy) per public secondary reporting, but SEC.gov blocked
direct fetch this session (403 errors on repeated attempts) -- flagged as an honest access gap rather than claimed as
fetched evidence. No company names vacuum capacitors specifically.

## 2. Competitors

- **CH:** Comet Group / Comet PCT (Flamatt, Switzerland) -- dominant incumbent; 2026 outlook is bullish on semiconductor
  upswing (AI, memory, advanced packaging, GAA/2nm transition) (**P3R2-F-22-S05**). Vacuum-capacitor manufacturing is
  concentrated in Flamatt, Switzerland (capacity repeatedly expanded); RF-match assembly has moved from San Jose, USA to
  Penang, Malaysia (**P3R2-F-22-S06**) -- i.e., **no US-based Comet manufacturing capacity for either product**, which is
  the underlying condition a US-based second source would exploit, though no buyer has yet acted on it.
- **US:** Jennings Technology -- legacy vacuum-capacitor line still catalogued/distributed (via multiple US RF-parts
  distributors and an ABB-hosted technical library), but current manufacturing ownership/investment status is unclear
  from public sources and was not independently resolved this session.
- **CN:** **Guoli (688103.SH)** -- direct, already-scaled domestic competitor, explicitly benchmarked against Comet and
  Meidensha, serving the same NAURA/AMEC-adjacent buyer set F-22 names as its CN target, and capacity-constrained
  (evidence of real pull-through demand, but also evidence the niche is already occupied).
- **JP:** Meidensha -- named directly by Guoli itself as a comparison benchmark (**P3R2-F-22-S03**).
- Net: this is a **crowded, not white-space**, competitive landscape on both legs.

## 3. Price signals and bottom-up arithmetic

- Guoli's H1 2025 semiconductor-vacuum-capacitor-segment revenue of RMB 73.0M annualizes to roughly **RMB ~150M
  (~US$20M)/year at ONE domestic competitor alone**, growing 81% YoY (**P3R2-F-22-S02**, source, not assumption).
- **ASSUMPTION, wide uncertainty:** the seed's own bottom-up CN estimate ($15M-$200M addressable range, built from an
  assumed 3,000-8,000 replacement units/year at $5K-$25K/unit, no direct unit-count source) should be revised toward its
  **lower** bound, or further discounted, given a credible domestic incumbent already captures a meaningful, fast-growing
  share of exactly this pool -- a new entrant (foreign or licensed) would be fighting for residual/overflow demand
  ("supply insufficient for demand" cuts both ways: it signals unmet demand, but Guoli is actively expanding into it).
- US-side bottom-up arithmetic could not be constructed: no buyer-disclosed volume, price, or qualification-spend figure
  was found for a hypothetical second vacuum-capacitor source.

## 4. Certification and export constraints

Neither Hengyunchang nor Sichuan Injet Electric (the idea's two named CN buyer-channel counterparties) appear in
OpenSanctions' aggregated sanctions/export-control datasets (**P3R2-F-22-S09**, **P3R2-F-22-S10**) -- clean, negative
finding as of 2026-07-13. The US Section 232 semiconductor tariff (effective 2026-01-15, 25% ad valorem) targets a
narrow category of advanced computing chips/derivative products under specific HTS logic-IC subheadings
(**P3R2-F-22-S07**); its published scope does **not** explicitly name passive RF components such as vacuum capacitors,
so it should not be over-claimed as a direct trigger for this product line, though it evidences a live US
reshoring/tariff-policy environment that a Swiss/Malaysian-manufactured Comet product could eventually be swept into if
scope expands.

## 5. US / CN verdicts

- **US: FAIL G1 (no primary buyer evidence).** Real underlying RF-power-and-matching market exists and is growing
  (Comet's own bullish 2026 outlook), but no named buyer has committed to, or even publicly signaled intent toward, a
  second vacuum-capacitor source.
- **CN: UNCERTAIN, materially downgraded from the seed's framing.** Real, dated, quantified demand exists, but the
  seed's central "own the tuning-component layer" CN white-space claim is directly contradicted by Guoli, a named,
  already-scaled, capacity-constrained domestic competitor selling into the identical buyer set. A licensed/JV entrant
  would be competing against an incumbent with an 81%-YoY growth trajectory and home-market advantages, not filling an
  empty niche.

## 6. 2030 timing (two independent sources, window verdict)

1. **Primary/official (CN):** Xinhua, China's 15th Five-Year Plan (2026-2030), published 2026-03-05
   (**P3R2-F-22-S08**) -- commits to "decisive breakthroughs across full chains" in integrated circuits and greater
   technological self-reliance through 2030.
2. **Primary/official (US):** White House Section 232 semiconductor-tariff proclamation, effective 2026-01-15
   (**P3R2-F-22-S07**) -- confirms an active US industrial-policy/tariff lever aimed at semiconductor supply chains,
   though not yet confirmed to cover this specific component class.

**Window verdict:** The CN window may be **narrowing faster than 2030**, not opening -- Guoli's 81%-YoY growth suggests
the domestic vacuum-capacitor substitution wave could mature or become heavily contested well before a new entrant's
2028-2029 qualification timeline (the idea's own binding 2027 manufacturing-partner-route gate and 2029 paid-OEM-
qualification gate). The US window's status is simply unproven: no buyer signal exists either way. Recommend the P4
binding condition ("map existing CN vacuum-capacitor makers... credible domestic caps collapse the CN licensing leg") be
read as **triggered** by this research -- Guoli is exactly that credible domestic cap, and the CN licensing leg should be
treated as substantially collapsed pending a specific rebuttal (e.g., a demonstrated performance/spec gap Guoli cannot
close, or a named buyer who explicitly wants a non-Guoli, non-Comet alternative).
