# P3R2-F-06 — Demand/Competitor Evidence

Concept: Precision wideband DC current/voltage sensing platform (optical + zero-flux hybrid) for
HVDC converter stations, electrolyzer plants, and magnet systems. **Primary market was downgraded by
the elegance judge from US+CN to US-only** ("HONESTY FLAG CHANGE... State Grid domestic-only, CN
revenue 'license royalties at best'"); `china_beachhead: false`. Per assignment instructions, this
file validates the **US leg on its own merits** and notes CN licensing realism only — CN is NOT
treated as a beachhead, market-size input, or buyer path.

## 1. Demand

**US leg (primary + independent second source):**
- **Primary/official, dated 2028-2035 trigger:** `P3R2-F-06-S02` (Pattern Energy, fetched) confirms
  Southern Spirit Transmission: a ~3,000MW, ±525kV HVDC line (~320 miles, Texas/Louisiana/Mississippi),
  "over $2.6 billion" privately funded, with "target full construction start: 2029" and "target
  in-service date: 2032." As of the fetched page, the project is in "field study, right-of-way
  acquisition, and permitting phase" — i.e., real, funded, dated, but pre-construction. This is the
  single named project generating the wideband DC-sensing demand F-06's `demand_trigger_2030_2034`
  cites.
- **Independent second source, near-term regulatory-compliance trigger:** `P3R2-F-06-S03` (NAES,
  fetched) confirms NERC PRC-029-1 "will take effect on October 1, 2026, for Bulk Electric System
  (BES) facilities," and PRC-028-1 "provides a phased approach... full compliance required by January
  1, 2030" — both are dated, official (NERC reliability-standard) compliance triggers requiring
  inverter-based-resource (and, per the idea's framing, broader grid-asset) instrumentation/protection
  upgrades, independent of the Southern Spirit project itself.
- **Verdict, US demand: directionally real but pre-construction/pre-order.** One large, named, funded,
  dated HVDC project (Southern Spirit) plus a dated, independent regulatory-compliance wave (NERC
  PRC-028/029) both point to genuine measurement-layer demand materializing through 2029-2032 — but
  **no named converter-OEM or EPC has placed an order for wideband DC sensing hardware** as of this
  session; this matches the idea's own `current_trl: 5` and 2029 binding-gate framing exactly (a
  protection-OEM/EPC evaluation agreement is the stated binding gate, not yet cleared).

**CN chapter (license-royalty upside only — not a market leg, per binding downgrade):**
- `P3R2-F-06-S04` (Yicai, fetched, 2026-05-07) confirms State Grid's 2026 UHV equipment tender: Xuji
  Electric (许继电气) won "直流输电相关产品" (DC-transmission-related products) worth a combined
  ¥1,274,703,500 (~¥12.75亿, ~$176M at ~7.25 CNY/USD), split ~¥1.171B for converter valves (换流阀) and
  ~¥104M for DC control-protection systems (直流控制保护系统); broader search context (not
  independently re-fetched) put the full 2026 State Grid UHV round at ~¥49.94B (~$6.9B) across 12
  packages and 7 winning enterprises, with Xuji and NR Electric-affiliated bidders splitting most of
  the converter-valve share. This confirms State Grid procurement is real, large, and recurring — but
  it is domestic-supplier-only procurement (Xuji, NR/南瑞), consistent with the idea's own "State Grid
  procurement is domestic" framing and the elegance judge's downgrade. **This session found no
  evidence contradicting the license-royalty-only characterization; CN is correctly excluded as a
  market leg and is noted here only as licensing-realism context, per the assignment's binding
  condition.**

## 3. Price signals and arithmetic

  sensing points (converter valve hall + line + protection redundancy, no direct source for this
  count), and each sensing point/transducer costs **assumption: $50,000-250,000** (generic HVDC
  instrumentation-transducer cost band, no direct source this session), one project's sensing-layer
  content is on the order of **$0.5-7.5M — a directional estimate only.** Scaling to a handful of
  similar US interregional HVDC projects reaching construction in the 2029-2032 window (Southern
  Spirit plus DOE Transmission Facilitation Program-adjacent projects, not independently counted this
  session) could put **cumulative US addressable sensing-layer demand in the low-tens-of-millions of
  dollars through 2032 — illustrative only, not validated.**

## 4. Certification and export constraints

- **US leg:** no export-control issue applies (US-side HVDC project, US/European component suppliers).
  The relevant "certification" hurdle is technical/performance qualification (the idea's own planned
  "accuracy/bandwidth round-robin published with a national lab") rather than a regulatory license —
  no such round-robin has occurred yet per this session's research.
- **CN chapter (licensing realism, not a market leg):** any license-out structure would need to route
  through, or license to, Xuji/NR-class State Grid-approved domestic suppliers, given State Grid
  procurement is domestic-only per `S04`'s own tender structure (winners are named CN firms, not
  international bidders) — this independently confirms the idea's own framing that direct CN sales
  are not realistic and a technology-license deal with a domestic partner is the only plausible CN
  structure, consistent with treating CN as royalty-upside-only.
- **NERC compliance wave (US, dated):** `P3R2-F-06-S03` confirms PRC-029-1 (Oct 1 2026 BES deadline)
  and PRC-028-1 (full compliance Jan 1 2030) are binding NERC reliability standards, not optional
  guidance — generator/transmission owners face real compliance deadlines that create measurement/
  protection-upgrade demand independent of any single HVDC project.
