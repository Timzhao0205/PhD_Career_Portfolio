## 4. Certification and export constraints (material finding)

**P3R2-E-04-S01** (Miller & Chevalier, on BIS's September 5, 2024 interim final rule): new ECCNs govern quantum-computing and cryogenic-cooling items -- notably **3A904** (cryogenic cooling systems delivering >=600uW at <=0.1K for 48+ hours), plus related cryo-CMOS/technology ECCNs. Critically, **licenses are required even for close US allies**: "Licenses will be required even for Australia, Canada, and the U.K." License Exception IEC is broadly available only for Italy and the UK; Japan qualifies "for certain ECCNs" only. **Taiwan and South Korea are not named as broadly eligible.**

**This is a material, previously unflagged risk:** E-04's own precompany plan calls for 2028-29 pilot work with SEEQC-ITRI (Taiwan) and reference/testbed engagement in Japan and Korea. Under the September 2024 rule, technology transfer of the cryo-CMOS mux + LNA readout chain to Taiwanese or Korean partners will likely require case-by-case BIS export licenses, adding real lead time the seed's timeline did not price in. There is also deemed-export exposure during the 2026-27 academic phase if any non-US-person researchers work on the cryo-CMOS/mux design.

No BIS Entity List issue for the US leg itself (no CN customer targeted).

## 5. US / CN verdicts

- **US: PASS.** Demand is real, dated, and government-anchored (DOE $625M + Fermilab-Qblox + pending NQI reauthorization); buyers are reachable via published DOE/national-lab programs. The open risk is technical/competitive (see below), not demand existence.
- **CN: N/A by design**, and correctly so -- China's cryo-hardware ecosystem (Origin Quantum/QuantumCTek, 80%+ domestic content) is both a competitive barrier and, per the new September 2024 export-control rule (S01), a jurisdiction where US-origin cryo-CMOS/cryogenic-cooling technology transfer would face presumption-of-denial-type scrutiny in any case.

## 6. 2030 timing (two independent sources, window verdict)

1. **Official/primary:** DOE $625M QIS Centers program (atlas L13-028), a multi-year federal program running through the DOE centers' award cycles into the early 2030s.
2. **Independent (industry roadmap):** **P3R2-E-04-S06** (IBM Quantum Starling/Blue Jay roadmap) -- Starling targets ~200 logical/~20,000 physical qubits by **2029**; Blue Jay targets ~2,000 logical/~100,000 physical qubits **beyond 2033**.

**Window verdict:** Opens as DOE center renewal cycles and >1,000-physical-qubit systems proliferate 2028-2032; genuinely at risk of narrowing for at least IBM's architecture specifically, since **IBM's own scaling path is modular chip-to-chip quantum links (Cockatoo, 2027) rather than more external wiring channels per qubit** -- a concrete, dated instance of the "on-chip multiplexing / optical cryo-links could leapfrog external harness density" risk the idea's own record already flagged, now with a named vendor and date attached. Combined with SEEQC's IPO-stage commercial traction and IBM/NVIDIA collaboration pipeline, the white space E-04 targets is real but narrower and more contested by 2028-2029 than the original seed assumed.
