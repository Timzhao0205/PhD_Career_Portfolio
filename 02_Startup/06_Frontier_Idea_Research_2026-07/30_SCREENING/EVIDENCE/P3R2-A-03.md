## Gate evaluation (decisive for this idea)
I located and fetched a written regional-entity compliance-guidance document: **Texas RE's "PRC-028-1 & PRC-029-1: Implementation Plans & Data Submittals" (NSRF presentation, March 26, 2026)** [P3R2-A-03-S01], a primary regional-entity (T1) source squarely matching what the gate asked for. Its compliance-evidence framework is **model submission (EMT/dynamic models) validated against actual disturbance-event data collected via PRC-028-1 monitoring equipment** -- not field MW-hardware disturbance-injection testing. This is independently corroborated by two further sources:
- HSI's compliance blog [P3R2-A-03-S02] names the actual compliance hardware being bought: Schweitzer Engineering Laboratories (SEL) Intelligent Electronic Devices, SEL-3350/3555 Real Time Automation Controllers, and SEL-2407/2488 satellite-synchronized clocks -- **passive sequence-of-events/fault/dynamic-disturbance recorders**, not MW-scale emulators.
- Elevate Energy (founded by a former NERC Director of Engineering) [P3R2-A-03-S03] describes two explicit pathways: "Design Compliance" (EMT ride-through simulation) and "Operational Compliance" (IBR plants "ride through actual grid events, leveraging the disturbance monitoring equipment and measurements from NERC PRC-028-1") -- i.e., verification is performed by watching real grid disturbances as they naturally occur through installed recorders, not by injecting an artificial MW-scale fault with a trailerized test unit.
No source found (government, regional-entity, or owner-operator) affirmatively requires or pays for field MW-hardware disturbance injection at plant sites for PRC-029-1 compliance. **The gate's kill condition is met: model-based compliance, validated with passive monitoring data, appears sufficient.**

## 1. Demand
**Primary regulatory trigger (real, T1):** NERC PRC-029-1 / FERC Order 909 (atlas L08-043) -- approved July 24 2025, effective Oct 1 2026 (BES IBRs) / Jan 1 2027 (non-BES). This is a genuine, dated compliance mandate.
**But the mandate's actual purchased-hardware demand flows elsewhere:** to (a) EMT-modeling/compliance-consulting services (Keentel, EPE, Elevate, gridstrong.ai -- an entire consulting cottage industry already exists), and (b) passive disturbance-monitoring hardware under the companion PRC-028-1 standard (SEL products named above; PRC-028-1 already in effect since April 1 2025, compliance dates 50% by 4/1/2028, 100% by 1/1/2030). No evidence of demand for a field-deployable MW-scale fault-injection/ride-through-verification trailer was found.
**Independent second source:** none supporting the standalone-hardware thesis was found despite a targeted search for IEEE-2800 field-commissioning practice; IEEE 2800-2022 commissioning verification is described as "type tests, design evaluation, EMT modeling, as-built evaluation, commissioning tests, and post-commissioning validation" -- commissioning tests reference protective-function and power-quality checks, not full MW-scale ride-through fault injection at the plant.
**Verdict: FAIL G1 for the standalone field-MW-hardware product thesis.** The regulatory trigger is real, but the specific product this idea proposes to sell does not have documented buyer demand; the money flows to modeling/consulting services and passive monitoring recorders instead.
