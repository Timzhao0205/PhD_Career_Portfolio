## 1. Demand

**PRIMARY buyer/procurement source:** SDA's own draft solicitation for the Tranche 3 Transport Layer Upsilon (T3TLu)
variant — ~40 space vehicles across four orbital planes, split between two vendor teams, "subject to available
funding" [new:P3R2-A-13-S01]. This is a first-party government procurement document naming a real, quantified buyer
requirement.

**Independent second demand source:** Payload Space's reporting on the SDA's ~500-spacecraft order and the
industry-wide thruster supply crunch — Redwire CTO Al Tadros cites demand of "100 [thrusters] a month" across the
industry, with Redwire/Phase Four targeting 300 units/year and Astra's supply disruption having cost Terran Orbital
$13M [new:P3R2-A-13-S05]. This independently corroborates real, currently-supply-constrained EP demand from a named
government buyer via named integrators, distinct from the SDA solicitation itself.

**Reinforcing, buyer-behavior evidence:** York Space Systems (NYSE:YSS, SDA Tranche 1 Transport Layer prime contractor,
post-$629M Jan-2026 IPO) acquired thruster-maker Orbion Space Technology in March 2026, after Orbion delivered 33
Aurora Hall-effect thrusters to York on Jan 13, 2026 for an SDA mission; Orbion is scaling to "hundreds of units
annually" [new:P3R2-A-13-S04]. This shows a named SDA prime is not merely buying thrusters/PPUs on the open market but
is now **vertically integrating** propulsion supply — both confirming demand is real and confirming the idea's own
named risk ("primes may vertically integrate PPUs") is already materializing.

Historical NASA precedent (AEPS $67M, NEXT-C $18.4M) remains in the atlas and is credible government demand-pattern
evidence [atlas:L09-034, L09-012]. Note: NASA's Gateway program (the PPE built on this AEPS/PPU lineage) was reported
paused in March 2026 with hardware potentially repurposed toward a nuclear-electric-propulsion concept (SR-1 Freedom);
this was not independently re-fetched this session (two fetch attempts on primary trade coverage were rate-limited/
blocked) and should be treated as directional context, not load-bearing — it does not change the AEPS/NEXT-C precedent
value as historical demand pattern evidence, but it does mean that specific program should not be relied on as a
still-active 2027-2030 pipeline without a fresh check.

**Verdict: G1 — PASS, with a caveat.** Real, named, government-anchored EP demand exists today (SDA constellations,
NASA heritage), but the largest forward-looking volume driver cited in the seed (Tranche 3/4/5 proliferation) is
currently budget-uncertain, not contracted. Treat 2030-2033 volume as directionally likely but not guaranteed at the
scale assumed.

## 2. Competitors

**United States:**
- **Rocketdyne (formerly L3Harris Space Propulsion and Power Systems)** — AE Industrial Partners is acquiring a 60%
  stake for $845M, explicitly to refocus L3Harris on missiles; the unit (RL-10, in-space electric thrusters, space
  power systems, nuclear-propulsion R&D) is being spun back out as a standalone, PE-backed "Rocketdyne," closing 2H2026
  [new:P3R2-A-13-S03]. This **updates and weakens** the idea's founding premise that the flagship incumbent is inert
  inside a missile-focused conglomerate — a re-capitalized, refocused Rocketdyne is a stronger 2027-2030 competitor
  than assumed.
- **York Space Systems / Orbion Space Technology** — newly vertically integrated (thruster-level; PPU integration risk
  by extension) SDA prime [new:P3R2-A-13-S04].
- **Busek** — capacity-constrained incumbent thruster/PPU house [atlas:L09-009, L09-053]; DoD-funded 10kW GaN-PPU
  SBIR work underway (Phase I award, 2024).
- **EPC Space** — rad-hard GaN component supplier (not a full PPU integrator), atlas-confirmed [atlas:L09-054, L09-055];
  its 300V EPC7030MSH part is a plausible BOM component for a competing or partner PPU design.
- **Redwire / Phase Four** — new entrant thruster partnership targeting 300 units/year, DARPA VLEO-linked
  [new:P3R2-A-13-S05].
- **Rocket Lab** — vertically integrated Hall thruster + GaN PPU (Gauss thruster), explicitly marketed as
  "ITAR/EAR-free," atlas-confirmed [atlas:L09-114].

**China:** State-led "PPU industry" with private-sector participation — CASC's 502 Institute and the Shanghai
Institute of Space Power Sources lead national programs; private firms Aozhan/Aotian Technology, Starchain Space, and
Yidong Aerospace integrate or co-develop PPUs; "core high-voltage power devices and control chips remain
import-dependent" [new:P3R2-A-13-S07]. China is not an addressable market or credible competitor-into-the-US given
export-control separation in both directions; relevant only as background technology-race context, not as a market or
buyer per the binding geography scope.

**Japan/Taiwan/Korea:** JAXA-Furukawa J-SPARC GaN Hall-thruster commercialization (Japan) and KARI full-EP GEO design
studies (Korea) remain valid secondary-market/partner leads per atlas [atlas:L09-042, L09-019] — unchanged this
session.

## 3. Price signals, installed base, and bottom-up arithmetic

- **Device-level price signal:** EPC Space's EPC7030MSH 300V rad-hard GaN FET: $349/unit (space-qualified) or
  $236/unit (engineering model) at 500-unit quantities; two devices support a 5kW power stage
  [new:P3R2-A-13-S08].
- **Bottom-up arithmetic (labeled assumptions, wide uncertainty):**
  - SDA-class constellation-scale EP demand: ~100 thrusters/month industry-wide today per named executive
    [new:P3R2-A-13-S05] → ~1,200 thruster-and-PPU sets/year at current run rate (fact, not assumption, but a
    point-in-time snapshot that could rise or fall with Tranche 3 funding).
  - **Assumption:** a modular 2-20kW PPU BOM (GaN devices + magnetics + control + rad-hard qualification overhead)
    prices in the $50k-$150k range per unit at low-rate production, based on analogy to historical PPU program costs
    (AEPS ~$67M/36 months for a handful of qualification units plus NRE [atlas:L09-034]) scaled down for a merchant,
    catalog-built unit rather than a bespoke flight program. Uncertainty: +/-2x; no direct per-unit PPU price was
    found this session (PPU pricing is typically bundled into prime contracts, not published).
  - At ~1,200 units/year industry-wide and a $50k-$150k per-unit price assumption, the addressable SDA-adjacent PPU
    market is **very roughly $60M-$180M/year at current (constrained, Tranche-1/2-driven) run rate** — before any
    Tranche 3+ ramp, which is currently unfunded (§1). This is a rough order-of-magnitude sanity check, not a
    validated TAM; no market-research report was used for this figure per this engagement's sourcing rules (several
    "LEO satellite market" reports returned in search are excluded as low-confidence, largely India-origin-adjacent
    aggregator reports — see rejected sources below).
  - **Installed-base color:** proliferated LEO/MEO constellation buildout is real and ongoing (York alone delivered 21
    Tranche-1 satellites within hours of a single launch, per [new:P3R2-A-13-S04]), but the electric-propulsion
    fraction of that buildout specific to the idea's addressable niche (2-20kW modular PPUs, not integrated thruster
    units) is not separately quantified in any source found this session.

## 4. Certification and export constraints for a 2030 launch

- US space-related exports remain split between ITAR (USMLCategory IV/XV) and EAR; Oct 2024 reforms removed license
  requirements for Australia/Canada/UK on certain spacecraft items and created a new "Commercial Space Activities"
  license exception, but this does not extend blanket relief to electric-propulsion power electronics generally
  [new:P3R2-A-13-S06]. A US GaN-PPU company should expect ITAR/EAR licensing overhead for most non-Five-Eyes
  customers — a real cost and cycle-time tax against "ITAR/EAR-free" competitors like Rocket Lab's Gauss thruster.
- Interface standards: AIAA S-122 and ECSS-E-ST-20-20C remain the relevant qualification frameworks per the idea's own
  framing; no change found this session.
- Radiation qualification (SEE/TID dossiers) is the real technical gate, not a regulatory one; DoD/NASA heavy-ion test
  facility access is a de-risking dependency already reflected in the idea's precompany plan.

## 5. Primary-market verdicts

- **US verdict:** Demand is REAL and buyer-reachable (SDA is a public-solicitation government buyer; NASA heritage
  programs are a matter of public record). The **window is real but narrower and more contested** than the 2026 seed
  assumed: Tranche 3 funding uncertainty [new:P3R2-A-13-S02] and rapid prime-level vertical integration (York/Orbion,
  and a re-capitalized Rocketdyne) both compress the merchant-PPU opportunity. A founder pursuing this idea should
  treat 2026-2028 as a genuine derisking window but should not assume the SDA volume ramp is secured.
- **CN verdict:** Not a market for this idea (china_beachhead: false, correctly assigned). China has a real, if
  import-dependent, domestic PPU industry [new:P3R2-A-13-S07] but is neither an addressable customer (export controls
  cut both ways) nor a source of components. No change to this exclusion is warranted.

## 6. 2030 timing

**Timing source 1 (primary/official, trigger inside 2028-2035 window, but presents downside risk):** SDA's own
Tranche 3 Transport Layer Upsilon solicitation named ~40 satellites and a two-vendor award structure, originally
targeting late-2028 launches [new:P3R2-A-13-S01], but the FY2026 defense budget request "zeroes out funding for
Tranche 3" and SDA has paused solicitations "until further budgetary guidance," pushing the timeline past 2028 with no
firm restoration date [new:P3R2-A-13-S02].

**Timing source 2 (independent, industry-behavior-based):** York Space Systems' acquisition of Orbion (March 2026),
explicitly timed "ahead of SDA Tranche 2 missions," with production scaling to hundreds of thruster units/year by
Q3 2026 [new:P3R2-A-13-S04] — shows real, near-term (2026-2027) EP demand execution independent of the contested
Tranche 3 line.

**Verdict: window OPENS in the near term (2026-2028, Tranche 1/2-driven) but its PERSISTENCE through 2030-2034 at the
scale the idea needs is genuinely uncertain**, contingent on (a) Tranche 3+ funding being restored, and (b) the
merchant PPU segment surviving prime-level vertical integration. Recommend the founder treat "SDA volume from 2030"
as a monitored assumption to be re-verified in 2027-2028, not a locked-in trigger.
