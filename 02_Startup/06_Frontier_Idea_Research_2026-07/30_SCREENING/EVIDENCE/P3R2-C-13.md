# P3R2-C-13 — Demand/Competitor Evidence

Concept: Precision GaN pump-driver and laser-power modules — nanosecond-class programmable current
drive as a merchant subsystem for industrial and defense-adjacent laser OEMs. Primary market: US+CN
(dual). **Binding pre-promotion conditions (`fix_applied_notes`)**: (1) a 2028 gate requiring OEM
buy-vs-build discovery to convert into two PAID evaluations (one US, one CN commercial), else fold
into P3R2-D-10; (2) a day-one structural requirement to run US(DEW)/commercial as two separate
entities with no CN ownership links on the DEW line. **This session's research produces a
significant, concrete finding directly relevant to both conditions: the seed's named CN buyer
example, Wuhan Raycus, is confirmed on the BIS Entity List.**

  the seed's number should be reconciled against its original atlas source (`L12-031/032`) before

  reconciled number.
- Q1 2026: nLIGHT revenue $80.18M (+55% YoY), record aerospace/defense activity, 2026 full-year

**Verdict: US demand is real, dated, and strongly primary (nLIGHT's own JLWS/HELSI-2/DE M-SHORAD
contracts). CN demand (Raycus/Han's Laser fiber-laser volume and margin pressure creating cost-down

with the clearest public financial trail (Raycus) is now confirmed export-blocked from receiving
US-origin content (see Section 4) — a structural problem for that specific named relationship, not
for CN demand in general.**

## 4. Certification and export constraints — the decisive finding

- **Wuhan Raycus is BIS Entity-listed (2021-07-09/12), presumption of denial.** Since Raycus is
  named as a primary CN buyer example in this idea's own longlist record, **any US-entity driver
  module containing US-origin content cannot legally be sold to Raycus without a BIS license, and
  such a license would face a presumption of denial.** This directly and materially undermines the
  seed's CN commercial-line demand thesis as written (which names Raycus specifically): the CN leg's
  demand may still be real in aggregate (Han's Laser and other CN OEMs are not confirmed Entity-
  listed), but **the seed's own named example customer is the wrong one to cite, and the two-entity
  structural requirement in the seed's own `fix_applied_notes` needs to be read as also applying to
  the "commercial" line's specific counterparty selection, not just the DEW/commercial split** —
  a US-entity-clean commercial-line company selling to Raycus would still be blocked; only a fully
  separate, US-content-free CN-domestic supply chain (or a different CN customer entirely, e.g.
  Han's Laser, pending its own independent clearance) could serve this specific named buyer.
- The US DEW line (nLIGHT/JLWS-class) requires ITAR/EAR-compliant, US-entity-only structuring per
  the seed's own day-one two-entity requirement — this session's findings (large, real, growing DoD
  contract ceilings) make that structural discipline more, not less, important, since the DEW
  business appears to be the stronger and faster-growing of the two legs.

## 5. US/CN primary-market verdicts

- **US: demand real, dated, buyer-reachable (nLIGHT's own contracts are the clearest evidence
  possible — a buyer's own DoD-funded program). Window open through at least 2028 (JLWS
  demonstration target) and plausibly beyond given HELSI-2/DE M-SHORAD's parallel funding.**
- **CN: demand real in aggregate (fiber-laser price war is genuine, company filings confirm it),
  but buyer reachability for the seed's own named example (Raycus) is now confirmed blocked at the
  US-content level by an Entity List presumption-of-denial. The CN leg survives only if re-targeted
  to a non-listed buyer (e.g., Han's Laser, pending independent clearance) or structured as a fully
  CN-domestic-content supply chain with no US-origin BOM — a materially narrower path than the
  seed's text implies.**

## 6. 2030 timing

1. **Primary/official, US:** JLWS OTA award, $44M initial/$627M ceiling, announced 2026-07-09, with
   operational demonstrations planned "as early as 2028" (investing.com, fetched) — an explicit,
   dated, primary DoD-program trigger squarely inside the 2028-2035 window.
2. **Primary, CN (context, not a "trigger" per se but a continuity signal):** Raycus's own Q1 2026
   filing (fetched) shows continued revenue growth and a sharp profit rebound, indicating the CN
   fiber-laser market is not contracting into the late 2020s — supports the CN leg's continued
   existence through 2030, independent of the Raycus-specific export block.

**Verdict: the window is open on the US leg (JLWS 2028 demonstration target, growing DoD backlog)
and the underlying CN commercial market persists through 2026, but the CN leg's specific named buyer
example is compromised by this session's decisive Entity List finding. The 2028 two-paid-evaluations
gate and the day-one two-entity structural requirement in the seed's own record are the right
controls; this evidence adds that the CN-side counterparty list itself needs re-screening against
the BIS Entity List before any evaluation is pursued.**

## Gate
**G1: PASS, with a material caveat.** US demand is strongly proven via nLIGHT's own contracts. CN
demand is proven in aggregate via company filings, but this session's central finding — Wuhan
Raycus, the seed's own named CN buyer example, is BIS Entity-listed with a presumption-of-denial
license policy — means the CN leg as currently named is not viable for a US-content product without
re-targeting to a different, screened buyer or a fully CN-domestic-content structure. This is exactly
the kind of "prior group found this kills some CN legs" finding flagged in the task brief; it should
be treated as a required fix before this idea is scored, not a footnote.
