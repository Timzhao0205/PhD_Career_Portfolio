## What he wants (hard preferences)

1. **Launch window:** found the company in **2029/2030**, after the PhD. Evaluate everything
   against that date.
2. **Hardware + software combined.** Software-only or hardware-only ideas are weak fits.
3. **Low cleanroom dependence.** Integration of acceptable-TRL components is preferred over
   process/device development that requires owning fab time. (Using purchased dies/wafers is
   fine; a business whose core loop is cleanroom fabrication is not.)
4. **"Extreme" as identity:** extreme energy, extreme power, extreme power/energy density,
   extreme environments, or an order-of-magnitude performance edge over conventional tech.
5. **High-end niche first, expansion later.** The initial market can be small, but it must
   (a) genuinely need the extreme performance, (b) pay high-end prices, (c) be winnable by a
   tiny team, and (d) open onto adjacent larger markets and a big frontier vision.
6. **Standalone-product bias.** He is explicitly wary of an all-diagnostics idea list.
   Diagnostics/test/monitoring ideas are allowed but capped (see MISSION_BRIEF diversity rules);
   the majority of ideas must deliver end value through the product itself.
7. **Markets:** interested in **China-first then US**, or **parallel** — but the US market must
   be covered efficiently either way. Sequence recommendations must engage the real 2026-era
   geopolitical/regulatory situation (Phase 5), refreshed with current sources at run time.
8. **Sector scope:** energy, semiconductors, biomedical, or AI — alone or combined.
