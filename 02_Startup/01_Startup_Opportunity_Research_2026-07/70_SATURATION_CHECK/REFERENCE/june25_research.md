# DEEP-DIVE: Startup Research OS 2026–2030 — Top-25 Diligence & Recommended Top-12

## TL;DR
- **The strongest opportunities cluster in data-center power/thermal (Cluster A) and standards-driven grid power-quality (Cluster B), where customer pain is anchored in dated, citable triggers** — NVIDIA's 2027 800VDC transition, OCP Deschutes/Diablo specs, IEEE 2800.2-2026 + NERC's May 2026 Level-3 alert, and ISO 10218:2025 cobot safety. These give procurement/regulation/budget anchors that survive the strict evidence test, unlike most TAM-only stories.
- **Recommended TOP-12: RID-002, RID-003, RID-008, RID-024, RID-001, RID-030, RID-053, RID-009, RID-033, RID-034, RID-055, RID-078.** All have at least one Tier-1 or Tier-2 customer/standards anchor, small-team prototype feasibility 2026–2028, low cleanroom burden (except RID-034/078, flagged), and a credible China wedge. RID-004 (MV-SiC SST) is the leading near-miss; RID-102 (fusion HTS), RID-111 (piezo) and RID-016 (second-life) are strong holds just below the cut.
- **Biggest systemic risks to manage:** (1) most data-center "pain" is **company-supported (NVIDIA) or Tier-2 consortium (OCP)** — never upgrade to independent market fact; (2) the 800VDC timeline is real but **not yet a hard 2026 requirement** (racks to 2027 still run on 3-phase AC per SemiAnalysis); (3) China wedge is genuine for power/storage/robotics but **export-control volatility (TRIG-003) and SiC tariff uncertainty (TRIG-004) are live watch items, not kills.**

## DELIVERABLE 2 — One-page diligence memos (24 points each)

### RID-002 — Capacitorless DC buffer + supercapacitor (Cluster A, score 84) — PURSUE
1. **Problem.** AI training GPUs draw synchronized µs–ms power swings (compute vs. all-reduce phases); amplitude scales with cluster size and, if harmonized with grid frequencies, can physically damage grid infrastructure (FACT, Tier-1 PP arXiv:2508.14318 "Power Stabilization for AI Training Datacenters"). 2. **First product.** Rack/tray-level capacitorless DC-link buffer + hybrid-supercapacitor module that absorbs sub-second transients, reducing upstream overbuild. 3. **First customer/buyer.** Neocloud & hyperscale GPU-rack integrators (CoreWeave, Lambda, Nebius, OCI, Together — all named NVIDIA 800VDC designers); PO signed by **data-center hardware/power-platform procurement** at the integrator or ODM (Vertiv/Foxconn). 4. **Pain severity.** Procurement/operational-loss anchor: NVIDIA states 20× more rack energy storage in Vera Rubin to "keep power steady" (CS); NERC issued a **Level-3 "Essential Actions" alert (May 4, 2026)** after repeated events where 1,000+ MW of computational load dropped in seconds (FACT, Tier-1) — directly ties transient mismanagement to bulk-power-system risk and forthcoming reliability obligations. 5. **Existing solution & failure.** MLCC banks (inductance-limited), rack BBUs (minutes-scale, cannot do µs); supercaps complement not replace (Tier-1 PP arXiv:2603.00415: supercaps "cannot provide minutes of ride-through and therefore complement…rack BBUs"). 6. **Architecture.** Low-inductance supercap bank + bidirectional DC/DC + fast controller co-designed with firmware power-shaping (NVIDIA NVML telemetry). 7. **Hardware.** Hybrid supercaps/pouch cells, SiC/GaN DC-DC, low-inductance busbar; GB300 NVL72 already uses hybrid supercaps for rack-level transient shaping (CS, Tier-4). 8. **Software.** Closed-loop transient controller; NVML power-limit integration. 9. **Data.** Per-rack power traces (DGX-H100/Rubin), frequency spectra of training jobs. 10. **Control loop.** Sense DC-link droop → actuate supercap injection within µs; this is a genuine control loop (clears "dashboard" aversion). 11. **Prototype plan.** 2026 single-tray buffer bench rig; 2027 rack-level demo with an ODM; 2028 multi-rack pilot. Small team feasible. 12. **TRL.** Now 3–4 → 2029 6–7. 13. **Capex.** Low ($1–3M); power-electronics bench, no cleanroom. 14. **China wedge.** China hyperscalers (Alibaba, Tencent, ByteDance) + SuperX/Zhonhen 800VDC JV (Tier-4) building domestic 800VDC; strong electromechanical/cap supply base. 15. **US path.** Sell into NVIDIA-ecosystem neoclouds/ODMs; OCP contribution route. 16. **Regulatory.** NERC large-load reliability standard forthcoming 2026 (not final — do not upgrade). 17. **Export-control.** Supercaps/power electronics not advanced-computing-controlled; low risk (but BIS TRIG-003 volatile — watch). 18. **Supply chain.** Supercap cells (Chinese/Japanese), SiC dice. 19. **Incumbents/startups.** Vertiv, Eaton, Delta, Navitas; supercap players (Maxwell/Eaton, Skeleton, CAP-XX); Volfpack (GPU supercap). 20. **Defensibility.** Low-inductance packaging + co-designed control IP; integration know-how. 21. **Failure modes.** Hyperscalers solve in-house via firmware shaping (NVIDIA "power burner" mode); supercap energy-density/volume limits. 22. **Kill criteria.** If NVIDIA/ODM reference design absorbs transients without a discrete buffer module by 2027, kill. 23. **Evidence strength.** Strong (Tier-1 preprints + NERC Tier-1 + NVIDIA CS). 24. **Weakness (per-flag).** NVIDIA "−30%" is **company-supported**, OCP is Tier-2 — addressed: pain is independently corroborated by **Tier-1 NERC alert + peer-reviewed-track preprints**, not by the NVIDIA figure alone.
