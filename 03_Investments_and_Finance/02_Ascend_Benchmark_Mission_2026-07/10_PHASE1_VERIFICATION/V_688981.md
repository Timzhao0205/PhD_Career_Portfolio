## Claim under test

Two claims, audited separately because they have different evidentiary ceilings:

**(i) Customer/factual claim — "SMIC fabricates Huawei's Ascend AI-accelerator dies."**
Specifically: Ascend 910B/910C on SMIC's 7nm-class (N+1/N+2) DUV-multipatterning process, and
the newer Ascend 950PR on SMIC's N+3 (~5nm-class) process. **Expected ceiling: Tier B at best.**
SMIC is a US Entity-List company (since 2020-12, restricted from EUV and other sub-10nm tools/
tech under a "presumption of denial" license policy — see Risks section); naming a specific
end-customer for its most advanced node would be commercially and geopolitically sensitive, so
the prior expectation (stated in the task and the watchlist note) is that SMIC's own filings and
investor replies will **never** confirm this by name. This file tests that expectation directly
against SMIC's FY2025 annual results, Q1 2026 quarterly results, and the company's own investor-
relations replies — and confirms it holds (see "What SMIC itself says/declines" below).

**(ii) Structural/bottleneck claim — "SMIC's capacity and yield are the binding constraint on
domestic Ascend-class AI-chip output."** This is auditable directly from SMIC's own disclosed
revenue, capex, capacity, and utilization figures (all disclosed; only the *customer* attribution
is withheld), cross-referenced against tier-3 estimates of Ascend-specific capacity/yield.

## Tier assigned + reasoning

**Tier B for "SMIC fabricates Huawei's Ascend AI-accelerator chips."** Tier A is not reachable and
is not claimed: no SMIC filing, no SMIC official reply (including a direct, dated, on-the-record
decline-to-confirm in [S282]), and no Huawei disclosure found this session names the other party.
This is not a search failure — it is the expected, structurally-forced outcome of SMIC's Entity-
List status, and this file's own-filing review (four separate tier-1 documents, five years apart)
confirms the pattern holds through 2026. The claim clears **Tier B, not C or UNVERIFIED**, because
the evidence table above rests on four independently-fetched, named, dated outlets — TrendForce
(a SOURCE_STANDARDS-designated-priority outlet) three times, including two 2026-dated pieces that
name specific Ascend chip models (910C, 950PR) and specific process nodes (7nm, N+3/~5nm-class)
attributed to SMIC by name, plus SemiAnalysis's detailed, dated, capacity-quantified independent
analysis — not a repeated forum claim (SOURCE_STANDARDS: "a forum claim repeated 10x is still C").
The bottleneck/structural half of the claim (capacity+yield constrain Ascend output) is
substantiated at **tier 1 for the capacity/utilization/capex inputs themselves** (SMIC's own
disclosed numbers, above) but at **tier 3 only for the Ascend-specific allocation of that capacity**
(SemiAnalysis/TrendForce estimates, which conflict on scope and are not averaged) — consistent
with an overall Tier B rating for the combined claim as stated on the watchlist.
