## Claim under test (two distinct claims, assessed separately as instructed)

**Claim 1 — SUPPLY LINK:** "华丰科技 supplies high-speed/backplane connectors that go into Huawei's
Ascend-era AI-compute systems (data-center servers)."

**Claim 2 — DOMINANCE:** "华丰科技 holds a dominant share of Ascend backplane-connector supply" — the
watchlist's literal intake framing, sourced only to a forum-aggregation roll [S009, tier 4] at intake.

These need different evidentiary bars. Claim 1 is a binary supply-relationship question. Claim 2 is a
competitive-market-share question — 华丰's share of HUAWEI's/ASCEND's connector-vendor panel versus
rival suppliers — which is analytically the OPPOSITE ratio from customer concentration (HUAWEI's share
of 华丰's own revenue). This audit keeps the two directions of that ratio separate throughout; conflating
them is the single most likely way this claim gets overstated.

1. FY2025 total revenue = CNY 2,527,729,770.66 [S266].
2. FY2025 largest customer ("客户一," anonymized in the filing itself) = **60.52%** of revenue = CNY
   ~1.530bn [S266, cross-checked against S268's stable percentage figures: 60.52%/4.56%/3.45%/2.91%/1.81%,
   top-5 total 73.25% — matches S266's own CNY 185,150.88万元/73.25% exactly: 1,851,508,800 /
   2,527,729,770.66 = 73.25%].
3. Customer-1's identity as Huawei is **not named in the filing itself**, but is corroborated by: (a) the
   company's own, directly-quoted 2023-10-09 earnings-briefing statement naming Huawei as largest customer
   and quantifying "华为占公司通讯类业务的比重均超60%" (a different base — the COMMUNICATIONS SEGMENT, not
   total company revenue) [this statement is quoted in full in Risks/notes below and is drawn from the
   same evidentiary base as S271]; (b) three independently-dated 2026 tier-3 media reports [S275, S276,
   S278] all citing the same CNY ~1.53bn/60.52% figure for FY2025 specifically as Huawei's amount — an
   exact arithmetic match to the filing's anonymized top-customer figure, which is strong (though not
   itself Tier-A) triangulation.
4. A separate report [S275] gives an H1-2025-specific figure of **64.29%** (a different, shorter period,
   also not independently filing-confirmed by this audit).
=> **Huawei-overall (ALL product lines — telecom infrastructure + any AI-server business combined) ≈
60-64% of FY2025/H1'25 total revenue.** This is a CEILING for "Huawei-linked revenue," not an
Ascend/AI-server-specific figure.

**Ascend/AI-server-specific sub-share: NOT-ESTIMABLE.** No fetched source anywhere in this session
decomposes the Huawei relationship by end-application (Ascend-branded AI-compute servers versus legacy
telecom base-station/router/switch equipment, which 华丰 has supplied Huawei for far longer, per the
21.40-35.87% 2019-2021 baseline in Evidence §C, well before the current AI buildout). The company's own
Q1-2026 language attributes recent GROWTH specifically to "AI服务器、数据中心等终端产品国产化替代" [S270],
suggesting the AI/Ascend-linked slice of the Huawei relationship is both real and rising, but no source
gives it a percentage. The historic ">95% of the high-speed-connector line" figure [S273] cannot be
carried forward as an Ascend-specific estimate: it is a 2019-2021, pre-AI-buildout, legacy-generation
(sub-56Gbps) figure describing a different technology tier than the 112G/224G products the company now
associates with AI-server demand.

**Net: exposure_est_pct = 60-64% (Huawei all-products ceiling, filing-anchored); Ascend/AI-server-specific
sub-share NOT-ESTIMABLE with any defensible arithmetic from sources fetched this session.**

## Risks/notes for later phases (facts only, no scores/rankings)

- **Customer-concentration trend has two distinct episodes, both company-relevant to cyclicality:**
  (1) IPO-era zigzag: Huawei = 21.40% (2019) → 35.87% (2020) → 20.75% (2021) of total revenue [S272,
  S273], coinciding with a 42.24% YoY plunge in communications-connector sales volume and a capacity-
  utilization collapse from 84.04% to 51.61% in 2021 [S272] — contemporaneous with the 2020-2021 US
  sanctions pressure on Huawei's own equipment business, i.e., a real, filing-documented instance of
  Huawei-driven revenue volatility at this company. (2) A renewed, steeper 2023-2025 climb: 25.36% (2023)
  → 37.11% (2024) → 60.52% (2025) per one tier-3 source's trend line [S276] — noting a SEPARATE tier-3
  source's FY2024 figure (~35%/CNY 382mm) [S278] does not match [S276]'s FY2024 figure (37.11%/CNY 505mm)
  exactly; both are recorded here per SOURCE_STANDARDS conflict-handling rather than averaged, since
  neither was independently filing-confirmed by this audit for FY2024 specifically (only FY2025's 60.52%
  is filing-anchored, via S266/S268).
- **Defense-segment order-timing uncertainty is a company-acknowledged risk**, distinct from the
  Huawei/communications risk: at the company's own 2023-10-09 earnings briefing (reported via [S271-type]
  sourcing consistent with the earnings-briefing content documented in this audit's research), the company
  stated "公司在防务领域订单改善时点还有不确定性，公司也正在关注和跟踪行业的变化情况" (timing of defense-
  segment order improvement carries uncertainty; the company is monitoring industry developments) —
  a direct acknowledgment that the non-communications (defense) segment is lumpy/order-driven, relevant to
  assessing how "diversified" the business really is away from the Huawei-communications concentration.
- **Huawei is simultaneously customer, technology-development partner, and (indirectly, via its Hubble
  investment vehicle) shareholder** — a structure that raises its own disclosure/governance questions,
  which the SSE itself flagged at IPO review ("上交所在第二轮问询中就与客户华为的业务合作进行了重点关注"
  [S274]). Hubble subscribed CNY 60mm for a 3.47% stake in Dec 2021 at CNY 4.42/share [S273]; a separate,
  non-fetched-this-session search suggests this diluted to ~2.95% by end-2025 with a mark-to-market value
  cited (non-load-bearing, snippet only) around CNY 1.3-1.7bn in early 2026 amid the AI-driven share-price
  rally [S275 gives one fetched checkpoint: "如果以2月27日股价测算，其持股市值将进一步攀升至17.4亿元，四年
  间浮盈超27倍" as of 2026-02-27]. Separately, an IP/non-compete clause tied to jointly-developed 56Gbps+
  connector technology bars 华丰 from selling resulting products to Huawei's mainland domestic
  telecom-equipment competitors [S273] — this is double-edged: it reinforces how structurally locked-in
  the Huawei relationship is at that technology tier, but it also caps the addressable customer set for
  that specific tier, a relevant fact for assessing diversification potential.
- **Diversification progress away from Huawei has historically been slow-moving.** At IPO review
  (2022-12-09), the company's own disclosed status was that ZTE/Nokia/Fiberhome/New H3C were still at
  sample-shipment/small-batch stage for high-speed backplane products, with **no mass orders yet secured**
  beyond Huawei [S274]. The company's current-era reply set [S267] shows broader relationship-building
  activity (Alibaba, Tencent, ByteDance, XFusion, Cambricon; SGMW, BYD in the EV-connector line) and a 2025
  capacity-expansion plan explicitly naming ByteDance and Alibaba alongside Huawei as target customers
  [S277] — real evidence of diversification effort, but this audit found no fetched source quantifying
  what share of CURRENT (FY2025) revenue these newer relationships represent versus Huawei's 60.52%.
- **A precise 通信/防务/工业 (communications/defense/industrial) segment percentage split could not be
  reliably extracted from any source fetched this session**, despite repeated attempts on multiple mirrors
