## Claim under test

tier B, sourced only from a financial-media iFinD algorithmic screen [S011]. Per the watchlist's own
note, TWO THESES must be kept separate: (1) Innolight is overwhelmingly an overseas/Nvidia-and-
hyperscaler-chain 800G/1.6T optics supplier riding the global AI-capex cycle (>90% of revenue — see
EXPOSURE SPLIT below); (2) a much smaller domestic (境内) slice of revenue may include Huawei/Ascend
-linked demand, but "domestic revenue" is NOT itself a Huawei/Ascend number — the domestic slice also
includes ZTE, telecom carriers, and Chinese cloud majors that are not necessarily running Huawei
silicon. This audit tests whether Innolight's own filings/IR substantiate a direct, named Huawei/
Ascend relationship (vs. a media inference), and whether the domestic slice can be further
decomposed into an Ascend-specific figure.

## Exposure estimate (Ascend-linked only — NOT domestic-revenue share)

**Step 1 — establish a ceiling, not an estimate.** Any Huawei/Ascend-linked revenue Innolight books
must fall within 境内 (domestic) revenue: Ascend AI accelerators are a China-only product under US
export controls, and no source found this session suggests Innolight books any Huawei/Ascend-related
sales as export/境外 revenue. Therefore Ascend-linked revenue ≤ FY2025 domestic revenue = **9.42% of
total revenue** [S205, fetched] (FY2024 ceiling would be 13.19% [S207, fetched]). This is a CEILING
from the geography split, not an Ascend-specific figure — conflating the two is exactly the error the
task instructions warn against.

**Step 2 — no source decomposes the ceiling further.** No company filing, IR reply, or tier ≤3 source
found this session breaks out a Huawei-specific or Ascend-specific revenue figure within Innolight's
domestic segment. The only source that attempts a number (15% Huawei-order share, [S154], tier 4) is
(a) larger than the entire domestic ceiling itself (arithmetically impossible) and (b) unsupported by
any citation — it cannot be used.

**Step 3 — qualitative color that shrinks (not disproves) the plausible fraction of the ceiling.**
The company's own repeated language [S209, S210, and historically S211] states that DOMESTIC optical-
module demand is concentrated in 400G/800G rather than 1.6T, while 1.6T/800G TOP-of-line demand
(the tier most associated with the newest, largest AI-supernode clusters) is "primarily overseas."
This suggests — without proving — that whatever slice of the 9.42% domestic ceiling is Ascend-cluster
-linked is not the majority of that slice; a meaningful part of domestic revenue is plausibly legacy
telecom/enterprise/general cloud demand unrelated to any AI supernode.

**Conclusion: NOT-ESTIMABLE as a specific point or range.** Defensible statement for downstream use:
Ascend-linked share of FY2025 total revenue is bounded above by 9.42% (the domestic-revenue ceiling,
itself falling YoY in share terms), and the true figure is almost certainly a fraction of that ceiling
— plausibly low-single-digit percent of total company revenue or less — but no company disclosure or
credible tier ≤3 source permits a specific point estimate or a range narrower than "somewhere between
0% and 9.42% of FY2025 revenue." Per instructions, domestic revenue share (9.42%) is NOT reported as
the Ascend-linked estimate.
