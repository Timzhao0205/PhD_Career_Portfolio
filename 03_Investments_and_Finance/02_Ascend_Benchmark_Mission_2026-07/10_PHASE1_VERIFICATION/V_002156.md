Reasoning: Seven tier-1 primary documents were fetched and read in full — 2 annual-report
vintages' worth of text (summary + full), 3 official investor-relations activity records spanning
May 2025–April 2026, 1 quarterly report, and 1 trading-fluctuation announcement. **None contains

generic non-denial — contrasted directly, in the same document, with an unambiguous "no" on Intel
(G). Per this assignment's explicit instruction, "a non-denial or generic reply does NOT upgrade
the tier," so E/F/G together still do **not** substantiate the Ascend link at tier A. Three tier-3
sources (one financial newspaper, two named sell-side notes including a major foreign bank) that
cover TFME's FY2025/Q1-2026 results and customer concentration in detail build the entire
investment thesis around AMD + the AI/HPC advanced-packaging supercycle and contain **zero**
Huawei/Ascend content — so tier B ("named sell-side/major media names TFME as Ascend packaging
supplier") is also not met. The specific, colorful claims actually driving the Ascend narrative
("2.5D验证通过昇腾910B," "国产替代订单增长53%," "昇腾910C独家封装订单," "昇腾950供应链占先进封装

Per this assignment's rule, "a forum claim repeated 10x is still C." This is not UNVERIFIED,

did not come back empty) — it is exactly what "C = forum-only" describes.

Distinguish clearly: TFME's status as a large **advanced-packaging** OSAT (2.5D/3D/flip-chip/
Chiplet capability, ~70% of revenue in advanced packaging per I) is well-substantiated at tier A

## Exposure estimate

**NOT-ESTIMABLE.**

Method/why: No tier ≤B source fetched this session ties any CNY figure or percentage of TFME
revenue to Huawei/Ascend specifically. The only quantified customer-concentration figures
available are: top-5 customers = 69.54% of FY2025 revenue (CNY 19.416bn of CNY 27.921bn) and the
single largest (unnamed) customer = 52.29% (≈CNY 14.60bn) [S131] — both are, per every source
that offers an attribution at all (tier 3/4, not the filing itself), attributed to **AMD**, not
Huawei. Arithmetically, that leaves at most 100% − 52.29% = 47.71% of FY2025 revenue spread
across "Customer 2" through thousands of unlisted smaller customers combined (mobile SoC,
PMIC/power management, display-driver, automotive, storage — all separately named as growth
drivers in [S133] Q15 answer, none Huawei-labeled). Treating any slice of that 47.71% residual as
a "Huawei estimate" would require stacking an unconfirmed customer-identity assumption on top of
a residual-of-a-residual — exactly the kind of invented figure the mission's NUMBER TRUTH RULE
prohibits. No named sell-side report, no company filing, and no official IR reply gives a
Huawei/Ascend-specific revenue line, model, or percentage to compute from. Conclusion:
NOT-ESTIMABLE.

## Risks/notes for later phases (facts only — no scores/rankings)

- Customer concentration is real and high regardless of the Huawei question: top-5 = 69.54%,
  largest single (unnamed) customer = 52.29% of FY2025 revenue [S131]; company itself states it is
  "AMD's primary packaging supplier, >80% of AMD's relevant products" [S133].
- Export/FX exposure: 66.59% of FY2025 revenue is export vs. 33.41% domestic [S131]; filings flag
  RMB/USD movements as a direct risk to export revenue — consistent with, but not proof of,
  AMD-customer concentration.
- Advanced packaging (flip-chip etc.) ≈ 70% of FY2025 revenue per the company's own confirmation
  [S133] — this is genuine, tier-1-substantiated exposure to the "advanced packaging" layer
  generically, independent of the Huawei question.
- Chiplet-specific high-density packaging is disclosed by the company's own FY2025 R&D-project
  table as "研发进行中" (R&D in progress), not yet a described commercial revenue line [S131] — in
  tension with tier-4 claims of concrete Chiplet revenue growth (%) seen in self-media [S140].
- HBM/domestic-memory packaging: company states this space is "still dominated by international
  Memory IDM giants," i.e., no disclosed domestic-HBM (CXMT) foothold as of 2026-04-28 [S133] —
  relevant if later phases model an Ascend-HBM-packaging sub-thesis for any ticker.
- No US Entity List / export-control designation for TFME itself was found in any document fetched
  this session; a dedicated OFAC/BIS-list check was not performed and is flagged as a gap, not a

- The one 2026-01-20 abnormal-trading-fluctuation announcement found in this search window is

- Qualitative asymmetry worth carrying forward: TFME gives an unambiguous "no" when a relationship

  itself evidence of a Huawei relationship, and should not be treated as such by later phases.
