## Claim under test

industry-chain roll-up [S009, already in ledger] claiming it is a "supernode" (超节点)
optics supplier for Huawei's Ascend AI compute clusters. This audit asks whether
Accelink's OWN annual/quarterly filings, exchange announcements, or 互动易 investor
replies substantiate a direct, named Huawei/Ascend optical-module supply relationship
(vs. forum inference), and what share of FY2025 revenue is plausibly Huawei/Ascend-linked.

Technology Group) — its controlling shareholder was restructured in 2026 from 烽火科技
to 芯珂(武汉)技术有限公司, both inside 中国信科集团 (search-level color, not
independently fetched this session). 中国信科 is a STATE-OWNED telecom-equipment group
organizationally SEPARATE from Huawei (a competitor in some segments) — so no
related-party-transaction disclosure route to Huawei exists via the parent.

## Tier assigned + reasoning

**Tier C (confirms the watchlist's starting assessment; NOT upgraded).** Despite an
extensive search (13 fetched tier-3 sources, plus documented tier-1 access attempts
across 5 distinct filings/mirrors), no source at tier ≤3 substantiates a Huawei/Ascend-
specific optical-module supply relationship for Accelink:
- The company's OWN FY2025 annual report summary, Q1 2026 report, and H1 2025 report
  contain no "华为" or "昇腾" in the text this session could access.
- The ONE time a company representative was asked THE exact question under test — "is
  Accelink a core optical-module supplier for Huawei's Ascend ecosystem, and is its 800G
  module Ascend-certified?" — the reply [S162] was a non-answer, not a confirmation.
- The company's only repeated, on-the-record Huawei characterization [S163, S166] is
  generic ("an important customer" in general optical communications), predates the
  Ascend/AI narrative (2020, 2023), and was never updated with an Ascend-specific
  confirmation in anything fetched this session.
- When pressed for Huawei order-volume specifics [S174], the company invoked trade-secret
  confidentiality rather than confirming or quantifying anything.
- The ONLY sources making the specific, extraordinary "supernode"/Ascend-percentage
  claims [S172] are anonymous forum/community posts with zero cited support — squarely
  tier 4.
This pattern (generic historical Huawei-customer acknowledgment + confidentiality-based
non-denial on Ascend specifics + silence in the annual-report text + unsourced forum
hype) is evidentially different from this mission's audit of a different watchlist name
(002261/拓维信息), where the company's OWN annual report explicitly named "鲲鹏/昇腾 AI" as
its product's technical architecture. No equivalent self-disclosure exists for Accelink
in anything fetched this session — hence tier C, not A or B.

## Risks/notes for later phases (facts only, no scores/rankings)
- **Disclosure posture / confidentiality pattern:** across 2020 [S166], 2023 [S163], 2024
  [S174], and 2025 [S162], Accelink's investor-facing statements about Huawei moved from
  generic acknowledgment ("important customer") to explicit confidentiality refusals and
  non-answers on anything quantitative or Ascend-specific. Later-phase analysts should
  not expect this posture to change absent a new company disclosure.
- **Segment-reporting granularity has decreased, not increased, over this period:** the
  company used to report 传输类/接入类/数据通信类 as three lines; recent periods (2023
  onward, per snippet-grade color in [S173], not independently confirmed) report only
  传输类 vs. 数据与接入类 (data+access merged) — this merger makes it structurally HARDER,
  not easier, to isolate a datacenter/AI-specific (vs. legacy telecom-access) revenue
  slice from published segment tables, itself a relevant fact for anyone trying to size
  Ascend-specific exposure in Phase 2.

  self-consistent):** Data & Access revenue CNY 5.101bn (FY2024) → CNY 8.463bn (FY2025),
