## Tier assigned + reasoning
**Tier A.** The company's own FY2025 annual report [S100] explicitly names 鲲鹏
(Kunpeng) and 昇腾 (Ascend) as the processor basis of its 神州鲲泰 own-brand hardware line
and gives that segment's revenue. Separately, the company's own investor-relations activity
record [S101] and at least three dated 互动易 replies quoted by named financial media
[S102][S105][S106] independently confirm the Huawei Ascend/Kunpeng partnership in the
company's own words. This exceeds the Tier-A bar ("company's own filing/exchange
announcement/official IR reply substantiates the link") by a wide margin — the original
WATCHLIST tier-C forum claim [S009] is upgraded on primary evidence. Note the important
scope correction from the forum framing: filings describe 神州数码/神州鲲泰 as an **own-brand
OEM/systems-integration partner** that designs, assembles and sells servers built on
licensed Kunpeng/Ascend modules (with its own R&D — KTransformers inference-optimization
work, an "Open Lab" serving ~300 ecosystem partners) — not a pure logistics reseller of
Huawei-badged boxes. The "distribution" label in the WATCHLIST layer field is accurate for
~94% of total revenue (the IT-distribution/component segment, thin-margin, multi-brand) but
imprecise for the smaller, faster-growing, higher-margin own-brand slice where the Ascend
link actually sits.

## Exposure estimate
**≈5% of FY2025 total revenue (upper bound; NOT separable into Ascend-only vs. Kunpeng-only
from disclosed data).**

Method / arithmetic:
- FY2025 own-brand product-business revenue ("自有品牌产品业务") = RMB 7,440,000,000 (74.4亿元),
  +62.4% YoY [S100]. The annual report's entire narrative for this segment discusses only
  神州鲲泰 (Kunpeng+Ascend based); no other own-brand line is described as revenue-material for
  FY2025 (a JD-co-branded consumer AIPC, "灵觅," is discussed only as a forward/H2-2025
  initiative in [S101], immaterial to the FY2025 actual).
- FY2025 total revenue = RMB 143,751,044,503.13 (1,437.51亿元), +12.16% YoY [S100].
- **74.4 ÷ 1,437.51 = 5.18% ≈ 5%.**
- This is a combined Kunpeng(CPU)+Ascend(NPU) figure — the filing does not split 神州鲲泰
  revenue between its "通用计算" (general compute, Kunpeng CPU) and "AI智算" (AI compute, Ascend
  NPU) product lines, so a pure Ascend-only % is **NOT-ESTIMABLE** from what the company
  discloses; treat 5% as a ceiling, not a point estimate of Ascend-specific exposure. The one
  data point that speaks to the Ascend-only sub-slice — 1H2024 "神州鲲泰人工智能服务器" (AI
  server, i.e., Ascend-class) revenue of RMB560m on <1% of that period's total revenue
  [S106][S111] — suggests the AI/Ascend-specific share of the own-brand segment was
  meaningfully smaller than the whole-segment figure as recently as mid-2024, though the
  company states 273% YoY growth in that sub-line, so the FY2025 gap between "whole segment"
  and "Ascend-only" has likely narrowed (not quantifiable further from filed data).
- Historical anchor for context (not the headline FY2025 number): FY2023 self-brand revenue
  was 3.20% of total revenue (RMB 3.829bn ÷ RMB 119.62bn) [S103][S104], cross-validated
  against [S100]'s own 3-year revenue table (119,623,887,693.45 元 for 2023) — internally
  consistent (38.29 ÷ 1,196.24 = 3.20%, exact match).
- **A second, structurally distinct Huawei exposure channel exists and is NOT-ESTIMABLE:**
  the IT-distribution segment's 电子元器件 (electronic-components) sub-segment (RMB 28.24bn,
  +39.6% YoY [S100]) carries HiSilicon (海思, Huawei's chip-design arm) product alongside
  ~9 other named brands (MetaX/Moore Threads/Biren/Intel/AMD/Phytium/Loongson/UNISOC/
  Rockchip) with no brand-level revenue split disclosed anywhere found. This is a real but
  un-sizeable second exposure path (pure component distribution, thin margin, not
  Ascend-server-specific) — flagged, not counted in the 5% figure above to avoid double
  counting or invention.
- **Do not equate top-customer/top-supplier concentration with Huawei:** FY2025 top-5
  customers = 43.04% of revenue (largest single customer 37.82%); top-5 suppliers = 60.74%
  of procurement (largest single supplier 31.86%) [S109, cross-validated: percentages sum
  exactly to the stated totals]. **Neither the #1 customer nor the #1 supplier is named in
  any source found** — standard non-disclosure practice for this filing type. There is no
  evidence identifying either counterparty as Huawei; asserting that link would violate the
  "never silently equate top customer/supplier with Huawei" instruction, so it is not done
  here despite the obvious temptation given the Kunpeng/Ascend narrative elsewhere in the
  filing.

## Risks/notes for later phases (facts only, no scores/rankings)
- **Margin structure by segment, FY2025** (after-tax net profit ÷ segment revenue, both
  from [S100]): IT distribution & VAS 7.4亿 ÷ 1,355.2亿 = 0.55%; own-brand 0.7754亿 ÷ 74.4亿
  = 1.04%; data-cloud services & software 1.539亿 ÷ 36.7亿 = 4.19%. Confirms the WATCHLIST's
  original caution ("distribution = thin margin") for the ~94%-of-revenue distribution core,
  while the smaller own-brand and software segments run at meaningfully higher (though still
  low single-digit to mid-single-digit) net margin.
- **Segment-sum vs. total-revenue gap:** IT distribution (1,355.2亿) + own-brand (74.4亿) +
  data-cloud (36.7亿) = 1,466.3亿, ~28.8亿 above the reported consolidated total revenue of
  1,437.51亿 [S100]. Not reconciled in the abstract (likely intersegment items or differing
  scope conventions in the full report's segment note) — worth resolving against the full
  financial statements in Phase 2.
- **Consolidated net profit fell 30.5% YoY despite every core segment growing revenue and
  after-tax profit.** The company's own explanation [S100]: heavy AI-related R&D spend,
  lower government subsidies, fair-value losses on equity investments, and — the largest
  named factor — impairment and financing-cost drag from an unrelated real-estate JV
  (深圳市神州数码国际创新中心/IIC). This is a non-Ascend-related drag on headline profitability
  that Phase 2/3 should separate from the AI/Kunpeng/Ascend growth story.
- **Operating cash flow swung negative:** FY2025 OCF = −24.26亿元 vs. +25.05亿元 in FY2024
  (−196.85% YoY) [S100], explained by the company as deliberate stock-up on chips/storage/
  compute components ahead of large own-brand and distribution orders. A genuine
  working-capital fact, not necessarily distress, but relevant to any cash-conversion
  analysis later.
- **Order-backlog vs. booked-revenue distinction:** the China Telecom ARM-A/GPU-server
  framework agreement (标包4 ¥5.93bn-package/11% share, 标包9 ¥2.29bn-package/10% share,
  signed March 2025 [S100]) and the >RMB6bn company-wide 信创 order backlog cited as of
  2025-09-02 [S101] are **tender/backlog figures, not confirmed recognized revenue** —
  flagged so later phases do not conflate pipeline with realized sales.
- **Governance flag (factual, not a score):** controlling shareholder 郭为 (Guo Wei, 21.39%)
  has 77,388,902 of his shares (≈50% of his stake) in frozen/冻结 status per the FY2025
  shareholder table [S100]; a separate 4.35%-holder (中国新纪元) and a 1.98%-holder (中国希格玛)
  each show pledged shares. Worth carrying forward as a capital-structure fact.
- **Verticalization angle:** filings show the company moving from pure component/IT
  distribution → own-brand Kunpeng/Ascend server OEM (神州鲲泰) → AI software/agent platform
  (神州问学, +165.4% YoY on a small RMB114m base [S100]) → networking hardware via the 2025
  acquisition of 智邦科技's mainland business through JV 木犀智能, explicitly framed by the
  company as building an "算力+网络" (compute+networking) product matrix toward an "AI
  Factory" model [S101]. This is a widening-scope fact pattern, not a valuation judgment.
- **Diversification away from single-chip dependence:** the company's own IR record
  states it is running deep tests with MetaX (沐曦), a non-Huawei domestic GPU maker,
  expected to land contracts as soon as H2 2025 [S101] — relevant context against treating
  神州数码/神州鲲泰 as a Huawei-exclusive play.
