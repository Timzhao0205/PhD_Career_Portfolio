1. **The Ascend-specific split is still unobservable everywhere.** Unchanged from v1, now
   verified across 19 names instead of 14. If H1-2026 reports or Huawei Connect 2026
   produce the first company-confirmed Ascend order books, the top half reshuffles — the
   most upgrade-sensitive rows are 688629 (W1 3→5 on a single 昇腾-naming reply) and
   688141/688519 (whose anonymous-customer forks resolve on any identity disclosure).
2. **Valuation regime risk dominates selection risk.** 17 of 19 names are PFP; the 58%
   cash answer is a claim about PRICE. A sustained domestic AI-capex boom makes this key
   right on process and wrong on outcome; a de-rating makes even rank-1 fall hard. The
   patch sharpened rather than resolved this: it found MORE reasons prices embed
   perfection (recurring-basis multiples, commodity-cycle growth mix, dilution overhangs).
3. **Judgment-layer residuals.** The patch's own rulings are the new fragility: R0.5's
   chain-nexus line (002916 in, 002156 out) is defensible but debatable — a reader who
   rejects it should re-add ~0.20 to 002156 and subtract the same from 002916 and see
   whether the portfolio changes (it barely does); R0.8's C-residue treatment of 688347
   could flip BACK if the parent later commits the Fab-6 asset to the listco (watch the
