## 3. Implied expectations

At CNY338.00/share and CNY79.49bn total market cap (2026-07-07 post-close [S1140;S1141]), 南亚新材 trades at
215.30× ttm P/E (330.76× on a static FY2025-only basis — richer, not cheaper, once the Q1 roll is removed) and
13.01×–15.21× P/S — multiples that sit at or above the richest names already logged in this mission's universe
(cf. VAL_688629.md's 216.20×/260.39× for a similarly Huawei-adjacent connector maker), even though 南亚新材's own
FY2025 gross margin (11.80%) and net margin (4.60%) [F_688519.md] remain modest in absolute terms and are only
two years removed from a FY2023 trough of a 4.16% gross margin and -4.34% net margin. For today's price to be
earned rather than merely hoped for, the market must be underwriting several things at once. First, continued
*acceleration*, not just continuation, of the current growth cadence: revenue growth itself sped up from
FY2025's +55.52% to Q1-2026's +92.36%, and net-profit growth sped up even faster, from +377.60% to +610.83%
[F_688519.md; V_688519.md, S994] — the static-above-ttm P/E relationship shows the market pricing in more of
this trajectory, not a reversion toward FY2025's already-elevated pace. Second, that the accelerating
high-speed+IC-substrate product mix — the RMB925m (9.25亿元) bucket that grew +80.60% YoY and is the only
disclosed segment cut specific enough to plausibly house any Huawei/Ascend-linked demand — keeps gaining share
fast enough to pull company-wide gross margin up from today's still-modest 11.80% toward levels that could
retroactively justify a 200×+ earnings multiple. But that segment is itself capped at ≤17.70% of FY2025 revenue
even under Phase 1's explicit overstatement-bound method (925.0 ÷ 5,227.8 [V_688519.md, S990;S991]), and Phase 1
found that ceiling bundles together Huawei/Ascend, 海光/Hygon (named in the identical company sentence), generic
AI-server/switch/optical demand, legacy 5G/6G telecom (the original, longest-certified end-market), and DRAM/NAND
packaging materials, with no disclosed sub-split isolating a Huawei/Ascend-only figure — so even full success
across that entire bucket caps the AI/Ascend-specific growth driver at a minority of a minority of revenue, and
the chain runs through named PCB-fabricator intermediaries (奥士康/深南电路/景旺电子/沪电股份/胜宏科技/方正科技/
江西旭昇/特创集团/生益电子 [V_688519.md §C]), never a direct, Nanya-controlled Huawei relationship. Third, and
most concretely, the price assumes that the cash-quality problem which triggered SSE's own regulatory inquiry
letter is a temporary, growth-financing artifact that resolves cleanly rather than an early warning sign: FY2025
operating cash flow swung to CNY-83.18M (8,318.46万元 [S1084]) from +CNY325.31M in FY2024 even as reported net
profit grew 377–379% YoY, receivables rose 68.59% YoY to as much as 61% of revenue, and FY2025 was the first of
three years the company shows net debt rather than net cash [V_688519.md §I; F_688519.md] — and this session's
fresh Q1-2026 fetch shows operating cash flow still negative (CNY-66.13M [S1143]), i.e. the pattern has not yet
reversed in the most recently disclosed quarter. Stacking a 215×–331× earnings multiple on top of an unresolved,
regulator-flagged cash-conversion gap, a still-single-digit-to-low-double-digit gross margin, and a hard,
unquantified ceiling on the specific Ascend-linked growth story leaves very little room for any of these threads
to disappoint.

**Flag: PRICED-FOR-PERFECTION** — a 215× ttm / 331× static P/E and 13–15× P/S on a company with only an 11.80%
FY2025 gross margin, negative operating cash flow now spanning FY2025 and Q1-2026, an active SSE regulatory
inquiry into profit-cash-flow divergence, and a Huawei/Ascend-linked revenue ceiling of at most 17.70% (itself an
unquantified minority-share bucket shared with Hygon/telecom/memory-packaging demand) demands years of
uninterrupted hypergrowth and margin re-rating with essentially no discount for any of the flagged risks.
