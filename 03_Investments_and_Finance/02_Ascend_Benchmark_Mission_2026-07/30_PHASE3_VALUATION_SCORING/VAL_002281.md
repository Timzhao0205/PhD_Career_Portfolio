## Implied expectations

At CNY221.67/share and CNY183.45bn total market cap (2026-07-07 post-close [S628;S629]), Accelink
trades at 177.05x TTM P/E (TTM attributable net profit CNY1,036.13M) and 14.70x TTM P/S (TTM
revenue CNY12,479.37M) [S449;S458;S161;S167;S630] — multiples that require several more years of
something close to Accelink's own best-ever growth just to look reasonable: at FY2025's own
+44.20% revenue growth [S161], a 177x trailing P/E implies a PEG-style ratio near 4.0x
(177.05/44.20), and even using Q1 2026's re-accelerated +59.76% attributable-profit growth [S167]
the implied ratio is still ~3.0x (177.05/59.76) — rich by conventional growth-adjusted yardsticks
even before asking whether growth continues, given that revenue growth itself has already begun
decelerating (Q1 2026 +24.79% vs. FY2025 +44.20% [S167;S161]). The genuine, verifiable part of the
bull case is real: Accelink's global optical-component market share rose from an estimated 5.3%
(#5 worldwide, as of Q1 2025 [S630]) to 5.9% (#5 worldwide, #4 in datacom optics specifically, FY2025
[S161]), amid what the company itself frames as a broad domestic-算力(compute)-capex wave —
"国产算力的需求会带动光模块及其他产品的需求增加" ("demand for domestic compute power will drive
increased demand for optical modules and other products") [S162, V_002281.md] — backed by a
CNY3.5bn placement devoting 59.5% of proceeds to an AI-compute-center optical-capacity project
[S171, per V_002281.md]. But the single largest, most specific driver of upside now circulating —
an "exclusive" Huawei/Ascend supernode-optics role worth an estimated 35%-40% of FY2025 revenue
(28% claimed for FY2024, same unsourced post) — is tier-C, forum-only evidence [S172] that the
company itself declined to confirm when asked the
exact question point-blank ("是华为昇腾生态的核心光模块供应商吗" / "is the company a core
optical-module supplier in Huawei's Ascend ecosystem" → a non-answer about generic domestic-compute
demand instead [S162, V_002281.md]), and it sits atop a diversified customer base (~71% 数据与接入
segment [S169], anonymized top-5 customer concentration 48.49% FY2025 [S160], no single customer
nameable as Huawei) that is structurally unlike a single-customer bet. (Search-only color, NOT
fetched, NOT load-bearing: several sell-side/aggregator price targets located this session, e.g. a
CNY30 target and a ~CNY101 average 12-month target, sit far below today's CNY221.67, and an
implied FY2026-consensus forward P/E near 117x was found even off those older, lower price levels
— internally inconsistent across outlets and not independently fetch-confirmed, so no number from
this thread is used above, but directionally it is consistent with a price that has run well ahead
of pre-existing institutional coverage.) For today's multiple to be earned rather than merely
carried by momentum, the market needs both the verified broad domestic-AI-optics upcycle to keep
compounding near its current pace for years and Accelink to convert a historically thin,
diversified-customer-base optical-component margin (~7.93% FY2025 net margin, attributable basis
[F_002281.md]) into a durably higher-margin franchise — while any Huawei/Ascend-specific kicker
beyond that broad upcycle remains exactly what it is today: tier-C and explicitly unconfirmed by
the company. Flag: **PRICED-FOR-PERFECTION** — a ~177x TTM P/E and ~14.7x TTM P/S against an
already-decelerating revenue-growth rate and a still-single-digit net margin prices in several more
years of Accelink's own best-ever growth, leaning in part on a Huawei/Ascend-specific narrative
that remains tier-C forum-only and explicitly unconfirmed by the company itself.
