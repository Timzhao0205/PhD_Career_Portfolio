[F_300308.md, S484/S205] off an FY2024 base that itself grew +122.64%/+137.93% [F_300308.md, S485/S207],
so today's multiple is being paid against a THIRD consecutive year of triple-or-near-triple-digit growth,
and for the multiple to normalize toward levels comparable with slower-growing hardware peers,
overseas-hyperscaler-driven (Microsoft/Amazon/Meta/Google-adjacent, per V_300308.md) demand for 1.6T
optics — already 90.58% of FY2025 revenue and RISING (up from 86.81% in FY2024) [F_300308.md, S205/S207]
— needs to keep compounding at a high rate for several more years. Critically, essentially none of that
required forward growth can plausibly come from the Huawei/Ascend-linked domestic thesis this mission
otherwise tracks: domestic revenue is a bounded CEILING of ≤9.42% of FY2025 revenue (not an Ascend-specific
figure) and itself FELL in share terms YoY (13.19%→9.42%) [V_300308.md], and the company's own IR
language states that even within that shrinking domestic slice, demand skews toward legacy 400G/800G
rather than 1.6T [V_300308.md, S209/S210] — so the Ascend/domestic story can, at best, contribute a
rounding error to this valuation, not carry it. The overseas concentration this price actually depends on
is precisely where the newest geopolitical risk sits: the 2026-06-08 US DoD Section-1260H "Chinese
military company" listing [S213] names exactly the Microsoft/Google/Meta/Amazon/Nvidia customer
relationships behind 90%+ of revenue, with the reported near-term risk being a slow-moving customer
supply-chain "de-risking" process rather than an immediate sales bar — a risk this price does not appear
to discount.
