## 3. Verticalization test (Huawei internalizes the layer)

If Huawei internalizes supernode optics, this thesis does not degrade — it evaporates, because the
internalization is not hypothetical: Huawei already fields a full HiSilicon optical-transceiver
product line spanning datacom, telecom, FTTx, wireless and transmission [S708], and in March 2025 it

[S711], with no third-party module vendor named anywhere in the launch material. The in-house HBM
precedent (RB_02 read #3) shows the pattern: when a layer is strategic to supernode economics, Huawei
absorbs it, and RB_02 read #2 says interconnect is the MOST strategic layer in a systems-over-die
architecture — the worst possible layer for an external supplier to bet on Huawei's restraint. The
arithmetic makes the downside trivially small but the upside identically small: Ascend-linked revenue
is already bounded at ≤9.42% of revenue and falling, skewed to legacy 400G/800G [S205, S209, S210],
so full internalization costs Innolight at most a low-single-digit revenue slice — but it also
deletes the entire remaining in-universe justification for the name (W1 collapses to a debunked
lead, W2 to verified ~0), leaving a pure Nvidia-chain stock ranked #2 in an Ascend benchmark. The
mirror-image verticalization is the one that actually moves the P&L: the overseas customer that sets
