## Implied expectations

At ¥26.82/share and a ¥27.27bn market cap, 000034 trades at ≈49.9x trailing (ttm) earnings and
≈52.1x FY2025 earnings, alongside a much lower ≈0.18x trailing P/S — the classic signature of a
thin-margin distributor (FY2025 net margin 0.36%, down from 0.98% in FY2023; FY2025 attributable
profit fell 30.5% YoY on heavy AI R&D spend, lower government subsidies, and an unrelated
real-estate-JV impairment) being priced for a materially different future than its consolidated
numbers currently show. FY2025 revenue did grow a healthy +12.16% YoY, and Q1-2026 shows genuine
reacceleration (revenue +27.6% YoY, AI-linked revenue +119% YoY to ¥15.5bn), but consolidated
net-profit growth remains barely positive (Q1-2026 NP +8.6% YoY) and both gross margin
(3.99%→4.21%→3.40%) and net margin have compressed, not expanded, across FY2023-FY2025. The one
segment growing fast **and** at a better (though still modest) margin is 神州鲲泰, the Kunpeng
(CPU)/Ascend (NPU)-based own-brand hardware line — only ≈5% (5.18%) of FY2025 revenue but +62.4%
YoY, and running roughly double the group's net margin (≈1.04% vs. ≈0.55% for the core IT
distribution segment that is ~94% of revenue). Today's ~50x trailing multiple is therefore
effectively a bet that this small Kunpeng/Ascend sliver keeps compounding at a 50%+ pace for
years and eventually re-rates the whole company's margin structure — the 94%-of-revenue
distribution core alone would typically command a single-digit-to-low-teens P/E, not 50x.

**Flag: PRICED-FOR-PERFECTION** — a ~50x trailing-earnings multiple on a business whose
consolidated net margin is under 0.4% and whose attributable profit just fell 30.5% YoY requires
the ~5%-of-revenue Ascend/Kunpeng own-brand story to scale far faster and further than anything
yet visible in the +8.6% Q1-2026 consolidated net-profit print.
