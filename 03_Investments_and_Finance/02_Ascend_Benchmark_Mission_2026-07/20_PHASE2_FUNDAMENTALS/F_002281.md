## Conflict log: FY2025 net profit 9.46亿 [S161] vs 9.32亿 [S169]

V_002281.md flagged this exact conflict (per SOURCE_STANDARDS rule 5: record both, do not
average, prefer the more primary) without resolving its cause. **This session's own fetch
of the underlying Sina income-statement page [S449] resolves it directly:** the FY2025
income statement carries TWO distinct net-profit line items —
- 净利润 (net profit, TOTAL, includes minority/non-controlling interest) = 93,185.71万元
  = CNY 931.86M — matches already-ledgered [S169]'s "9.32亿" (932M) to within 0.14M
  (rounding).
- 归属于母公司所有者的净利润 (net profit ATTRIBUTABLE to parent-company shareholders only)
  = 94,632.08万元 = CNY 946.32M — matches already-ledgered [S161]'s "9.46亿" (946M) to
  within 0.32M (rounding).

**Both cited figures are correct under their own definitions; this is not a data error.**
The ~14.46 CNY M gap (946.32 − 931.86) is minority interest attributable to
non-controlling shareholders of partly-owned subsidiaries for FY2025 — not noise, not a
mistake, and not something to average. This file uses the ATTRIBUTABLE-to-parent basis
(CNY 946.32M / "9.46亿", [S161;S449;S458], all three independently agreeing to the
hundred-thousand-yuan) as PRIMARY throughout (standard equity-analysis convention, ties to
EPS), and reports the total-incl.-minority basis (CNY 931.86M ≈ "9.32亿", matching [S169])
as a labeled alternate in the table above. [S169] remains separately flagged as
AI-auto-generated per V_002281.md; its net-profit figure is now understood to be an
internally-valid TOTAL-basis figure, not a wrong number — it was simply reporting a
different (correct) line item than [S161] did, without stating which basis it used.

## Methodology note

This ticker was explicitly flagged in advance (per the task brief and V_002281.md) as
having unreliable aggregator data and a documented history of WebFetch summarization
errors. This session's approach was accordingly conservative: every load-bearing figure
was sought from ≥2 independently-fetched pipelines (Sina statement pages + Eastmoney
datacenter-web JSON API) before being trusted, and any single-pipeline ratio/summary column
(as opposed to a raw absolute figure) was treated as unverified until cross-checked by
arithmetic on independently-fetched absolutes. This discipline caught 5 distinct errors
(see Data-quality catches above) that a single-pass extraction would have propagated,
including one (catch #2) that would have reported FY2023 revenue as GROWING (+1.75%) when
it in fact DECLINED (-12.31%) — a sign-flip-adjacent error with real analytical
consequence had it gone uncaught.
