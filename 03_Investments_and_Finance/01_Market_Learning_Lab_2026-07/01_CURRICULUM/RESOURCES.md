# RESOURCES — vetted list (keep short; add only after finishing something)

## Foundations
- Bogleheads wiki — index-fund baseline, terminology. Free.
- Investopedia — definitions + free paper-trading simulator (US tickers).
- Bogle, *The Little Book of Common Sense Investing* — the null hypothesis.
- Lynch, *One Up on Wall Street* — circle-of-competence investing, readable.
- Graham, *The Intelligent Investor* — read ch. 8 (Mr. Market) and ch. 20 (margin of
  safety) only; skim the rest.

## Statements & valuation
- Ittelson, *Financial Statements* — gentlest complete intro.
- Damodaran (NYU Stern) — free lecture videos, notes, and valuation spreadsheets;
  the reference standard. pages.stern.nyu.edu/~adamodar
- Primary filings: SEC EDGAR (US), HKEXnews (HK), cninfo 巨潮资讯网 (A-shares),
  SSE/SZSE announcement pages, 互动易 / 上证e互动 (official investor Q&A — companies'
  own words on rumors; excellent for tier upgrades).

## Semiconductors / AI compute
- Miller, *Chip War* — geopolitical + industry map.
- TrendForce, SemiAnalysis, IEEE Spectrum — industry analysis (Tier 3; IEEE Spectrum is
  the designated-priority trade source in this portfolio).
- Huawei annual reports (huawei.com/cn/annual-report) — Tier 2 primary for the case study.
- SIA / SEMI data releases — industry-level shipments and capex.

## Market data (observation only — no accounts needed)
- TradingView — charts incl. A-shares/HK; free tier sufficient.
- 东方财富 eastmoney.com — A-share quotes, filings, data (Tier 3 for its news; quotes fine).
- Yahoo Finance — US/HK quotes.
- 雪球 xueqiu.com — sentiment and leads ONLY (Tier 4; never load-bearing).

## For Claude Code missions later
- Python: `pandas`, `yfinance` (US/HK), `akshare` (A-shares, free), `matplotlib`.
- Keep everything on-demand scripts + CSV. No databases until the CSVs actually hurt.

## Anti-resources (deliberately excluded)
- Day-trading courses, signal groups, "AI stock-picker" apps, price-prediction models.
  This lab trains judgment and calibration, not tick forecasting — as a PhD student you
  can verify: markets at short horizons are ~efficient noise for retail participants.
